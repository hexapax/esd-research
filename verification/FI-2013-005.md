## Corrections Needed

1. **DATE IS WRONG.** The original file states "July 4, 2013." Multiple independent sources (FOX8, Greensboro News & Record, Fox News, and the obituary) confirm the incident occurred on **July 16, 2013** (Tuesday evening). The date should be corrected from "July 4, 2013" to "July 16, 2013."

2. **Location should be more specific.** The original says "Davidson County NC." While technically correct, the more precise location is "Brookside Swim Club, Lexington, Davidson County, North Carolina." The pool was not a residential pool but a community swim club.

3. **Description needs technical clarification.** The original says "downed power line near pool energized ladder." This is accurate but incomplete. The fire marshal's investigation revealed that a failed connector on a power pole caused the line to fall, and the electrical current traveled through saturated ground to the pool area, where it energized the metal ladder. The mechanism was ground current from the downed line, not direct contact with the line itself.

4. **Victim identification.** The original refers to "11yo girl" but does not name her. Independent sources identify her as Lauren Suzanne Cecil, age 11, of Lexington, North Carolina.

5. **This is NOT technically an "Electric Shock Drowning" case.** Traditional ESD involves electrical current in the water from marina/dock electrical systems, boat wiring, or shore power. This case involved a utility power line failure that sent current through the ground to a swimming pool. While the mechanism (electricity in water causing death) is similar, the electrical source is different. This is more accurately classified as an electrocution in a pool caused by external electrical infrastructure failure, rather than a classic ESD case involving watercraft or dock electrical systems.
