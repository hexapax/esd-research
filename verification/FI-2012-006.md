## Corrections Needed

1. **CRITICAL DATE ERROR.** The original file states "July 25, 2012." Multiple independent sources consistently confirm the incident occurred on **July 25, 2011**. The year must be corrected from 2012 to 2011. This is a one-year error.

2. **Description is misleading regarding gender.** The original says "14yo boy and girl." All independent sources confirm **both victims were girls** (Hannah Kendall and Jade Garza, both 14 years old). There was no boy victim. This should be corrected to "two 14-year-old girls" or similar.

3. **Classification as ESD is questionable.** This incident did not occur in or near a body of water used for swimming, boating, or recreation. The victims were electrocuted in an agricultural field while working. They stepped into a pool of water (likely rainwater or irrigation runoff) that had become electrified due to a faulty irrigation system meter. While technically involving water and electricity, this is more accurately classified as an agricultural/workplace electrocution rather than an "Electric Shock Drowning" incident. ESD typically refers to electrocutions in lakes, marinas, docks, and swimming areas. This incident should potentially be reclassified or removed from the ESD database.

4. **Cause description could be more precise.** The original states "faulty irrigation pump post-lightning strike." The more precise cause, based on investigation reports, was that lightning struck a meter box associated with the center pivot irrigation system, causing the electrical system and components to become energized, releasing electrical current into standing water in the field. The issue was the meter/electrical system, not specifically the pump.

5. **Missing critical context.** The original file does not mention that eight other workers were injured (one critically), which is significant for understanding the scope of the incident.
