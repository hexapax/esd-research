## Corrections Needed

1. **DATE IS WRONG.** The original file states "August 8, 2010." Multiple independent sources (Poynter and HuffPost) confirm the incident occurred on **July 10, 2010**. The date should be corrected to July 10, 2010.

2. **Victim identities.** The original file says "a woman was shocked" and "a man was shocked trying to help her." The independent sources reveal:
   - First victim: **Beth Waite** (woman swimming behind houseboat)
   - First rescuer: **John Childress** (Beth's boyfriend, who felt shocks but avoided full incapacitation by staying on the boat ramp)
   - Second rescuer/second shock victim: **Bobby Gullett** (houseboat owner, who was severely shocked during rescue attempt after power was supposedly turned off)

3. **Sequence of events more complex than described.** The original file makes it sound like both the woman and the helping man were shocked, then pulled out with a rope after power was turned off. The actual sequence was more complex:
   - Beth Waite was shocked while swimming and began drowning
   - John Childress felt shocks from the boat ramp and avoided entering the water
   - Bobby Gullett turned off the boat power
   - Bobby Gullett jumped in to rescue Beth but was shocked by voltage still present in the water
   - Both Beth Waite and Bobby Gullett were then pulled from the water (method not clearly specified, but original file mentions rope)

4. **Critical detail: Power shutoff didn't stop the voltage.** The original file says "Both were pulled out with a rope after turning off the boat power," which implies turning off the power solved the problem. The independent sources emphasize that Bobby Gullett was shocked **after** the power was turned off, indicating the voltage persisted in the water even after the houseboat's electrical system was supposedly de-energized. This is an important safety detail.

5. **Outcome.** The original file says "Both were treated and released." The independent sources confirm both received emergency medical care but don't explicitly confirm both were "released" (as opposed to being admitted). The original file's statement may be accurate but cannot be fully verified from the available sources.
