## Corrections Needed

1. **DATE DISCREPANCY RESOLVED.** The original file notes a discrepancy between June 27, 2012 (listed in the ESD section) and July 4, 2012 (listed in the NM section). All independent sources unanimously confirm the incident occurred on **July 4, 2012**. The June 27 date is incorrect. The incident should be listed as July 4, 2012.

2. **Death timeline clarification.** While both boys died as a result of the July 4 incident, they did not die simultaneously:
   - Noah Dean Winstead, 10, died on July 4, 2012 (the day of the incident)
   - Nate Lynam, 11, died on July 5, 2012, after being on life support at East Tennessee Children's Hospital

   This detail should be included for accuracy.

3. **Victim identification missing.** The original file should include the victims' names: Noah Dean Winstead (age 10) and Nate Lynam (age 11), both of Morristown, Tennessee.

4. **Additional victims not mentioned.** The original file states "Dead: 2" but does not mention that Nate Lynam's sister (8 years old) was also shocked and hospitalized overnight, and his brother was present. While she survived, this context is important for understanding the full scope of the incident.

5. **Time of incident.** News reports indicate the boys were shocked around 2:20 p.m. on July 4, 2012. This detail could be added.

6. **Location within marina.** The boys were swimming between two boats (houseboats) and were shocked while playing near a metal ladder on one of the boats. This detail clarifies the specific location within the marina.

7. **Cause is accurate but can be elaborated.** The description "killed in water between two houseboats" is accurate. The electrical source was frayed/faulty wiring aboard a houseboat. The lack of ground fault circuit interrupters at the marina compounded the hazard by allowing the electrical fault to energize the water without automatic shutdown.

8. **Legislative impact should be noted.** This incident led to the passage of Tennessee's "Noah Dean and Nate Act" in 2014, which created comprehensive marina electrical safety requirements including mandatory GFCI installation, state fire marshal inspections, and warning signage. This represents one of the most significant legislative outcomes of any ESD incident.
