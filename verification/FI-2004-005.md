# FI-2004-005 — Research Notes

## Incident Summary (from source PDF)
A 10-year-old boy drowned near a dock boat lift at Lake Waccamaw, North Carolina on June 5, 2004. The boat lift frame was energized due to an improperly connected bonding conductor, causing an electric shock drowning.

## Sources Found

- **Title:** The Case of the Floating Dock | EC&M
- **Date:** Not specified (article analyzing the incident)
- **Summary:** Provides detailed technical analysis of a June 19, 2004 Lake Waccamaw incident where a grandfather raised a boat lift, inadvertently exposing a live 120V line. A 10-year-old boy was electrocuted when hanging from metal rails with feet in water. Investigation found either motor had internal short to casing or water-filled junction box created conductive path between hot and ground wires.

## Conflicting Information

## Additional Details Found
From technical analysis: Grandfather was present and initially dismissed children's reports of feeling "tingles" in water. After confirming electrical shock by touching rails himself, ordered children out. Boy was found floating face down. Technical cause involved either motor short or water infiltration in junction box creating path between hot wire and improperly connected ground wire.
