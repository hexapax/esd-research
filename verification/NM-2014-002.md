# Research: NM-2014-002 — Lake of the Ozarks Electric Shock Near Miss (2014)

## Original Incident Summary

Per the ESDPA-derived file, on July 4, 2014, people were feeling tingles at the 7 mile marker on Lake of the Ozarks, Missouri. Turning off dock power didn't help. The source was a short 100 yards away at an abandoned boat ramp.

## Corrections Needed

1. **Victim count.** The original file states "Dead: 0, Injured: 0, Shocked/survived: multiple." This is accurate. Independent sources confirm "several people" felt the shock and all safely exited the water with no reported injuries.

2. **Abandoned boat ramp description is partially accurate.** The original file describes the source as "an abandoned boat ramp." The independent sources provide more detail: it was a ramp where a dock had been disconnected years prior, but the electrical service was never properly disconnected. The breaker was simply turned off and the wire was wrapped around the ramp frame. Someone later turned the breaker back on, energizing the ramp. While "abandoned" captures the general idea, "improperly decommissioned dock ramp" would be more technically accurate.

3. **"Turning off dock power didn't help" is confirmed.** The original file states that turning off dock power didn't resolve the issue, which is confirmed by independent sources noting the source was 100 yards away at a different location.

4. **Additional context available.** The original file does not mention:
   - The 120-volt level of the current in the water
   - The duration the hazard existed (estimated one month)
   - The specific mechanism: breaker turned off but not disconnected, wire wrapped around ramp frame
   - The historical context: this near-miss occurred on the same holiday (July 4) as a fatal 2012 incident at the same lake
   - The fact that no injuries occurred because swimmers recognized the danger

5. **Classification is correct.** This was appropriately classified as a "Near Miss" - multiple people were shocked but all survived without reported injuries.

6. **Date and location are accurate.** July 4, 2014, at the 7 Mile Marker of Lake of the Ozarks, Missouri, is confirmed by independent sources.
