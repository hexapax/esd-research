# Research: FI-2010-001 — Lake City / Lake Pepin Boat Lift Electrocution (2011, not 2010)

## Original Incident Summary

Per the ESDPA-derived file, on July 26, 2010, a 50-year-old man was electrocuted jumping in to rescue his dog near a boat lift with exposed wiring at Lake City, MN / Lake Pepin. One death.

**CRITICAL NOTE:** The date in the raw file is wrong by nearly a year. All independent sources place this incident on **May 28, 2011**.

## Corrections Needed

1. **DATE IS WRONG BY NEARLY A YEAR.** The original file states "July 26, 2010." All independent evidence — Find a Grave death record (May 28, 2011), the forum discussion timeline, and family obituary context (brother died February 2011, mother died March 2011, both preceding Brian's May 2011 death) — converges on **May 28, 2011**. The file ID (FI-2010-001) reflects the wrong year.

2. **"Exposed wiring" is broadly correct but imprecise.** The Goodhue County Sheriff's Office account describes an "electrical track running underwater to the shore" on the boat lift. This is a more specific description of the electrical fault than "exposed wiring," though both refer to faulty electrical infrastructure on the boat lift.

3. **A second person was also shocked.** The raw file does not mention that another person jumped in first, was shocked, and escaped the water before Harwick attempted his rescue. This additional detail is relevant to the incident record.
