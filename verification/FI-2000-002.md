# Research: FI-2000-002

## Incident Summary (from source file)

- **Date:** September 30, 2000
- **Location:** Put-in-Bay, OH
- **Outcome:** 1 dead
- **Description:** A young bystander disappeared while trying to retrieve sunglasses that went overboard near a Grand Banks 42 at Put-in-Bay, Ohio. Electric shock drowning was the cause.

## Independent Source Search

### Search Strategy

Extensive searches were conducted across multiple platforms and source types:
- News article searches (local Ohio newspapers including Sandusky Register, Port Clinton News Herald; Cleveland TV stations; wire services)
- OSHA fatality/accident database searches
- USCG marine casualty report searches
- Court record and lawsuit searches
- NFPA, CSB, and ODNR investigation searches
- Wikipedia and academic source searches
- General web searches with various keyword combinations including the victim description, boat type (Grand Banks 42), location (Put-in-Bay, Crew's Nest), and circumstances (sunglasses, electrocution, drowning)

### Results: No Valid Independent Sources Found

Despite extensive searching, **no independent primary sources** (news articles, investigation reports, court records, or academic papers) were found that independently document this specific September 30, 2000 incident at Put-in-Bay.

Every source that mentions this incident traces back to the Shafer/Rifkin ESD incident list maintained by the Electric Shock Drowning Prevention Association (ESDPA), or to sites that republish that same list. These excluded sources include:
- The ESDPA incident list PDF (electricshockdrowning.org)
- Mike Holt forums/documents (mikeholt.com)
- AuGres Maritime Museum ESD victim summary (augresmaritimemuseum.org) -- hosts a copy of the Shafer/Rifkin list
- Various ESD awareness sites that appear to republish data from the same list

- **URL:** https://www.visitputinbay.org/electrocuted-at-put-in-bay/

- **Limitation:** The full article text could not be extracted via automated tools due to the page being heavily JavaScript-rendered. The quoted passage appeared in web search result snippets but could not be independently confirmed from the rendered page content.
- **Assessment:** This appears to be local community knowledge rather than a sourced journalistic or investigative report. The reference to "the Crew's Nest" (a marina/private club at Put-in-Bay) as the location is a detail **not present** in the ESDPA incident file, which only says "Put-in-Bay."

- **Cleveland19 (WOIO):** Reported the incident without referencing any prior Ohio ESD deaths.
  - URL: https://www.cleveland19.com/story/35687986/19-year-old-dies-after-electrocution-accident-on-put-in-bay/

- **News5 Cleveland (WEWS):** Cited the ESDPA as having "tracked 77 deaths associated to ESD" nationally but did not reference any prior Ohio cases.
  - URL: https://www.news5cleveland.com/news/local-news/oh-cuyahoga/teens-death-surfaces-the-scary-truth-about-electric-shock-drowning

- **WCBE (Columbus NPR affiliate):** Reported ODNR found "a faulty cord or outlet led to the electrocution."
  - URL: http://www.wcbe.org/post/odnr-faulty-electrical-equipment-led-death-dublin-man-ohio-marina

- **EC&M (trade publication):** Provided details of the incident without referencing prior Ohio cases.
  - URL: https://www.ecmweb.com/safety/shock-electrocution/article/20902758/ohio-teen-electrocuted-in-water-near-familys-boat

If the 2017 Evan Currie death was indeed "the first electric-shock drowning ever reported in the state of Ohio," this raises significant questions about the FI-2000-002 incident:
1. Was the 2000 incident not officially reported or classified as an ESD at the time?
2. Was it only retrospectively identified as an ESD by Shafer/Rifkin during their research?
3. Was the original cause of death classified differently (e.g., simple drowning)?
4. Is the date or location in the ESDPA record possibly inaccurate?

### Additional Context: The 2017 Put-in-Bay ESD (Evan Currie)

For reference, the well-documented 2017 incident involved:
- **Victim:** Evan Currie, 19, Xavier University student, Dublin, OH
- **Date:** June 16, 2017, approximately 6:30 PM
- **Location:** Miller Marina, Bayview Ave (later reports say Langram Road), Put-in-Bay
- **Boat:** 33-foot Sea Ray Sundancer
- **Circumstances:** Family dog fell into water after boat was connected to shore power; father Jeffrey Currie jumped in and was shocked; both sons entered water to help; mother unplugged shore power cord at bystanders' direction
- **ODNR finding:** Faulty cord or outlet caused the electrocution
- **Sources:** Extensively covered by Cleveland19, News5 Cleveland, WCPO, Fox 8, ABC6, WTOL, WCBE, EC&M, Cleveland Scene, and others

This incident is clearly distinct from FI-2000-002 (different year, different boat type, different circumstances, different marina).

## Corrections Needed

1. **Unverifiable incident:** No independent primary sources (news reports, investigation records, court documents) were found to confirm this incident occurred. The only sources are the ESDPA list and its derivatives. This does not mean the incident did not happen, but it cannot be independently verified from publicly available records.

2. **Possible tension with "first ESD in Ohio" reporting:** If the 2017 Evan Currie death was truly the first reported ESD in Ohio (as stated by WTOL), then either: (a) the 2000 incident was not reported/classified as an ESD at the time, (b) the WTOL characterization was inaccurate, or (c) there is an error in the ESDPA record for this incident. This warrants further investigation.

3. **Location specificity:** The visitputinbay.org reference (if reliable) places the incident "in front of the Crew's Nest," which is a specific marina/club on Put-in-Bay. The incident file does not include this level of detail. The Crew's Nest is a different facility from Miller Marina (where the 2017 Currie incident occurred).

4. **Mechanism of death:** The visitputinbay.org reference suggests the victim "grabbed the boat while in the water and was immediately electrocuted," implying direct contact electrocution rather than electric shock drowning (where the current in the water itself causes muscular paralysis). This distinction could affect the classification.

5. **Date uncertainty:** The incident file states September 30, 2000 as the date, but the ESDPA list's own classification notes acknowledge uncertainty (item number is "inferred" from position in the list), and the visitputinbay.org reference gives no date at all. The specific date cannot be independently confirmed.

6. **Victim description:** The incident file describes "a young bystander" while the visitputinbay.org reference says "a young man." Neither provides a name, making independent verification extremely difficult.
