## Technical Analysis

### Significance of 4 amps leakage with 8 VAC hull potential:

This represents an **extremely dangerous condition**:

1. **4 amps of leakage current:**
   - Normal boat leakage should be well under 30 milliamps (0.030 amps)
   - 4 amps is more than 130 times the normal safe level
   - This level of current in fresh water can easily create lethal voltage gradients
   - Indicates major electrical fault, not just minor deterioration

2. **8 VAC hull potential:**
   - In fresh water, as little as 5-10 VAC can cause skeletal muscle paralysis
   - A swimmer touching the hull or swimming near it could be paralyzed
   - The child who reported tingling was very fortunate to have only experienced minor symptoms
   - This voltage indicates the hull was energized relative to the water

### Likely causes:
- **Reverse polarity or neutral-ground fault** in shore power connection
- **Damaged shore power cord** with insulation breakdown
- **Faulty onboard inverter** (as suggested by the detailed source mentioning inverter neutral/ground fault)
- **Lack of galvanic isolator** allowing AC current to flow through bonding system
- **Missing or failed GFCI protection** on shore power

### Why the child survived:
- Brief contact time (child immediately left the water and reported tingling)
- May not have contacted the hull directly (experienced voltage gradient in water)
- Fresh water conditions vary (minerals, temperature affect conductivity)
- Child's size and position in water may have limited current flow through vital organs
