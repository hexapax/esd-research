## Corrections Needed

1. **DATE IS WRONG.** The original file states "May 20, 2013." All six independent sources (Lexington Herald-Leader x2, Commonwealth Journal/Firehouse.com, Sentinel-Echo, and the Bowling Funeral Home obituary) confirm the incident occurred on **June 30, 2013** (Sunday). The date should be corrected from "May 20, 2013" to "June 30, 2013."

2. **Victim identification.** The original does not name the victim. Independent sources identify him as **Kevin Curtis Short, age 34, of Lily, Kentucky** (specifically Robinson Creek Road). He was employed as an area manager at the Walmart Distribution Center in London, Kentucky, and was a member of the National Guard.

3. **Child's age is slightly different.** The original states "2yo son" but the obituary indicates Short had three children (Kailee, Kevin Christopher, and Jakob). While news reports describe "his child" or "his son," the specific age of 2 years old was not confirmed in the sources found. The obituary does not list ages for the children. The description "young child" or "young son" may be more accurate than specifically "2yo."

4. **More complete description of the electrical failure.** The original states "frayed wire" which is accurate, but additional details are available:
   - An **extension cord** was being used to power a **fish finder** device (per Commonwealth Journal) or a **light** at the dock (per Herald-Leader) -- sources differ on the purpose of the cord
   - The cord had **slipped behind the ladder** and rubbed against a **sharp rock**, causing the insulation to wear through and expose the wiring
   - Alternatively, Short's weight on the ladder **pushed the ladder down onto the cord**, which had a nick in it (Herald-Leader account from Officer King)
   - The ladder was **aluminum**, which conducted the electricity when energized by the frayed cord
   - The voltage was **110 volts** (standard household current), which normally wouldn't be immediately fatal but was lethal due to the victim being in the water
   - Cause of death was ruled **low-voltage electrocution** sufficient to stop Short's heart
   - **Other family members received shocks** when they tried to pull Short away from the ladder (Herald-Leader detail not in other sources)

5. **Heroic action should be emphasized.** The original mentions "2yo son moved to safety" but doesn't clearly indicate Short's heroic action. News reports specifically state that **Short pushed his son past the ladder to safety before succumbing to the electrocution himself.** This was a deliberate, selfless act in his final moments.

6. **Location details.** The original description is accurate but could be more specific: The incident occurred at a **private dock approximately eight miles out Garland Bend Road** in southern Pulaski County, near Burnside, on Lake Cumberland.

7. **Legislative impact.** Short's death was cited in the Kentucky legislature as one of the motivating incidents for House Bill 107 (the Samantha Chipley Act), which sought to regulate swimming near electrified docks and require ground-fault protection at marinas.
