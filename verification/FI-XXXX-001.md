3. **Location could be more specific.** The original says "Lake Freeman, Carroll County IN." The specific location was **Angel Shores Resort (also called Angel Shores Mobile Home Park) on Lake Freeman, Carroll County, Indiana**. The incident occurred at a home leased by Bryan and Donna Wagner within the resort community.

4. **Incident description is generally accurate.** The original states "13yo fell back in water." Court records and news coverage confirm that Crays was swimming with three other children when they started to feel a tingle. The 13-year-old attempted to exit the water at the dock but fell back into the water. The other children managed to exit the water. This matches the "fell back in water" description.

5. **Electrical source is confirmed.** The original correctly identifies "water-soaked junction box on dock" as the source. News reports confirm that Carroll County REMC crews found a water-soaked electrical junction box at the scene, and officials said it may have conducted electric currents through the metal on the dock. Court records indicate the electrical system was faulty.

6. **Cause of death mechanism.** Investigators determined Crays was shocked by electricity before he drowned — a classic electric shock drowning scenario. The electricity immobilized him in the water, causing him to drown.

7. **Legal outcome provides important context.** The wrongful death lawsuit resulted in a $3 million jury award, with fault assigned 95% to the property lessee (Bryan Wagner) and 5% to the property owner (Angel Shores). This indicates significant negligence in maintaining safe electrical systems near the water.

8. **Classification is appropriate.** This is a clear case of electric shock drowning (ESD) — the victim was immobilized by electricity in the water and drowned as a result. The water-soaked junction box on the dock created a hazardous electrical field in the water.
