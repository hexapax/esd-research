## Corrections Needed

1. **DATE IS COMPLETELY WRONG.** The original file states "Apr 2, 2019." Multiple independent sources (ABC10, CBS Sacramento, Fox40, KSBY, Today, Inside Edition) all confirm the incident occurred on **Sunday, July 14, 2019**, not April 2, 2019. 2. **Age discrepancy.** The original file states "9 year old girl." Most sources confirm she was 9, but some sources (KSBY, Inside Edition) say she was 10. This discrepancy likely depends on her recent birthday. The predominant reporting says 9 years old, so the original is likely correct on this point.

3. **Victim identification.** The original file does not name the victim. She was McKenzie Kinley, age 9 (or possibly 10), of Citrus Heights, California.

4. **Location clarification.** The original says "at her home." More precisely, it was at her father's home in Citrus Heights. This distinction suggests her parents were separated/divorced and she may not have lived there full-time.

5. **Number of other children.** The original says "Four other children in the pool were not injured." Most sources confirm this, though one source (Inside Edition) says "three friends" were with her. The "four others" count appears to be correct based on multiple source confirmation.

6. **Mechanism of injury.** The original file accurately states she "grabbed a wire attached to one of the underwater pool lights that were under repair." More specifically:
   - She touched a "hot wire" (electrically live wire) on a recessed pool light
   - The light fixture was not properly sealed
