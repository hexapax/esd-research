## Corrections Needed

1. **CLASSIFICATION IS WRONG — THIS WAS A DOUBLE FATALITY, NOT A NEAR MISS.** The raw record states "All survived: 13yo boy saved, 2 adults shocked but recovered." In reality, both adults — **Donna Berger (55) and Randy Freeney (74)** — died on June 17, 2016 at Chickamauga Lake. Only the 13-year-old boy (Zachary Berger) survived. This should be reclassified as a Fatal Incident (FI), not a Near Miss (NM). The raw record appears to have misread the WSOC-TV article, conflating the boy's survival with the adults' outcome.

2. **BOTH DATES ARE WRONG.** Neither May 29, 2016 (ESD section) nor July 3, 2016 (NM section) is correct. The obituary and multiple news reports confirm the incident occurred on **June 17, 2016**. The July 19, 2016 WSOC-TV article publication date may explain the July 3 date (approximate recall of when the article appeared).

3. **LOCATION IS WELL-DOCUMENTED, NOT "UNKNOWN."** The raw record says "A Tennessee Lake, Tennessee (exact location unknown)." The location is well-documented by multiple Chattanooga-area news outlets as **Chickamauga Lake, near Sale Creek, Hamilton County, Tennessee**.

4. **ELECTRIC SHOCK DROWNING IS UNCONFIRMED.** The ESD theory originates solely from Annette Goodman's belief, as reported by WSOC-TV. The Tennessee Wildlife Resource Agency (TWRA) investigated and described this as a standard double drowning. No official source — not the TWRA, not any of the four Chattanooga-area news outlets — mentions electric shock as a cause. The official accounts describe both victims as struggling in the water without flotation devices.
