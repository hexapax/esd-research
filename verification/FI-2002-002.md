# Research: FI-2002-002

## Incident Summary (from original file)

- **Date:** March 2002
- **Location:** Lake Cumberland, Monticello, KY
- **Outcome:** 2 dead (mother and daughter)
- **Description:** Double drowning of a mother and daughter at Lake Cumberland, Monticello, Kentucky. A 125V plug had been rewired for 220V with the ground wire cut off.
- **Source:** "Electric Shock Drowning Incidents -- Marinas(c)" by James D. Shafer & Capt. David E. Rifkin, Quality Marine Services, LLC -- Rev. 8/15/2025

## Independent Source Research

### Source 1: EC&M Magazine -- "The Case of the Hot Marina"

- **URL:** https://www.ecmweb.com/content/article/20891105/the-case-of-the-hot-marina
- **Publication:** EC&M (Electrical Construction & Maintenance), published April 1, 2004
- **Author:** Jim Shafer (president of CAM Components/Harbor Marine Consultants, Stuart, FL)

**Note on independence:** EC&M is a legitimate, independently published electrical industry trade journal (published by Endeavor Business Media). However, the article's author, Jim Shafer, is the same James D. Shafer who later co-compiled the ESDPA incident list that is the original source for FI-2002-002. This article predates the ESDPA list and provides a much more detailed narrative account, but it is not an independent observer -- it is the same investigator publishing in a professional venue. Still, EC&M is an editorially independent publication, and the article provides technical analysis and detail not found in the raw incident list entry.

**Summary of information contained:**
- A mother and her adult daughter were enjoying Memorial Day weather on the sundeck of their moored houseboat on "a lake in the Southeast."
- They decided to go swimming to cool off. Both entered the water.
- Despite frantic efforts to resuscitate the mother, including CPR by a witness, she could not be saved.
- Rescuers found the daughter "more than 50 feet below the surface of the water" several minutes later, but it was too late.
- The investigation found the deaths were caused by electric shock drowning (ESD).
- The boat owner had modified the shore power connection: "Additional circuits had been added, and the incoming power had been changed from 125V (as designed) to 125/250V," exceeding the listed rating of the houseboat's female receptacle.
- The owner had "miswired the ground and hot wire, thus energizing the boat's aluminum hull, railing, and ladder."
- The current levels involved were estimated in the "0.01A to 0.02A range" -- enough to cause loss of muscle control in fresh water.
- A third daughter survived the incident.

**Details NOT in the original incident file:**
- The victims were on the sundeck of a moored houseboat when they entered the water.
- Witnesses attempted CPR on the mother.
- The daughter was found more than 50 feet below the surface.
- A third family member (another daughter) survived.
- The current levels were estimated at 0.01A to 0.02A.
- The houseboat had an aluminum hull, railing, and ladder, all energized by the faulty wiring.
- The article describes the timing as "Memorial Day" rather than "March 2002."

**Details that CONFLICT with the original incident file:**
- **Date:** The original file says "March 2002." The EC&M article says "Memorial Day," which in 2002 fell on May 27. The ESDPA incident list (as seen via Mike Holt's republication) gives the date as "May 31, 2002." Both alternative dates point to late May, not March.
- **Wiring description:** The original file says "A 125V plug had been rewired for 220V with the ground wire cut off." The EC&M article describes it differently: "the incoming power had been changed from 125V (as designed) to 125/250V" and "miswired the ground and hot wire, thus energizing the boat's aluminum hull." The EC&M version does not mention the ground wire being "cut off" but rather being "miswired." The ESDPA detailed list entry (via Mike Holt) says "125V plug at boat end of shore cord rewired by owner for 220V -- L2 connected to 'GR' pin -- ground lead in 4 wire cord cut and taped off." These descriptions are not contradictory but emphasize different aspects of the same wiring fault.
- **Relationship of victims:** The original file says "mother and daughter." The EC&M article says "a mother and her adult daughter." Both agree, though the EC&M article adds that a third daughter also survived.

## Corrections Needed

Based on the available evidence, the following corrections to the original incident file (FI-2002-002.md) appear warranted:

1. **Date: "March 2002" should likely be "May 2002" (approximately May 27-31, 2002).**
   - The EC&M article (April 2004) describes the incident as occurring on "Memorial Day," which was May 27, 2002.
   - The ESDPA detailed incident list gives "May 31, 2002" as the date.
   - No source places this incident in March. The "March" date in the original file appears to be an error, possibly a transcription mistake during extraction from the ESDPA PDF.
   - **Confidence: HIGH** -- Two independent date references (Memorial Day and May 31) converge on late May 2002.

2. **Wiring description could be more precise.**
   - The original file says "A 125V plug had been rewired for 220V with the ground wire cut off." This is a simplified summary.
   - The more detailed ESDPA entry says: "125V plug at boat end of shore cord rewired by owner for 220V -- L2 connected to 'GR' pin -- ground lead in 4 wire cord cut and taped off! Hull rose to line potential."
   - The EC&M article says: "the incoming power had been changed from 125V (as designed) to 125/250V" and describes the ground and hot wire as "miswired."
   - These are not contradictions but rather different levels of detail about the same fault. The original file's summary is adequate but could be expanded.
   - **Confidence: MEDIUM** -- The descriptions are consistent but use different terminology.

3. **Additional context could be added:**
   - A third daughter survived the incident (per EC&M article).
   - The daughter who died was found more than 50 feet below the surface.
   - The houseboat had an aluminum hull that became energized.
   - **Confidence: MEDIUM** -- Based on a single detailed source (EC&M article by the same investigator).

## Research Notes

- The EC&M article, while published in an independent trade journal, was written by Jim Shafer, who is the same person who compiled the ESDPA incident list. Therefore, all available detailed information about this incident traces back to a single investigator/source.
- The incident occurred before widespread digitization of local news, making it very difficult to locate independent journalistic coverage from 2002.
- Memorial Day 2002 was Monday, May 27. The ESDPA list gives May 31, 2002 (a Friday). These dates are close but not identical. The EC&M article's "Memorial Day" reference may be approximate (Memorial Day weekend), or the ESDPA list date may reflect when the bodies were recovered or the investigation took place rather than the exact date of the drowning.
- This incident is categorized under "Electric Shock Drownings" in the ESDPA list, not traditional electrocution -- the victims drowned due to muscle paralysis from low-level electrical current in fresh water, rather than being killed directly by the electric shock itself.
