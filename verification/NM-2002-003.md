## Technical Analysis

The phrase "high-level fault currents in the dock frame" suggests:
- Electrical current was flowing through the metal structure of the dock
- This current was measurably high (implying measurements were taken with appropriate equipment)
- The dock frame was likely energized relative to the water or earth ground
- Fresh water environment increases danger (lower conductivity means higher voltage gradients around energized objects)

Possible causes of fault currents in dock frames:
1. **Faulty boat shore power connection** leaking current to dock through grounding
2. **Damaged dock wiring** with insulation failure allowing current leakage
3. **Utility infrastructure problem** (similar to EC&M article case) where neutral conductor failure creates current paths through water and grounded structures
4. **Lack of GFCI protection** on dock outlets allowing fault currents to persist
5. **Improper grounding** creating unintentional current pathways

The fact that currents were described as "high-level" suggests this was a significant electrical hazard that could have caused serious injury or death if the swimmer had remained in the water longer or had been closer to the energized structure.
