## Corrections Needed

1. **DATE IS COMPLETELY WRONG.** The original file states "Nov 4, 2020." Multiple independent sources (NBC DFW, KTXS, KTAB, KSAT, obituaries) all confirm the incident occurred on **Wednesday evening, June 2, 2021**, with death occurring **Thursday morning, June 3, 2021**. The month (November vs. June), day (4 vs. 2-3), and year (2020 vs. 2021) are all incorrect. The date should be corrected to "June 2-3, 2021" (incident June 2, death June 3).

2. **Victim identification.** The original file does not name the victim. He was Trevor Cate, age 18, a 2021 Clyde High School graduate from Clyde, Texas.

3. **Description is generally accurate but could be more precise.** The original states "the sailboat he was swim towing struck a power line." More precisely:
   - Trevor Cate was in the water pulling/towing a sailboat back to shore
   - The sailboat had become disabled (mechanical failure)
   - Other people were inside the sailboat while he was towing it
   - The boat (not Trevor directly) struck the overhead power line
   - The power line broke and contacted the water, creating an electrical field

4. **"The line broke and fell in the water" is confirmed.** The power line did contact the water after being struck by the sailboat, electrifying the water around the boat.

5. **"Could have been an electrocution or an electric shock drowning" - Resolution:** Based on the sources, Trevor Cate was "electrically shocked and went underwater" and later "died from his injuries" at the hospital. This suggests he was rescued from the water but succumbed to electrical injuries, which could indicate direct electrocution rather than drowning. However, the exact cause of death (direct electrocution vs. electric shock drowning) is not definitively stated in the sources.

6. **"Power crews secured power to the line before retrieving the boy" is essentially confirmed.** Sources state "Clyde Lake officials cut the power at the lake" before "officers found Cate and pulled him to shore." The original file's statement is accurate, though sources indicate he was retrieved (still alive but critically injured) and taken to a hospital where he later died, rather than being retrieved deceased.

7. **Missing context about his recent graduation.** Trevor Cate had just graduated from Clyde High School in 2021, and the incident occurred very shortly after his graduation, adding to the tragedy. He was described as "an exceptional student who excelled at Clyde High School."
