# FI-1997-001 — Research Notes

## Incident Summary (from source PDF)
A boy walking on ice slipped and grabbed exposed wires on a dock at an Air Force Base in Washington, DC and was electrocuted. The source document lists the date as "July 1997" (approximate, month only) and classifies the body of water type as not specified. One fatality is recorded. Listed as PDF Item #101 from the "Electric Shock Drowning Incidents -- Marinas" document by James D. Shafer and Capt. David E. Rifkin, Quality Marine Services, LLC (Rev. 8/15/2025).

## Conflicting Information

1. **Internal contradiction — date vs. circumstances:** The source document lists the date as "July 1997," but the incident description states the boy was "walking on ice." July is midsummer in Washington, DC, making ice on the ground essentially impossible. This is a significant internal inconsistency that suggests either: (a) the "July" month designation is an error in the source PDF and the actual incident occurred in a winter month (January is a candidate, as "July" and "January" could be confused in some transcription contexts), or (b) "ice" in the description refers to something other than frozen water (unlikely given the dock/water context). The source PDF classification notes acknowledge only "Approximate date (month only)." The actual month is almost certainly not July.

2. **Possible base identification uncertainty:** Washington, DC has two major Air Force facilities: Bolling Air Force Base (on the Anacostia River, in DC proper) and Andrews Air Force Base (in Prince George's County, Maryland). The source document says only "AF Base, Washington DC." Bolling AFB, situated on the Anacostia River waterfront with marina/dock facilities, is the more likely location given the dock/water context.

## Additional Details Found

- A separate but potentially related fatal incident occurred at Bolling AFB, Washington, DC (FI-1994-001): a young boy grabbed the support structure of an electrical junction box while in the water and received a lethal shock. The occurrence of two dock electrocution fatalities involving boys at Air Force bases in Washington, DC within approximately 3 years raises the question of whether these occurred at the same facility.
- The Anacostia waterfront at Bolling AFB (now part of Joint Base Anacostia-Bolling, JBAB) does freeze in cold winters, which would be consistent with the "walking on ice" description if the month is corrected to a winter month.
- No victim name, age, or other identifying information was found in any source.
- Military base incidents may be subject to limited public reporting, which could explain the absence of news coverage.

## Suggested Corrections
- **Date should likely be corrected from "July 1997."** The "July 1997" date is almost certainly incorrect because the incident involved a boy walking on ice, which is impossible in Washington, DC in July. A winter date (e.g., January-February 1997 or December 1997-February 1998) would be consistent with the ice conditions described. "January" is a plausible candidate, as "July" and "January" could be confused in transcription.
