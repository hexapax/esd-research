# Research: FI-1991-002 — Lake Hamilton Canoe Drowning (1991)

## Original Incident Summary

In July 1991, one of four boys in a canoe continued into an electric field and drowned at Lake Hamilton, Hot Springs, Arkansas. The cause was broken dock wire insulation. The incident is classified as a fatal incident with 1 death. It is listed as PDF item #108 in the "Electric Shock Drowning Incidents -- Marinas" document by James D. Shafer and Capt. David E. Rifkin (Rev. 8/15/2025). The classification notes indicate an approximate date (month only) and that the same lake was the site of ESD #70 (2007) and ESD #89 (2001).

## Additional Context

- **Lake Hamilton ESD history:** Lake Hamilton has been the site of at least three ESD fatalities: this 1991 incident, a 2001 incident, and the August 2008 death of Sarah Crotts (age 14, of Sherwood, AR), who was electrocuted near a dock with a faulty halogen light that energized the dock frame and swim ladder to 103 VAC. The 2008 incident also injured Tamara Keele (age 40) and her son Devin Keele (age 7).

- **Regulatory response:** The pattern of repeated ESD deaths on Lake Hamilton and other Arkansas lakes contributed to the passage of Jesse's Law in Arkansas in 2011, which established minimum electrical standards for boat docks and marinas, though the law lacks enforcement penalties.

- **Utility company response:** In 2017, Entergy Arkansas Inc. began requiring electrical inspections prior to boat dock permit transfers on Lakes Hamilton and Catherine, reflecting ongoing efforts to address electrical safety hazards at Arkansas lake docks.
