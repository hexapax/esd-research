# Research: FI-2011-002 — Clinch Marina Electric Shock Drowning (2011)

## Original Incident Summary

Per the ESDPA-derived file, on May 28, 2011, an 18-year-old boy died in the West Arm of Grand Traverse Bay near Clinch Marina, Traverse City, MI. The cause was a dock wiring fault. One death.

## Corrections Needed

1. **DATE IS WRONG.** The original file states "May 28, 2011." Every independent source — the obituary (Mortensen Funeral Home), the AP/CBS wire report, Interlochen Public Radio's same-day coverage (August 16, 2011), court records, and all subsequent news coverage — unanimously confirms the date was **August 15, 2011**. The May 28 date is definitively incorrect.

2. **"18yo boy" is accurate** but the victim was from Mancelona, MI, not Traverse City. Michael Scott Knudsen was 18 years old, confirmed by obituary (born July 29, 1993; died August 15, 2011).

3. **"Dock wiring fault" is correct but substantially understates the cause.** The actual cause involved multiple compounding failures: a short circuit in a 220-volt line with corroded/worn insulation, metal junction boxes installed instead of specified fiberglass, a failed circuit breaker, and prior unaddressed reports of users feeling electrical current near the dock.

4. **Outcome count is incomplete.** The raw file lists "Dead: 1" but does not mention that friend Zachary Kott-Millard was also shocked and survived. Kott-Millard later filed his own lawsuit.
