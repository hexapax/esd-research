# ESD Incident Search Terms

Useful search queries and strategies for finding news articles, coroner reports, and other sources about electric shock drowning (ESD) incidents.

## General Strategies
- Try victim age + location + year: `"18 year old" drowned "Smith Mountain Lake" 2024`
- Use "electric shock drowning" + location
- Use "electrocuted" + "water" + location
- Try the lake/marina name + "electrocution"
- Search for coroner/medical examiner reports: `"cause of death" electrocution [location] [year]`
- Check local news outlets for the area
- Try OSHA incident reports for workplace-related incidents

## Core Search Terms
- "electric shock drowning"
- "electrocuted in water"
- "electrocution drowning dock"
- "electrocution marina"
- "electrocuted swimming"
- "stray voltage water"
- "dock electrocution"

## Additional Effective Strategies (Learned from Batch 1)

### Victim Identification
- Once you find a victim name, search `"[name] obituary [city] [year]"` — obituaries provide DOB, family, occupation
- Search `"[name] electrocuted [city]"` for follow-up coverage
- Funeral home websites often have detailed obituaries with family info

### Legal/Lawsuit Sources
- Search `"[marina/location] wrongful death lawsuit"` — lawsuits contain detailed factual allegations
- Law firm blog posts often provide detailed incident narratives not in news articles
- NEC (National Electrical Code) violations cited in lawsuits provide technical details

### Professional/Industry Forums
- Mike Holt Electrical Forum (forums.mikeholt.com) — electricians discuss incidents with technical detail
- EC&M (Electrical Construction & Maintenance) — industry coverage of ESD events

### Follow-up Investigation Sources
- Search `"[location] dock electrical code changes"` — code changes after incidents reveal investigation results
- Search `"stray voltage" [lake name]` — may find broader local coverage of the problem
- Local building inspector discussions after fatalities

### Basement/Flood Electrocution Searches
- `"electrocuted flooded basement [city]"` — more effective than "electric shock drowning" for indoor incidents
- `"plumber electrocuted [city]"` — for incidents involving tradespeople
- Power company names (e.g., "DTE Energy" for Detroit) can help narrow results

### Calendar Verification
- Always check if the day of week matches the date — discrepancies between ESDPA date and news "Thursday/Friday" can reveal date errors
- News publication dates may differ from incident dates by 1 day
