# NEW-2013-001: Raul Hernandez — Houston, TX (Pool Electrocution)

## Incident Summary
- **Victim:** Raul Hernandez, age 27, of Richmond, TX
- **Date:** August 31, 2013 (incident); died September 6, 2013
- **Location:** Hilton Houston Westchase hotel pool, 9999 Westheimer Road, Houston, TX
- **Type:** Pool electrocution (faulty pool light wiring)
- **Outcome:** Fatal

## Description
Raul Hernandez was swimming with family at the Hilton Westchase pool during Labor Day weekend. When pool lights activated at dusk, swimmers began complaining of being shocked. Hernandez's 11-year-old brother was overcome by electrical shock in the deep end. Hernandez dove in to rescue his brother, successfully pushing him to the side where a bystander pulled him out. Hernandez and his mother were still in the pool and could not get out. Eventually the lights were turned off, and both were pulled from the water. Hernandez went into cardiac arrest and never regained consciousness, dying September 6, 2013.

## Cause
The hotel had hired Brown Electric Inc. in June 2013 to replace pool wiring and bonding. Electricians Jason Joseph Gorczyca and James Ray Pyle performed the work in a substandard fashion without a permit. The pool lacked a ground fault circuit interrupter (GFCI) on the pool lighting system, violating the National Electric Code.
