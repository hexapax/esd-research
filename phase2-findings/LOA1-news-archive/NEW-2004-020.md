## Incident Description

Ken Lutrick, 16, was swimming at Main Harbor Marina on Ross Barnett Reservoir in Ridgeland, Mississippi. He reached for a ladder on a houseboat to pull himself out of the water. The ladder was electrified due to a faulty shore power connection and he was electrocuted. A friend who was also in the water nearby felt an electrical shock sensation but survived.

The cause was identified as a homemade shore power cord whose insulation had been cut by repeated boat motion, energizing the hull and all metal surfaces of the houseboat including the ladder.

His mother, Elise Lutrick (of Jackson, Mississippi), subsequently became an ESD awareness advocate, creating a Facebook awareness page and speaking publicly about the incident. She testified about the incident in the HuffPost article published in September 2010 (timed to the 6th anniversary of Ken's death), placing the incident on "that Saturday" of September 2004.

## Geographic Note

Ross Barnett Reservoir is a reservoir on the Pearl River in central Mississippi, administered by the Pearl River Valley Water Supply District. Main Harbor Marina is located in Ridgeland, Mississippi (Madison County), not in Jackson (Hinds County). The HuffPost article's reference to "Jackson, Michigan" is doubly erroneous — wrong city and wrong state.
