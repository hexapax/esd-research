## Analysis

### Why the gap years likely reflect genuine absence of documented incidents

1. **1990:** Bracketed by FI-1989-001 (Dec 1989, Oklahoma, submersible pump) and FI-1991-001 (Jul 1991, Oklahoma, dock wiring) and FI-1991-002 (May 11, 1991, Lake Hamilton, AR). The CDC MMWR explicitly lists zero Oklahoma lake electrocution cases in 1990. No credible secondary source mentions any 1990 US ESD fatality.

2. **1992:** Bracketed by FI-1991-001/002 and FI-1993-001 (Jul 1993, Oklahoma) / FI-1993-002 (Sep 1993, OK) / GOV-1993-001 (Aug 1993, Alexandria Bay NY double drowning). The CDC MMWR lists zero Oklahoma cases in 1992. The one potential 1992 "swimming + electrocution" result (Lake Miramar, CA) is definitively not ESD — it was direct contact with an overhead power line on a pump tower.

3. **1996:** Bracketed by FI-1995-001 (Feb 1995, Bolling AFB DC) and FI-1997-001 (Jul 1997, Lake Mead NV). No national ESD surveillance existed. No news archive or secondary source references any 1996 US dock/marina ESD incident.

### Structural reasons for gaps
- National ESD incidence in this era was approximately 3-6 deaths/year; some years may genuinely have zero documented incidents
- No national ESD surveillance mechanism existed before ~2000
- ESD deaths were frequently classified as simple drownings with no electrical investigation
- Pre-digital era: newspaper archives are not fully indexed online; incidents may have occurred but left no accessible online trace
