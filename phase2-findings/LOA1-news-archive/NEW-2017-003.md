# NEW-2017-003: Boy Scout Troop 620 — Lake O' The Pines, TX (Aug 5, 2017)

## Status
NEW INCIDENT — ESD-ADJACENT (power line strike, not dock/marina fault) — VERIFIED

## Basic Info
- **Date:** August 5, 2017 (two additional deaths on Aug 6 and Aug 7)
- **Location:** Alley Creek alcove, Lake O' The Pines, near Avinger, Marion County, East Texas (35 miles north of Longview)
- **Victims:**
  - Will Brannon, 18 — Fatal (died at scene)
  - Heath Faucheaux, 16 — Fatal (died at scene)
  - Thomas Larry, 11 — Fatal (died Aug 7, 2017 at Shreveport hospital)
- **Outcome:** Fatal x3

## ESD Mechanism
The three Boy Scouts from Troop 620, Hallsville TX were sailing a Hobie Cat catamaran in Alley Creek. The catamaran's 30-foot mast struck an overhead transmission power line operated by Upshur Rural Electric Cooperative. The line contacted the boat and/or broke and fell, electrifying the water and causing fire on the boat. The two older scouts were found dead — one onboard, one in the water. Thomas Larry was found unresponsive in a rescuer's boat nearby and was airlifted in critical condition to CHRISTUS Shreveport-Bossier, where he died August 7.

**ESD classification note:** This is a power line strike incident rather than traditional dock/marina/shore power ESD. The electricity entered the water from an overhead transmission line (not dock or boat wiring). The ESDPA incident list does not appear to include this specific incident in its standard marina-ESD list. However, it is classified as in-water electrocution because at least one victim was in the water when electrified. One source (electrocuted.com) explicitly categorizes it under ESD incidents. **Recommend flagging as ESD-ADJACENT / BORDERLINE ESD** — the mechanism differs from dock/marina wiring faults that define classic ESD.

**Precursor:** A nearly identical incident occurred at the same lake in December 1982, when attorney G. Brockett Irwin was electrocuted after his sailboat's mast struck a power line over Lake O' The Pines near Johnson Creek (less than a mile from the 2017 site). Three other occupants survived. Upshur Rural Electric Cooperative reportedly raised the line and placed danger buoys after 1982, but clearance issues persisted.

## Verification Status
VERIFIED — 10+ independent news sources including national outlets (USA Today, NPR, Washington Post, CBS, Fox News) and local Texas outlets. Texas Parks & Wildlife confirmed the investigation.

## ESDPA Status
This incident is NOT in the standard ESDPA marina/dock incident list reviewed. It appears in the California DBW ESD training document as a Lake ESD example. The Harbor Marine Consultants incident list reviewed does not include this specific event. Consider for ESDPA review.

## Deduplication
Not a duplicate of any known ESDPA incident. The Oakland Lake Park, Fort Worth incident (March 29, 2017 — Lopez brothers, downed power line in park puddle) is a separate event.

## Notes
- All three victims were members of the Hallsville High School band; the sons of the troop's Scoutmaster and Assistant Scoutmaster were among the dead.
- The NESC requires minimum clearance of 37.5 ft above waterways over 2,000 acres suitable for sailing; Lake O' The Pines is 18,680 acres. Investigation focused on whether Upshur Rural Electric Cooperative violated this standard.
- U.S. Army Corps of Engineers had jurisdiction over the lake area.
- This is the second fatal power line/sailboat mast incident at the same lake (first in 1982).
