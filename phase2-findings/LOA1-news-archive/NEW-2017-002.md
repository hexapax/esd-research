**Description:** Cole Grad, age 15, was electrocuted in the Columbia River near Pasco, Washington. Faulty pump house wiring electrified the water. His death was initially ruled a drowning and only reclassified as electrocution after autopsy results were returned 11 days later. This case illustrates the diagnostic challenge of ESD — coroners may resist electrocution findings because submerged victims show no electrical burns (skin resistance is eliminated when the body is immersed in water).
