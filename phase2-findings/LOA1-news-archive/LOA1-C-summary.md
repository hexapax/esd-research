## Verified Incidents (5)

### NEW-2013-001: Raul Hernandez — Houston, TX
- **Pool electrocution** at Hilton Houston Westchase, Aug 31, 2013
- Faulty pool light wiring, no GFCI, substandard electrician work
- Hernandez died rescuing his brother from electrified pool
- Electricians charged with criminally negligent homicide; civil settlement with Hilton
- **6+ independent news sources** (Houston Chronicle, Houston Press, ABC13, EC&M, Click2Houston)

### NEW-2020-001: Khaleel Marcos Reynolds — Houston, TX
- **Pool electrocution** at North Villa Inn motel, Aug 29, 2020
- Exposed wires from removed pool light; pool had failed inspections since 2018
- 15-year-old electrocuted; died at hospital
- Mother filed wrongful death lawsuit
- **5+ independent news sources** (ABC13, Star-Telegram, Service Industry News)

### NEW-2020-002: Timothy & Michael Miller — Lake Pleasant, AZ
- **Marina ESD** at Scorpion Bay Marina, July 12, 2020
- Boat had incompatible electrical connection with marina; bypassed safety features
- Two brothers died; one woman (Alicia) injured with burns on legs
- Marina infrastructure found safe; boat modifications were the cause
- **9+ independent news sources** (FOX 10, Fox News, ABC15, KTAR, 12News, Peoria Times, Patch, Sacramento Bee)

### NEW-2017-003: Boy Scout Troop 620 — Lake O' The Pines, TX
- **Power line/sailboat mast strike** at Alley Creek, Lake O' The Pines, Aug 5-7, 2017
- Hobie Cat catamaran's 30-foot mast struck Upshur Rural Electric Cooperative transmission line
- Three boys killed: Will Brannon (18), Heath Faucheaux (16), Thomas Larry (11)
- Preliminary TPWD investigation; Upshur Rural Electric Cooperative under scrutiny for line clearance violations (NESC requires 37.5 ft minimum for lakes over 2,000 acres)
- **10+ independent sources** (USA Today, NPR, CBS, Dallas Morning News, Fox News, KLTV, Providence Journal)
- **ESD Classification: ESD-ADJACENT** — mechanism is power line strike, not dock/marina/shore power wiring fault. Victims were in water or on boat when electrified. ESDPA standard list does not include this event.

### NEW-2021-001: Trevor Cate — Lake Clyde, TX
- **Power line/sailboat mast strike** at Clyde Lake, June 3, 2021
- 18-year-old swim-towing disabled sailboat; mast hit overhead power line, line fell in water
- Trevor Cate shocked in water, went under, pulled to shore; died at Hendrick Medical Center Abilene
- Clyde Lake temporarily closed
- **2 independent news sources** (CBS Austin, KTXS); ESDPA list corroborates
- **ESD Classification: ESD-ADJACENT** — ESDPA notes "Could have been an electrocution or an electric shock drowning"

## Key Findings and Analysis

### Texas Gap — Updated Assessment

Texas now has a more complex picture than the initial search suggested:

**Classic dock/marina/pool ESD incidents in Texas:**
- **Known ESDPA:** FI-2014-004 (Andrew Orvis, Lake Conroe, 2014); FI-2021-003 (Gustavo Delgado, Mission TX, 2021)
- **New verified:** NEW-2013-001 (Raul Hernandez, Houston pool); NEW-2020-001 (Khaleel Reynolds, Houston pool)
- **New unverified:** ~1994 Texas boatlift (salt water); Lake Travis two juvenile females (date unknown)

**Power line strike ESD-adjacent incidents in Texas:**
- **New verified:** NEW-2017-003 (Boy Scout Troop 620, Lake O' The Pines, 3 dead); NEW-2021-001 (Trevor Cate, Lake Clyde, 1 dead)
- **New unverified:** NEW-1982-001 (G. Brockett Irwin, Lake O' The Pines, 1 dead)

**Total Texas incidents documented (all types):** ~9-10 across 40+ years

The Texas gap for **traditional dock/marina ESD** (where current leaks from dock/boat wiring into water) remains significant: no verified dock/marina ESD incidents were found at major Texas lakes (Lake Texoma, Toledo Bend, Canyon Lake, Possum Kingdom, Sam Rayburn, Lake Lewisville, Lake LBJ/Buchanan, Lake Whitney). Extensive searching of these lakes yielded only standard drownings.

However, Texas has a distinct pattern of **power line/boat mast strike incidents** on lakes, with at least 4 deaths documented at Lake O' The Pines alone (1982 and 2017).

### Arizona/Nevada Gap

- **Lake Pleasant (AZ):** Miller brothers incident (2020, NEW-2020-002) is well-documented classic ESD
- **Lake Mohave (AZ):** 1999 houseboat incident (NEW-1999-001) exists in specialist lists but has no independent news verification
- **Lake Mead (NV):** 1997 houseboat incident (NEW-1997-001) exists in specialist lists but has no independent news verification
- **Lake Havasu:** No ESD incidents found in any search
- **Lake Powell (AZ portion):** No Arizona-based incidents found; the 2014 Bullfrog Marina incident is in Utah
- **Lake Powell (UT):** Alec McQueen 2014 — verified classic ESD, but Utah not Arizona

### New Mexico
**Zero ESD incidents found** for New Mexico across all searches and all years.

### Possible Explanations for Gaps
1. **Underreporting/misclassification:** ESD deaths are frequently classified as simple drownings when no witnesses report electrical symptoms
2. **Lack of investigation:** Many jurisdictions do not test for electrical current during drowning investigations
3. **Salt water vs. fresh water:** Texas coastal areas use salt water, which reduces ESD risk; but inland lakes are fresh water
4. **Regulatory differences:** State electrical code enforcement varies significantly
5. **Climate patterns:** Southwest states may have different dock/marina usage patterns and seasonal infrastructure
6. **Texas lake infrastructure:** Overhead power lines crossing Texas lakes (common in rural East Texas) create a different hazard profile than dock/marina AC power faults seen in Midwest/Southeast lakes
