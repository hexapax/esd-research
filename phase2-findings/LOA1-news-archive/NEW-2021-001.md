# NEW-2021-001: Trevor Cate — Lake Clyde, Clyde TX (Jun 3, 2021)

## Status
NEW INCIDENT — ESD-ADJACENT (power line strike while victim in water) — VERIFIED

## Basic Info
- **Date:** June 3, 2021 (died within days, June 3 per Clyde PD)
- **Location:** Clyde Lake (also called Lake Clyde), Clyde, Callahan County, Texas (near Abilene)
- **Victim:** Trevor Cate, 18, of Clyde TX — 2021 Clyde High School graduate
- **Outcome:** Fatal

## ESD Mechanism
Trevor Cate was in the water swim-towing a disabled sailboat back toward shore. A second group of people were inside the sailboat. The sailboat's mast struck an overhead power line running over the lake, causing the power line to break and fall into the water near Cate. The energized line electrified the water. Cate was electrically shocked while in the water, went underwater, and was found and pulled to shore by officers. He was taken to Hendrick Medical Center in Abilene and died from his injuries on Thursday (approximately June 3, 2021).

After the incident, Clyde Lake officials cut power to the lake. The lake was temporarily closed.

**ESD classification note:** The ESDPA Harbor Marine Consultants incident list (2022 and later) includes this as entry #2 or #3 with the notation: "Could have been an electrocution or an electric shock drowning. Power crews secured power to the line before retrieving the boy." This indicates ambiguity about whether the victim was killed primarily by direct electrocution or by electric shock drowning after muscle paralysis in the water. The victim was in the water when the line fell, making this a borderline ESD case — the electricity entered the water from a downed line rather than from dock/boat/marina wiring. **Classify as ESD-ADJACENT / BORDERLINE ESD.**
