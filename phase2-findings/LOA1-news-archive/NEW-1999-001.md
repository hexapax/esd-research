# NEW-1999-001: Unknown Male — Lake Mohave, AZ (Houseboat ESD)

## Incident Summary
- **Victim:** Young man (name unknown)
- **Date:** July 1999
- **Location:** Lake Mohave, Arizona
- **Type:** Electric shock drowning (houseboat electrical fault)
- **Outcome:** Fatal

## Description
A young man swimming toward the stern of a houseboat became disabled and drowned in fresh water. The boat had a neutral-ground bond. A homemade shore cord "Y" adapter became partly disconnected, causing the hull to become energized. Only 17 VAC was measured behind the stern-drive — demonstrating that extremely low voltages can be lethal in fresh water.

## Cause
Homemade shore cord "Y" adapter became partly disconnected. Boat had an improper neutral-ground bond. Hull became energized, creating voltage gradient in surrounding water.
