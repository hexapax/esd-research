## Key Observations

### 1. Electrical Source Patterns
- **Classic marina/dock ESD:** Only 2 incidents (Lucas Ritz in OR, Lake Chelan in WA) involve the traditional dock/marina/shore power scenario
- **Irrigation pump electrification:** 2 of 3 verified fatal incidents (Roskie, Grad) involved irrigation pumps electrifying river water -- a mechanism consistent with ESD but distinct from the typical marina scenario. This is notable for the Pacific Northwest where agricultural irrigation along rivers is common.

### 2. Underreporting Thesis
The near-zero documented ESD count for the Upper Midwest is striking given that:
- Minnesota has ~11,842 lakes and extensive dock/marina infrastructure
- Wisconsin has ~15,000 lakes with similar infrastructure
- Illinois has significant freshwater marina activity
- An entire organization (electricshockdrowningmn.com) was created specifically for ESD awareness in the MN/WI area
- The LMCD (Lake Minnetonka Conservation District) has an ESD awareness page
- CBS Minnesota reported on a "Big Lake death" that "shines spotlight on electric shock drowning" (this was the known Petersen incident)

The combination of high lake recreation activity, extensive electrified dock infrastructure, active ESD awareness organizations, and near-zero documented incidents strongly supports the underreporting hypothesis. Many ESD deaths are classified as ordinary drownings because there is no post-mortem evidence to distinguish them, and most law enforcement lacks the technical skills to investigate electrical causes.

### 3. Investigation Challenges
- Lucas Ritz's death was initially ruled a drowning; only reclassified because his father (a marine technician) investigated
- Cole Grad's death was initially ruled a drowning; only corrected after autopsy results 11 days later
- Coroners routinely argue against electrocution findings because victims show no burns (skin resistance is eliminated when submerged in water)
