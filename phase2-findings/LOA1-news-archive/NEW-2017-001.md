# NEW-2017-001: Kayla Matos — Toms River, NJ

## Incident Summary
- **Date:** June 17, 2017 (Saturday evening)
- **Location:** Lagoon behind home on Tobago Avenue, Shelter Cove section of Toms River, Ocean County, New Jersey
- **Victim:** Kayla Matos, age 11, of Newark, NJ
- **Outcome:** Fatal (died at Community Medical Center, Toms River)
- **Type:** Electric Shock Drowning / Direct Electrocution — contact with energized boat lift

## Description
Kayla Matos, an 11-year-old from Newark, was visiting friends at a home in Toms River's Shelter Cove waterfront neighborhood. She and two friends were using an inflatable raft and swimming in the lagoon behind the home, wearing life jackets, under adult supervision. Kayla touched the metal rail of a boat lift that had become electrified due to a corroded electrical junction box. The boat lift had reportedly been dormant for years, but a corroded circuit box caused the energization. The two other girls were evaluated at the scene and were not injured.

First responders performed CPR and used an Automated External Defibrillator, then transported Kayla to Community Medical Center in Toms River, where she died later that evening.

The Toms River mayor subsequently encouraged all residents to get their boats, pools, and docks inspected by a qualified electrician.

## Classification
- **ESD Confirmed:** Yes — direct contact electrocution from energized boat lift in water (dock/boat lift equipment)
- **Verification:** VERIFIED (6+ independent sources)
