# LOA1-B Summary: Gap Year Search (2004, 2007, 2009)

**Date:** March 6, 2026
**Scope:** Systematic search for ESD incidents in 2004, 2007, and 2009 — years with few or zero documented incidents in the ESDPA database despite surrounding years having multiple incidents.

---

## Search Methodology

For each of the three target years, the following search queries were executed using Perplexity AI and Brave Web Search:

1. "electric shock drowning" [YEAR]
2. "electrocuted" drowning dock marina [YEAR]
3. "electrocuted while swimming" [YEAR]
4. "electrocuted in water" [YEAR]
5. "dock electrocution" [YEAR]
6. "houseboat" electrocution [YEAR]
7. "boat lift" electrocution [YEAR]
8. "shore power" electrocution drowning [YEAR]
9. drowning electrocution marina [state names] [YEAR]
10. Additional targeted searches based on leads (victim names, specific locations, lawsuit records)

Excluded sources per protocol: ESDPA, electricshockdrowning.org, mikeholt.com, ontime59.com, augresmaritimemuseum.org.

---

## Results by Year

### 2004

**Existing ESDPA entries:** FI-2004-001 through FI-2004-005 (5 fatal), NM-2004-001 through NM-2004-003 (3 near-miss)

**New incidents found:** 0

**Corrections to existing entries found:**
- **FI-2004-002** (Lake of the Ozarks, MO, Sept 13, 2004): Victim identified as **Tyler Deeds**, 22, of Olathe, Kansas. He was "shocked by a dock cable and fell into the water while helping to remodel a lake house." His death prompted 2006 code changes at Lake of the Ozarks. (Sources: Kansas City Star, Columbia Missourian)

### 2007

**Existing ESDPA entries:** FI-2007-001 (1 fatal), NM-2007-001 through NM-2007-004 (4 near-miss)

**New incidents found:** 0

**Notes:** One Perplexity result suggested a "July 28, 2007, Lake of the Ozarks" incident involving a 24-year-old woman on a metal ladder, but cross-referencing shows this is FI-2006-001 (July 24, 2006, Lake of the Ozarks) with the date slightly garbled — the source was augresmaritimemuseum.org (excluded source). No genuinely new 2007 incident was found.

### 2009

**Existing ESDPA entries:** 0 (no fatal incidents, no near-misses)

**New incidents found:** 0

**Leads investigated but not qualifying:**
- ECM "Case of Stray Voltage in a Lake" (published Nov. 2009): This article describes the Spring Lake/Summerset incident near De Soto, MO. Despite the Nov. 2009 publication date, the incident occurred March 18, 2006 (already ESDPA FI-2005-001 with wrong date). Victim: Nic Harbison, 16 (died); Morgan Milfeld, Tim Fitzpatrick (brain injuries); Joshua McClure (injured). $2.3M verdict against AmerenUE in 2009.
- CPSC reports 14 pool/spa electrocution fatalities for 2002-2009 combined but provides no year-by-year breakdown or individual incident details for 2009.
- No dock, marina, boat lift, or houseboat electrocution incidents in the U.S. in 2009 were identified from any source.

## Conclusions

1. **No new ESD incidents were found for 2004, 2007, or 2009** that are not already documented in the ESDPA database.

2. **The gap years appear to be genuine low-activity periods** rather than artifacts of underreporting. The extensive searches using multiple query patterns, search engines, and domain-specific sources failed to turn up any undocumented incidents.

3. **Two victim identifications and one major date correction** were found as byproducts of the search:
   - FI-2004-002: Tyler Deeds identified
   - FI-2005-001: Date corrected from June 2005 to March 18, 2006; victim Nic Harbison identified; three additional injured victims identified

4. **Possible explanations for gap years:**
   - 2004 actually has 5 fatal incidents — it is not truly a gap year in the ESDPA. The "gap" perception may come from the FI-2005-001 misdating (actually March 2006).
   - 2007 has 1 fatal incident (Lake Hamilton AR). This is a lower count but not zero.
   - 2009 is the only true zero year. Given that 2008 has 1 fatal incident and 2010 has 4, a single zero year is plausible as natural variation rather than systematic underreporting.
