# LOA1-B Summary: Gap Year Search (2004, 2007, 2009)

**Date:** March 6–7, 2026
**Scope:** Systematic search for ESD incidents in 2004, 2007, and 2009 — years with few or zero documented incidents in the ESDPA database despite surrounding years having multiple incidents.

---

## Search Methodology

For each of the three target years, the following search queries were executed using Perplexity AI and Brave Web Search:

1. "electric shock drowning" [YEAR]
2. "electrocuted" drowning dock marina [YEAR]
3. "electrocuted while swimming" [YEAR]
4. "electrocuted in water" [YEAR]
5. "dock electrocution" [YEAR]
6. "houseboat" electrocution [YEAR]
7. "boat lift" electrocution [YEAR]
8. "shore power" electrocution drowning [YEAR]
9. drowning electrocution marina [state names] [YEAR]
10. Additional targeted searches based on leads (victim names, specific locations, lawsuit records)

Excluded sources per protocol: ESDPA, electricshockdrowning.org, mikeholt.com, ontime59.com, augresmaritimemuseum.org.

## Results by Year

### 2004

**Existing ESDPA entries:** FI-2004-001 through FI-2004-005 (5 fatal), NM-2004-001 through NM-2004-003 (3 near-miss)

**New incidents found:** 1

#### NEW-2004-020: Kenneth (Ken) Lutrick, 16 — Ross Barnett Reservoir, Ridgeland, MS
- **Date:** September 12–13, 2004 (Sep 12 per HuffPost; Sep 13 per ESDPA excluded source)
- **Location:** Main Harbor Marina, Ross Barnett Reservoir, Ridgeland, Mississippi
- **Outcome:** Fatal
- **Mechanism:** Reached for ladder on houseboat energized by faulty homemade shore power cord (insulation cut by boat motion); hull and all metal surfaces were electrified. A friend in the water also felt a shock but survived.
- **Verification status:** VERIFIED — 2+ independent non-excluded sources
- **Sources:**
  1. HuffPost — https://www.huffpost.com/entry/esd-hidden-danger-in-fres_b_693454 (first-person testimony from mother Elise Lutrick; note: article incorrectly states "Jackson, Michigan" — actual location is Ridgeland, Mississippi)
  2. Holland Sentinel — https://www.hollandsentinel.com/story/news/2010/09/06/group-warns-rare-boating-danger/45298206007/ ("Elise Lutrick, of Jackson, Mississippi, created the Facebook page after her son was killed in a 2004 ESD incident")
  3. CA Boating/CRLEA PDF — https://dbw.parks.ca.gov/crlea/Files/Electric-Shock-Drowning-The-Invisible-Killer-CRLEA.pdf (names "KENNETH LUTRICK, 16" in victim list)
- **See:** NEW-2004-020.md

**Corrections to existing entries found:**
- **FI-2004-002** (Lake of the Ozarks, MO, Sept 13, 2004): Victim identified as **Tyler Deeds**, 22, of Olathe, Kansas. He was "shocked by a dock cable and fell into the water while helping to remodel a lake house." His death prompted 2006 code changes at Lake of the Ozarks. (Sources: Kansas City Star, Columbia Missourian)

**Leads investigated but not qualifying:**
- **Lake Waccamaw, NC (June 19, 2004):** ESDPA excluded source lists a 10-year-old boy electrocuted by a boat lift. No independent news sources found during this search session. Remains an unverified lead.
- **Lake Wylie, NC (June 5, 2004):** ESDPA excluded source lists two boys shocked near a houseboat. No independent news sources found. Remains an unverified lead.
- **Lake of the Ozarks, MO (September 2004, unnamed 22yo):** One source describes "a 22-year-old male stepped on an electrical cable exiting the water." This may overlap with SUSP-2004-044 or FI-2004-002 (Tyler Deeds, also 22, also Lake of the Ozarks, September 2004). Likely the same incident; not a new find.

### 2007

**Existing ESDPA entries:** FI-2007-001 (1 fatal), NM-2007-001 through NM-2007-004 (4 near-miss)

**New incidents found:** 0

**Notes:**
- One Perplexity result mentioned a "July 28, 2007, Lake of the Ozarks" incident involving a 24-year-old woman on a metal ladder. Cross-referencing with FI-2007-001 (Lake Hamilton, AR) and the excluded source augresmaritimemuseum.org confirms this is either FI-2006-001 (Lake of the Ozarks, July 24, 2006) with a garbled date, or FI-2007-001 misidentified. No independent confirmation of a new 2007 Lake of the Ozarks fatal incident was found.
- A September 2007 Franklin Lock Campground, FL incident involving a boat owner cleaning a prop shaft was a near-miss (no fatality, no swimmer in water). Not an ESD incident per definition.
- A July 1, 2007 Collins Bay, Lake Ontario incident was Canadian — excluded from U.S.-only scope.

### 2009

**Existing ESDPA entries:** 0 (no fatal incidents, no near-misses documented)

**New incidents found:** 0

**Leads investigated but not qualifying:**
- ECM "Case of Stray Voltage in a Lake" (published Nov. 2009): Describes the Spring Lake/Summerset incident near De Soto, MO. Despite the Nov. 2009 publication date, the incident occurred March 18, 2006 (already documented as FI-2005-001 with incorrect date). Victim: Nic Harbison, 16 (died); Morgan Milfeld, Tim Fitzpatrick (brain injuries); Joshua McClure (injured). $2.3M verdict against AmerenUE rendered in 2009. Not a new 2009 incident.
- CPSC reports 14 pool/spa electrocution fatalities for 2002–2009 combined but provides no year-by-year breakdown or individual incident details for 2009.
- No dock, marina, boat lift, or houseboat electrocution incidents in the U.S. in 2009 were identified from any source.

## Conclusions

1. **One new ESD fatal incident was found for 2004:** Kenneth (Ken) Lutrick, 16, Ross Barnett Reservoir, Ridgeland, MS. See NEW-2004-020.md.

2. **No new incidents were found for 2007 or 2009.** The gaps for those years appear to be genuine low-activity periods, not artifacts of underreporting.

3. **Two victim identifications and one major date correction** were found as byproducts of the search:
   - FI-2004-002: Tyler Deeds identified by name (22, Olathe KS, Lake of the Ozarks)
   - FI-2005-001: Date corrected from June 2005 to March 18, 2006; victim Nic Harbison identified; three additional injured victims identified

4. **Possible explanations for gap years:**
   - 2004 had at least 6 fatal incidents (5 in ESDPA + NEW-2004-020). It is not a gap year.
   - 2007 has 1 fatal incident (Lake Hamilton, AR). Lower than adjacent years but not zero.
   - 2009 is the only true zero year. Given 2008 has 1 fatal incident and 2010 has 4, a single zero year is plausible as natural variation.
