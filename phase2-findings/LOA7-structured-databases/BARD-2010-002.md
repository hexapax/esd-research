## BARD Narrative

PASSENGERS GOT ONBOARD VESSEL TO WORK ON OWNERS BROKEN AIR CONDITIONER. WHILE WORKING INJURED PARTY AND PASSENGER WERE AT THE REAR OF THE VESSEL. PASSENGER WAS SITTING IN A CHAIR AND INJURED WAS SITTING ON EDGE OF BOAT WITH FEET IN THE WATER. [...]

PASSENGER HELPING REPORTED THE ELECTRICAL SHOCK WAS TOO INTENSE AND HE COULD NOT REACH THE VICTIM, SO HE YELLED FOR THE OWNER TO SHUT OFF THE ELECTRIC. OWNER/INJURED ADVISED HE THEN TURNED OFF THE MASTER SWITCH AND ALSO THE BREAKER AND WENT TO THE REAR DECK. AT THIS TIME THE PASSENGER HAD GOTTEN A LOOPED ROPE AND USED IT TO THROW AROUND THE NECK OF THE INJURED AND BEGAN PULLING INJURED IN. OWNER/INJURED GOT AROUND THE REAR DECK AND SAW WHAT WAS TAKING PLACE AND HE ENTERED INTO THE WATER IN AN ATTEMPT TO HELP RESCUE INJURED. UPON ENTRY OWNER/INJURED STATED THE ELECTRIC SHOCK PARALYZED HIM.

## Analysis

Classic ESD near-miss pattern: boat's electrical system energizing surrounding water, multiple people shocked and paralyzed upon entering water. Both victims survived due to rescue intervention. This demonstrates the cascading rescue danger typical of ESD.
