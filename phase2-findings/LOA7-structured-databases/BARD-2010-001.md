## BARD Narrative

3 JUVENILES WERE SWIMMING OFF THE STERN OF THE BOAT WHILE MOORED AT THE MARINA. ONE OF THE JUVENILES EXITED THE WATER WITHOUT INCIDENT AND WAS STANDING ON THE BOAT. THE VICTIM AND OTHER JUVENILE APPROACHED THE SWIM LADDER TO CLIMB OUT. BOTH RECEIVED AN ELECTRICAL SHOCK. THE VICTIM SLIPPED UNDER THE WATER, THE OTHER JUVENILE WAS ABLE TO GET OUT OF THE WATER AND WAS TREATED AND RELEASED.

## Analysis

This is a textbook Electric Shock Drowning incident: swimmers near a boat moored at a marina, electrical shock felt upon approaching the swim ladder, victim incapacitated in water and drowned. The cause of death is listed as "Hypothermia" rather than "Electrocution" — a classic example of the misclassification that makes ESD so difficult to track in official databases.
