## Key Findings

### 1. Lake of the Ozarks 2012 Was a Three-Death Cluster
The known list entry "2012" for Lake of the Ozarks refers to the Anderson children (Alexandra, 13, and Brayden, 8 — July 4), documented in LEGAL-2012-001. Jennifer Lankford (26) died three days later on July 7 at a different dock on the same lake. All three deaths occurred within a single week. LEGAL-2012-003 documents Lankford as a distinct third victim.

### 2. No Lawsuits Found for Missouri Private-Dock ESD Deaths
None of the three confirmed Lake of the Ozarks ESD deaths at private docks (Deeds 2004, Lankford 2012, Colburn 2015) resulted in identified civil lawsuits. The Missouri Supreme Court's 2015 dismissal of Anderson v. Ameren on Recreational Use Act grounds is the likely deterrent. Private dock owners enjoy broad immunity under MO Rev. Stat. §§ 537.345-537.348 when users are admitted free of charge.

### 3. Ohio and New Jersey 2017 Cases Lack Confirmed Legal Filings
Both the Currie (Put-in-Bay, OH) and Matos (Toms River, NJ) deaths occurred within 24 hours of each other in June 2017, generated substantial national media coverage, and appear to involve clear liability theories (boat shore power fault; homeowner negligent maintenance of live boat lift), but no civil lawsuit filings have been confirmed in open sources as of this research session.

### 4. ODNR Fault Attribution Complicates Currie Litigation
ODNR's determination that the fault was in the Currie family's own boat — not the marina's dock wiring — substantially complicates any premises liability claim against Miller Marina. Potential defendants would be the shore power cord manufacturer or the Sea Ray Sundancer boat manufacturer, which would require a products liability theory rather than premises liability.
