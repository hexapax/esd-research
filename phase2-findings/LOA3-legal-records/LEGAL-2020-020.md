## ESD Mechanism
Brothers Timothy and Michael Miller were at Scorpion Bay Marina's Dock A with their wives aboard their docked boat. Timothy jumped into the water and immediately experienced electrical shock symptoms, going under. Michael and Timothy's girlfriend jumped in to help; both experienced the same electrical current. Bystanders pulled Michael and the girlfriend out; Timothy was located and recovered by divers overnight. Michael was transported to the hospital and died. Power to Dock A was cut with assistance from APS (Arizona Public Service) before emergency personnel could safely enter the water.

**Disputed source:** The Maricopa County Sheriff's Office (MCSO) concluded that the brothers' own boat had an incompatible electrical connection system with the marina's receptacle, and that "electrical safety features were bypassed by the intentional and improper modification of the boat's electrical connection system." The family's attorney disputed this finding, stating: (1) the family had only recently acquired the boat and made no modifications; (2) witness statements contradicted the sheriff's version; and (3) a boat belonging to an adjacent slip holder — not the victims' boat — had a cord in the water, and there had been prior complaints about GFI circuit failures at Scorpion Bay Marina.

## Notes
- The MCSO's determination that the boat's own modifications caused the discharge is disputed by the family. Attorney Pearson cited prior complaints about GFI circuit failures at the marina and a cord from an adjacent boat that was in the water, suggesting the marina or another vessel may have contributed.
- The "intentional and improper modification" language in the MCSO release is unusually strong and may have discouraged or complicated a civil lawsuit against the marina.
- No civil court filing or settlement has been identified, which may reflect: (a) MCSO's determination effectively shielding the marina; (b) a confidential settlement; or (c) no suit being filed.
- This incident is consistent with classic marina ESD: shore power compatibility failure discharging AC current into marina water, paralyzing swimmers near the dock.
- Victim ages (50 and 53) are notably older than typical ESD victims; both were experienced boaters (marina members for one month).
- The Scorpion Bay Marina is operated on U.S. Army Corps of Engineers land within Lake Pleasant Regional Park; the City of Peoria electrical inspector was involved in the post-incident investigation, and the marina's infrastructure was declared safe by MCSO.
- Lake Pleasant is a freshwater reservoir approximately 40 miles NW of Phoenix; it is the setting for seasonal recreational boating and ESD risk is consistent with freshwater marina dynamics.
- No National Park Service concessioner context (contrast with McQueen/Aramark case, LEGAL-2014-003); Scorpion Bay is a private marina operator.
