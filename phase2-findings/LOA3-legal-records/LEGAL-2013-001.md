# LEGAL-2013-001: Hernandez v. Hilton Houston Westchase — Houston, TX

## Incident Summary
- **Victim:** Raul Hernandez Martinez, age 27 (died September 6, 2013)
- **Date:** August 31, 2013
- **Location:** Hilton Houston Westchase Hotel pool, Houston, Harris County, Texas
- **Type:** Pool electrocution — electrocuted by faulty pool lights while rescuing nephew; no GFCIs installed; unpermitted electrical work by Brown Electric Inc.

## Legal Details
- **Plaintiff:** Family of Raul Hernandez Martinez
- **Defendant:** Hilton Hotels & Resorts; Interstate Hotels and Resorts; WS Westchase LLC; Brown Electric Inc.
- **Law Firm:** Thomas J. Henry Law (referenced in coverage)
- **Case Type:** Wrongful death, gross negligence
- **Outcome:** Settlement reached in 2015 with all defendants — confidential amount; included college funds and annuities for family members (mother, girlfriend, nephew, girlfriend's daughter). Did not go to trial.
- **Criminal Charges:** Electricians Jason Joseph Gorczyca and James Ray Pyle of Brown Electric Inc. were charged with **criminally negligent homicide** for performing substandard electrical work without a permit. The pool lacked GFCI protection on the lighting circuit, violating the National Electrical Code. This is one of the few known ESD-related cases resulting in criminal charges against individuals.
