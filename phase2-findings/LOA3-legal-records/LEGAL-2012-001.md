# LEGAL-2012-001: Anderson v. Union Electric (Ameren Missouri) — Lake of the Ozarks, MO

## Incident Summary
- **Victims:** Alexandra Anderson, age 13-14; Brayden Anderson, age 8 (both died)
- **Date:** July 4, 2012
- **Location:** Lake of the Ozarks, Morgan County, Missouri (family vacation home dock)
- **Type:** ESD — siblings electrocuted by stray current from family dock while swimming; drowned

## Legal Details
- **Plaintiff:** Angela Anderson (mother)
- **Defendant:** Union Electric Company d/b/a Ameren Missouri
- **Case Filed:** July 1, 2013
- **Plaintiff's Argument:** Ameren charges dock permit fees, can revoke permits for unsafe docks, was aware some docks lacked GFCI protection
- **Defense:** Protected by Missouri Recreational Use Act (free public recreational access)
- **Outcome:**
  - Morgan County Circuit Court: Dismissed (Ameren immune under Recreational Use Act)
  - Missouri Court of Appeals: Reversed dismissal (June 2014)
  - **Missouri Supreme Court: Upheld dismissal in 5-2 decision (June 16, 2015)** — ruled dock permit fees distinct from swimming access; Ameren immune
  - **No recovery for family**
