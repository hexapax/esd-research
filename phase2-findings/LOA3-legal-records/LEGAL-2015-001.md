# LEGAL-2015-001: Estate of Colburn — Lake of the Ozarks, MO (Incident Record — No Lawsuit Found)

NEW INCIDENT — No civil lawsuit identified; incident record only

## Basic Info
- **Incident Date:** June 21, 2015 (Father's Day)
- **Location:** Woods Hollow Cove, Osage Arm, Lake of the Ozarks, Osage Beach, MO (22.2 Mile Marker)
- **Victim:** Marcus Colburn, 21, of East Moline, IL
- **Outcome:** Fatal

## Legal Action
- **Case Name:** None identified
- **Court:** N/A
- **Case Number:** N/A
- **Filing Date:** N/A
- **Resolution:** No civil lawsuit found. Investigated by Missouri State Highway Patrol (Cpl. Scott White) and Osage Beach Fire Protection District. Cause determined: faulty breaker box on the dock.

## ESD Mechanism
Colburn was on family vacation and swimming near a private residential dock on Valley Drive in Osage Beach. He and fellow swimmer Taylor Curley felt electricity in the water. Occupants of the house had noticed the dock's circuit breaker tripping repeatedly before the incident, but reset it without identifying the cause. When Colburn attempted to exit the water using a metal dock ladder, he received a fatal shock and fell face-down into the water. Curley had minor injuries; Colburn was pronounced dead at 10:14 p.m. The Osage Beach Fire Protection District's post-incident report identified a "faulty junction box between dock and residence" as the cause. The faulty breaker box had passed two external (visual/watertightness) inspections the prior year but had not had the internal components inspected.
