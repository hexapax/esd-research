## Key Legal Findings (Combined Across All Sessions)

1. **Governmental immunity** is a major barrier: Both the Knudsen (MI) and Anderson (MO) cases were dismissed on immunity grounds despite clear ESD causation. Michigan Courts of Appeals (2014) also granted harbormaster immunity.
2. **Recreational Use Acts** shield utilities/landowners: Missouri Supreme Court ruled 5-2 that Ameren was immune despite charging dock permit fees.
3. **Known settlement/verdict amounts range from $150K to $6M** for marina/dock/pool ESD (Crays $150K, Chipley $700K, Harbison $2.325M, lifeguard $6M).
4. **Many settlements are confidential**: Davenport, Hernandez, Chipley (partial) cases settled but amounts undisclosed.
5. **NEC Article 555.24** (marina warning signs) is increasingly cited in complaints (Marina Shores/Gonzalez case).
6. **Product liability** (Pentair pool lights, pool pumps) is a distinct litigation track from marina/dock ESD.
7. **Boat modification/compatibility disputes** can complicate marina liability: Scorpion Bay Marina case illustrates how MCSO findings attributing fault to victims' own boat may have deterred or complicated civil litigation.
8. **The 2009 AmerenUE verdict** in the Harbison case (Spring Lake, MO) is described by Gray Ritter Graham law firm as the first successful US utility lawsuit for stray voltage causing death in a lake — a landmark precedent.
9. **Criminal charges remain extremely rare**: Only one confirmed ESD criminal prosecution identified (Houston pool electricians, 2015, criminally negligent homicide).
10. **Boat lift electrical boxes** are confirmed as a distinct ESD source: three documented cases (Kayla Matos/NJ, Thomas Milner/GA, Virginia Coast Guard officer/VA) all involve improperly grounded boat lifts.
