# LEGAL-2023-001: Davenport v. Harbourside Place — Jupiter, FL (NEW INCIDENT)

## Incident Summary
- **Victim:** Nathaniel "Nate" Davenport, age 45, of Jupiter, FL (died)
- **Date:** October 22, 2023
- **Location:** Harbourside Place fountain/amphitheater area, Jupiter, Palm Beach County, Florida
- **Type:** Electrocution in water — faulty light fixtures in fountain leaked electrical voltage into water; victim entered fountain to rescue children being shocked

## Narrative
On October 22, 2023, Nate Davenport and a friend had taken some of their children to Harbourside Place, a retail/restaurant complex on the Intracoastal Waterway in Jupiter, FL. Three children began playing in the splash area and fountain. The children started screaming that they were being shocked. Davenport jumped into the fountain, pulled his children out, but was overcome by the electrical current. He and four others were rushed to hospitals. Davenport died at Jupiter Medical Center. Three children were treated at St. Mary's Medical Center in West Palm Beach.

The Town of Jupiter determined that light fixtures in the fountain were leaking electrical voltage into the water, and that no ground fault circuit interrupter (GFCI) had been installed for the fountain's electrical system.

A prior incident had been reported on July 10, 2023, when Dina Fleck of Palm Beach Gardens told police she and friends felt electrical shocks at the same fountain, but a Harbourside Place employee "dismissed it."

## Legal Details
- **Plaintiff:** Amy Davenport (wife), on behalf of estate and four minor children
- **Defendant:** Harbourside Place (HarPla Holdings LLC or related entity)
- **Filed:** December 27, 2023, Palm Beach County Circuit Court
- **Case Type:** Wrongful death; negligent infliction of emotional distress
- **Allegations:** Harbourside Place failed to install GFCI for fountain, lights, and electrical systems; knew or should have known of dangerous conditions; failed to maintain safe premises
- **Damages Sought:** More than $50,000 (Florida threshold filing amount)
- **Outcome:** Settled via mediation on March 6, 2024; settlement amount confidential; approved by judge (required under Florida Wrongful Death Act for minor children). Additional lawsuit filed in February 2024 by another man who was shocked while attempting to pull Davenport and children from the water.
