# LEGAL-2012-040: Rhine v. State of Georgia — South Georgia Technical College Fountain Electrocution

## Status
NEW INCIDENT (for LEGAL series; incident previously documented as FI-2012-003)

## Basic Info
- **Incident Date:** September 15, 2012 (some sources say "late September 2012")
- **Location:** South Georgia Technical College campus fountain, Americus, Sumter County, Georgia
- **Victim:** Adriana Rhine (sometimes spelled Adrianna), age 18–19 (sources differ; 18 per most)
- **Outcome:** Fatal

## Legal/Regulatory Action
- **Agency:** Georgia State Government (sued via wrongful death claim under state tort law)
- **Case/Citation Number:** Unknown (Sumter County probate court, likely also Superior Court)
- **Attorney for Plaintiff:** Yehuda Smolar
- **Penalty/Settlement:** **$1,400,000** (record wrongful death settlement in Georgia at the time)
  - $1,000,000 for Rhine's wrongful death (maximum allowed under Georgia's tort claims cap)
  - $400,000 for the emotional distress suffered by Rhine's 3-year-old son Zi'Quan Bennett, who witnessed his mother's death
- **Settlement Date:** November 2013 (reached); approved by probate court in March 2014
- **Resolution:** Settled; criminal charges not filed by Sumter County Sheriff's Department or DA

## ESD Mechanism
Rhine entered a ground-level decorative fountain pond on the South Georgia Technical College campus to retrieve her young son's ball. The fountain's wiring — for the lighting and motor under the fountain — had at least 17 code violations described by plaintiff's attorneys as "incompetent and negligent." When Rhine entered the water, she was electrocuted. Multiple bystanders attempted rescue but were themselves shocked and had to withdraw. A prior electrical shock at the same fountain had occurred approximately one month earlier (another student was "severely electrocuted"); the college's only response was to drain, wash, and refill the fountain without repairing the wiring.

The mechanism is consistent with ESD/in-water electrocution: AC current from the submersed fountain motor/lighting entered the water, incapacitating Rhine who then drowned. This is a fountain rather than a dock/marina but involves the same water-electrification mechanism.

## Notes
- This is the **only known ESD-type settlement against a U.S. state government** for a water electrocution incident found in this research series.
- The $1 million cap reflects Georgia's sovereign immunity tort limit at the time; the $400K for the child was structured as a separate claim to exceed the cap.
- The college taught residential and commercial electricians — an irony noted by the family's attorney.
- **17 NEC violations** is one of the highest documented violation counts in any ESD case in this dataset.
- The prior shock incident (one month earlier) is a key "knowledge" fact: the institution knew of the hazard and failed to remediate it.
- Incident cross-reference: **FI-2012-003** in the ESDPA-derived dataset. That file documents the incident; this LEGAL file adds the settlement details not previously captured in the LEGAL series.
- The fountain is not a dock/marina, but the ESD mechanism (submersed electrical equipment energizing water in which a person is immersed) is identical. The ESDPA includes this incident in its list.
