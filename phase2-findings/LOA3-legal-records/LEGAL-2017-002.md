## ESD Mechanism
The Currie family docked their 33-foot Sea Ray Sundancer at Miller Marina and plugged the shore power into the boat. When the family dog fell into the water, Evan's father Jeffrey Currie jumped in and was immediately immobilized by electric current. Evan and his brother both jumped in to help; both struggled. Bystanders alerted Mrs. Currie (who remained on board) to unplug the shore power cord. When she did so, the current in the water stopped and all three Curries and the dog were retrieved from the water. However, Evan Currie was unresponsive and convulsing; CPR was administered en route to Port Clinton, where he was pronounced dead. Ohio Department of Natural Resources (ODNR) investigation determined that a faulty cord or outlet caused the electrocution. An independent electrician hired by ODNR found all dock power outlets at Miller Marina functioning normally, placing the fault source in the boat's shore power system. ODNR took possession of the boat and associated equipment.
