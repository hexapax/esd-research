- **Victim:** Kayla Matos, 11, of Newark, NJ (Ironbound neighborhood)

## ESD Mechanism
Kayla Matos and two friends were visiting a home on Tobago Avenue in Toms River, where they were playing with an inflatable raft in the backyard lagoon. The homeowners did not own a boat and the metal boat lift on the dock had been dormant for years. However, an old, corroded circuit box had energized the metal rail of the boat lift. When two of the girls touched the rail, one (Kayla) received a fatal electric shock. Adults at the scene performed CPR; arriving EMS applied a defibrillator. Kayla was transported to Community Medical Center in Toms River, where she died that evening. The other two girls were uninjured. The boat lift had not been operated by current homeowners, but the wiring was apparently still live.

## Notes
- DUPLICATE OF KNOWN: "Toms River NJ 2017 (Kayla Matos)" — this is the known incident.
- No civil lawsuit announcement found in news searches. Potential defendants: homeowner of the dock (premises liability, negligence in maintaining dangerous condition) and potentially the contractor who last worked on the boat lift's electrical system.
- The Mayor of Toms River (Thomas Kelaher) stated that the boat lift was "not used" by current residents, indicating an inherited hazard — which could strengthen a premises liability claim against the homeowner if negligent in failing to inspect/deactivate the equipment.
- The incident occurred one day after Evan Currie's death in Put-in-Bay, OH; both cases received simultaneous national media attention.
- NEC Article 555 requires GFCI protection for boat lift circuits; the dormant breaker bypass would be a code violation if discovered.
