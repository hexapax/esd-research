## Key Findings from Session 2

### 1. Insurance Industry Recognizes ESD Liability but Discloses No Claims Data
Multiple major marine insurers publish detailed ESD safety advisories (Cincinnati Insurance, Chubb stray current checklist, Merrimac Marine, Global Marine). All confirm ESD is a covered liability and that they actively advise marina clients on prevention. None publish claims data, loss statistics, or settlement amounts. This is consistent with the prior LOA3-C finding that insurance claim data is not publicly accessible.

### 2. No State Attorney General Has Filed ESD Enforcement Actions
Comprehensive search across all query patterns confirms no state AG has ever filed an enforcement action, lawsuit, or investigation targeting a marina or dock owner for ESD. The Rhine case — the closest to regulatory enforcement — was a civil wrongful death suit by a private family against the state, not an AG action.

### 3. Tennessee Noah Dean and Nate Act (T.C.A. § 68-102-602) — Significant Regulatory Framework
The only mandatory state regulatory enforcement program for marina ESD prevention was enacted in Tennessee in 2014, triggered by the Cherokee Lake 2012 deaths. The law requires:
- 100 mA ground fault protection on main overcurrent devices
- Mandatory SFMO inspection every 5 years (204 marinas inspected 2015–2017)
- Required "NO SWIMMING WITHIN 100 YARDS" signage
- Criminal penalties: up to Class E felony ($50,000 fine) for violations causing death
- No known prosecutions under this law were found in this search

### 4. OSHA-Documented Marina Electrocution Fatalities (Workers Only)
Three OSHA-documented marina/dock electrocution fatalities were confirmed. All involve workers in direct contact with energized equipment; none involve the ESD mechanism. All are already documented in GOV-series files:
- GOV-2008-001: Ocean Isle Beach Marina, NC (drill user, no GFCI)
- Mid River Marina, NY (2022, overhead power line): OSHA 148483.015
- Marine construction pier, NC (2023): OSHA 160119.015

### 5. Fountain Electrocutions Follow Same Legal Pattern as Marina ESD
The Rhine case confirms that fountain/pool electrocutions follow the same legal liability pattern as marina ESD: multiple NEC violations, prior knowledge, failure to remediate, large wrongful death settlement. CPSC's aggregate data covers pool and spa electrocution deaths.

### 6. EC&M Web "Engine Room Electrocution" Case
The EC&M Web article (www.ecmweb.com/maintenance-repair-operations/article/20896482/) describes an anonymized marina case where a boat owner was electrocuted in an engine compartment. The case settled confidentially. The expert testified that marinas with "superior knowledge" have a higher duty than boat owners. This represents an additional data point on marina liability without identifying the incident.

## Regulatory Significance

### State-Level ESD Enforcement Programs Found:
1. **Tennessee** (TN Code § 68-102-602): Mandatory SFMO inspection every 5 years; criminal penalties up to Class E felony for violations causing death; enacted 2014, 300+ marinas inspected
2. **West Virginia**: Michael Cunningham Act (named after Stonewall Jackson Lake 2010 victim); requires ELCI on boats using shore power at WV marinas
3. **Indiana**: No specific state ESD statute found; Indiana DNR investigating Marina Shores 2025 incident (LEGAL-2025-001)

### No Federal ESD Enforcement Program Exists
The regulatory gap persists: no single federal agency has jurisdiction over recreational ESD deaths. The CPSC has not issued any dock/marina product recall; OSHA covers workers only; USCG covers vessels on navigable waters. State fire marshals with active programs (TN, WV) were prompted by specific child deaths.
