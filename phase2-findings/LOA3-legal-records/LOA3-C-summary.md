## Key Findings

### 1. No Attorney General Enforcement Actions Exist
No state attorney general in any U.S. state has filed an enforcement action, lawsuit, or investigation related to ESD or marina/dock/pool electrocution deaths. All legal accountability has come through private civil wrongful death lawsuits filed by victims' families.

### 2. Criminal Charges Are Extremely Rare
The Hernandez/Houston case (2013) is the only confirmed instance where criminal charges (criminally negligent homicide) were filed against individuals responsible for electrical work that led to an ESD death. No marina owner, dock owner, or property manager has been criminally charged for an ESD death.

### 3. OSHA Has Minimal ESD Coverage
OSHA's jurisdiction covers workers, not recreational swimmers. Only 3 marina/dock electrocution fatalities appear in OSHA records (2008-2023), and none involve the classic ESD mechanism of current leaking through water to a swimmer. The vast majority of ESD victims are recreational users (children, swimmers, boaters) outside OSHA's jurisdiction.

### 4. CPSC Has No Product-Specific Recalls for Dock/Marina Equipment
Despite decades of ESD deaths caused by dock wiring, shore power connections, battery chargers, and boat lifts, CPSC has issued no product-specific recalls for these categories. CPSC's involvement is limited to aggregate pool/spa electrocution statistics and a 2003 safety advisory.

### 5. Insurance Industry Data Is Not Publicly Available
No insurance company or industry association publishes ESD-specific claims data, loss statistics, or settlement aggregates. Several insurers (Cincinnati Insurance, Merrimac Insurance) publish ESD safety bulletins for marina clients, confirming the industry recognizes the liability risk, but no quantitative loss data is accessible.

### 7. Regulatory Gap
The search confirms a regulatory gap first identified in the LOA4-C summary: no single federal agency has jurisdiction over recreational ESD deaths. OSHA covers workers only. USCG covers vessels on navigable waters. CPSC covers consumer products but has not acted on dock/marina equipment. State fire marshals (TN, WV) have the most active programs, but only after specific legislative mandates triggered by child deaths.

## Search Limitations

- **OSHA database:** Only indexes workplace fatalities; most ESD victims are recreational users. OSHA Accident Search does not support full-text keyword queries, limiting discovery.
- **Attorney general records:** No centralized database of state AG enforcement actions; searched via web queries for each state with known ESD incidents.
- **Insurance claims:** Protected by confidentiality; no public databases exist for marina liability claims.
- **Court records:** Settlement amounts are typically confidential; only 2 of 11 documented lawsuits have publicly reported settlement figures.
- **CPSC:** SaferProducts.gov consumer complaint database was not directly queried (requires individual product category searches); aggregate reports lack individual incident detail.
