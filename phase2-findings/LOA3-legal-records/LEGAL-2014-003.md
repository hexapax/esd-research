# LEGAL-2014-001: McQueen v. Aramark Corp — Bullfrog Marina, Lake Powell, UT

## Incident Summary
- **Victim:** Alec McQueen, age 22, of Salt Lake City, UT (died)
- **Date:** June 10, 2014
- **Location:** Bullfrog Marina, Lake Powell, Kane County, Utah
- **Type:** Electric shock drowning — victim jumped from boat into water near dock; electrified by shore power receptacle fault

## Legal Details
- **Plaintiffs:** Whitney McQueen and Jenny Wesenberg (parents)
- **Defendants:** Aramark Sports and Entertainment Services (marina concessioner for NPS); Joshua Smith (boat owner who plugged into faulty receptacle)
- **Case:** McQueen v. Aramark Corp, Case No. 2:15-cv-00492-DAK-PMW, U.S. District Court for the District of Utah
- **Filed:** July 2015
- **Case Type:** Wrongful death, negligence
- **Allegations:** Aramark was negligent in allowing water around the dock to become electrified; a 30-amp boat power cord was plugged into a 50-amp shore power receptacle that Aramark was responsible for maintaining; the boat began discharging electricity into surrounding water
- **Outcome:** Not publicly reported; case involved discovery disputes (court ordered Aramark to identify employees responsible for shore power maintenance/inspection)
