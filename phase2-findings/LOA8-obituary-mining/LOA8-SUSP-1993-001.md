# LOA8-SUSP-1993-001 — Obituary & Source Research

## Target Summary

ESDPA list entry #24 (Harbor Marine Consultants, June 2004 list) / variously numbered #25, #64, #89 in later ESDPA revisions (Shafer & Rifkin).

**Known details from ESDPA list:**
- **Date:** August 1993 (some versions say July 1993; the 8/15/2025 revision says "July 1993")
- **Location:** Alexandria Bay, Jefferson County, New York (St. Lawrence River, Thousand Islands region)
- **What happened:** Two teenage girls snorkeling near a dock were paralyzed by electric shock and drowned. Two bystanders entered the water to lend assistance but were shocked by the voltage in the water (they were treated at hospital and released).
- **Electrical fault:** Dock wiring gnawed by rodents (per later ESDPA list versions); earlier Harbor Marine version says "Fault thought to be in dock wiring, not confirmed."
- **Victim names:** Unknown
- **Specific dock/marina:** Not named in any ESDPA source

### 1. Date Discrepancy: July vs. August 1993
| Source | Date Given |
|--------|-----------|
| Harbor Marine (June 2004, earliest) | August 1993 |
| ESDPA list 5/9/2016 | August 1993 |
| ESDPA list 5/21/2016 | August 1993 |

| ESDPA list 8/15/2025 (most recent) | **July 1993** |
| AuGres Museum list | August 1993 |
| 2CoolFishing forum | August 1993 |
| LOA1-A summary in this project | August 1993 |

The date changed from "August 1993" to "July 1993" only in the most recent (8/15/2025) ESDPA revision. All prior versions consistently say August. It is unclear whether this reflects a correction based on new evidence or is a transcription error in the latest revision.

### 2. Wiring Fault: "Not Confirmed" vs. "Gnawed by Rodents"
| Source | Wiring Fault Description |
|--------|-------------------------|
| Harbor Marine (June 2004, earliest) | "Fault **thought to be** in dock wiring, **not confirmed**" |
| ESDPA list 5/9/2016 and all later versions | "Fault **was** in dock wiring **gnawed by rodents**" |

The earliest version (2004) presents the dock wiring fault as unconfirmed. By 2016, the ESDPA list asserts the fault as confirmed and specifies the cause as rodent damage to wiring insulation. This likely reflects additional investigation or information obtained by Shafer/Rifkin between 2004 and 2016, but the underlying evidence for this change is not cited.

### 3. Bystander Description: "Unable to" vs. "Were Shocked"
| Source | Bystander Description |
|--------|----------------------|
| Harbor Marine (June 2004, earliest) | "Two bystanders attempted to enter water to lend assistance, but **were unable to do so**" |
| ESDPA list 5/9/2016 and later | "Two bystanders **entered the water** to lend assistance, but **were shocked** by the voltage in the water (**they were treated at hospital and released**)" |

The earliest version implies bystanders could not enter the water at all. The later versions say they did enter the water, were shocked, and received hospital treatment. The later version is more detailed and internally consistent (you would not be "treated at hospital" if you never entered the water). This likely reflects more complete information obtained over time.

## Assessment

This is the **only incident in the ESDPA list for which zero independent primary sources have been found** despite extensive searching across three separate lines of analysis (LOA1, LOA2, and now LOA8). The incident description is highly specific and detailed (snorkeling, paralysis, bystander shock, rodent-damaged wiring, hospital treatment), which argues against fabrication. However, the complete absence of any newspaper coverage, obituary, court record, or government record in any freely accessible online database is notable for a double-fatality drowning event.

The most likely explanation is that this incident occurred in a small tourist village in 1993, was covered by the Watertown Daily Times and/or local weeklies that have not been digitized for this date range, and was classified as a drowning without the electrical component being recognized at the time. The ESDPA compilers likely learned of it through industry contacts (marine electrical professionals working in the Thousand Islands region) rather than from news coverage.
