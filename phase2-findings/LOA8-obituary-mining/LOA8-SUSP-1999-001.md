## New Incident Confirmed?

**NO -- Incident remains UNVERIFIED from primary sources.**

The incident is consistently documented across all versions of the ESDPA/Harbor Marine Consultants list (from June 2004 through August 2025), but every online reference traces back to a single secondary source: the compiled list originally created by James D. Shafer of Harbor Marine Consultants, Inc. No independent primary source (news article, police report, coroner's report, court record, government investigation, or obituary) has been found online.

However, the incident should NOT be dismissed:
- The level of technical detail (40-year-old wiring, insulation failure, substantial ground fault under moored boats) suggests an actual investigation was conducted.
- The ESDPA list notes it was compiled "from various sources" including "circumstantial evidence, i.e., multiple deaths, eye witnesses, considerable distress, cries for help, shock sensation reported by rescuers."
- The Delta Marina Yacht Harbor in Rio Vista, built in the late 1950s, has infrastructure age consistent with the described "forty-year-old power wiring."
- Rio Vista is a real freshwater marina community on the Sacramento River Delta where the ESD mechanism described is plausible.

## Conflicting Information

1. **Date ambiguity:** The ESDPA list says "Approx. 1999" -- the incident could have occurred anytime in the late 1990s, possibly 1997-2000.
2. **No victim count discrepancy:** All sources consistently say two boys aged 8-10 drowned.
3. **No marina name provided:** Despite Rio Vista having identifiable marinas (Delta Marina Yacht Harbor being the most prominent), no version of the ESDPA list names the specific marina.
4. **No names provided in any source revision:** Across 20+ years and multiple revisions of the ESDPA list, victim names have never been added to this entry, which is unusual -- many other entries on the list have been updated with names over time. This may indicate the original report came through an informal channel (e.g., word-of-mouth from a marina operator or electrician) rather than from a news report or official investigation.

## Assessment

This is a plausible ESD incident with consistent documentation in the ESDPA list across two decades. The technical detail about 40-year-old wiring and insulation failure suggests a genuine investigation was conducted. However, the complete absence of primary sources online, the lack of victim names after 25+ years, and the "Approx." date qualifier all suggest this may have been reported to James Shafer through informal industry channels (e.g., a marina electrician or insurance adjuster) rather than through official or media channels.
