## Sources Found

### Source 1: CDC / NIOSH MIFACE Report 17MI208 (PRIMARY SOURCE — already in SUSP file)
- **URL**: https://stacks.cdc.gov/view/cdc/164906/cdc_164906_DS1.pdf
- **Type**: Federal government occupational fatality investigation report
- **Date**: Report date March 9, 2020 (incident: Summer 2017)
- **Key info**: This is the foundational source already cited in SUSP-2017-020. Key facts confirmed by the CDC report itself:
  - Male marina owner in his 80s drowned in approximately six feet of water at a boat slip on a river in Michigan
  - Victim was planning to sell the marina; had been upgrading and renovating it
  - Spouse described him as a "good swimmer"
  - Found floating upright in the water under a boat slip walkway, crown of head visible
  - Tools found in nearby Boat A with keys in ignition (suggesting he was doing maintenance)
  - Neither the sheriff nor the medical examiner investigator checked the water for stray electrical current
  - MIFACE learned of the incident upon locating an obituary (the obituary triggered the investigation)
  - Report published March 9, 2020 — approximately 2.5 years after the incident

## Electrical Investigation Status

**No electrical investigation was conducted.** This is explicitly documented in the CDC FACE report: "Neither the sheriff or the medical examiner investigator checked the water for stray electrical current." The CDC report did not recommend electrical testing as a follow-up action; instead, recommendations focused on personal flotation devices and safety protocols.

This represents a significant missed opportunity: a marina environment with active boat slips and shore power infrastructure is precisely the setting where ESD must be excluded before a drowning classification is finalized.

2. **Michigan medical examiner records**: Request the medical examiner investigator's report for a drowning of a male in his 80s at a marina on a river in Michigan in Summer 2017.

5. **Electrical testing**: Commission current testing of the affected boat slip (if the marina can be identified) to determine whether stray current existed or currently exists at the location.
6. **Victim's obituary**: If the MIFACE program can be contacted, they may be able to identify the obituary source (newspaper, state, funeral home) without disclosing the victim's name, enabling targeted search.
