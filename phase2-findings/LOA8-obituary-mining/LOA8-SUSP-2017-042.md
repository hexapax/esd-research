# LOA8-SUSP-2017-042 — Obituary & Source Research

## Target Summary

On Friday, April 14, 2017, Shelly Marie (Kincaid) Darling (34) and Mary Elizabeth "Liz" Whipple (41) went to Darling's family lake house at the 15000 block of West Winds Drive in Northport, Alabama, on Lake Tuscaloosa to sunbathe on the dock. Darling's husband, Chris Darling (Assistant Athletics Director for Development at the University of Alabama), last saw his wife at 8:00 a.m. when he left to play golf. When Darling failed to return for dinner Friday evening, family members went to the lake house and found the women's belongings on the dock, but the women were missing. Tuscaloosa Police and dive teams were called. During the search, a Tuscaloosa police officer/investigator was shocked by electrical current on the dock where the women had been sunbathing. The officer was not injured but the shock confirmed a live electrical hazard at the location. A dive team and lake patrol recovered both bodies from the lake at approximately 4:30 a.m. Saturday, April 15, 2017.

Preliminary autopsies determined the cause of death for both women as electrocution. Their deaths were ruled accidental. Tuscaloosa County Metro Homicide Unit Capt. Kip Hart stated: "We know there was electricity going through at least part of the pier." Hart also noted that divers could see arcing between the police boat and the dock as they approached. The exact source of the electricity was believed to be dock wiring powering a boat lift and electrical outlets, but a specific fault was never publicly identified.

Both women worked at the University of Alabama School of Law's legal clinics. Darling was a staff attorney in the Elder Law Clinic since 2014. Whipple was the interim director of the Domestic Violence Clinic since 2015.

This incident is already cataloged in the dataset as **FI-2017-012** (with a date error of March 29, 2017 corrected to April 14-15, 2017) and as **SUSP-2017-042** in the LOA2 misclassified drownings work.

## Conflicting Information

1. **Date discrepancy in ESDPA list vs. original FI file:** The original FI-2017-012 file listed the date as March 29, 2017. All independent sources confirm the correct date is April 14-15, 2017. This date error has been previously identified and noted.

2. **Vititoe Law Group date error:** The Vititoe Law Group article states the women "went missing after sunbathing on Lake Tuscaloosa on April 16" -- this is incorrect. All primary sources confirm they went to the lake on Friday April 14 and were found early Saturday April 15.

3. **Waterway Guide date error:** This source states the incident occurred "on April 20, 2017" -- this is the date of autopsy results being announced, not the incident date. It also incorrectly groups Carmen Johnson (Smith Lake, Winston County, 2016) with Darling/Whipple (Lake Tuscaloosa, 2017) as if they occurred on the same date and lake.

4. **Electrocution vs. Electric Shock Drowning:** Capt. Hart's press conference transcript suggests the medical examiner ruled the deaths as electrocution (not drowning), which is significant. In the YouTube transcript, Hart discusses this distinction: a questioner asks "so they were shocked, they didn't get shock and then drown?" and Hart responds about the electrocution ruling. This means the current was strong enough to kill directly, rather than the ESD mechanism of muscular paralysis followed by drowning. This is an important distinction -- these may technically be dock electrocution deaths rather than classic ESD.

5. **Eric Hughes death ruling:** Hughes's death was officially ruled drowning/asphyxia, NOT electrocution, even though investigators believed electricity was involved. This is consistent with the broader pattern of ESD deaths being miscoded as drownings.
