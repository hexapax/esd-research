- **Type:** Technical/trade magazine article (independent of ESDPA)
- **Date:** April 2004
- **Author:** Not bylined (ECM Web staff)
- **Key info:** The primary independent source for this incident. Confirms:
  - Mother and adult daughter died on Memorial Day at a Southeast lake resort houseboat
  - Both described as "good swimmers" by surviving daughter
  - Post mortem found "neither body had suffered any physical trauma"
  - A second daughter who had not entered the water discovered her mother floating face-down near the swim ladder
  - Daughter was found "more than 50 feet below the surface"
  - Boat owner (husband/father) had been converting shore power cord that morning; miswired ground and hot at female plug connector, energizing the aluminum hull, railing, and ladder
  - Boat's internal wiring was also altered; incoming power changed from 125V to 125/250V, exceeding rated capacity
  - Investigation confirmed ESD as cause
  - Victim identities and location deliberately anonymized

## Independent Source Assessment

The EC&M article is an **independent, non-ESDPA source** that was published as an industry training case study. The article deliberately anonymizes location and victims to protect family privacy and avoid litigation complications — this is standard practice for engineering case study publications. The anonymization means:

- No obituary can be located without the victims' names
- No news reports can be located without state/county identification
- No court records can be located without jurisdiction
- Searches for "Malone," "Hartley," and other potential name guesses yielded no relevant results

The SUSP file references academic ESD literature potentially linking this to the "Malone/Hartley case." No academic article using those names was located in searches. This linkage may be speculative or from a different case.

**The EC&M article IS the primary independent documentation of this incident** — it was not published by ESDPA, MikeHolt, or OnTimeService. It is a standalone engineering trade press article that independently documents and analyzes the incident with full technical detail, including the miswiring mechanism.
