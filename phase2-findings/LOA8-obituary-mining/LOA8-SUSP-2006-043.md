## Conflicts with SUSP/ESDPA Data

**Location name discrepancy:** The SUSP file uses "Summerset Lake" while independent sources consistently call the body of water "Spring Lake" (within the "Summerset Lake" gated subdivision/neighborhood). The lake itself is Spring Lake; the surrounding subdivision is Summerset Lake. This is not a factual conflict — the ESDPA list uses "Summerset Lake near DeSoto" as a geographic descriptor for the subdivision, while the actual lake where the drowning occurred is Spring Lake.

**Victim description:** SUSP file said "teenage boy, name not in public record." Independent sources confirm victim's full name is Nicholas Justin "Nic" Harbison, age 16, born October 4, 1989, died March 18, 2006 in Jefferson Memorial Hospital, Crystal City, MO.

**Utility fault details:** The SUSP file says "defective or missing neutral on a nearby underground power distribution cable." Independent sources (jury verdict) confirm AmerenUE's 7,200-volt underground cable under the lake had failed, consistent with ESDPA description.

## ESD Probability Assessment

**VERIFIED** — This is the strongest independently documented case in this batch. Multiple major independent news sources (St. Louis Post-Dispatch, KWMU/STLPR, LakeExpo), multiple independent memorial/obituary databases (Find a Grave, WeRemember, Jefferson County Online), and multiple independent legal databases (MoreLaw, Gray Ritter Graham, Claims Journal) all confirm: Nicholas Justin "Nic" Harbison, 16, died March 18, 2006 in Spring Lake at the Summerset Lake subdivision near DeSoto, Jefferson County, Missouri, after AmerenUE's defective underground power cable energized the lake water, causing him to drown while swimming toward the ladder. A Jefferson County civil jury in 2009 awarded $2.3 million to the three victims' families and found AmerenUE liable. The electrical cause was confirmed by investigation and jury verdict.
