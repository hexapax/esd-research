## Verdict

**POSSIBLE ESD — HIGH PROBABILITY (unchanged from SUSP-2016-020 assessment)**

The obituary and memorial mining confirms all previously documented details and adds significant biographical information. Neither obituary mentions ESD or electrical cause; both describe the deaths as a "tragic accident while boating" (Berger) or simply note the date of death (Freeney). The TWRA ruling remains "drowning" with no documented electrical investigation.

However, the behavioral evidence remains strongly consistent with ESD:
1. Two experienced, strong swimmers incapacitated in the same location within moments of each other
2. Classic ESD rescuer pattern: Freeney "literally just stopped and rolled over on his side" immediately upon entering the water
3. Berger showed signs of paralysis/incapacitation ("shook her head no and dropped her head") despite being an "exceptional swimmer"
4. Open-water freshwater lake environment during boating season (potential for electrical faults from nearby boats)
5. No weather, current, or intoxication factors present
6. Family (Annette Goodman) convinced both deaths were ESD, giving interviews to Charlotte media specifically about ESD

The verdict remains POSSIBLE ESD with HIGH probability. No electrical investigation was ever documented for this incident.
