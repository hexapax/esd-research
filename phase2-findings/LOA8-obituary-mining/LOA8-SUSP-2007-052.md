## Conflicts with SUSP/ESDPA Data

**Date discrepancy within ESDPA:** The 2024 ESDPA list (most recent) uses "July 24, 2006" while earlier lists (2016–2022) use "July 28, 2007." The SUSP file uses July 28, 2007 — consistent with the earlier majority of ESDPA versions. The 2024 date change appears to be a list-processing error.

**Outcome clarification:** The SUSP file says "paralyzed" but does not firmly confirm the woman died. The ESDPA language says "caused her to fall back into the water and drown" — indicating she drowned. SUSP file appears to have been conservative in stating the outcome as "paralyzed" when in fact the ESDPA text (the source) clearly states she drowned.
