## Water Type and ESD Feasibility Analysis

The Shrewsbury River at Monmouth Beach is a **tidal brackish/saltwater estuary**. The SUSP-2017-021 file flags this as a limiting factor for ESD.

**Additional research on water salinity:**
- The Shrewsbury River is a tidal inlet with salinity that varies by location and season
- In March (cold water, lower tidal mixing), salinity at inland marina locations (like the Channel Club Marina on West Street) may be reduced compared to summer months
- The Channel Club Marina is located approximately 2 miles inland from the river mouth at Sandy Hook Bay
- At 2 miles from the ocean, in March, the Shrewsbury River at this point may have significantly reduced salinity compared to the open bay
- However, without specific salinity measurements at this location in March 2017, ESD cannot be confirmed or excluded on salinity grounds

**ESD plausibility assessment (updated):** The Shrewsbury River at the Channel Club Marina location is sufficiently inland that reduced salinity in March is plausible, which could make ESD physically possible. However, this remains uncertain without salinity data.

## Alternative Explanations for Double Drowning

In cold water (March, NJ), the following non-ESD mechanisms can cause rapid incapacitation of both victims:
1. **Cold shock response**: Sudden immersion in cold water causes involuntary gasping, hyperventilation, and cardiac arrhythmia; this could incapacitate even a capable swimmer within minutes
2. **Hypothermia**: Progressive loss of muscle control; in 40-50°F water (typical for NJ in March), incapacitation can occur within minutes to hours
3. **Rescue drowning** (non-electrical): A panicking primary victim can pull a rescuer under; Hollen-Lappin entered cold water voluntarily with no safety equipment

These alternatives are not present in the SUSP file discussion and should be weighed against the ESD hypothesis.
