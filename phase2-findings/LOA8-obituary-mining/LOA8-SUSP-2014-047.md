## Electrical Cause (from independent sources)

- **Faulty boat lift motor** shorted out and transferred electrical current to the pontoon boat
- The pontoon boat acted as a conductor, energizing its ladder
- When Sarah Grace and the 17-year-old grabbed the ladder to climb out of the water, the ladder was electrically charged
- Tensas Parish Sheriff Ricky Jones identified the boat lift malfunction as the probable cause
- Classification by expert analysis: direct electrocution upon contact with energized ladder (not classic ESD-paralysis-and-drowning); however the outcome is the same

## Context: Church Youth Retreat

The incident occurred at a private home on Lake Bruin during a **church youth retreat** organized by First United Methodist Church of West Monroe. The teenagers were using the pontoon boat's ladder as a way to get in and out of the lake. The boat lift malfunction that energized the ladder would not have been visible or detectable to the teenagers or adult chaperones.

## ESD Probability Assessment

**HIGH — CONFIRMED ELECTROCUTION**

Official investigation by Tensas Parish Sheriff's Office confirmed electrocution via an energized pontoon boat ladder caused by a faulty boat lift motor. Sarah Grace Tellifero died from electrocution upon contact with the charged ladder. The 17-year-old who also grabbed the ladder survived. Multiple independent news sources (Shreveport Times, Ouachita Citizen, Scuttlebutt, Waterhawk) confirm the incident with consistent details. Official obituary confirmed at Mulhearn Funeral Home.

**Classification note**: Like the Lake Cumberland 2013 case, this is technically a direct electrocution upon contact with an energized ladder in water, rather than classic ESD (which involves paralysis while swimming through an electrified water field). The boat lift malfunction directly energized the ladder; both girls touched the ladder and received the shock simultaneously. The Scuttlebutt analysis makes this distinction explicitly.
