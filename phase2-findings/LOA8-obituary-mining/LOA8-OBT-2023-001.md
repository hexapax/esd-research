## Incident Description

On October 22, 2023, Nate Davenport visited Harbourside Place, an outdoor waterfront shopping/dining complex in Jupiter, Florida, with his friend Seth Kozak and several of their children. After lunch, three of the children began playing in the splash area and decorative fountain at the north end of the complex.

One child fell into the fountain water — described as having only about 30 seconds of contact before feeling an electrical current going through his body and losing consciousness. Another child felt a shock in one leg. A third child did not feel a shock. The children screamed for help.

Davenport heard the children's cries and jumped into the fountain to rescue them. He immediately froze and fell over upon entering the electrified water. He was able to throw the children out of the fountain before collapsing. He was transported to Jupiter Medical Center, where he died from his injuries.

Syler Sparks (age 20, Palm Beach Gardens), who was nearby, also entered the fountain in an attempt to help and was also shocked. He sustained injuries and was hospitalized. Seth Kozak (Davenport's friend) also required hospital care. A total of five people were taken to hospitals: three children (to St. Mary's Medical Center, West Palm Beach) and two adults (Davenport to Jupiter Medical Center, others to local hospitals).

## Electrical Cause

A post-incident inspection by the Town of Jupiter Building Department confirmed:
- Light fixtures inside the fountain were **leaking "electrical voltage" into the water**.
- The fixtures were broken; the fountain had not been properly maintained or inspected.
- Florida state law does **not require regular inspections of public fountains**.
- The fountain had been reported to have caused a shock in July 2023 — approximately 15 weeks before Davenport's death — when a woman named Dina Fleck let the water run over her hand and felt an electrical shock. She reportedly reported it to a Harbourside Place employee at the time.

## Prior Warning Evidence

A woman (Dina Fleck, Palm Beach Gardens) told the Palm Beach Post that she felt an electrical shock in the Harbourside Place fountain in July 2023 — about 15 weeks before Davenport's death — and tried to tell a complex employee. If substantiated, this constitutes prior notice.

The Town of Jupiter letter to Harbourside Place (November 2023) found the light system had leaked electrical voltage and directed the owners to repair or demolish the fountain within 90 days. The fountain has been out of service, drained, and boarded up since the day of the incident.

## ESD Classification

**ESD-ADJACENT / PUBLIC FOUNTAIN ELECTROCUTION**

This is not a traditional dock/marina ESD. The electricity came from broken light fixtures in a decorative public fountain rather than from shore power to a boat dock. However:
- The ESDPA incident list explicitly includes "in-water electrocution fatalities" beyond the standard dock/marina category.
- The mechanism (AC current leaking from electrical infrastructure into water, paralyzing swimmer) is functionally identical to classic ESD.
- The ESDPA website news feed covers this incident.
- The victim's cause of death was direct electrocution, not ESD paralysis-then-drowning (his head was not submerged — he froze and fell over).

**ESD Probability: HIGH for inclusion in ESDPA scope; mechanism = confirmed electrical current in water.**
