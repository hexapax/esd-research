## Verdict

### The Missourinet "Four Deaths Since 2015" Claim Is Almost Certainly a Reporting Error

After exhaustive searching across 19+ search queries, 13+ source articles, obituary databases, funeral home records, and the Missouri State Highway Patrol drowning database, **no additional ESD deaths at Lake of the Ozarks between 2016 and 2017 were found.**

The key evidence is:

1. **The Missourinet itself corrected the figure** in an April 24, 2017 follow-up article, which reads "Since **2012**, four people have died at the lake from electric shock drowning" -- correcting the March 31, 2017 article's "since 2015" to "since 2012."

2. **The count "four deaths since 2012" is exactly correct:**
   - July 4, 2012: Alexandra Anderson, 13 (FI-2012-009) -- 1 dead
   - July 4, 2012: Brayden Anderson, 8 (FI-2012-009) -- 1 dead
   - July 7, 2012: Jennifer Lankford, 26 (FI-2012-008) -- 1 dead
   - June 21, 2015: Marcus Colburn, 21 (FI-2014-001 / SUSP-2015-054) -- 1 dead
   - **Total: 4 dead from ESD at LOTOZ since 2012**

3. **Other drowning deaths at LOTOZ in 2016-2017 were NOT ESD:**
   - Alexander G. Keith, 19 (July 5, 2016) -- PWC-related drowning, no dock or electricity involved
   - Connor W. Mohr, 25 (July 8, 2017) -- fell off dock while fishing, standard drowning, no electrical cause

4. **No news outlet, funeral home, or government report documents any ESD death at LOTOZ in 2016 or 2017.** If such a death had occurred, it would have generated coverage given the heightened attention after the Anderson and Colburn tragedies and the ongoing legislative push for SB 622.

### Conclusion

There were **zero** additional ESD deaths at Lake of the Ozarks between 2016 and March 2017 beyond what is already in the ESDPA dataset. The Missourinet's March 31, 2017 statement "since 2015, four people have died" was a typo or editing error; the correct statement (per their own April 24, 2017 article) is "since **2012**, four people have died" -- counting the 3 deaths in July 2012 plus Marcus Colburn in June 2015.

Investigation team: Osage Beach Fire Chief Dorhauer, Fire Marshal Nicholson, Ameren officials Jeff Green and David Hagan, Water Patrol Captain Matt Walz, electrician Mark Beaubout.
