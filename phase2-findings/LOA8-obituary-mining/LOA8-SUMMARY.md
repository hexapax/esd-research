## File-by-File Findings

### LOA8-LOTOZ-2015-2017.md — Lake of the Ozarks 2015–2017 Deep Dive

**Question:** Were there additional ESD deaths at Lake of the Ozarks between 2016 and March 2017, as implied by a March 31, 2017 Missourinet article that stated "since 2015, four people have died at the lake from electric shock drowning"?

**Conclusion:** **No.** Zero additional ESD deaths found for 2016 or 2017. The Missourinet "since 2015" statement was a copyediting error; their own April 24, 2017 article correctly states "since **2012**, four people have died." The four deaths are: Alexandra Anderson (13) + Brayden Anderson (8) + Jennifer Lankford (26), all July 2012; and Marcus Colburn (21), June 2015.

### LOA8-SUSP-2011-041.md — Michael Scott Knudsen, Traverse City MI, Aug 15, 2011

**Existing file:** FI-2011-002 (ESDPA date wrong), SUSP-2011-041 (LOA2), LEGAL-2011-001 (LOA3)

**LOA8 outcome:** Extremely well-documented. Found 20+ news sources, 5 Michigan Court of Appeals opinions, insurance industry publications, trade press, and obituaries. Key findings:
- **ESDPA date (May 28, 2011) is WRONG.** Correct date is **August 15, 2011** — confirmed across every source.
- The case produced 5 MI Court of Appeals opinions through 2023 (primarily attorney malpractice against Geoffrey Fieger).
- Fieger's claim that Knudsen "died because he was using drugs" (made in malpractice defense) is directly contradicted by the autopsy: "asphyxia by drowning due to low voltage electrocution."
- Multiple prior incidents at the same F dock documented: Keisha Bell and Jordan Newstead shocked earlier that summer; Michelle Mackey paddle-boarder warned June 25, 2011; arcing at E dock the day before.
- **No further obituary/source research needed.** This is the most thoroughly documented non-fatal ESD incident in the Phase 2 dataset.

### LOA8-SUSP-2015-054.md — Marcus J. Colburn, Lake of the Ozarks MO, Jun 21, 2015

**Existing file:** SUSP-2015-054 (LOA2, LOA6); LEAD-047 (LOA6)

**LOA8 outcome:** **UPGRADED TO VERIFIED.** All key unknowns resolved:
- Victim identity: **Marcus J. Colburn, 21, East Moline IL** (previously unnamed)
- Survivor: **Taylor Curley** (previously unnamed "second swimmer")
- Location: **Woods Hollow Cove, 22.2 Mile Marker, Osage Arm, Camden County, MO**
- Electrical cause: Arcing junction box between house panel and dock disconnect, energizing dock ground rod. Junction box had 40-year-old wiring with degraded insulation.
- Property was a **rental vacation home**; family arrived only 6 hours before the fatal incident.
- Prior electrical inspections (May 30 and Oct 30, 2014) missed the internal arcing.
- Sources: LakeExpo.com, KRCG TV, Columbia Missourian, WQAD, Van Hoe Funeral Home obituary, Lake News Online.

### LOA8-SUSP-2017-042.md — Shelly Darling (34) & Elizabeth Whipple (41), Lake Tuscaloosa AL, Apr 14–15, 2017

**Existing file:** VID-2017-001 (LOA11); cross-referenced in LOA11-B-summary.md

**LOA8 outcome:** Multiple sources confirmed. Key finding:
- Jefferson County Coroner Bill Yates ruled cause of death as **ELECTROCUTION** (not drowning) — the victims were killed by current, not by drowning after being paralyzed. This is technically a dock electrocution death, not classic ESD.
- **Eric Hughes (Aug 8, 2015, Lake Tuscaloosa):** Capt. Hart publicly stated "we do believe that electrical current played a part" but official ruling was drowning/asphyxia. This disconnect is consistent with the broader ESD misclassification pattern.
- These deaths do **not appear in the ESDPA incident list** as of the Rev. 8/15/2025 edition used in this project.

**Key data errors found in secondary sources:**
- Vititoe Law Group states deaths occurred "April 16" — wrong; primary sources confirm April 14–15.
- Waterway Guide states "April 20, 2017" — that is the autopsy results announcement date, not the incident date.

## Cross-Cutting Findings

### Sourcing Effectiveness

| Source Type | Hit Rate | Notes |
|-------------|----------|-------|
| Legacy.com targeted searches | LOW | Most ESD victims not on Legacy.com; rural/informal obituary channels more common |
| Local funeral home websites | MODERATE | Chittagong, Williamson & Sons found for SUSP-2016-020 |
| Chattanoogan.com / regional outlets | HIGH | Small-city papers publish detailed obituaries |
| Court records via search | HIGH | Knudsen case produced 5 MI COA opinions |
| GoFundMe | LOW | Only a few ESD-related campaigns found (Marcus Colburn had one) |

### What LOA8 Did NOT Do (Future Work)

The broader LOA8 plan envisioned systematic Legacy.com searches for new, unknown ESD victims using keyword patterns ("drowning" + "dock" + "electric"). This was not executed in the current LOA8 campaign; the focus was on targeted follow-ups for known SUSP cases and hotspot deep dives. The systematic Legacy.com keyword search remains an open research track.
