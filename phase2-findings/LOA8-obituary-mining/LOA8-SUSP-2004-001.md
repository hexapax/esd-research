## Conflicting Information

1. **Joule marks discrepancy:** The earliest known version of the entry (reproduced on 2CoolFishing.com, likely from a 2004-2005 copy of the Shafer list) states "No injuries or toxic substance found on post mortem." Later revisions (from at least 2016 onward) add "but Joule marks (electrical contact points) found on right wrist and left leg and shin." This is a significant change -- the Joule marks detail was apparently added to the entry sometime between the initial listing and the 2016 revision. This could mean: (a) the autopsy detail was initially omitted and later obtained by Shafer/Rifkin, or (b) the Joule marks finding was added based on information received after the initial listing. Either way, the early version's "no injuries" statement contradicts the later "Joule marks" finding.

2. **CRLEA "Lake Travis, Texas" case:** The CRLEA law enforcement training presentation includes a separate, undated Lake Travis ESD case involving two juvenile females and dock wiring, with detective testimony about electric shock. This is clearly a different incident from the Aug 8, 2004 case (male victim vs. female victims, marina dock vs. dock, evening swimming vs. jumping off dock). It may be the "follow-up" referenced in the ESDPA list, suggesting that the marina at issue in the 2004 case was subsequently investigated and another ESD event was documented there. However, this is speculative -- the CRLEA presentation does not connect the two cases.

3. **"Accident under investigation" status:** Every version of the ESDPA entry, from the earliest (2004) through the latest (2025), says "Accident under investigation." After 21 years, no investigation outcome has ever been published in the ESDPA list or any other accessible source. This persistent "under investigation" status is unusual and may indicate that the investigation was never concluded, or that results were never communicated back to the ESDPA list maintainers.

## Assessment

This is a well-documented entry in the ESDPA incident list that has persisted through 20+ years of revisions, with the notable addition of Joule marks evidence in later versions. The Joule marks finding is forensically significant -- Joule marks (also called electrical marks or current marks) are characteristic burns at the entry and exit points of electrical current through the body. Their presence at autopsy on the right wrist and left leg/shin would be strong physical evidence of electrocution, distinct from typical drowning findings.

However, no independent primary source has been located to confirm this incident. The victim remains unidentified, the marina remains unnamed, and the investigation outcome remains unknown. The case should remain classified as **UNVERIFIED** pending records requests to Travis County and TPWD.

The existence of a SEPARATE Lake Travis ESD case in the CRLEA training presentation (involving two juvenile females) suggests that ESD hazards at Lake Travis marinas/docks have been recognized by law enforcement investigators, adding contextual plausibility to the August 8, 2004 incident even though primary source confirmation has not been obtained.
