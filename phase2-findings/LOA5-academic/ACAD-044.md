# ACAD-044: NASBLA — National Association of State Boating Law Administrators, ESD Resources

## Source Metadata
- **Type**: National boating regulatory body position statement / resource compilation
- **Publisher**: National Association of State Boating Law Administrators (NASBLA)
- **URL**: https://www.nasbla.org/nasblamain/lighthouse/get-equipped/esd-resources
- **Credibility**: High — represents all 50 state boating law administrators; official policy body

## Key Statements on ESD Epidemiology

### Quantitative Assessment
NASBLA's official characterization:
> "Nationally, relatively few **documented** electric shock drownings occur each year. However, an unknown quantity of ESDs may be misdiagnosed as drowning due to fatigue, alcohol consumption, cramping, or other factors."

This statement is significant for two reasons:
1. It comes from the authoritative body representing state boating regulators
2. The word "documented" is deliberately italicized/emphasized — acknowledging that documented cases are a fraction of true cases
3. NASBLA explicitly labels the undocumented portion as "unknown quantity"

### Reporting Requirements
NASBLA notes that USCG BARD requires incident reports when:
- ESD is determined to be the source of contact with electrical current from a vessel or its equipment
- Includes system failure and stray current cases

**Critical caveat**: This reporting requirement only applies when the electrical cause is identified. Since ESD rarely produces visible autopsy evidence, and electrical testing of the water is rarely performed post-incident, the reporting trigger is almost never activated.

### Misdiagnosis Mechanism
NASBLA's resource page confirms the systematic misclassification pathway:
- ESD causes muscular paralysis
- Victim drowns
- Autopsy shows cause of death as drowning (no burns, no electrical injury markers)
- Death is classified as ordinary drowning
- **Unless a witness experienced the electrical sensation**, the ESD etiology is never discovered

## Epidemiological Significance
NASBLA's position — representing all state boating regulators — is that ESD underreporting is systematic, structural, and of unknown magnitude. This is an official acknowledgment from the regulatory body best positioned to know the true scale of the problem. Their refusal to cite a specific number is itself informative: no reliable number exists.

## ESD Definition (NASBLA Official)
NASBLA defines ESD as:
> "The result of the passage of a typically low level AC current through the body with sufficient force to cause skeletal muscular paralysis, rendering the victim unable to help themselves, and ultimately drowning."

This definition emphasizes the paralysis mechanism and distinguishes ESD from direct electrocution death.
