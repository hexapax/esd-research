## Key Technical Content

Companion paper to ACAD-023, this paper develops the risk assessment framework for ESD. Authors are affiliated with the University of Maryland (Ayyub) and related institutions:

- Develops a structured risk assessment methodology for ESD using system reliability and probabilistic risk analysis techniques
- Identifies key failure modes that lead to ESD: (1) electrical fault on a vessel or dock, combined with (2) failed or absent ground fault protection, combined with (3) swimmer in energized water
- Quantifies risk factors including probability of fault occurrence, probability of protection device failure, probability of swimmer exposure, and probability of drowning given exposure
- Establishes a fault tree / event tree approach to characterize ESD risk in marinas
- Provides the analytical framework used in the subsequent NFPA FPRF Marina Risk Reduction project (ACAD-027)
- Notes data gaps in incident reporting that limit quantitative risk estimates
- Recommends prioritizing: mandatory GFP/GFCI installation, vessel leakage testing protocols, no-swimming rules near boats, and better incident data collection

## ESD Incidents Cited

References the Rifkin/Shafer incident database as the primary source of ESD case data for calibrating risk probabilities. Does not cite individual incidents by name.

## Regulatory/Legislative Impact

Provides the formal risk-assessment framework underlying the NFPA FPRF 2017 Marina Risk Reduction report. The methodology was presented at the 2015 NFPA FPRF Marina Shock Hazard Research Planning Workshop. Directly supports the regulatory shift toward quantitative risk-based performance requirements in NEC Article 555 and NFPA 303.

## Relevance Rating

HIGH — Establishes the formal risk-assessment framework for ESD in marina environments. Essential reference for understanding the probabilistic basis for protection requirements. Companion to the simulation paper (ACAD-023).
