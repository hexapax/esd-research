## Key Technical Content

This paper is the primary peer-reviewed IEEE study specifically on ESD. Prior to this work, no research or simulation had established the true bounds of ESD risk as a function of water conductivity — the prevailing literature simply asserted that ESD was exclusively a freshwater phenomenon.

Key technical contributions:
- Challenged the assumption that ESD risk is strictly limited to freshwater environments
- Quantified the relationship between water conductivity and voltage gradients that induce current through a submerged human body
- Established that in low-conductivity freshwater, the human body is significantly more conductive than the surrounding water, making it the preferred current return path
- In saltwater, the high conductivity of the water causes current to dissipate through the water rather than through the body, greatly reducing ESD risk — though not eliminating it
- Used simulation modeling to characterize the "zone of danger" around a fault source as a function of water conductivity
- Identified that current levels as low as 5 mA can cause muscle paralysis preventing swimming; 10–12 mA causes muscle tetanization; 100 mA through the cardiac path can cause ventricular fibrillation
- Discussed how the body's current exposure depends on the voltage gradient per unit distance (V/ft) in the water, not simply the voltage of the fault source

## ESD Incidents Cited

The paper is primarily theoretical/simulation-based. It references the general body of ESD incident data but does not cite specific named incidents. It notes that all documented ESD fatalities have occurred in freshwater environments.

## Regulatory/Legislative Impact

Published in IEEE IAS Electrical Safety Workshop proceedings and then expanded for the IEEE Transactions on Industry Applications. This is the foundational peer-reviewed scientific paper for ESD. It provides academic backing for the NEC Article 555 and NFPA 303 thresholds of 30 mA protection at shore power receptacles. It was followed by a discussion paper by Vesa Linja-aho (ACAD-021) debating ESD risk in European contexts.

## Relevance Rating

HIGH — This is the primary IEEE peer-reviewed publication specifically on ESD physics and risk. It provides the scientific foundation for understanding why freshwater poses greater risk than saltwater, and it supports the current-level thresholds used in NEC and NFPA standards. Essential reference for any ESD database.
