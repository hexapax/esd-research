# ACAD-047: NAFE Journal — Forensic Engineering Analysis of ESD Case (Pool, 2021)

## Source Metadata
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed forensic engineering journal article
- **Publisher**: Journal of the National Academy of Forensic Engineers (NAFE)
- **Volume**: 38, Issue 1
- **URL**: https://journal.nafe.org/ojs/index.php/nafe/article/view/64
- **Credibility**: Peer-reviewed academic journal — highest available for ESD-specific research

## Case Description

### Incident
- **Victim**: A minor who experienced an ESD **injury** (non-fatal) at a community swimming pool
- **Cause**: A severed neutral wire in the utility's three-phase service drop created stray voltage and AC current in the pool water
- **Symptom**: Victim felt shock near the pool ladder, convulsed but remained conscious, was released from hospital

### Forensic Engineering Conclusions
- The forensic engineer determined the pool association was **not negligent**
- Fault was attributed to the **electric utility's failure to permanently repair** a severed neutral wire
- Plaintiffs ultimately dropped electrical claims against the pool association
- Case classified as ESD caused by external utility infrastructure failure, not internal pool wiring failure

## Significance and Limitations

### What This Paper Demonstrates
- ESD can occur in pools due to utility-side faults, not just internal dock/marina wiring
- Forensic engineering analysis can identify the electrical cause even when conventional investigation would not
- Demonstrates that expert investigation reveals ESD cases that would otherwise be classified as "unexplained incident"

### What This Paper Does Not Provide
- No national epidemiological data
- No annual death estimates
- No percentage calculations
- This is a single case study, not a population-level study

## Broader Academic Literature Gap

### Findings on Peer-Reviewed ESD Research
After systematic search of PubMed, NCBI, and academic databases:
- **No peer-reviewed epidemiological studies** on ESD mortality rates, incidence, or national death toll were found
- **No published studies** using death certificate data (multiple cause of death) to identify W86 + drowning code co-occurrence
- **No public health surveillance studies** specifically designed to measure ESD prevalence
- The NAFE article is the only peer-reviewed academic publication specifically analyzing an ESD case found in the literature

### Adjacent Literature
- StatPearls (NCBI): General electrical injury chapter notes ~1,000 total U.S. electrical deaths/year, ~400 high-voltage, 50–300 lightning, but does not address ESD specifically
- Circulation (2003): "Recommended Guidelines for Uniform Reporting of Data From Drowning Studies" — does not mention electrical causes of drowning
- Journal of Forensic Science and Medicine (2018): "Deaths Due to Electrocution in Maryland, 2005–2015" — 55 electrocution deaths over 11 years; specific breakdown for water-related deaths not available from excerpts
- TechRxiv (preprint): "Examining the Risk of ESD as a Function of Water Conductivity" — engineering analysis of conductivity gradients, no epidemiological data

## Conclusion
The peer-reviewed literature on ESD epidemiology is essentially nonexistent. The NAFE case study is the only direct peer-reviewed source. This absence is itself a finding: ESD is sufficiently under-studied that no researcher has attempted a systematic national mortality analysis.
