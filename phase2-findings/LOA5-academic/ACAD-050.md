## ICD-10 Codes Relevant to ESD

### Primary Drowning Codes
- **W65**: Drowning/submersion while in bathtub
- **W66**: Drowning/submersion following fall into bathtub
- **W67**: Drowning/submersion while in swimming pool
- **W69**: Drowning/submersion while in natural water
- **W73**: Other specified cause of drowning/submersion
- **W74**: Unspecified cause of drowning/submersion

### Electrical Exposure Code
- **W86**: Exposure to unspecified electric current (unintentional)
  - W86.0: Exposure to domestic wiring and appliances
  - W86.1: Exposure to industrial wiring, appliances and electrical machinery
  - W86.8: Exposure to other electric current (includes wiring on boats and docks)

### The Double-Coding Problem
For an ESD death to appear as identifiable in death certificate data, the death certificate would need to carry **both** a drowning code (W65–W74) and an electrical exposure code (W86). However:
- CDC NCHS coding rules for underlying cause of death typically select **one** underlying cause
- Multiple cause of death databases do exist and can capture both codes
- But the medical examiner must **first identify** the electrical etiology to enter W86

### National Survey of Current Practice
No published study has performed a cross-tabulation of W86 and drowning codes from CDC vital statistics data. This analysis has never been done in the published literature, representing an actionable research gap.

## Total Electrical Death Context

### National Electrical Death Burden (All Types)
From StatPearls (NCBI, peer-reviewed):
- ~**1,000 deaths per year** from electrical injuries in the United States
- ~400 from high-voltage electrical injuries
- ~50–300 from lightning strikes
- Remainder (~300–550): low-voltage injuries (residential, occupational, water-related)

### ESD Within the Electrical Death Total
ESD causes death through drowning rather than direct cardiac arrest from electrocution. This means:
- ESD deaths do **not** typically appear in electrical death statistics (the death is drowning)
- ESD deaths do **not** typically appear in drowning statistics as electrical in nature
- They fall through the gap between two surveillance systems

## Historical Trend in Drowning Research
From ScienceDirect (peer-reviewed, 2022): "Drowning is preventable. Yet, every day 11 deaths are recorded from unintentional drowning in the United States, leading to 3,960 deaths annually." ICD-10 codes V90, V92, W65–W74 used. No mention of electrical cause subcategory or analysis.

## Recommended Research Methodology for Future Studies
A properly designed ESD surveillance study would need to:
1. Query CDC Wonder for all drowning deaths (W65–W74) where W86 appears as any cause on the death certificate
2. Review death certificates from drownings near powered water infrastructure
3. Implement prospective testing protocols for future drowning scenes near marinas/docks
4. Link CPSC pool electrocution data with drowning data from NCHS

No such study appears to have been conducted.
