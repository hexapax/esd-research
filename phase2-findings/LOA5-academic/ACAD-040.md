# ACAD-040: CDC MMWR — Electricity-Related Deaths on Lakes, Oklahoma 1989–1993

## Source Metadata
- **Type**: Government public health surveillance report
- **Publisher**: Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), Morbidity and Mortality Weekly Report (MMWR)
- **Title**: "Electricity-Related Deaths on Lakes — Oklahoma, 1989-1993"
- **Date**: 1995
- **URL**: https://www.cdc.gov/mmwr/preview/mmwrhtml/00042081.htm
- **Credibility**: High — primary CDC public health surveillance document; only dedicated CDC/MMWR report on lake electrocution deaths found in the literature

## Key Findings

### Case Data
- **5 deaths** among males aged 13–50 years across two Oklahoma lakes (1989–1993)
- **Lake A** (northeastern Oklahoma): 4 deaths; lake has approximately 4,000 private and 120 commercial docks
- **Lake B** (southern Oklahoma): 1 death
- **Mechanism**: Submersible irrigation pumps and dock wiring faults energizing lake water
- **Cause of death classifications**:
  - Electrocution: 3 cases
  - Drowning secondary to electrical shock: 2 cases

### Case Circumstances
1. Scuba diver near a submersible pump
2. Jumper from dock with faulty wiring
3. Worker on electrified dock
4. Individual rewiring a submerged water pump
5. Individual installing a fuel pump on a houseboat

### Surveillance Methodology
Oklahoma State Department of Health (OSDH) identified electricity-related lake deaths through a multi-source injury surveillance system: hospital medical records, Office of the State Medical Examiner, ambulance reports, Department of Public Safety reports, newspaper clippings, and vital statistics. This multi-source approach suggests the 5 cases represent a reasonably complete count for the state during that period.

### Broader Context (from MMWR text)
- Drowning accounts for approximately **4,200 deaths per year** in the US (figure cited in 1995 MMWR as baseline context)
- Report notes that ESD cases are likely underdetected in surveillance because deaths may be coded as drowning without electrical etiology investigation

## Epidemiological Significance
This is the only dedicated CDC surveillance document found that specifically tracks lake electrocution deaths as a cluster. Five deaths in one state over five years (1.0/year) suggests these events were already occurring at measurable rates before modern marina/dock electrification became widespread. The Oklahoma lake had 4,120 docks — extrapolating to the national dock/marina count implies significant national exposure.

## Recommendations Made
- Require electrical inspections before issuing dock permits
- Terminate service to non-compliant docks
- Mandate inspections every 3 years (commercial) and 2 years (private)
- National Electrical Code compliance enforcement

## Relevance to ESD Death Toll Estimation
- Pre-dates widespread shore power and modern dock electrification
- Demonstrates that deaths were occurring even in the early 1990s
- 5 deaths in one state over 5 years, captured by a multi-source surveillance system
- Supports the argument that modern figures (with far more powered docks) would be higher
