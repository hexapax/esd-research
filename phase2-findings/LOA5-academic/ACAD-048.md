## U.S. Marina and Dock Infrastructure

### Number of Marinas
- Approximately **10,445 marinas** in operation in the United States (most recent available data)
- Marina slip distribution:
  - 26% of marinas have 100–249 slips
  - 24% have 250–499 slips
- Large marinas with 800+ berths exist in major recreational water markets

### Powered Infrastructure
- The vast majority of modern marina slips have **shore power connections** (120V/240V AC)
- Shore power is the primary ESD risk vector for swimmers near docked vessels
- Additionally: dock lighting, pump systems, boat lifts, electric boat hoists — all potential ESD sources

### Market Scale
- U.S. docks market valued at **$1.91 billion** in 2025 (projected $2.55 billion by 2033)
- Fixed docks: 73% market share
- Boat docks and lifts market: ~$1.1 billion in 2023
- U.S. recreational boating industry: >$28 billion overall

### Privately-Owned Docks
- The Oklahoma MMWR (1995) noted one northeastern Oklahoma lake had **4,000 private docks and 120 commercial docks**
- National count of private docks is not published but likely in the millions
- Private docks are largely **unregulated and uninspected** for electrical safety
- Tennessee's Noah Dean and Nate Act covers only **public** marinas — private docks are excluded

## Risk Amplification Factors

### Historical Electrification Trend
- US Sailing Association notes that "20 years ago, very few docks were powered" — ESD risk has increased substantially as dock electrification expanded
- Growth in boat lifts (which require 240V power), shore power pedestals, dock lighting, and Wi-Fi/charging infrastructure at slips has dramatically expanded the electrification footprint
- This trend means the ESD risk in 2025 is substantially higher than in 1995 (when the MMWR Oklahoma cluster was documented)

### Marina Leakage Data
- A key finding from CRLEA report (ACAD-043): approximately **13% of boats at freshwater marinas leak lethal AC current** at any given time
- Applied to 10,445 marinas: if each has an average of 100 occupied slips, ~136,000 boats are present
- 13% of 136,000 = approximately **17,600 boats at any time leaking lethal current**
- This is a rough order-of-magnitude calculation, but it suggests the scale of latent ESD risk

### Freshwater Concentration
- ESD is primarily a freshwater phenomenon (lower conductivity creates larger voltage gradients)
- U.S. freshwater recreational boating activity is heavily concentrated in:
  - The Great Lakes region
  - Southeast lakes (Tennessee, Alabama, Georgia, Florida)
  - Midwest river/lake systems
  - Pacific Northwest lakes

## Epidemiological Significance
The infrastructure scale makes clear that ESD risk is not a fringe phenomenon. With potentially millions of powered private docks, 10,000+ marinas, and ~13% of boats leaking lethal current, the exposure population is enormous. The documented death toll of ~4–6/year (official confirmed cases) represents a vanishingly small fraction of the exposure population — consistent with the underreporting hypothesis.
