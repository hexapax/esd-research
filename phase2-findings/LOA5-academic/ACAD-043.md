## Key Quantitative Findings

### Fatalities and Casualties by Year (2010–2014)
Based on data extracted from this report (as cited in secondary sources):

| Year | Fatalities | Casualties |
|------|-----------|------------|
| 2010 | 3 | 20 |
| 2011 | 2 | 2 |
| 2012 | 12 | 15 |
| 2013 | 9 | 11 |
| 2014 | 3 | 9 |
| **TOTAL** | **29** | **57** |

**Five-year totals: 29 fatalities and 57 casualties (non-fatal injuries/near misses)**

### Annual Average
- Mean fatalities: **5.8 per year** over 2010–2014
- The 2012 spike (12 fatalities) is notable and may reflect the July 4, 2012 Cherokee Lake incident in Tennessee (2 child deaths)

### Geographic Scope
- Secondary sources describe this as a **national U.S. figure**, not California-only
- The document was published by a California agency but appears to compile nationwide ESD incidents
- This aligns with it being used as a national reference in other publications

### Marina Electrical Leakage Data
- Report cites that approximately **13% of boats at freshwater marinas leak lethal AC current**
- This figure (source: ABYC/USCG research cited within the CRLEA report) has significant implications for national risk assessment

## Epidemiological Significance

### Why 2012 Was the Worst Year
The 12-fatality figure in 2012 includes the July 4, 2012 Cherokee Lake Marina deaths (Noah Dean Winstead, 10, and Nate Lynam, 11) which received national attention and prompted Tennessee's Noah Dean and Nate Act. This year's spike drove the legislative response.

### Comparison to ESDPA Incident List
- ESDPA lists approximately 175 incidents spanning 1981–2025 (~4/year documented)
- CRLEA shows 29 documented in just 5 years (5.8/year) for roughly the same period
- These numbers are not identical, suggesting different collection methodologies and possibly different inclusion criteria

### Underreporting Context
The report acknowledges that documented cases are "just the tip of the iceberg" — the autopsy-invisible nature of ESD means most cases are never identified. The 29 documented fatalities represent only cases where the electrical cause was definitively confirmed.
