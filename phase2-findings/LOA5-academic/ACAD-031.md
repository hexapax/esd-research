## Key Technical Content

This is the most technically detailed industry article on ESD physics, by the same author as the 2020 IEEE paper (ACAD-020). Key contributions:

- Introduces and defines the "Zone of Danger" — the spatial region around a fault source in water where current density through a swimmer's body exceeds the threshold for muscle incapacitation
- Uses finite element analysis (FEA) to model electric field and current density distributions in water around fault sources (boats, docks, shore power connections)
- Demonstrates quantitatively that:
  - In freshwater, voltage gradients per unit distance are high, the Zone of Danger extends significantly from the fault source
  - Water conductivity is the primary determinant of Zone of Danger size
  - At typical freshwater conductivities, dangerous current levels (>5 mA) can occur several meters from a fault source
- Identifies that the human body (primarily saltwater, ~70% water by weight, high ionic content) is significantly more conductive than freshwater, making it the path of least resistance
- Discusses body resistance variation: dry skin ~20,000 ohms; wet skin can drop to 300–600 ohms, substantially increasing current at a given voltage gradient
- Explains the drowning mechanism: AC current at 5 mA causes voluntary muscle interference; at 10–12 mA causes paralysis preventing swimming; victim cannot call for help; bystanders see someone struggling then submerging without apparent cause

## ESD Incidents Cited

Morse uses the general body of ESD incident data to motivate the analysis but does not cite specific named incidents in this article.

## Regulatory/Legislative Impact

EC&M article served as the publicly accessible companion to Morse's 2019 IEEE ESW conference paper. Made the finite element analysis methodology accessible to electrical inspectors, marina operators, and standards committee members. EC&M published this as part of an EBook compilation "Electric Shock Drowning: The Truth Behind the Tragedies" (2018) that collected multiple EC&M articles on ESD.

## Relevance Rating

HIGH — The primary industry-level technical explanation of ESD physics using rigorous engineering methodology. The Zone of Danger concept from this article is now widely used in ESD safety training and education. Accessible version of the science behind the IEEE paper.
