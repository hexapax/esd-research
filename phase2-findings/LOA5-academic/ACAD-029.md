## Key Technical Content

NEC Article 555 is the primary US electrical installation code for marina environments. It originated in 1968 as "Boat Harbor Wiring" with minimal provisions and has undergone major expansions in each edition since 2011 in response to ESD incidents.

**Edition-by-edition evolution on ESD protection:**

- **2011 NEC**: Introduced Section 555.3, "Ground-Fault Protection," requiring GFPE on main overcurrent devices; initial threshold set at 100 mA. Required warning signs: "WARNING — POTENTIAL SHOCK HAZARD — ELECTRICAL CURRENTS MAY BE PRESENT IN THE WATER"
- **2014 NEC**: Technical committee recognized 100 mA was "far above Class A GFCI levels of 4–6 mA" and insufficient to prevent muscle tetanization in children. Expanded scope; maintained 100 mA with committee debate over reduction
- **2017 NEC**: Breakthrough edition — following NFPA FPRF recommendations and research: feeder GFPE ≤ 100 mA; receptacle GFPE ≤ 30 mA; leakage current measurement devices required where >3 receptacles serve vessels; GFCI for boat hoists ≤240V (moved from 210.8(C)); extended to dwelling unit docking facilities
- **2020 NEC**: Complete restructuring of Article 555. Tiered approach:
  - 100 mA GFPE for feeders and branch circuit conductors on docking facilities
  - 30 mA GFPE for shore power receptacles
  - Class A GFCI (4–6 mA) for general-purpose 15A/20A receptacles
  - Individual branch circuits required per vessel
  - Minimum 12-inch height for shore power connections above deck
  - Leakage current measurement devices required
- **2023 NEC**: Reorganized into sub-sections (feeder, branch circuit, receptacle). Added Section 555.15 for equipment replacement compliance. Added 555.36 (effective 2026): pre-connection vessel leakage testing required; marinas must deny connection to non-compliant vessels.

**Incidents prompting key changes:**
- The 2012 Independence Day cluster (Cherokee Lake, TN — Noah Dean Winstead, age 10, and Nate Lynam, age 11; Lake of the Ozarks, MO — Brayden and Alexandra Anderson; Lake Lanier, GA — Jennifer Lankford) prompted Tennessee's "Noah Dean and Nate Act" (2014) and accelerated NEC committee action on lowering GFP thresholds
- Lucas Ritz (1999, age 8) — death near a boat dock; his father Kevin Ritz became the founder of the Electric Shock Drowning Prevention Association and a major driver of code change advocacy

## ESD Incidents Cited

The NEC as a standard does not cite incidents in normative text. The incidents above are documented in surrounding literature as motivating the code changes.

## Regulatory/Legislative Impact

NEC Article 555 is the primary US electrical installation code adopted by all 50 states (though with varying edition lag). The 2017 NEC's reduction of shore power receptacle protection to 30 mA was a watershed change. NEC 555.36 (2023/2026) mandating vessel pre-connection leakage testing mirrors the NFPA 303 2021 requirement and represents the most aggressive operational requirement yet.

## Relevance Rating

HIGH — The primary US legal electrical code governing marina wiring installations. The history of NEC Article 555 is essentially the regulatory history of ESD recognition and response in the US. Essential reference for the regulatory history section of any ESD database.
