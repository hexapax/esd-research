## Key Technical Content

EC&M's compilation eBook includes multiple forensic engineering case studies of ESD and dock electrocution incidents:

**"The Case of the Hot Marina" (April 2004)**
- Forensic examination of an inadequate grounding system on a docked boat
- Documents how a grounding failure on a single vessel can energize the water around the entire dock

**"The Case of Stray Voltage in a Lake" — Donald R. Johnson, P.E. (November 2009)**
- Covers faulty concentric neutrals on high-voltage underground cables leading to electrocution in a lake
- One teenage swimmer drowned; two others suffered brain injuries
- Demonstrates that the stray voltage source does not have to be a marina or boat — utility distribution faults can energize natural water bodies

**"The Case of the Floating Dock" — John Cavallaro, P.E. (July 2010)**
- Fatal electrocution from a severed ground wire and water buildup in a junction box on a floating dock
- Demonstrates how floating dock movement can sever ground wires, eliminating fault protection
- Documents specific failure mode: water intrusion into electrical junction boxes that are submerged due to dock settling

## ESD Incidents Cited

- July 2010: Fatal electrocution at floating dock (severed ground wire and water in junction box)
- November 2009: Teenage swimmer drowning and two brain injuries from utility stray voltage in lake (faulty concentric neutrals)
- April 2004: Marina "hot" boat incident (inadequate grounding)

## Regulatory/Legislative Impact

EC&M case studies serve as forensic engineering reference cases for litigation, insurance investigations, and code development. The "floating dock" case study demonstrates the specific hazard of floating dock movement causing ground wire failure — now addressed in NFPA 303 annual inspection requirements. The "stray voltage in lake" case is notable for showing utility distribution equipment (not marina wiring) as an ESD source.

## Relevance Rating

HIGH — Forensic engineering case studies documenting specific ESD incidents with detailed electrical analysis.
