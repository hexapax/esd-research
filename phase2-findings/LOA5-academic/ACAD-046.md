## Tennessee: Noah Dean and Nate Act (2014)

### Legislative Background
- Named after **Noah Dean Winstead (age 10)** and **Nate Lynam (age 11)**
- Both died July 4, 2012, electrocuted while swimming at Cherokee Lake Marina near Morristown, Tennessee
- Seven other swimmers injured at the same incident

### Statutory Requirements (TN Code §68-102-602)
- All Tennessee public marinas and docks must install equipment leakage circuit interrupters (ELCI) or ground fault circuit interrupters (GFCI) meeting 100-milliampere limits
- **Mandatory inspections** every 5 years by the State Fire Marshal's Office (SFMO)
- **Permanent safety signage** required: "ELECTRIC SHOCK HAZARD RISK: NO SWIMMING WITHIN 100 YARDS OF THE BOAT DOCK" (legible at 80 feet)
- Non-compliant marinas can be declared threats to public safety and ordered closed

### Tennessee SFMO Inspection Results
- **158 marinas inspected** in 2016 (first full year of program)
- Inspections revealed **"numerous" electrical violations**, including at least one case of 480-volt electrical service energizing marina water (posing immediate risk of death)
- One source notes Clarksville Marina passed its December 2016 inspection — implying not all marinas passed
- SFMO posts inspection results monthly online
- Inspections occur every 5 years per law; second round began approximately 2020

### Significance for ESD Prevalence
The Tennessee inspection data is notable: in the first round of inspections, "numerous violations" were found across 158 marinas, including at least one instance of extremely dangerous high-voltage exposure. This empirically confirms that marina electrical hazards are widespread — not rare edge cases.

## Pennsylvania: ESD Prevention Legislation (Proposed)

### Status
- **Reintroduced June 2025** by Senators Jay Costa (D) and Camera Bartolotta (R)
- 16 cosponsors — bipartisan
- Not yet passed as of research date (March 2026)

### Background
- Prompted by the death of **James DeAngelo (age 23)** on July 4, 2021
- Championed by the James DeAngelo Foundation

### Proposed Requirements
- Boat docks and marinas must install overcurrent protective devices or ground fault protection
- Initial electrical inspections required, then every 5 years
- Permanent safety signage about ESD hazards

## Missouri: Alexandra and Brayden Anderson Act (SB 622)

### Status
- Proposed as "Alexandra and Brayden Anderson Electric Shock Drowning Prevention Act"
- Named after siblings killed by ESD
- Passage status unclear from available sources

## Legislative Significance for ESD Epidemiology

### What Legislative Activity Reveals
1. **Multiple states have determined ESD is a serious enough public health problem** to require legislation
2. Tennessee's 158-marina inspection program finding "numerous violations" suggests marina electrical non-compliance is widespread
3. Legislative proposals consistently reference **"hundreds of unreported cases"** — this characterization has been used in legislative advocacy even if not precisely sourced
4. The bipartisan nature (both Republican and Democratic sponsors) indicates ESD is not contested as a real hazard — only the regulatory response is debated

### Gap: No Congressional Action
- No federal legislation specifically addressing ESD has been identified
- No congressional hearings on ESD have been found in available sources
- The issue remains primarily a state-level regulatory concern
- NFPA 303 (marinas), NEC Article 555 (marinas), and ABYC E-11 (boat AC systems) provide national standards but are not federal law
