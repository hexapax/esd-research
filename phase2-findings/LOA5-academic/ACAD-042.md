# ACAD-042: USCG BARD — Boating Accident Report Database: Electrocution Death Classification

## Source Metadata
- **Type**: Federal accident reporting database / annual statistical report
- **Publisher**: U.S. Coast Guard (USCG), Office of Auxiliary and Boating Safety
- **Primary Reference**: Recreational Boating Statistics 2024 Report
- **URL**: https://www.uscgboating.org/library/accident-statistics/Recreational-Boating-Statistics-2024.pdf
- **Data access**: https://www.data-liberation-project.org/datasets/uscg-boating-accident-report-database/
- **Credibility**: High institutional source, but significant structural limitation for ESD detection

## Key Findings

### USCG BARD Cause-of-Death Categories (2024 Report)
The 2024 USCG Recreational Boating Statistics report tracks these primary causes of death:
1. **Drowning**: 365 deaths (primary category)
2. **Trauma**: 101 deaths
3. **Hypothermia**: 8 deaths
4. **Cardiac arrest**: 5 deaths
5. **Carbon monoxide poisoning**: 2 deaths

**Electrocution is not listed as a separate cause-of-death category in the USCG annual statistics.**

### What This Means for ESD Surveillance
- ESD victims who drown are coded as "Drowning" (cause of death: drowning)
- The electrical etiology is not separately captured in annual statistical tables
- USCG BARD does require reports when "source is determined to be contact with electrical current from a vessel or its equipment, including system failure and stray current" — but this only captures incidents where the electrical cause is **identified at the time of report**
- In the majority of ESD cases, the electrical cause is never identified, so the case enters BARD as a standard drowning (if reported at all)

### Total Boating Fatalities Context
- 2024: 556 total boating fatalities (down from 564 in 2023)
- 2010: 736 total boating fatalities
- Long-term trend: generally declining
- Drowning accounts for 75–76% of known fatalities

### Data Liberation Project Raw Data
- USCG BARD contains 58,430 boating accidents from 2009–2023
- 8,935 connected deaths
- 36,773 injuries
- This raw dataset allows custom queries but ESD has not been systematically extracted from it in published analyses

### LOA7 Context (ESD Capture Rate)
- Prior LOA7 BARD analysis found BARD captures only approximately **8%** of known ESD fatalities
- 2 of 24 dock/marina ESD incidents found in BARD in that analysis
- This catastrophic undercount confirms that BARD annual statistics are not a reliable measure of ESD incidence

## Epidemiological Significance
USCG BARD is the primary federal data system for recreational boating deaths, yet it systematically fails to capture ESD deaths. The structural reason: BARD records cause of death as presented at the time of report — and ESD victims die of drowning with no visible electrical injury. Without a dedicated ESD category and active investigation protocol, BARD will continue to undercount ESD.
