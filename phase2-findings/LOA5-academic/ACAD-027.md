## Key Technical Content

NFPA FPRF commissioned this study following the 2015 Marina Shock Hazard Research Planning Workshop, which identified critical gaps in marina electrical safety knowledge. The report:

- Develops an ESD Concepts Tree (ESDCT) — a decision tree for ESD risk evaluation across different water environments (fresh, salt, brackish) and settings (public marinas, private docks, floating homes)
- Applies a socio-technical systems (STS) framework to characterize risk holistically, including motorcraft subsystem, marina infrastructure, regulatory framework, and policy environment
- Reviews prior ESD research including USCG 2008 grant study (ACAD-026) and Ayyub et al. 2016 simulation papers (ACAD-023, ACAD-024)
- Identifies ESD causes: ground faults on vessels, exposed conductors in water, bonding system failures, utility distribution faults
- Recommends mitigation: RCDs/GFCIs, periodic inspection protocols, no-swimming rules, improved incident data collection
- Documents that the 30 mA GFPE threshold for shore power receptacles is proportionally justified based on body mass (30 mA for adults; lower for children)
- Notes that bonding mitigates step/touch voltages but has limitations if the bonding system itself fails
- Identifies critical research gaps: quantitative field data on voltage decay with distance, private dock incident rates, inspection effectiveness, cost-benefit of different protection devices
- Recommends quantitative risk assessments and field tests to validate mitigation effectiveness

The report grew from the 2015 NFPA FPRF Workshop which convened marina safety experts, electrical inspectors, USCG representatives, and standards body members to identify priority research needs.

## ESD Incidents Cited

References the Rifkin/Shafer ESD incident database and the 2008 USCG grant study. Cites general incident patterns: primarily freshwater, primarily from faulty shore power cords or dock wiring, victims are swimmers near powered boats. Does not cite individual named incidents within the technical report.

## Regulatory/Legislative Impact

Directly influenced NFPA 303 2021 edition requirements, particularly the retroactive vessel ground-fault testing requirements (all vessels to be tested at initial marina connection; vessels with >30 mA leakage denied connection). Also supported NEC Article 555 2020 edition's tiered protection approach (100 mA feeders, 30 mA receptacles, GFCI for general outlets). The ESDCT framework provides a regulatory risk-analysis tool used by state marina regulators.

## Relevance Rating

HIGH — Official NFPA research report that directly shaped current marina electrical safety standards. Provides the technical justification for major code changes in NFPA 303 (2021) and NEC Article 555 (2020). Contains the ESDCT decision framework and identifies key research gaps that remain relevant.
