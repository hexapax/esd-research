## Abstract/Summary
Develops a systematic risk assessment methodology for electric-current induced drowning in marina and houseboat environments. Published as vol. 2, no. 3, article 031004.

Authors:
- Bilal M. Ayyub: University of Maryland, College Park (Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering)
- T.S. Koko: Lloyd's Register (tamunoiyala.koko@lr.org)
- Andrew Nyakaana Blair: Institution not specified in available sources
- U.O. Akpan: Institution not specified in available sources

A companion paper co-authored by Koko, Ayyub, and Keith Gallant — "Simulation of Electric-Current-Induced Drowning Accident Scenarios for Boating Safety" — was published in the same journal issue (DOI: 10.1115/1.4032262).

## Notes
This is significant as the most rigorous published attempt to formally quantify ESD risk in marina environments, using standard engineering risk assessment methods (Ayyub is a prominent risk engineering researcher). The Lloyd's Register co-author brings maritime insurance/risk perspective. The companion simulation paper (DOI: 10.1115/1.4032262) provides the numerical/computational basis for the risk methodology. Together these papers represent the most technically sophisticated peer-reviewed treatment of ESD risk. Full text is behind the ASME paywall.
