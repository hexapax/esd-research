# ACAD-041: CPSC Pool and Spa Electrocution Fatality Data (2002–2018)

## Source Metadata
- **Type**: Government consumer product safety data
- **Publisher**: U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC)
- **Primary URL**: https://www.cpsc.gov/safety-education/safety-guides/pools-and-spas/dont-swim-shocks-electrical-safety-and-around-pools
- **Press Release (2003)**: https://www.cpsc.gov/Newsroom/News-Releases/2003/Dont-Swim-With-Shocks-CPSC-American-Red-Cross-Warn-of-Electrocutions-in-Swimming-Pools-Hot-Tubs-and-Spas
- **Electrocutions data PDF**: https://www.cpsc.gov/s3fs-public/Electrocutions-2011-to-2020.pdf
- **Credibility**: High — federal agency mandatory reporting data, though based on voluntary incident reports and may undercount

## Key Data Points

### CPSC Reported Fatalities
- **2002 through 2018**: 33 fatalities from electrocutions in swimming pools and spas
  - Rate: approximately **2 deaths per year** (16-year period)
- **2002 through 2018**: 47 total shock-related incidents in pools or spas (33 deaths + 14 non-fatal)
- **Prior period (1990–2002)**: 60 deaths and nearly 50 serious shocks reported involving electrical hazards in and around swimming pools, hot tubs, and spas (~4.6 deaths/year over 13 years)

### Scope Limitations
- CPSC data covers **pools and spas only** — not marinas, docks, open water, or lake/river ESD
- This is the narrowest and most conservative slice of ESD; dock/marina ESD is not captured
- Data is based on incidents **reported to CPSC**, not a systematic national surveillance system
- CPSC itself acknowledges this represents only reported incidents

### Trend Observation
- 1990–2002: ~4.6 pool/spa electrocution deaths per year
- 2002–2018: ~2.0 pool/spa electrocution deaths per year
- This decline likely reflects GFCI installation requirements and Code improvements in pool electrical systems
- **No CPSC data covers dock, marina, or open freshwater ESD**

## Epidemiological Significance
The CPSC pool/spa data represents the most systematically collected subset of ESD data, but it covers only one venue type. The broader ESD problem — at marinas, docks, boat lifts, and shore power connections in open water — is entirely outside CPSC's pool/spa reporting scope. The declining pool ESD rate suggests electrical codes work; the absence of marine/dock-equivalent data is a critical surveillance gap.

## Cross-Reference
- CPSC does not track marina or dock ESD separately
- USCG BARD tracks marina/dock incidents but captures only a fraction (LOA7 analysis found ~8% capture rate)
- Combined, these two systems miss the bulk of ESD deaths
