## Abstract/Summary
Case study of a minor who suffered electrical shock while swimming at a membership pool. Tree trimmers had severed the service drop's neutral return wire; the utility repaired it temporarily but did not permanently replace it until years later — after the injury occurred. The forensic engineer concluded that the pool association and its president were not negligent, attributing responsibility to the electric utility's failure to maintain proper service infrastructure.

Key topics covered:
- Electric shock drowning (ESD) mechanisms
- Three-phase electric power systems
- Stray voltage and stray current phenomena
- Consequences of severed neutral conductors
- Current flow analysis in compromised electrical systems

Published as vol. 38, no. 1.

## Notes
This paper represents the forensic engineering side of ESD — analyzing the electrical fault that caused an in-water shock injury to determine legal liability. It is significant for the ESD incident database because it demonstrates the fault mechanism (severed neutral wire causing stray voltage in pool water) and shows how forensic engineering analysis is used in ESD litigation. The Journal of the National Academy of Forensic Engineers (JNAFE) is a peer-reviewed professional journal for forensic engineers, making this a legitimate academic source. The utility (not the pool operator) was found liable, illustrating a common finding in ESD litigation.
