## Key Technical Content

One of two companion papers published simultaneously on ESD risk assessment (see also ACAD-024). This paper focuses on computational simulation of the electric current distribution around boats with ground faults:

- Developed simulation models of electric current distribution in water around boats connected to shore power with a ground fault
- Modeled scenarios including different fault locations (hull, engine, battery charger), different water conductivities (freshwater, brackish, saltwater), and different boat configurations
- Simulations used to characterize dangerous zones around fault-source boats and nearby vessels/swimmers
- Demonstrated quantitatively how freshwater's low conductivity creates extended dangerous zones compared to saltwater
- Provided current density maps around faulty boats showing how swimmers at various distances from the hull would experience different current levels
- Established the scientific basis for the "zone of danger" concept used in ESD prevention education
- Published alongside companion risk assessment methodology paper (ACAD-024)

## ESD Incidents Cited

Paper references the general body of ESD incident data as motivation but focuses on simulation methodology. Incidents are treated statistically as a class rather than individually cited.

## Regulatory/Legislative Impact

Provides the computational foundation for understanding ESD hazard zones. Results informed the 2017 NFPA Fire Protection Research Foundation Marina Risk Reduction report (ACAD-027). The simulation results support current NEC Article 555 and NFPA 303 requirements for no-swimming zones near powered boats and docks.

## Relevance Rating

HIGH — First peer-reviewed simulation study specifically modeling ESD current distribution around boats. Provides the quantitative basis for understanding hazard zones and current thresholds that inform marina safety standards.
