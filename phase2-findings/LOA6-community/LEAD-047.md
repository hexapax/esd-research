# LEAD-047: Lake of the Ozarks, Missouri — June 21, 2015 Marcus Colburn ESD Death

## Status
VERIFIED (multiple independent primary news sources)

## Basic Info
- **Date:** June 21, 2015 (Sunday, Father's Day, approximately 9:00 p.m.)
- **Location:** Woods Hollow Cove, 22.2 mile marker, Osage arm of Lake of the Ozarks, Osage Beach, Missouri
- **Victim:** Marcus Colburn, 21, of East Moline, Illinois
- **Outcome:** Fatal

## Mechanism
Marcus Colburn and his girlfriend, Taylor Curley, were swimming in Woods Hollow Cove when they felt electricity in the water near a dock. Colburn attempted to exit the water using the dock ladder; upon grabbing the ladder he received a fatal shock and fell face-down into the water. Someone on shore ran to shut off power to the dock, which likely saved Curley. Colburn was pronounced dead at Lake Regional Hospital just after 10:00 p.m. The Osage Beach Fire Protection District and a subsequent investigation by the Osage Beach Fire Department, Ameren, and Missouri Highway Patrol found the cause was a short circuit at the junction box between the dock and the residence — the circuit breaker had been tested twice previously (passing both times) but a visual inspection of the watertight exterior would not have detected the internal fault.

## Notes
- The Missourinet (2017) article quoting Angela Anderson says "Since 2015, four people have died at the lake from electric shock drowning" — Colburn is the most clearly documented 2015 victim. The reference to "four" suggests three additional 2015–2017 deaths at the lake beyond Colburn.
- This incident directly prompted Osage Beach to discuss dock inspection ordinances; a new dock inspection requirement was subsequently established (mentioned in LakeExpo follow-up).
- The Columbia Missourian article references the 2012 Anderson family lawsuit against Ameren, highlighting the ongoing regulatory failure context.
- This is a distinct, additional Lake of the Ozarks ESD fatality not already in the known FI-2012-009 or SUSP-2015-054 records.
