# LEAD-049: Smith Mountain Lake, Virginia — Pre-2024 Stray Voltage Near-Misses and Tingle Reports

## Status
LEAD — NEAR-MISS CLUSTER (multiple corroborated reports of stray voltage at docks; no additional fatalities confirmed pre-2024 beyond FI-2024-001)

## Basic Info
- **Date range:** Approximately 2017–2023 (documented tingle/shock reports; testing began 2017)
- **Location:** Smith Mountain Lake, Bedford/Franklin/Pittsylvania Counties, Virginia
- **Victims:** Multiple unidentified persons who experienced tingling/shock sensations at lake docks; no additional fatalities
- **Outcome:** Non-fatal near-misses (multiple)

## Mechanism
Stray voltage from boat lifts energized by ground-wire leakage from residential electrical systems. When boat lifts are in the water, metal contact with the energized frame creates voltage gradients in the water near docks. A swimmer who enters or contacts metal surfaces can receive a shock sufficient to cause paralysis.

## Notes
- This is a near-miss/risk cluster rather than a single incident. The systematic testing data (200+ docks with stray voltage) is extraordinary — it implies substantial ongoing ESD risk.
- The ESDPA (via Cardinal News) documented only the 2001 Lake Montclair, VA incident as prior Virginia ESD before FI-2024-001. The SML stray voltage findings suggest significant under-reporting.
- Fire Chief Talley's statement that the July 4, 2024 death "is the first time" implies no prior SML ESD fatalities known to emergency services — consistent with near-misses only.
- The passage of only one known fatality before 2024 at a lake where 97% of tested docks have stray voltage is statistically remarkable and supports the ESDPA's thesis that ESD is massively under-reported.
