# LEAD-023: Shelly Darling & Elizabeth Whipple — Lake Tuscaloosa, Alabama (April 2017)

## Status
LEAD — NEEDS VERIFICATION (not in known deduplication list; preliminary autopsy confirmed electrocution; multiple Alabama news sources)

## Basic Info
- **Date:** April 14, 2017 (Friday; bodies found early Saturday April 15)
- **Location:** Private dock at 15000 block of West Winds Drive, Northport, on Lake Tuscaloosa, Tuscaloosa County, Alabama
- **Victims:** 
  - Shelly Darling, 34 (clinic staff attorney, University of Alabama School of Law Elder Law Clinic); 
  - Elizabeth (Liz) Whipple, 41 (interim director, University of Alabama School of Law Domestic Violence Clinic)
- **Outcome:** Fatal for both — preliminary autopsy listed accidental electrocution as cause of death

## Mechanism
Both women went to a family lake house on Lake Tuscaloosa to sunbathe on Friday. When Darling did not return home for dinner, her husband called police. Family found their belongings on the dock but the women were missing. During the search, a police officer was shocked by electrical current on the dock. Dive teams recovered the bodies from the lake around 4:30 a.m. Saturday. Preliminary autopsies confirmed accidental electrocution as cause of death. Investigators believe faulty dock wiring sent current through the water/pier, immobilizing the swimmers. Tuscaloosa County Metro Homicide Unit investigated; no foul play suspected.
