# LEAD-020: Michael Knudsen — Traverse City, Michigan (August 2011)

## Status
LEAD — NEEDS VERIFICATION (may be in ESDPA list; not in known deduplication list provided)

## Basic Info
- **Date:** August 15, 2011
- **Location:** Duncan L. Clinch Marina (also called Clinch Park Marina), West Grand Traverse Bay, Traverse City, Michigan
- **Victim:** Michael Knudsen, 18, of Mancelona, Michigan
- **Outcome:** Fatal — died of asphyxia by drowning due to low-voltage electrocution

## Mechanism
Knudsen jumped off Dock F (F-Dock) into West Grand Traverse Bay inside the marina breakwall despite posted "No Swimming" signs. Electricians who inspected the dock post-incident found corroded, broken, and overheated equipment ground materials and an ungrounded conductor whose insulation had worn through and made contact with a junction box, apparently due to repeated dock motion/vibration. A friend swimming with Knudsen also felt an electric shock. Marina employees cut power to the dock after rescuers arrived; Knudsen was found unresponsive under the dock. Autopsy showed a combination of electrical shock and drowning.

## Notes
- $50M lawsuit filed by estate of Michael Knudsen in Grand Traverse County Circuit Court (attorney Geoffrey Fieger). Traverse City government was eventually dismissed on governmental immunity grounds; suit against marina manager proceeded.
- A friend, Zachary Kott-Millard, also suffered electrical shock and filed a separate lawsuit.
- Witnesses prior to the incident had reportedly felt electricity in the water near the dock but did not report it to marina staff.
- The BoatUS 2012 awareness article and multiple industry safety publications reference this incident by name.
