# LEAD-042: Norris Lake, Tennessee — April 10, 2001 Whitman Hollow Marina ESD Death

## Status
LEAD — NEEDS VERIFICATION (appears in ESDPA list; confirm not already in known list as FI-200X-XXX)

## Basic Info
- **Date:** April 10, 2001
- **Location:** Whitman Hollow Marina, Norris Lake, LaFollette, Tennessee
- **Victim:** One unidentified teenage boy (drowned/electrocuted); second teenage boy (survived)
- **Outcome:** Fatal (1 boy) + Non-fatal near-miss (1 boy)

## Mechanism
Two teenage boys were swimming behind a houseboat at the marina. One boy climbed onto the swim platform complaining of feeling a severe shock; the other boy fell back from the ladder — his head was reported as not below water (possible ventricular fibrillation). He could not be resuscitated. The cause was a damaged power cable to the boat: the black lead energized the hull, the ground wire was burned in two, and the circuit breaker did not trip due to an incorrect connection. The houseboat was energizing the water via its hull.

## Notes
- Norris Lake is a TVA reservoir in northeastern Tennessee.
- This event preceded the Noah Dean and Nate Act by more than a decade; it illustrates the persistent ESD hazard at Tennessee lakes.
- The mechanism (energized houseboat hull, failed circuit breaker) is virtually identical to the 2012 Cherokee Lake deaths — same lake region.
- The ESDPA entry for Sept. 30, 2000 Tims Ford Lake (see LEAD-043) is in the same document set, showing Tennessee as a multi-decade cluster state.
