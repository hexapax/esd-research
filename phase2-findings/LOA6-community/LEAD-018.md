## Mechanism
Six teenagers at a lakehouse hot tub decided to swim from a small dock. After several minutes they felt electric current flowing through their bodies. One boy experienced inability to swim and sank as he lost muscle control. Adults ran out and dove in to retrieve children. Three were recovered unresponsive; one was pronounced dead at hospital, the other two suffered brain damage. Investigation revealed dock wiring was not the cause — instead, an electric utility's underground high-voltage cables beneath and around the lake had severely corroded "concentric neutrals," causing stray current to flow through the lake water. The utility had known of the corrosion for up to nine years prior. A jury found the local utility at fault. Testing showed currents up to 0.5A and voltages up to 6.2V (well within the range to cause paralysis/drowning in fresh water).
