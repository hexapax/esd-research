# LEAD-048: Lake Lanier, Georgia — July 24, 2006 ESD Incident (17-year-old, outcome unclear)

## Status
LEAD — NEEDS VERIFICATION (outcome — fatal or non-fatal — not confirmed from available open sources; mechanism confirmed as ESD)

## Basic Info
- **Date:** July 24, 2006
- **Location:** Private dock, Lake Lanier, Cumming, Georgia (Forsyth County)
- **Victim:** Unidentified male, age 17
- **Outcome:** Unclear — described as "was overcome by electric shock" — possibly fatal, possibly non-fatal

## Mechanism
A 17-year-old boy was in the water near a private dock working on a jet ski with two friends. An extension cord with damaged insulation caused the metal dock to become energized. The boy was overcome by electric shock. Two friends were also shocked and "partially disabled" — they could not help their friend. His father entered the water (felt electric shock) and was involved in a rescue attempt.

## Sources Found
1. ESDPA Incident List (multiple versions): "July 24, 2006 Lake Lanier, Cumming, GA. Seventeen year old boy in water near a private dock, working on a jet ski with two friends, was overcome by electric shock. Extension cord with damaged insulation caused the metal dock to become energized. Friends also shocked, and partially disabled, could not help their friend. Father of victim and friend rushed forward – boy on ladder said he was being shocked, other boy in water not moving. Friend rushed aft to pull shore cord as father went onto water – his son could not be resuscitated." (From later versions of the list) — from augresmaritimemuseum.org reproduction
2. The ESDPA list update adds: "Father went onto water — his son could not be resuscitated" — implying a fatal outcome. URL: https://www.mikeholt.com/files/PDF/Electric_Shock_Drownings_Update.pdf

## Notes
- The ESDPA phrase "his son could not be resuscitated" in the later/longer versions of the list strongly implies this was a fatality, not a near-miss.
- If fatal, this is the FIRST documented ESD death at Lake Lanier — 17 years before the 2023 Thomas Milner death (GOV-2023-001).
- The mechanism (damaged extension cord energizing dock) is very common and preventable.
- WSB-TV Atlanta (2016 report) mentions "a Forsyth County man was swimming off a family dock at Lake Lanier about three years ago [i.e., ~2013] when he felt an electric shock and was able to get himself and his daughter out of the water" — that is a separate near-miss and may be NM-2013-003.
- The 2023 Fox5 Atlanta article on Thomas Milner explicitly states neighbors say they "never heard of anyone being electrocuted in the lake before" — potentially unaware of the 2006 event.
