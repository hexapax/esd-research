## Findings by Organization

### BoatUS / BoatUS Foundation
- **boatus.com** has an extensive ESD Resource Center with multiple articles (2012–2022).
- The **October 2012 article** ("Raising Awareness About Electric Shock Drowning") is the most incident-rich, naming: Cherokee Lake TN (Noah/Nate — KNOWN), Lake of the Ozarks double death July 4 2012 (Anderson children — KNOWN), Lake of the Ozarks July 7 2012 (26-year-old woman — see notes below).
- The **2013 OWAA article** distributed by BoatUS named all four July 4, 2012 victims explicitly: Alexandra Anderson (13), Brayden Anderson (8), Noah Winstead (10), Nate Parker Lynam (11).
- BoatUS does not name specific incidents post-2013 in publicly accessible articles found.

### ABYC (American Boat and Yacht Council)
- ABYC issued a **Safety Alert on July 10, 2012** and hosted a Kevin Ritz webinar (July 19, 2012) on ESD. No specific new incidents beyond those already in news.
- ABYC's online learning platform has an ESD course by Kevin Ritz ("Electric Shock Drowning: The Invisible Killer") which references unspecified case studies.
- ESDPA press log confirms: "The American Boat and Yacht Council Responds to Electric Shock Drowning Incidents, ABYC, July 10, 2012."

### ESFI (Electrical Safety Foundation International)
- ESFI has a dedicated ESD/Boating and Marina Safety section with multiple awareness pages, a toolkit PDF, and a brochure.
- **No specific named incidents** found on ESFI pages. Content is general awareness/prevention focused.
- ESFI issued tips in May 2015 ("Memorial Day") — referenced in ESDPA press log but no new victims named.

### NASBLA
- NASBLA ESD Resources page is comprehensive on definitions, standards, and prevention but **does not name specific incidents**.
- NASBLA ERAC has an ESD Response and Investigation Checklist (2025 update) — useful for investigators, not for incident discovery.
- NASBLA links to an "Electrocution Risk at Marinas" document (fatality/contributingfactors/children tags) which was not fully accessible.

### NFPA
- NFPA ESD awareness pages, blog posts, and press releases (2022–2025) are prevention-focused with **no specific named incidents**.
- NFPA Research Foundation: **Marina Shock Hazard Research Planning Workshop** (August 13, 2015) and the resulting WPI report by Park & Meacham (August 2017) reference statistical summaries from the Quality Marine Services ESD list but do not add new named incidents.
- NFPA 303 (2016 edition) changes driven by ESD deaths — referenced generally, no new incidents named.

### Other Industry Sources Found
- **Bayview Yacht Club (BYC) Safety Series PDF (April 2017):** Names Michael Knudsen (Michigan, August 2011), Alec McQueen (Lake Powell UT, June 2014), Carmen Johnson (Alabama, April 2016) — all new leads or cross-references.
- **AMI (Association of Marina Industries) 2017 Conference Presentation PDF:** General ESD education, no specific incidents named.
- **IAEI Magazine (2007):** Technical analysis of ESD mechanism; references several investigated cases without naming victims.
- **IAEI Magazine (July/August 2014 "Safety in Marinas"):** Names Noah and Nate (KNOWN), Michael (18-year-old who grabbed a handrail — possibly Michael Cunningham or another unidentified case), Lucas Ritz (KNOWN ESD founder's son, 1999, Portland OR).
- **Safe Electricity (safeelectricity.org / NRECA):** Carmen Johnson (2016, Alabama) and Kayla Matos (2017, NJ) prominently featured.
- **lifesaving.com (June 2017):** References two women killed in Lake Tuscaloosa in April 2017 (Darling/Whipple), citing ESDPA's David Rifkin on 88 total ESD reports received.

## Notes on Source Behavior

1. **BoatUS.com**: Rich ESD content but the main 2012 article is paywalled/JavaScript-rendered (WebFetch returned CSS only). Key incident details were captured via search snippets and secondary sources.
2. **ESFI**: Awareness-only content; no incident database or named victims.
3. **NASBLA**: Policy/standards focused; no incident specifics.
4. **NFPA**: Prevention and code-standards focused; aggregate statistics from the WPI/NFPA Research Foundation paper reference Quality Marine Services data.
5. **ABYC**: Technical standards and training focused; Kevin Ritz webinar references his son's case and unnamed "case studies."
6. **Key discovery mechanism**: The Bayview Yacht Club (BYC) Safety Series PDF from 2017 and industry aggregator sites (Safe Electricity, lifesaving.com, Cardinal News) were the richest sources for named incidents, having compiled information from multiple news outlets.
