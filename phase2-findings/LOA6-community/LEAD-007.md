# LEAD-007: Keith Elkins (Diver) — Prospect Yacht Club, Ohio River, Louisville, Kentucky, April 2022

## Status
VERIFIED (WAVE3 TV investigative report + ESDPA press release + OSHA referral)

## Basic Info
- **Date:** April 2022 (before Thunder over Louisville event; approximate pre-event date not confirmed)
- **Location:** Prospect Yacht Club, Ohio River, Louisville/Oldham County, Kentucky
- **Victim:** Keith Elkins, commercial diver
- **Outcome:** Fatal (electrocuted while diving)

## Mechanism
Keith Elkins was a commercial diver working on an assignment at the Prospect Yacht Club on the Ohio River, completing work before the Thunder over Louisville event. He was in the water when he suffered a fatal electrocution. A WAVE3 investigative report found that the Kentucky Labor Cabinet had issued a stop-work order to the yacht club two days before the fatal dive. The investigative report also found that Oldham County had not issued any electrical permit for work at the site in 2021 or 2022. OSHA and the U.S. Coast Guard were both referred the case.

## Notes
- The WAVE3 article quotes ESDPA Vice President Kevin Cunningham: "We call it the silent killer because electricity could be leaking in there and no one knows it."
- This incident differs slightly from a typical recreational ESD incident in that the victim was a professional diver working at the marina, not a swimmer. It would still qualify as an in-water electrocution if the current was in the water rather than from direct contact.
- The stop-work order issued two days prior is a significant regulatory detail — suggests known hazards were present and not addressed.
- The Ohio River is fresh water, consistent with ESD conditions.
