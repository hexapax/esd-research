# LEAD-021: Michael Cunningham — Stonewall Jackson Lake, West Virginia (May 2010)

## Status
LEAD — NEEDS VERIFICATION (not in known deduplication list provided; high confidence real ESD incident; named legislation passed)

## Basic Info
- **Date:** May 29, 2010 (Memorial Day)
- **Location:** Stonewall Jackson Lake Marina, Weston, West Virginia (Lewis County)
- **Victim:** Michael Cunningham, 15, of Bridgeport, West Virginia (Bridgeport High School student)
- **Outcome:** Fatal — instant electrocution (autopsy showed no water in lungs; died instantly)

## Mechanism
Michael Cunningham and friends were swimming behind a houseboat docked at the lake's marina. The houseboat was leaking electric current into the fresh water. When Cunningham grabbed the boat's ladder to climb out of the water, he received a fatal electrical shock. His friend Hallie Kittle, swimming nearby, also received an electrical shock. The current from the dock had leaked into the water around the houseboat.

## Notes
- This incident directly led to the "Michael Cunningham Act" (West Virginia), signed into law May 29, 2013 by Gov. Earl Ray Tomblin. The law requires ground-fault circuit interrupters on public boat docks and marinas connected to electricity.
- Kevin Cunningham (father) co-founded the Electric Shock Drowning Prevention Association (ESDPA).
- His friend Hallie Kittle is referenced in some sources as also suffering an electrical shock but surviving.
- One of the most legislatively significant ESD incidents — directly produced state legislation and helped found the ESDPA.
