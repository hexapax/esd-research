## Key Findings by Location

### Lake Hamilton / Arkansas
- KATV (2016) confirmed **5 total Arkansas ESD deaths**: 3 at Lake Hamilton, 1 at Lake Ouachita (Jesse Fortner), 1 at Bull Shoals Lake.
- The 3 Lake Hamilton deaths likely include: (1) Sep 15, 2001 Farr Shores rescuer; (2) one of the 2007–2009 cluster deaths; (3) possibly the May 11, 1991 canoe incident.
- Jesse's Law (2011) was named after Jesse Fortner (Lake Ouachita, 2005/2006), not a Lake Hamilton victim.
- No additional Lake Hamilton deaths found beyond this cluster in 2010–2024 in open sources (ordinary drownings and boating accidents found, but no ESD).

### Oklahoma Cluster
- CDC MMWR Lake A = most likely **Grand Lake O' the Cherokees + Lake Hudson** (Markham Ferry Reservoir), NE Oklahoma.
- "Lake B" (southern OK) = most likely Lake Murray or Lake Texoma.
- The ESDPA list documents 4 Oklahoma deaths: Dec 1989 (submersible pump), Jul 1991 (dock wiring), Jul 1993 (dock lights), Sep 1993 (submersible pump). The CDC counts 5 deaths (4 at A, 1 at B) — the GOV-1989-001 June 1, 1989 event at Lake B is the 5th.
- No additional Oklahoma post-1993 ESD deaths found in open-source searches.

### Tennessee Cluster
- Significant new discovery: **Tims Ford Lake** has at least 3 documented ESD events (Sep 30, 2000 — 2 deaths; Sep 27, 2012 — 1 death; ~2013 per FI-2013-009), making it a major Tennessee hotspot alongside Cherokee Lake.
- **Norris Lake** had an April 10, 2001 death at Whitman Hollow Marina — likely not in the known list.
- **Cedar Hill Lake / Smithville, TN** had a July 18, 1999 death of a 7-year-old at Sligo Marina.
- No post-2012 Cherokee Lake additional deaths found in open sources; the Noah Dean and Nate Act may have improved safety at that specific marina.

### Smith Mountain Lake / Virginia
- No pre-2024 fatalities confirmed, consistent with Fire Chief Talley's statement.
- Extraordinary stray voltage data: 201–203 of 209 tested docks (97%) had stray voltage from boat lifts. This is documented from 2017 testing by Neil Harrington.
- Jim Erler (physicist) lobbied Virginia DHCD in January 2020 without success.
- Stray voltage confirmed at 70% of 125 docks tested post-July 2024.

### Michigan Marinas
- No additional Michigan marina ESD deaths found beyond known incidents (FI-2011-002 Traverse City, BARD-2010-001 WV).
- The Michigan DNR has an active ESD awareness page, indicating recognition of the hazard.
- Lake Macatawa / Bay Haven Marina (Park Township, MI) had an 18-year-old boy ESD event in July 1988 — predating all known FI- coded records.

### Lake Lanier / Georgia
- **July 24, 2006 incident** found: 17-year-old boy "could not be resuscitated" after dock ESD — if fatal, this is the first Lake Lanier ESD death, 17 years before GOV-2023-001 (Thomas Milner).
- No other Lake Lanier ESD events found in 2007–2022 window beyond the 2013 near-miss (NM-2013-003) and the 2023 Milner death.
- CBS News article on the 2023 Milner death noted neighbors said they had "never heard of anyone being electrocuted in the lake before" — possibly unaware of 2006 event.

### Lake of the Ozarks / Missouri
- Significant cluster confirmed with at least **5–6 ESD fatalities** at this single lake:
  - ~2004: Tyler Deeds (LEAD-052, single source)
  - Jul 28, 2007: Unidentified 24yo woman (LEAD-051, verified)
  - Jul 4, 2012: Anderson children x2 (known FI-2012-009)
  - Jul 7, 2012: Jennifer Lankford, 26 (LEAD-046, verified)
  - Jun 21, 2015: Marcus Colburn, 21 (LEAD-047, verified)
- Missourinet (2017) quotes Angela Anderson: "Since 2015, four people have died at the lake from electric shock drowning" — implying 3 additional 2015–2017 deaths beyond Colburn that have not yet been identified.
- Additional near-misses documented: May 28, 2012 (3 women shocked, survived); Jul 23, 2012 (3 swimmers shocked, survived); 2013 (Dock Lifeguard reference to "three ESD deaths" in 2013 triggering the company's founding).
