# LEAD-040: Lake Hamilton, Arkansas — May 11, 1991 Canoe ESD Near-Miss (One Boy Drowned)

## Status
LEAD — NEEDS VERIFICATION / POSSIBLE DUPLICATE OR EXPANSION OF FI-2007-003 CLUSTER

## Basic Info
- **Date:** May 11, 1991
- **Location:** Lake Hamilton, Hot Springs, Arkansas
- **Victim:** One unidentified boy (age unknown) — drowned; three other boys survived
- **Outcome:** Fatal (1 boy) + 3 Non-fatal near-misses

## Mechanism
A canoe carrying four young boys tipped over a few dozen yards from a dock. As the boys swam toward the dock they felt a light tingling sensation. Three of the boys escaped; one drowned. The electrified dock energized the water near the dock exit point.

## Notes
- The ESDPA list entry is truncated in searchable versions and the full text of what happened to the fourth boy is not found in open sources.
- If this is indeed a fatality, it predates the Jesse Fortner legislation trigger and represents the earliest known Lake Hamilton ESD death.
- The May 11, 1991 date is a Saturday, consistent with recreational lake use.
- The KATV article (June 2016) says three Hamilton deaths total; the known ESDPA list documents 2007 and 2009 deaths at Hamilton plus the Sept 15, 2001 Farr Shores incident — the 1991 event could be the third, or the count might reflect a different grouping.
