# LEAD-043: Tims Ford Lake, Tennessee — September 30, 2000 Double ESD Death

## Status
LEAD — NEEDS VERIFICATION (may overlap with FI-2013-009 which is noted as "~2013 Tims Ford" — this is a DIFFERENT, earlier event)

## Basic Info
- **Date:** September 30, 2000
- **Location:** Tims Ford Lake, Winchester, Tennessee (Franklin County)
- **Victim:** Two young adult males, ages 21 and 22 (both fatal)
- **Outcome:** Fatal (2 victims)

## Mechanism
Two young men (ages 21 and 22) experienced electric shock drowning near a dock at Tims Ford Lake. A live wire was in the water near the dock. A rescue diver who responded subsequently felt an electric shock in the water.

## Notes
- Tims Ford Lake is a TVA reservoir in Franklin County, TN, near Winchester.
- The 2000 event is one of the earliest documented Tennessee ESD double fatalities.
- The ESDPA list also documents a SEPARATE Tims Ford Lake event on September 27, 2012 (39-year-old male, see LEAD-044), confirming the lake as a multi-incident hotspot.
- The rescue diver feeling an electric shock is a critical corroborating detail that confirms electrical involvement beyond a simple drowning.
- Both the 2000 and 2012 events predate and may have contributed to the legislative momentum that produced the Noah Dean and Nate Act (passed 2014, effective 2015).
