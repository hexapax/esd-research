# LEAD-045: Cedar Hill Lake (Sligo Marina), Tennessee — July 18, 1999 ESD Death (7-year-old)

## Status
LEAD — NEEDS VERIFICATION (appears in ESDPA list; confirm not already catalogued under FI identifier)

## Basic Info
- **Date:** July 18, 1999
- **Location:** Sligo Marina, Pier B, Cedar Hill Lake (also listed as "Smithville, TN"), Tennessee
- **Victim:** One male child, age 7 (fatal); one male child, age 8 (survived/recovered)
- **Outcome:** Fatal (age 7) + Non-fatal near-miss (age 8)

## Mechanism
Two young boys, both wearing flotation devices, were discovered face-down in the water a few feet behind a houseboat. The 7-year-old could not be revived; the 8-year-old recovered. The mechanism was electric shock drowning, with the houseboat as the energizing source.

## Notes
- The flotation devices are a significant detail: both boys were wearing life preservers and still died/were incapacitated, which strongly supports ESD (paralysis) rather than ordinary drowning.
- This 1999 event predates the Jesse Fortner (2005 Lake Ouachita) and the 2007–2009 Lake Hamilton cluster, showing Tennessee had documented ESD fatalities years before the 2012 landmark cases.
- Cedar Hill Lake in the Smithville TN area context may refer to Cordell Hull Lake (a Corps of Engineers reservoir on the Cumberland River) or a smaller private impoundment near Smithville, DeKalb County.
