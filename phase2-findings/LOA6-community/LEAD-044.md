# LEAD-044: Tims Ford Lake, Tennessee — September 27, 2012 ESD Death (39-year-old male)

## Status
LEAD — POSSIBLE DUPLICATE OF FI-2013-009 (which is noted as "Unknown, Tims Ford Lake, ~2013") — BUT DATE IS SEPTEMBER 2012, NOT 2013. May be a distinct event or the FI-2013-009 date may be wrong.

## Basic Info
- **Date:** September 27, 2012
- **Location:** Tims Ford Lake, Franklin County, Tennessee
- **Victim:** Unidentified male, age 39
- **Outcome:** Fatal

## Mechanism
A 39-year-old man was swimming from a dock when he died from electrical shock. Faulty wiring on the dock is blamed for the death. The home had been struck by lightning in the recent past, though it is unknown whether the lightning strike contributed to the fault condition.

## Notes
- If this is distinct from FI-2013-009, Tims Ford Lake has THREE documented ESD events: 2000 (2 fatalities), 2012 (1 fatality), and ~2013 (sparse details).
- The lightning-strike angle is unusual — prior lightning damage could have compromised insulation or grounding, creating a latent fault activated later under wet conditions.
- Tims Ford Lake, a TVA impoundment near Winchester in Franklin County, is a significant multi-incident hotspot that deserves its own investigation file.
- The 2012 and 2013 events both occurred within months of the July 4, 2012 Lake of the Ozarks and Cherokee Lake deaths, when national ESD awareness was at its peak.
