# LEAD-026: Marcus Colburn — Lake of the Ozarks, Missouri (June 2015)

## Status
LEAD — NEEDS VERIFICATION (not in known deduplication list; documented by Lake Expo, Missouri State Highway Patrol report, Quality Marine Services ESD list)

## Basic Info
- **Date:** June 21, 2015 (Sunday, ~9:00 p.m.)
- **Location:** Woods Hollow Cove, 22.2 Mile Marker, Lake of the Ozarks, Missouri
- **Victim:** Marcus Colburn, 21, of East Moline, Illinois
- **Outcome:** Fatal — electrocuted when grabbing dock ladder; ruled "electrical shock" by highway patrol

## Mechanism
Colburn and fellow swimmer Taylor Curley felt electricity in the water while swimming near a dock. Colburn tried to exit the water by using the dock ladder; when he grabbed the ladder, he received a fatal electrical shock and fell face-down in the water. A faulty junction box between the dock and residence is suspected as the source. Witnesses reported the circuit breaker would trip after 10–15 seconds of being reset; the last attempt to reset the breaker coincided with the swimmers being near the dock as it grew dark (the breaker controlled dock lights). Osage Beach Fire Protection District responded; electric service to the dock was subsequently removed.

## Notes
- This was described as the fifth electrocution fatality at Lake of the Ozarks since 2007 (per Lake Expo).
- Families of Colburn and other victims organized to petition Osage Beach, Missouri Board to require annual dock GFCI inspections.
- East Moline, Illinois connection: Colburn's family joined with the Anderson family (Lake of the Ozarks 2012 victims) in advocacy.
- The 2012 Lake of the Ozarks deaths (Alexandra Anderson, 13, and Brayden Anderson, 8) are in the known deduplication list; this is the 2015 follow-on incident at the same lake.
- Quality Marine Services ESD incident list entry #3 (in June 2015 timeframe) matches this incident description.
