# LEAD-050: Oklahoma "Lake A" Identification — Likely Grand Lake O' the Cherokees (NE Oklahoma)

## Status
LEAD — ANALYTICAL FINDING (identification of the CDC MMWR "Lake A" from 1996 report)

## Basic Info
- **Date range:** 1989–1993 (four deaths at "Lake A")
- **Location:** "Lake A" — two adjoining lakes in northeastern Oklahoma (CDC MMWR language); "Lake B" — one lake in southern Oklahoma
- **Victims (known):** FI-1991-001 (50yo male, July 24, 1991), GOV-1993-001 (13yo boy, July 27, 1993), FI-1993-002 (46yo male scuba diver, Sep 5, 1993) — plus a 4th "Lake A" death not separately catalogued
- **Outcome:** 5 total deaths (4 at Lake A, 1 at Lake B)

## Mechanism (general)
The CDC MMWR (1996) describes deaths from dock electrical faults at two lake systems. An inspection of 116 commercial docks at "Lake A" in 1989 found 96% violated the National Electrical Code, with ungrounded electrical systems as the most common violation. Faults included: dock lights energizing dock frame (July 1993), dock wiring fault (July 1991), submersible pump fault (December 1989), and unknown fault (July 27, 1993 — 13yo).

## Sources Found
1. CDC MMWR (1996): "Electricity-Related Deaths on Lakes -- Oklahoma, 1989-1993." Describes "Lake A" as two adjoining lakes in NE Oklahoma; "Lake B" as one lake in southern Oklahoma. Four deaths at Lake A, one at Lake B. URL: https://www.cdc.gov/mmwr/preview/mmwrhtml/00042081.htm
2. US Sailing / ussailing.org cites the same CDC data, quoting "Lake A" in northeastern Oklahoma for the 1989 inspection. URL: http://www.ussailing.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/Electricity_Along_The_Shore.pdf
3. ESDPA Incident List (2004 Harbor Marine version) lists: "July 1993 Oklahoma, single drowning, fault in dock lights"; "July 1991 Oklahoma, single drowning, fault in dock wiring"; "Dec 1989 Oklahoma, single drowning, fault in submersible pump"; "Sept 1993 Oklahoma, single drowning, fault in submersible pump."
4. PubMed abstract confirms: "Four deaths occurred at two adjoining lakes in northeastern Oklahoma (lake A), and one at a lake in the southern part of the state (lake B)." URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/8618544/

## Analysis: Lake Identification
The CDC MMWR describes "two adjoining lakes" in northeastern Oklahoma. The most likely candidate is **Grand Lake O' the Cherokees** and **Lake Hudson** (also called Markham Ferry Reservoir), which are adjoining/connected impoundments on the Grand/Neosho River in Mayes and Delaware counties, NE Oklahoma. Grand Lake is the largest recreational lake in northeastern Oklahoma, with extensive marina and houseboat infrastructure, making it the most plausible location for 116 commercial docks inspected in 1989.

Alternative candidates: Lake Eufaula (too large/southeastern), Lake Keystone (central OK, not NE), Lake Tenkiller (Cherokee County, NE Oklahoma — possible), Fort Gibson Lake (Cherokee County — adjacent to Grand Lake system).

"Lake B" (southern Oklahoma): Most likely Lake Murray (Carter County) or Lake Texoma (Bryan/Marshall counties), both large recreational lakes with marina infrastructure in southern Oklahoma.

## Notes
- The 4th Lake A death may be GOV-1989-001 if that record's lake identification is wrong — OR there is an additional death not in the known list.
- The CDC MMWR is one of the most authoritative ESD documents ever published; the lake identifications being anonymous ("A" and "B") was deliberate to avoid stigmatizing specific bodies of water.
- GRDA (Grand River Dam Authority) is the agency that polices Grand Lake — a 2024 search found multiple GRDA drowning reports from modern years, confirming ongoing activity there.
- If Lake A = Grand Lake / Lake Hudson, this would make Grand Lake a significant multi-decade ESD hotspot deserving a dedicated investigation.
