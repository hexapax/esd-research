# LEAD-022: Carmen Johnson — Smith Lake, Winston County, Alabama (April 2016)

## Status
LEAD — NEEDS VERIFICATION (not in known deduplication list provided; well-documented; strong ESD confirmation)

## Basic Info
- **Date:** April 16, 2016
- **Location:** Family boat dock on Smith Lake (Lewis Smith Lake), Winston County, Alabama (near Priceville, Morgan County area)
- **Victim:** Carmen Johnson, 15, Priceville High School cheerleader
- **Outcome:** Fatal — electric shock drowning

## Mechanism
Carmen Johnson and friends were swimming at the family lake house. A metal ladder lowered into the lake was energized by electrical current — the family later determined that water had filled an electrical box on the boat dock (possibly from flooding on Christmas Day), sending electric charge through the metal dock structure into the water. Carmen's father Jimmy Johnson jumped in to save her but blacked out from the electric shock. Her mother managed to turn off the electrical supply, saving Jimmy, their son Eric (who also jumped in), and Carmen's friend Reagan — but Carmen had already disappeared below the surface and drowned.

## Notes
- The Johnson family became prominent ESD awareness advocates; father Jimmy Johnson gave numerous media interviews.
- Safe Electricity (safeelectricity.org), the ESFI, and multiple marina safety publications use Carmen's story as a cautionary example.
- Within a year of Carmen's death, two more women died in Lake Tuscaloosa (Alabama) in a similar ESD incident (see LEAD-023).
- Some sources describe the source as a "faulty light switch" energizing a ladder; the family's own account says a flooded electrical box on the dock.
