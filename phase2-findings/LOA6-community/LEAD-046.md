# LEAD-046: Lake of the Ozarks, Missouri — July 7, 2012 Jennifer Lankford ESD Death

## Status
VERIFIED (multiple independent news sources; not in the known ESDPA list under a FI- ID based on available known IDs)

## Basic Info
- **Date:** July 7, 2012 (Saturday, approximately 7:45 p.m.)
- **Location:** Dry Branch Cove, Morgan County, Lake of the Ozarks, Missouri (5-mile marker)
- **Victim:** Jennifer Lankford, 26, of Hazelwood, Missouri
- **Outcome:** Fatal

## Mechanism
Jennifer Lankford was swimming in Dry Branch Cove with her two step-brothers, ages 11 and 13, when the group felt a current in the water. The step-brothers swam to a different dock and were unharmed. Lankford swam toward the energized dock and may have been electrocuted upon touching something metallic there. She could not be revived at the scene. The dock where Lankford died had "several electrical problems," per Missouri Highway Patrol. The incident occurred three days after the July 4 Anderson children deaths (also Lake of the Ozarks).

## Notes
- This is the THIRD Lake of the Ozarks ESD death in one week (July 4: Anderson children x2; July 7: Lankford).
- The step-brothers' survival by swimming away demonstrates the voltage gradient — those farther from the energized dock survived, those near it were paralyzed.
- The Missourinet (2017) article quoting Angela Anderson says "Since 2015, four people have died at the lake from electric shock drowning" — combined with the 2012 three, this equals at least 7 Lake of the Ozarks ESD fatalities through 2017. Marcus Colburn (2015, see LEAD-047) plus possibly others in 2015–2017 not yet identified.
- This incident is distinct from the FI-2012-009 (Anderson children) known record.
