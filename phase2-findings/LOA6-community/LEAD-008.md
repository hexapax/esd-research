# LEAD-008: Nate Davenport — Harbourside Place Fountain, Jupiter, Florida, October 22, 2023

## Status
VERIFIED — NOTE: This is a fountain electrocution at a shopping center, not a marina/dock incident. Included as borderline ESD (electricity in water causing death). Classification uncertain.

## Basic Info
- **Date:** October 22, 2023 (~3:30 p.m.)
- **Location:** Harbourside Place shopping center fountain, Jupiter, Palm Beach County, Florida
- **Victim:** Nate Davenport, a U.S. Navy veteran (father of four)
- **Outcome:** Fatal (Davenport died from injuries); four others injured

## Mechanism
A child entered a decorative fountain area at the Harbourside Place shopping center. The fountain was electrified. Nate Davenport entered the fountain water to rescue his sons and was fatally electrocuted. Palm Beach County Fire Rescue and Jupiter Police responded to a call of a child in distress in the fountain. Eight people ultimately needed assistance; multiple received electrical shocks while in the water. The fountain is a public display, not a marina or private dock.

## Notes
- This incident is BORDERLINE for ESD classification. Standard ESD definition requires electricity leaking into water from infrastructure (docks, marinas, boats, shore power, pumps). A shopping center fountain is infrastructure-electricity-in-water. However, the mechanism is similar — an electrical fault in pool/fountain equipment causing the water to become electrified.
- The ESDPA listed it in their press releases, suggesting they consider it within their scope.
- The victim died attempting to rescue others from electrified water, paralleling many classic ESD rescuer scenarios.
- This is Florida (salt water state) but the fountain is fresh/treated water — consistent with ESD conditions.
- Included here for completeness as ESDPA referenced it, but primary ESD researchers should assess whether it fits their classification criteria.
