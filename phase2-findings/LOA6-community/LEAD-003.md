# LEAD-003: Thomas Milner — Lake Lanier, Cumming (Forsyth County), Georgia, July 27, 2023

## Status
VERIFIED (multiple independent major news outlets)

## Basic Info
- **Date:** July 27, 2023 (~5:30 p.m.); died July 28 or shortly after at hospital
- **Location:** Private family dock with boat lift, Lake Lanier, Cumming, Forsyth County, Georgia
- **Victim:** Thomas Milner (also spelled "Shepard Milner" in some sources), 24
- **Outcome:** Fatal

## Mechanism
Milner jumped off a family dock into Lake Lanier. He was immediately heard screaming for help. A family friend attempted to use a ladder to retrieve him — unsuccessful. Neighbors navigated a boat over; one jumped into the water to help and described "a burning sensation he recognized as an electric shock." That neighbor swam ashore, turned off a power box, then re-entered the water and pulled Milner onto the dock. His uncle administered CPR until medics arrived. Milner died at a local hospital. Investigation by Forsyth County Sheriff's Office and Georgia DNR was ongoing. The family stated the dock (less than three years old) was installed by a licensed electrician.

## Notes
- ESDPA listed this in their 2023 press releases: "July 31, 2023: Man dies after being electrocuted at Georgia's Lake Lanier."
- Army Corps of Engineers said they had no prior recorded ESD cases at Lake Lanier despite inspecting docks every 5 years.
- A WSBTV (Atlanta) article mentioned a Forsyth County man had been shocked at Lake Lanier approximately 3 years earlier (circa 2013) and managed to save himself and his daughter — that prior near-miss is itself an unverified community-level lead (see LEAD-004).
- Lake Lanier has approximately 700 miles of shoreline and is described as the most popular lake in the Southeast.
