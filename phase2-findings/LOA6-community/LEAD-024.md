# LEAD-024: Kayla Matos — Toms River, New Jersey (June 2017)

## Status
LEAD — NEEDS VERIFICATION (not in known deduplication list; well-documented; confirmed by police; note: victim age listed as 10 in some sources, 11 in others)

## Basic Info
- **Date:** June 17, 2017 (Saturday evening, ~8:12 p.m.)
- **Location:** Private home lagoon, Tobago Avenue, Shelter Cove section, Toms River, Ocean County, New Jersey
- **Victim:** Kayla Matos, approximately 10–11 years old, of Newark, New Jersey (visiting friends in Toms River)
- **Outcome:** Fatal — electrocuted (not a drowning in the classic sense; direct fatal electrocution)

## Mechanism
Kayla Matos and two friends were swimming/rafting in the backyard lagoon of a private home while wearing life jackets, in the presence of adults. Two of the girls touched the rail of a metal boat lift. An electric current had energized the boat lift equipment. One friend also touched the rail but was uninjured; Kayla received a fatal electrical shock. Police said the electric current "appears to have energized the equipment causing the fatal injury." Toms River Mayor said the boat lift was installed and inspected in 2001 (16 years earlier); the current homeowners bought the home in 2004 and did not own a boat and told police they had never used the lift. A neighbor noted that many boat lifts in the area were damaged by Superstorm Sandy.

## Notes
- Occurred same weekend as Evan Currie's death in Put-in-Bay, Ohio (see LEAD-025), prompting widespread national media coverage.
- Safe Electricity (safeelectricity.org) referenced this as "In 2017, a 10-year-old girl died in a New Jersey lagoon after touching an electrically-charged metal boat lift rail."
- Toms River code enforcement officials announced they would inspect the boat lift equipment.
- Lagoon is saltwater/brackish (tidal lagoon, Jersey Shore); this may be one of the rare cases where ESD-like conditions occurred in non-standard water. However, mechanism was direct contact with electrified rail, not classic ESD muscle-paralysis-in-water. Classification borderline.
