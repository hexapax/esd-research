# LEAD-051: Lake of the Ozarks, Missouri — July 28, 2007 ESD Death (24-year-old woman)

## Status
VERIFIED (multiple independent sources; appears in ESDPA list and pre-ESDPA Harbor Marine document)

## Basic Info
- **Date:** July 28, 2007
- **Location:** Private dock, Lake of the Ozarks, Missouri
- **Victim:** Unidentified female, age 24
- **Outcome:** Fatal

## Mechanism
A 24-year-old woman attempted to exit the water using a metal ladder at the end of a private dock. She experienced a paralyzing electric shock which caused her to fall back into the water and drown. Several people had previously reported being shocked by the ladder; the dock owner had gone to shut the power off (but apparently did not do so in time). Investigation found the dock power wiring termination was submerged under the dock near the exit point.

## Notes
- This is the FOURTH distinct Lake of the Ozarks ESD death found: 2007 (this event), July 4, 2012 (Anderson children — FI-2012-009), July 7, 2012 (Lankford — LEAD-046), June 21, 2015 (Colburn — LEAD-047).
- The fact that multiple people had previously reported being shocked by the same ladder, yet the dock remained energized, is a critical safety failure pattern.
- Columbia Missourian (2015) mentions a 2004 death at Lake of the Ozarks involving "Tyler Deeds of Olathe, Kan., who died two years earlier when he was shocked by a dock cable" — suggesting a 2004 or 2006 incident may also exist (see LEAD-052).
