# LEAD-025: Evan Currie — Put-in-Bay, Ohio (June 2017)

## Status
LEAD — NEEDS VERIFICATION (not in known deduplication list; well-documented; ODNR investigated; source of current never officially confirmed)

## Basic Info
- **Date:** June 16, 2017 (Friday evening, ~6:30 p.m.)
- **Location:** Miller Marina (Miller Docks), Langram Road/Bayview Ave., Put-in-Bay, South Bass Island, Lake Erie, Ottawa County, Ohio
- **Victim:** Evan Currie, 19, of Dublin, Ohio (sophomore business major at Xavier University)
- **Outcome:** Fatal — electrocuted/drowned; pronounced dead at Port Clinton after CPR and transport

## Mechanism
The Currie family had just docked their 33-foot Sea Ray Sundancer at Miller Marina and plugged in to shore power. The family dog fell into the water and struggled. Father Jeffrey Currie jumped in to rescue the dog and began struggling himself. Seeing this, Evan and his brother also jumped in. All people in the water experienced what appeared to be electrical current. Evan's mother, on the boat, unplugged the shore power cord after bystanders instructed her to do so. When the plug was removed, the electrical current in the water stopped, and the father and brother reboarded the boat, but Evan was unresponsive and convulsing. An independent electrician checked the dock under police supervision and found no problems with the dock wiring; ODNR's recreation of the accident with the Currie boat also showed no problems with the boat. Source of electrical current was never officially confirmed publicly.

## Notes
- ODNR spokesman Eric Heis stated this appeared to be the first ESD death investigated by ODNR in Ohio.
- The incident occurred the same weekend as Kayla Matos's death in New Jersey, generating significant national media coverage of ESD.
- Shore power was definitively cut by unplugging the boat, confirming electrical current in water linked to shore power connection.
- No burns on victim consistent with ESD (low-voltage AC paralysis followed by drowning).
- Miller Marina co-owner Julene Market released a statement; dock electrical independently passed inspection.
