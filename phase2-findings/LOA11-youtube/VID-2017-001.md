# VID-2017-001: Shelly Darling & Elizabeth Whipple — YouTube Discovery

## Incident Summary
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Date** | April 14–15, 2017 (reported missing Friday evening April 14; bodies found early Saturday April 15) |
| **Location** | Lake Tuscaloosa, 15000 block of West Winds Drive, Northport, Alabama |
| **Classification** | Fatal ESD — dock electrocution |
| **Outcome** | Dead: 2 (Shelly Darling, age 34; Elizabeth Whipple, age 41), Injured: 1 officer shocked at scene (treated at scene) |

**Description:** Shelly Darling (34) and Elizabeth Whipple (41), both attorneys at the University of Alabama School of Law, went to the Darling family lake house to sunbathe on Friday April 14. Shelly's husband, an assistant athletics director for UA, contacted police when she didn't return for dinner. Family found belongings on the dock but the women were missing. Tuscaloosa dive teams located the bodies in the lake early Saturday morning. During the initial investigation, a police officer was shocked by a current on the dock where the women had been. Subsequent investigation confirmed electricity was running through the dock, likely from the boat lift power supply. The Medical Examiner ruled both deaths accidental electrocution (not ESD — the current was strong enough to cause direct electrocution, not just paralysis/drowning). Whipple was interim director of the UA Domestic Violence Clinic; Darling was a staff attorney at the Elder Law Clinic. A previous Tuscaloosa-area death: Dr. Eric Hughes, 37 (orthodontist), died August 2015 in similar circumstances at a lake near his pontoon boat — investigators believe he was shocked before he drowned.

## Notes
Medical Examiner ruled direct electrocution (not drowning), so may be classified differently from typical ESD. However, the mechanism (dock electrification causing victim to enter electrified water) is the same. The ESDPA newsletter (April 20, 2017) explicitly references this incident in connection with Carmen Johnson advocacy.

## Related Incident — Dr. Eric Hughes (August 2015)
Tuscaloosa County Homicide Capt. Kip Hart mentioned at the press conference: "we had one a couple of years ago back August of 2015... a doctor... his cause of death was not ruled electrocution but we do believe that electrical current played a part." The victim is identified in separate sources as Dr. Eric Hughes, 37 (orthodontist), who "had gone swimming in the lake after cutting his grass one afternoon. His friends found him in about four feet of water near his pontoon boat. Investigators believe that he was shocked before he drowned." This is a separate, potentially unreported incident from August 2015 in the Lake Tuscaloosa area.
