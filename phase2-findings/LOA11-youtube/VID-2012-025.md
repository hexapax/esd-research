# VID-2012-025: Alexandra & Brayden Anderson + Jennifer Lankford — Lake of the Ozarks, Missouri (Three Deaths in Four Days)

**Description:** Three separate electrocution deaths at Lake of the Ozarks within four days, covered extensively by national news.

**INCIDENT 1 (July 4):** Alexandra Anderson, 13, and her 8-year-old brother Brayden Anderson, of Ashland, Missouri, were swimming near their family's private dock in the Gravois Arm at the 6.5-mile marker when electricity from a faulty dock wiring system entered the water. Adults who jumped in to help also felt the current. The children were pulled out but pronounced dead at Lake Regional Hospital in Osage Beach. The dock had no ground fault circuit interrupter (GFCI) as required; the electrical fault may have come from a boat lift or water slide pump. Their mother, Angela Anderson, later became a prominent advocate for dock safety legislation at the Missouri legislature, suing Ameren (which owns the lake) and lobbying for GFCI requirements.

**INCIDENT 2 (July 7):** Jennifer Lankford, 26, of Hazelwood, Missouri, was swimming with two stepbrothers near a dock in Dry Branch Cove (approximately 5-mile marker near Osage Beach, Morgan County) when all three felt an electrical current in the water. The stepbrothers swam away from the dock; Lankford swam toward it and was electrocuted when she touched it. She died. The dock had several electrical problems.

## Notes
These are among the most documented ESD incidents in the United States — they triggered legislation attempts in Missouri, lawsuits against Ameren (which went to the Missouri Supreme Court), and generated sustained news coverage. Angela Anderson became the face of Missouri dock safety reform. The ESDPA list contains both incidents. The July 23, 2012 Lake of the Ozarks near-miss (two children and one adult shocked but survived, citing dock under repair) is a fourth event in the same summer. The ABC News YouTube video (Dst7CW2PNxc) explicitly covers both the Tennessee and Missouri deaths on the same day, making it one of the most important ESD awareness videos.
