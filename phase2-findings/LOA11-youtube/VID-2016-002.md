# VID-2016-002: Jim Tramel — YouTube Discovery

| **Date** | March 27, 2016 (Easter Sunday) |
| **Location** | Palm Springs, California (private vacation home pool, Indian Canyons neighborhood, Yosemite Drive) |
| **Classification** | Fatal ESD — pool electrocution |
| **Outcome** | Dead: 1 (James "Jim" Tramel, age 43, VP Sales at RevJet, Burlingame/Hillsborough CA), Injured: 6 others (including two daughters in critical/stable condition) |

**Description:** James Tramel, a Silicon Valley tech executive, was spending Easter with family at his Palm Springs vacation home. During an afternoon pool party with 7 total swimmers, a faulty pool light fixture (missing screws, likely had a power surge) electrified the water. His 9-year-old daughter sank to the bottom turning blue. Tramel dove in to save her and immediately lost consciousness from the electrical shock. His 10-year-old daughter also suffered serious injuries. Police received calls that "swimmers were turning blue." Tramel was pulled from the pool and transported to Desert Regional Medical Center where he was pronounced dead. His younger daughter was in critical condition at Loma Linda University Medical Center. The other five swimmers (including Tramel's wife) were treated at the scene. The homes in the area were built in the 1960s with old electrical systems.
