# VID-2016-003: Rachel Rosoff — YouTube Discovery

## Incident Summary
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Date** | September 3, 2016 |
| **Location** | Heritage Point community pool, Valley Forge Road, Raleigh (Heritage Point neighborhood), Wake County, North Carolina |
| **Classification** | Fatal ESD — pool electrocution |
| **Outcome** | Dead: 1 (Rachel Rosoff, age 17, senior at Enloe High School), Injured: 0 (rescuer felt shock but not seriously injured) |

**Description:** Rachel Rosoff, a 17-year-old lifeguard at Heritage Point community pool, was working alone when she apparently entered the pool to check chemicals, grabbing a metal bar/ladder for support. The pool pump motor had failed and, because the grounding conductor had corroded and broken (installed in 1979, never inspected for electricity), the fault current traveled into the water instead of tripping the breaker. Rachel was knocked unconscious by the electrical shock and drowned. She was found floating face-down by a fellow employee arriving for work. The caller to 911 said: "The pool water seems to be electrified in some sense." A preliminary autopsy confirmed electrocution followed by drowning. Investigation revealed a corroded wire from 1979 (never inspected electrically during county pool inspections) was to blame. Rachel's mother Michelle Rosoff became a major advocate for electrical pool inspection laws in North Carolina.
