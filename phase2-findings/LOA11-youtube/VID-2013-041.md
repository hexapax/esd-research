# VID-2013-041: Raul Hernandez Martinez — Houston Hilton Hotel Pool Electrocution

## Incident Summary
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Date** | August 31, 2013 |
| **Location** | Houston, Harris County, Texas (Hilton Houston Westchase, 9999 Westheimer Road) |
| **Classification** | Fatal electrocution (pool) |
| **Outcome** | Dead: 1 (Raul Hernandez Martinez, age 27), Injured: 2+ (mother Maria Isabel Duran; 11-year-old brother David) |

**Description:** Raul Hernandez, 27, was swimming with his family at the Hilton Westchase outdoor pool over Labor Day weekend. At dusk the pool lights came on and people began complaining of shocks. His 11-year-old brother David began convulsing in the deep end. His mother Maria Isabel Duran tried to help and was shocked unconscious. Raul dove in and pushed his brother to the edge where bystanders pulled him out, but Raul could not escape the energized water. He was pulled out unresponsive and taken to hospital, where he died on September 6 after nearly a week of ICU care. A subsequent investigation confirmed the pool lacked GFCI protection on pool lighting, which had recently been wired improperly by contractor Brown Electric. Two electricians (Jason Gorczyca and James Pyle) were charged with criminally negligent homicide. Both lost their electrician licenses. A civil settlement was reached in April 2015 with Hilton Hotels and Brown Electric, with Hernandez's brother reportedly receiving at least $2.2 million.
