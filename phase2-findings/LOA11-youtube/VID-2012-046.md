# VID-2012-046: Robert Stoen — Minnesota Pokegama Lake Boat Lift Electrocution

## Incident Summary
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Date** | July 25, 2012 |
| **Location** | Pokegama Lake, Cohasset, Itasca County, Minnesota (Hill Top Trail) |
| **Classification** | Fatal ESD — extension cord fell from boat lift into water |
| **Outcome** | Dead: 1 (Robert Stoen, age 22, of Owatonna MN), Injured: 2 (Eric Hancock, 35, serious; Cheryl Hancock, 57, serious — both from Owatonna) |

**Description:** An extension cord fell into the water from a boat lift at a private dock on Pokegama Lake. Three people — Robert Stoen (22), Eric Hancock (35), and Cheryl Hancock (57) — were shocked. Robert Stoen was found unresponsive in the water and was pronounced dead at Grand Itasca Medical Center. Eric and Cheryl Hancock were listed in serious but stable condition at the hospital. The Itasca County sheriff's office was called to Hill Top Trail in Cohasset on reports of possibly shocked individuals. Authorities investigated the source as an extension cord that fell from the boat lift structure into the water. This incident occurred less than a month after the July 4, 2012 Cherokee Lake (Tennessee) deaths and was part of a summer cluster of ESD fatalities that drew national attention.

## Notes
Minnesota had at least two fatal ESD incidents in 2012-2013 (Pokegama Lake and Eagle Lake), neither of which is widely known outside Minnesota. This case lacks dedicated YouTube video coverage, unlike the Dan Peterson/Eagle Lake case. Worth noting that Owatonna, Minnesota is 2+ hours from the lake — the victims were likely visiting. The combination of two Minnesota incidents within 10 months strongly suggests the state's lake culture creates routine ESD risk that is underdocumented.
