# VID-2018-001: Wesley Seeley — YouTube Discovery

## Incident Summary
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Date** | September 30, 2018 |
| **Location** | Bricktown Canal, 101 South Mickey Mantle Drive, Oklahoma City, Oklahoma |
| **Classification** | Fatal electrocution (waterway) |
| **Outcome** | Dead: 1 (Wesley Seeley, age 23, of Tuttle, Oklahoma), Injured: 1 (Brandon Gann, age 31, of Crawford County/Van Buren Arkansas — shocked, hospitalized, medically induced coma, eventually recovered) |

**Description:** Wesley Seeley fell into the Bricktown Canal while apparently leaning against or pulling on a small decorative lamp post (called a "bollard") along the canal walk, possibly posing for a photograph. The bollard fell into the water with Seeley, and exposed wires electrocuted him. Brandon Gann (who had gotten married the day before) jumped into the canal to save Seeley and was also severely shocked. Firefighters pulled both men from the canal and took them to hospitals. Seeley was pronounced dead at OU Medical Center. Gann was in a medically induced coma and later recovered. Oklahoma City released video of the incident; inspection found 16 bollards were loose and 2 were beyond repair. A subsequent jury verdict (November 2021) found Libra Electric Company 75% responsible and Oklahoma City 25% responsible. The Seeley family was awarded $3 million; Gann and his wife were awarded $5.5 million.
