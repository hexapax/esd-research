# VID-2023-048: New Rochelle Marina Near-Miss — New York Energized Dock Railing

## Incident Summary
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Date** | July 9, 2023 |
| **Location** | Marina, New Rochelle, Westchester County, New York |
| **Classification** | Near Miss — electric shock at energized marina railing |
| **Outcome** | Dead: 0, Injured: 2 (man and woman, both pulled from water unconscious; survived) |

**Description:** A man and a woman were heading to a jet ski at a New Rochelle marina when both touched an energized railing. Police believe the railing was conducting electric current, possibly due to storm-related damage. Both victims were found unconscious near the dock — the woman came to quickly while the man was face down in the water, spitting up a lot of water when revived. A bystander at the marina (Anthony Giacobbe) spotted them and used a net to pull the woman out; other members helped pull the man out. Both survived. The ESDPA listed this on their press releases as: "2 shocked after boat ramp at New Rochelle marina becomes electrified."

## Notes
New York State is underrepresented in ESD databases. This New Rochelle near-miss shows ESD risk in suburban NYC waterways. No fatality, but both victims were unconscious in the water — the outcome could easily have been fatal. The storm-related electrical fault triggering an energized railing is a documented ESD hazard pattern. ESDPA noted it in their press releases. The ABC7 NY coverage is primarily web-article format without a confirmed standalone YouTube video. Further investigation of the marina's identity could help establish whether the source was marina wiring or a neighboring boat. New York has extensive waterfront real estate with many privately wired docks, suggesting systematic underreporting.
