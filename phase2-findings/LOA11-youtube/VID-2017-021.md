# VID-2017-021: Evan Currie — Put-in-Bay, Ohio (Miller Marina, Lake Erie)

## Incident Summary
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Date** | June 16, 2017 |
| **Location** | Put-in-Bay, Ottawa County, Ohio — Miller Marina, South Bass Island, western Lake Erie |
| **Classification** | Fatal ESD |
| **Outcome** | Dead: 1 (Evan T. Currie, 19), Injured: 2 (Jeffrey Currie — father, and younger brother, both shocked but survived) |

**Description:** Evan Currie, 19, a Xavier University sophomore from Dublin, Ohio, was at Miller Marina with his family aboard their 33-foot Sea Ray Sundancer. After plugging the boat into shore power at approximately 6:30 p.m., the family dog Daisy fell into the water and began struggling. Evan's father Jeffrey jumped in to rescue the dog and immediately felt a strong electric shock ("like a stun gun"). Evan and his younger brother then jumped in to help their father. Both boys were shocked. Their mother, still aboard, heard bystanders shouting to unplug the shore power; when she did, the electrical current stopped. Jeffrey and the younger brother returned to the boat, but Evan was unresponsive and convulsing. CPR was performed and the family transported him to Port Clinton, but he died at the scene. ODNR dispatched an independent electrician who examined the dock and found no problems with its ground wires; the electrical current is believed to have come from the boat's own shore power connection. ODNR called it the first electric shock drowning investigated in Ohio.
