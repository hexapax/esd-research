# VID-2015-022: Marcus Colburn — Lake of the Ozarks, Missouri

## Incident Summary
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Date** | June 21, 2015 |
| **Location** | Woods Hollow Cove, 22.2 Mile Marker, Lake of the Ozarks, Morgan County, Missouri |
| **Classification** | Fatal ESD |
| **Outcome** | Dead: 1 (Marcus Colburn, 21), Injured: 0 (fellow swimmer Taylor Curley escaped after power cut) |

**Description:** Marcus Colburn, 21, of East Moline, Illinois, was swimming at a private dock at the 22.2 Mile Marker (Woods Hollow Cove) at approximately 9 p.m. with fellow swimmer Taylor Curley. Both felt electricity in the water near the dock. Colburn grabbed the dock ladder to exit the water; the ladder was energized and he was electrocuted and fell back into the water. Someone ran ashore and turned off the power at the residence, likely saving Curley. Colburn was recovered but could not be revived. A faulty junction box between the dock and residence was suspected; the circuit breaker would trip after 10-15 seconds of being turned on, and the last attempt to reset it coincided with the swimmers being near the dock as it got dark (the breaker controlled dock lights). The Missouri State Highway Patrol investigated and categorized the death as "electrical shock." The Osage Beach Fire Protection District removed electrical service to the dock. Colburn's death occurred three years after the 2012 Lake of the Ozarks deaths (Alexandra Anderson, Brayden Anderson, Jennifer Lankford), reigniting calls for dock safety legislation in Missouri.
