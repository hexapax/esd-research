# VID-2012-001: Noah Winstead & Nate Parker Lynam — YouTube Discovery

## Incident Summary
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Date** | July 4, 2012 |
| **Location** | Cherokee Lake, German Creek Marina, Bean Station, Tennessee |
| **Classification** | Fatal ESD |
| **Outcome** | Dead: 2 (Noah Winstead, age 10; Nate Parker Lynam, age 11), Injured: Multiple bystanders shocked |

**Description:** Ten-year-old Noah Winstead and his 11-year-old best friend Nate Parker Lynam were swimming between two docked houseboats at German Creek Marina on Cherokee Lake on the Fourth of July. Both boys suddenly cried for help. Witnesses say they were caught in electric current leaking from faulty wiring on one of the houseboats. Several bystanders jumped in to help and were also shocked. Noah died almost immediately; Nate was rushed to the hospital and died overnight. An 8-year-old girl also swimming was shocked but pulled to safety. Their deaths led to Tennessee's "Noah Dean and Nate Act" requiring safety signage and state inspections at public marinas.
