# VID-2017-027: Shelly Darling & Elizabeth Whipple — Lake Tuscaloosa, Alabama

## Incident Summary
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Date** | April 14, 2017 |

| **Classification** | Fatal ESD (officially ruled electrocution) |
| **Outcome** | Dead: 2 (Shelly Darling, 34; Elizabeth Whipple, 41), Injured: 0 |

**Description:** Shelly Darling, 34, and Elizabeth Whipple, 41, went missing Friday afternoon, April 14, 2017, after reportedly sunbathing on a private dock on Lake Tuscaloosa. Their bodies were found in the lake the next morning. At least one investigator felt an electrical shock on the dock during initial emergency response; police officers on a responding boat also received shocks as the boat approached the dock, and divers in the water noticed arcing between the police boat and the dock. Tuscaloosa County Metro Homicide Unit Captain Kip Hart stated the medical examiner ruled both deaths as "electrocution" (not ESD/drowning — the current was apparently strong enough to cause death directly from electrical exposure rather than secondary drowning). An autopsy was completed but toxicology was still pending at time of reporting. The dock had electrical power to a boat lift and electrical outlets, which investigators believe was the source. At the press conference, Captain Hart noted a prior incident in August 2015 at Lake Tuscaloosa where a man's death was suspected to involve electrical current but was officially ruled drowning.

## Notes
Alabama is not one of the 11 hotspot states in the brief, but this incident has excellent YouTube documentation via the AL.com press conference video, which provides the most detailed public statement on the cause of death. Notably, the cause was ruled electrocution (not ESD) because the current was strong enough to kill directly rather than cause drowning. Captain Hart referenced a prior 2015 Lake Tuscaloosa incident involving electrical current as a contributing factor in another man's death (ruled drowning). The 2017 deaths occurred one year after the Carmen Johnson ESD death at Smith Lake, AL — the Carmen Johnson deaths are covered by a separate WBRC YouTube video (kDeir78Xn1E) from May 2025, and a WBRC June 2024 video (gqqtwWZ0nrw) about another Smith Lake survivor, Dianne Guyton.
