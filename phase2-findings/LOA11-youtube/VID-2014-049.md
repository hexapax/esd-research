# VID-2014-049: Andrew Orvis — Lake Conroe Texas Dock Electrocution (Second Texas Incident)

## Incident Summary
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Date** | July 10, 2014 (July 28, 2014 per some sources — July 10 per lawsuit filing) |
| **Location** | Piney Shores Resort, Lake Conroe, Montgomery County, Texas (League Line Road) |
| **Classification** | Fatal ESD — dock wiring electrocution |
| **Outcome** | Dead: 1 (Andrew Orvis, age 8), Injured: 0 |

**Description:** Eight-year-old Andrew Orvis was sitting on a dock at Piney Shores Resort on Lake Conroe, sticking his feet in the water, when "he had an unusual movement and fell into the water." His father Gerald Orvis saw his son and pulled him from the lake and performed CPR, but Andrew was unresponsive. He was airlifted by Life Flight to Memorial Hermann Hospital where he was pronounced dead. Days after the accident, City of Conroe inspectors discovered exposed, live electrical wires at the dock. The Harris County Medical Examiner determined the primary cause of death was electrocution (before he fell into the water). The family sued Piney Shores Resort and its parent company Silverleaf Resorts for wrongful death, alleging poor maintenance of electrical wiring. Silverleaf counter-sued the father for lack of supervision, which was contested. The resort had no comment. The lawsuit was filed in Dallas County (cause CC-14-03837-C) because Silverleaf Resorts was headquartered in Dallas.

## Notes
This is the second confirmed Texas lake dock ESD fatality (alongside the 2020 Harris County pool incident, which is a pool rather than lake). Lake Conroe is one of the most popular recreational lakes in Texas (near Houston). This incident received KHOU and KTRK/ABC13 coverage and was a central example in the subsequent wrongful death litigation. The Texas ESD gap (only 2 known incidents for 30M+ population) now has three confirmed incidents: this Lake Conroe case (2014), the Houston Hilton pool (2013), and the North Villa Inn pool (2020). All three are in the greater Houston area. The lake dock pattern (Andrew Orvis) is the most relevant to classic ESD risk.
