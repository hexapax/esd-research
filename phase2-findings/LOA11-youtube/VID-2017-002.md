# VID-2017-002: Kayla Matos — YouTube Discovery

| **Date** | June 17–18, 2017 |

| **Classification** | Fatal ESD — boat lift electrocution |
| **Outcome** | Dead: 1 (Kayla Matos, age 10–11, of Newark, NJ), Injured: 1 friend (evaluated at scene, not seriously injured) |

**Description:** Kayla Matos (10, also reported as 11) of Newark was visiting a friend's home in Toms River, NJ. She and two friends were on an inflatable raft in the lagoon wearing life jackets and with adult supervision. When Kayla got out of the raft and touched the metal rail of a boat lift, an electrical current from corroded wiring in the junction box ran through the equipment and electrocuted her. She was taken to Community Medical Center in Toms River where she died. The boat lift had been installed and inspected in 2001; current homeowners had purchased the home in 2004 and never used the boat lift. Over years of weather exposure, the wire inside the junction box had corroded. Her friend who also touched the rail was reportedly uninjured.
