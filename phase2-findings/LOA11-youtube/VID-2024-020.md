# VID-2024-020: Jesse Hamric — Smith Mountain Lake, Virginia

## Incident Summary
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Date** | July 4, 2024 |
| **Location** | Huddleston (Bedford County), Virginia — Smith Mountain Lake, private dock on Lee Drive |
| **Classification** | Fatal ESD / In-Water Electrocution |
| **Outcome** | Dead: 1 (Jesse Hamric, 18), Injured: 2 (two friends shocked while attempting rescue) |

**Description:** Jesse Hamric, an 18-year-old recent graduate of Steamboat Springs High School (Colorado) and son of school principal Jay Hamric, was visiting Smith Mountain Lake with family and friends on July 4 when he jumped into the water near a private dock. Witnesses reported he immediately went into distress. Two friends jumped in to help him and immediately felt electrical shocks. They were able to pull Hamric out and begin CPR before rescue arrived. The Smith Mountain Lake Marine Fire and Rescue Dive Team responded around 4 a.m. and confirmed electrical current at the dock. Hamric was transported to hospital and pronounced dead. The Bedford County Sheriff's Office determined the electrocution was caused by stray voltage at a private residential boat dock. The two rescuers were treated and released at scene. Hamric was described as a former football quarterback and baseball player at Steamboat Springs High School.
