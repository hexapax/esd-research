# VID-2021-047: James DeAngelo — Monongahela River, Pennsylvania Possible ESD

## Incident Summary
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Date** | July 4, 2021 |
| **Location** | Beach Club Marina, Monongahela River, New Eagle, Washington County, Pennsylvania |
| **Classification** | Suspected Fatal ESD / drowning near marina |
| **Outcome** | Dead: 1 (James DeAngelo, age 23, of Venetia PA), Injured: 1 (unnamed 23-year-old friend, felt severe shock, hospitalized) |

**Description:** James DeAngelo, 23, a Peters Township High School 2016 graduate and former Cal U student who played hockey, was at the Beach Club Marina on the Monongahela River on July 4 for a celebration. He was swimming in the river near a green-and-white houseboat docked at the marina when he was found unresponsive around 7:30 pm and pulled from the water. He was pronounced dead at Monongahela Valley Hospital at 8:18 pm. A friend who put on a life jacket and jumped in to search for DeAngelo told officers he felt "horribly shocked." Monongahela Police Chief Kevin Harris said they received reports of possible electrocution concerns and "received various views of what individuals saw." The Fish and Boat Commission and Allegheny Health Network contributed to the investigation. Cause of death was not determined immediately. The investigation into ESD was ongoing. His death inspired the James DeAngelo Foundation for ESD awareness and a Pennsylvania state bill (introduced 2025 by Senators Costa and Bartolotta) requiring ESD safety features at docks and marinas.

## Notes
Pennsylvania is severely underrepresented in ESD databases. DeAngelo's case became a landmark for PA ESD advocacy, resulting in a 2025 state senate bill. The James DeAngelo Foundation continues to operate. ESDPA listed this in press releases (July 7, 2021) but the cause of death was not officially confirmed as ESD. The friend's first-person account of being "horribly shocked" in the same water is strong corroborating evidence. Two WTAE-TV YouTube videos document this incident with full transcripts. Pennsylvania now has pending legislation that would require ESD protection features at marinas.
