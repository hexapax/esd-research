# VID-2014-043: Alec McQueen — Lake Powell Marina Electrocution

## Incident Summary
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Date** | June 10, 2014 |
| **Location** | Bullfrog Marina, Lake Powell, Kane County, Utah (Arizona-Utah border) |
| **Classification** | Fatal ESD / electrocution in water |
| **Outcome** | Dead: 1 (Alec McQueen, age 22), Injured: 1+ (friend Shaley Eiden, shocked in arm; 2 first responders shocked) |

**Description:** Alec McQueen, 22, of Salt Lake City, was working at Bullfrog Marina for the summer with two female friends. When their boat docked around 7 pm, McQueen jumped into the lake from the swim deck. His friend Shaley Eiden heard him call her name, turned, and saw him briefly in the water before he disappeared. Eiden put her arm in the water to search and received an electrical shock. Two first responders who arrived were also shocked when they touched the water. McQueen's body was not immediately found due to 90+ foot depths and zero visibility; he was recovered 113 feet below the surface two days later. A federal lawsuit filed in 2015 alleged that a neighboring boater had plugged a 30-amp cord into a 50-amp outlet (a "wrong size" fit made possible because the outlet had worn down and safety features were bypassed), causing electricity to discharge into the water. The marina operator Aramark and the neighboring boater were named as defendants.
