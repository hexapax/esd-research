# VID-2025-023: Gabriel Gonzalez — Marina Shores, Portage, Indiana

**Description:** Gabriel Gonzalez, 21, of Hebron, Indiana, was at Marina Shores Marina on July 10, 2025, cleaning a boat with his brother Francis Gonzalez, 23. An object belonging to a nearby boat owner fell from D dock into the water and began floating away. Gabriel voluntarily jumped in to retrieve the object for its owner. After retrieving it, he swam back toward D dock and appears to have entered an electrical field near the dock, going under the water's surface. Francis saw his brother go under and immediately jumped in to help, but was also electrocuted. A bystander threw Francis a flotation device, which likely saved him; he remained on the surface and was pulled to safety and taken to hospital. Gabriel went below the surface and was not found. A Portage police officer arrived at 11:07 a.m. and boarded a boat to help locate Gabriel. Once the water was confirmed to be safe, Portage Fire Department dive team entered and recovered Gabriel at 11:41 a.m. CPR was performed but he was pronounced dead at hospital. The Porter County Coroner ruled cause of death "drowning secondary to electrocution," accidental. Indiana DNR investigation with a licensed electrician concluded the shock was likely delivered by electrical equipment belonging to a nearby boat owner. A wrongful death lawsuit was subsequently filed by Schafer & Schafer LLP on behalf of the Gonzalez family, alleging failure to post mandated warnings required by National Electrical Code Article 555.24.
