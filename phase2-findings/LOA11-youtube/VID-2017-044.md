# VID-2017-044: Kayla Matos — Toms River, New Jersey Dock Electrocution

## Incident Summary
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Date** | June 17, 2017 (Saturday evening, ~8 pm) |
| **Location** | Toms River, Ocean County, New Jersey (Tobago Avenue / Shelter Cove lagoon) |
| **Classification** | Fatal ESD — dock boat lift electrocution |
| **Outcome** | Dead: 1 (Kayla Matos, age 11, of Newark NJ), Injured: 0 (two friends evaluated, not hurt) |

**Description:** Kayla Matos, an 11-year-old girl from Newark, was visiting friends at a home on Tobago Avenue in Toms River's Shelter Cove waterfront neighborhood. She and two friends were using an inflatable raft and swimming in the lagoon behind the home under adult supervision, wearing life jackets. Two of the girls touched the metal rail of a boat lift attached to the dock, and an electric current energized the equipment. Kayla was electrocuted; nothing happened to her friend who also touched it. Adults at the scene began CPR; arriving EMS used an AED and transported Kayla to Community Medical Center in Toms River, where she died. The boat lift had been installed and inspected in 2001; the current homeowners bought the home in 2004 and did not own a boat, so had never used the lift. Investigation revealed that the wiring inside the junction box had corroded over time. Toms River Mayor called for residents to have boat lifts inspected by a qualified electrician. The ESDPA stated the energized water could not be detected visually.

## Notes
New Jersey is severely underrepresented in ESD databases despite its extensive waterfront communities. This is a fully documented, well-covered case with multiple YouTube videos from New York/New Jersey market stations (CBS New York, wabc7). The corrosion-in-junction-box mechanism is textbook ESD — equipment that appears dormant can remain energized. The WCNC (Charlotte) 2017 awareness video also mentions Kayla's death as a warning case for the Carolinas market. The same weekend, a 19-year-old died in Put-in-Bay, Ohio, giving this moment national ESD attention.
