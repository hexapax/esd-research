# VID-2012-026: Noah Dean Winstead & Nate Lynam — Cherokee Lake, Tennessee

## Incident Summary
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Date** | July 4, 2012 |
| **Location** | German Creek Marina / Boat Dock, Cherokee Lake, Bean Station (Grainger County), Tennessee |
| **Classification** | Fatal ESD |
| **Outcome** | Dead: 2 (Noah Dean Winstead, 10; Nate Parker Lynam, 11), Injured: several (6+ adults and one 12-year-old who attempted rescue) |

**Description:** Noah Dean Winstead, 10, and his best friend Nate Parker Lynam, 11, were swimming between two houseboats at German Creek Marina on Cherokee Lake on the Fourth of July afternoon when they encountered an electrical current in the water. Both were electrocuted; Noah died at the scene and Nate was placed on life support, dying the following day. Six adults and a 12-year-old boy who jumped in to help were also shocked. An 8-year-old girl who was also swimming with the boys survived after being pulled to safety at a different dock. Faulty wiring on one of the houseboats (a shore cord) is believed to be the cause. This tragedy directly led to the Tennessee "Noah Dean and Nate Act" (2014), signed by Governor Bill Haslam, requiring inspections of all public marinas and docks in Tennessee by the State Fire Marshal's Office. Noah's mother, Jessica Winstead McClure, became a prominent ESD awareness advocate.

## Notes
These deaths are foundational to ESD awareness in Tennessee. The "Noah Dean and Nate Act" (2014) is the single most important state-level ESD legislation passed in the US. Multiple YouTube videos document this incident from different angles over a 10-year span. Jessica Winstead McClure (Noah's mother) has been interviewed many times and is the primary Tennessee ESD spokesperson. Nate's correct last name is Lynam (not Lyman or Linum as some sources report). The 8-year-old girl who survived is not named in public records. Six adult rescuers plus a 12-year-old were shocked.
