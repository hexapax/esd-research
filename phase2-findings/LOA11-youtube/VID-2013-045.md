# VID-2013-045: Daniel Peterson — Minnesota Eagle Lake Battery Charger Electrocution

## Incident Summary
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Date** | July 4, 2013 (death July 7, 2013) |
| **Location** | Eagle Lake (Orrick Township), Sherburne County, Minnesota (near Big Lake) |
| **Classification** | Fatal ESD — submerged battery charger |
| **Outcome** | Dead: 1 (Daniel "Dan" Peterson, age 46, of Big Lake), Injured: 1 (sister Kris Biser, age 49, shocked unconscious but survived) |

**Description:** On the Fourth of July, Dan Peterson was charging his boat battery while standing in approximately 20 inches of water at Eagle Lake. He was preparing to connect the charger to his houseboat. He lost his footing and fell while holding the plugged-in battery charger, immersing it. The electric field immediately paralyzed his muscles. His sister Kris Biser witnessed the fall and ran into the water to help; she was also shocked unconscious. Neighbors pulled both from the water and performed CPR. A third person who ran in was not shocked. Kris was taken to St. Cloud Hospital breathing on her own. Dan was airlifted to Hennepin County Medical Center (North Memorial), where he died on July 7. Witnesses described seeing the two floating unresponsive when deputies arrived. Whether the charger was properly grounded was not determined. This case became a focal point for the WCCO/CBS Minnesota ESD awareness report broadcast in June 2016.

## Notes
Minnesota is severely underrepresented in ESD databases. This is the most prominent Minnesota ESD fatality found, with a dedicated 3:34 WCCO CBS Minnesota YouTube video featuring the neighbor's testimony, the emergency medicine perspective, and ESD expert Ed Lethert. The WCCO segment was broadcast three years after the incident as a summer safety warning. The incident involved a classic non-marina scenario: private houseboat, DIY battery charging. The ESDPA also lists this incident. Minnesota has another incident at Pokegama Lake (Cohasset, 2012: Robert Stoen) detailed in VID-2012-046.
