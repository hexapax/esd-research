# VID-1999-001: Lucas Ritz — YouTube Discovery

## Incident Summary
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Date** | August 1, 1999 |
| **Location** | Scappoose, Oregon (marina on tributary of Willamette River near Portland) |
| **Classification** | Fatal ESD |
| **Outcome** | Dead: 1 (Lucas Ritz, age 8), Injured: 1 (Sheryl Ritz, his mother — paralyzed in water, survived) |

**Description:** Eight-year-old Lucas Ritz was swimming with his brother and friends at a freshwater marina where his family lived aboard their sailboat. As he approached a dock ladder to exit the water, he gasped and rolled onto his back unconscious. His mother Sheryl jumped in to rescue him and experienced limb paralysis from electric shock. Lucas was pulled out and CPR performed for ~20 minutes but he was pronounced dead at Portland's Emanuel Hospital at 6:30 p.m. Kevin Ritz's subsequent investigation found a 12V wire lying on an AC wire that had melted its insulation on a nearby powerboat, leaking 120V AC into the water. The coroner initially ruled it drowning; Kevin fought for electrocution to be recognized, eventually founding the Electric Shock Drowning Prevention Association (ESDPA).
