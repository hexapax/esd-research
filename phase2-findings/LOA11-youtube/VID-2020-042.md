# VID-2020-042: Timothy Miller & Michael Miller — Arizona Lake Pleasant Double Fatality

## Incident Summary
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Date** | July 12, 2020 |
| **Location** | Lake Pleasant, Peoria, Maricopa County, Arizona (Scorpion Bay Marina) |
| **Classification** | Fatal ESD / electrocution in water |
| **Outcome** | Dead: 2 (Timothy Miller, age 53; Michael Miller, age 50), Injured: 1 (Timothy's girlfriend, burns on legs/feet) |

**Description:** Brothers Timothy Miller (53) and Michael Miller (50) were celebrating an early birthday at Scorpion Bay Marina on Lake Pleasant when Timothy jumped into the water around 6:30 pm on July 12. He immediately experienced symptoms consistent with electrical current. Michael jumped in to help his brother and experienced the same. Michael and one of the wives who also entered the water were pulled out by bystanders; both experienced electric current symptoms (one with burns on feet and legs). Michael was transported to hospital where he died. Timothy's body was recovered underwater near the dock at approximately 1 a.m. on July 13 after the dock was cleared of electrical current. MCSO determined the source was the brothers' own boat, which had an incompatible electrical connection system that was modified in a way that bypassed safety features. However, the family's attorney disputed this, citing witness statements that implicated a cord from a neighboring boat and prior complaints about the marina's GFI circuits.

## Notes
Arizona had ZERO known ESD incidents before this case. This is the first-ever confirmed fatal ESD incident documented in Arizona — a double fatality making it especially significant. The ESDPA has it on their list but Arizona remains underrepresented in public awareness. The dispute over source (boat vs. marina wiring) is typical of ESD investigations. The family's attorney noted prior complaints about GFI circuits at the marina, suggesting systemic issues. Three YouTube videos document this incident with full transcripts.
