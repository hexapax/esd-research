# VID-2021-024: James DeAngelo — Monongahela River, Pennsylvania

## Incident Summary
| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Date** | July 4, 2021 |
| **Location** | Beach Club Marina, New Eagle, Washington County, Pennsylvania — Monongahela River |
| **Classification** | Suspected Fatal ESD / Drowning (ESD under investigation) |
| **Outcome** | Dead: 1 (James DeAngelo, 23), Injured: 1 (friend shocked while attempting rescue, hospitalized, survived) |

**Description:** James DeAngelo, 23, of Venetia, Pennsylvania (Peters Township HS 2016, attended California University of PA), was swimming in the Monongahela River near a green and white boat docked at the Beach Club Marina on the Fourth of July. He was found unresponsive around 7:30 p.m. and pulled from the water. A friend who saw DeAngelo in trouble put on a life jacket and jumped in to search for him; that friend was also electrocuted but survived because the life jacket kept him afloat until the electrical current pushed him out of range. He was hospitalized and told officers he had been "horribly shocked." Pennsylvania Boat and Fish Commission investigated the houseboat and its electrical systems the following day. WTAE Pittsburgh investigated ESD as a possible cause; the coroner was still investigating cause of death. DeAngelo was pronounced dead at Monongahela Valley Hospital at 8:18 p.m. He was reportedly engaged to his longtime girlfriend and was on the boat as a guest to celebrate the holiday.
