# SUSP-2004-044: Ten-Year-Old Boy Death — Lake Waccamaw, North Carolina — Multiple Children Shocked at Boat Lift, Adult Shocked on Contact

## Classification
- **ESD Probability**: HIGH
- **Incident Type**: Fatal drowning with simultaneous multi-person electrical sensation in water
- **File Number**: SUSP-2004-044
- **LOA Task**: LOA2-C

---

## Incident Summary

On June 19, 2004, a ten-year-old boy drowned while swimming with friends near a private dock boat lift at Lake Waccamaw, North Carolina. Multiple children in the water simultaneously reported being shocked, and an adult received a heavy shock upon touching the lift. The victim was found motionless and face-down in the water nearby and could not be revived. Investigation confirmed the boat lift frame had become energized due to a disconnected bonding conductor.

## HIGH-Probability ESD Indicators

### Indicator A: Multiple Persons Simultaneously Shocked in Same Body of Water
Multiple children swimming near the dock simultaneously reported feeling shocks in the water. This pattern — concurrent electrical sensations across multiple individuals in the same water at the same time — is not consistent with ordinary drowning and is a defining characteristic of Electric Shock Drowning caused by voltage gradient in the water column.

### Indicator B: Adult Shocked on Contact with Dock Infrastructure
An adult reported receiving a "heavy shock" when touching the boat lift. This confirms active energization of the dock structure at the time children were in the water below.

### Indicator C: Electrical Fault Confirmed by Investigation
Post-incident investigation established that the boat lift frame had become energized. The bonding conductor from the supply panel to the lift was found to be disconnected. This disconnected bond allowed fault current to enter the water rather than return safely along the grounding path.

## Witness Accounts / Key Language

- "Several children in the water reported being shocked."

---

## Electrical Investigation Findings

- **Confirmed fault**: Boat lift frame energized at time of incident.
- **Root cause**: Bonding conductor from supply panel to lift was not connected.
- **Mechanism**: Disconnected bonding conductor created a path for fault current to flow through the water rather than along the grounding/bonding conductors; children in the water were exposed to voltage gradient sufficient to cause electrical shock and, in the victim's case, skeletal muscle paralysis leading to drowning.
