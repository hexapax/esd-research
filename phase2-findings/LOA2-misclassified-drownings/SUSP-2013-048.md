# SUSP-2013-048: Man Electrocuted at Boat Ladder — Lake Cumberland, Kentucky — June 30, 2013 — Multiple Others Felt Shocks Attempting Rescue

## Classification
- **ESD Probability**: HIGH
- **Incident Type**: Fatal drowning with multiple rescuers simultaneously shocked at same ladder
- **File Number**: SUSP-2013-048
- **LOA Task**: LOA2-C

---

## Incident Summary

On June 30, 2013, a man was electrocuted at a boat ladder at Lake Cumberland, Kentucky. Multiple other individuals who attempted to rescue him also felt shocks as they entered the water or contacted the ladder. The victim did not survive. The pattern — primary victim incapacitated at ladder, multiple would-be rescuers also shocked at the same location — is the classic ESD "chain rescue" signature, where electricity in the water incapacitates both the initial victim and those who attempt to help.

## HIGH-Probability ESD Indicators

### Indicator A: Multiple Persons Shocked at Same Ladder During Rescue Attempts
"Others felt shocks trying to help the man" — this is the ESD chain-rescue signature. When multiple individuals feel shocks upon entering water or contacting a ladder where a victim is already incapacitated, the water itself contains an electrical hazard. This pattern cannot occur in ordinary drowning — it requires an active voltage gradient in the water.

### Indicator B: Victim Incapacitated at Ladder — Consistent with ESD Paralysis
The victim was electrocuted at the boat ladder. ESD at a ladder is one of the most common ESD scenarios: current flows from boat/dock wiring through the ladder into the water; a swimmer climbing the ladder or swimming near it enters the voltage gradient and experiences skeletal muscle paralysis, preventing them from leaving the water.
