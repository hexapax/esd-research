## Key Finding: The Forensic ESD Documentation Gap

**The central finding of this search** is that ESD creates a near-invisible forensic signature. As confirmed by primary sources including the BoatUS article quoting Kevin Ritz, the ESDPA incident database, and the ECM Web "Case of the Hot Marina":

> "There is no post-mortem evidence that electric shock was the cause [of ESD]. Therefore, many of the fatalities listed below are only the *known* electric shock caused drownings, which were investigated because of circumstantial evidence — multiple deaths, eye witnesses, considerable distress, cries for help, shock sensation reported by rescuers."

This means:
- **Searches for autopsy red flags typically yield nothing** because ESD victims' autopsies show only drowning findings with no burns or electrical markers.
- **The exception is Joule marks**: One case (Lake Travis, 2004, SUSP-2004-001) was found where Joule marks (electrical contact points) appeared at autopsy on the right wrist and left leg/shin.
- **Death certificate reclassification**: The clearest reclassification is Lucas Ritz (1999, Portland OR) — already documented in known list. The "Hot Marina" case (SUSP-2004-002) was reclassified from drowning to ESD through electrical investigation, not forensics.

## Autopsy Red Flag Findings — Specific Analysis

### "No Water in Lungs" at Dock
**No cases found** where a coroner explicitly reported "no water in lungs" at an ESD dock/marina scene that triggered reclassification. General drowning medical literature notes 7-10% of drowning cases involve no aspiration (dry drowning / laryngospasm). No dock/marina-specific news coverage linked this autopsy finding to ESD reclassification. This is likely because: (a) ESD victims typically DO aspirate water after being paralyzed; (b) coroners do not routinely publish "dry drowning" findings in search-indexed media; (c) the connection between "dry drowning" and ESD is not part of standard coroner training.

### Electrical Burns at Autopsy
**One confirmed case**: Lake Travis, TX, August 8, 2004 (SUSP-2004-001) — Joule marks on right wrist, left leg/shin. Joule marks are localized heating patterns at electrical contact points that can survive water immersion. This is the only confirmed autopsy ESD marker found in this search.

Additional related case: Samantha Chipley (2005, Scott Creek Marina, Cave Run Lake, KY) — "another girl sustained burns on her legs while reaching into the water" from the boat. These were contact burns from a high-current source on the rescuer, not the ESD paralysis victim.

### Death Certificate Amendments / Reclassifications
**Confirmed reclassifications found**:
1. **Lucas Ritz (1999)** — Already in known list. Changed from "drowning" to "electrocuted in water while swimming." Father-driven reclassification.
2. **"Hot Marina" case (2004)** — SUSP-2004-002. Initially ruled drowning ("no physical trauma"), reclassified to ESD after electrical investigation revealed miswired shore power.
3. **Darling & Whipple (2017)** — SUSP-2017-042. Initial "possible drowning" → days-later autopsy returned "electrocution." Not a years-later reclassification but a same-week correction.
4. **Knudsen (2011)** — SUSP-2011-041. Autopsy itself returned dual cause "asphyxia by drowning due to low voltage electrocution" — not reclassified from "pure drowning" but correctly identified as ESD from the outset.
5. **Gabriel Gonzalez (2025)** — SUSP-2025-001. Autopsy returned "drowning secondary to electrocution" — correctly identified as ESD from the start, demonstrating improved coroner ESD awareness.

**No cases found** of a death certificate amended years after the fact from pure "drowning" to "electrocution" in a dock/marina setting, beyond the Lucas Ritz case already in the known list.

## Pattern Analysis

The search revealed a consistent ESD detection pattern with three failure modes:

**Failure Mode 1 — No investigation**: Most ESD deaths at private docks/marinas are ruled simple accidental drowning with no electrical investigation because there are no witnesses to the electrical field. These remain permanently misclassified.

**Failure Mode 2 — Investigation triggered by circumstance**: ESD is identified only when: (a) a rescuer is also shocked (becomes a second victim or survives and reports it); (b) multiple simultaneous deaths occur; (c) a family member with electrical expertise (like Kevin Ritz) demands investigation; or (d) a responding officer/diver feels current at the scene.

**Failure Mode 3 — Correct identification but wrong label**: Cases like Gonzalez (2025) and Knudsen (2011) are correctly identified as electrically-caused drowning but may be indexed in vital statistics as "drowning" without the electrocution component — perpetuating undercounting.

## Recommendations for Further Research

1. **SUSP-2004-001 follow-up**: The Lake Travis 2004 Joule marks case warrants direct inquiry to the Travis County Medical Examiner's office. This is the only case found with an actual autopsy ESD marker (Joule marks). No news coverage naming the victim was found.

2. **Lake Cumberland KY, May 2002**: Double drowning (mother and daughter); houseboat with shore cord rewired for 220V, ground cut. High ESD probability. Check if a SUSP file already exists; if not, warrants one.

3. **Bay Haven Marina, Park Township MI, July 1988**: Referenced in ESDPA as "18-year-old boy falls off dock, in great distress, two attempts to assist thwarted because of severe electric shock as rescuers entered water." Verify whether this is the Bryan Higgins case in the known-reclassifications list.

4. **Oklahoma 1989-1993 series**: ESDPA lists multiple Oklahoma drownings (1989 submersible pump, 1991 dock wiring, 1993 dock lights). Oklahoma vital records research may reveal whether any death certificates were amended.

5. **Systematic coroner survey**: The search confirmed that the primary ESD forensic detection challenge is coroner unfamiliarity. The Lucas Ritz example — where the coroner's office said "we would not know how to test for something like that" in 1999 — illustrates the systemic gap. A survey of freshwater state coroner offices' ESD training status would be a high-value LOA4/LOA5 task.
