# SUSP-2020-001: Timothy & Michael Miller — Lake Pleasant / Scorpion Bay Marina Double Death

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Date** | July 12, 2020 |
| **Location** | Scorpion Bay Marina, Lake Pleasant, Peoria, AZ (Maricopa County) |
| **Victim** | Timothy Miller, 53, male (drowned); Michael Miller, 50, male (died at hospital) |
| **Initial Ruling** | "Drowning" with note that "there may be an electrical issue that contributed as a factor" |
| **Final Ruling** | Deaths caused by electrical current from boat; electrocution; "drownings" per MCSO but attributed to electrical fault |
| **ESD Probability** | HIGH (>70%) |
| **ESDPA Match** | In ESDPA incident list (July 12, 2020, Peoria AZ entry) |

## What Happened
On July 12, 2020 at approximately 6:30 p.m., brothers Timothy Miller (53) and Michael Miller (50), along with their wives, were aboard their boat docked at Scorpion Bay Marina at Lake Pleasant, Arizona. Timothy jumped into the water from their swim platform and immediately experienced what was described as "some type of electrical current inside the water." Michael then jumped in to help his brother and felt the same sensation. One of the wives also jumped in and experienced the same symptoms.

Bystanders were able to pull Michael and the wife out of the water, but Timothy could not be found. Timothy's body was located by divers under the dock around 1:00 a.m. on July 13. Michael was transported to a hospital where he died. The wife had burn marks on her feet and legs and survived.

MCSO initially called the cases drownings while noting possible electrical involvement. By July 15, MCSO confirmed the source: "The victim's boat had an electrical connection system which was not compatible with the marina's receptacle. Electrical safety features were bypassed by the intentional and improper modification of the boat's electrical connection system." This discharged electricity into the water around the swim platform. The marina's electrical system was separately confirmed to be operating properly.

The family's attorney disputed MCSO's finding, stating there were prior complaints about the marina's electrical circuits and that a neighboring boat's power cord was observed in the water.

## ESD Indicators Present
- **STRONGEST — Multiple simultaneous victims at same marina slip**: Two brothers and one wife all experienced electrical symptoms in the same water simultaneously. Timothy drowned; Michael died; wife survived with burns.
- **STRONGEST — Rescue attempt death**: Michael jumped in to rescue Timothy and was himself fatally electrocuted — the defining ESD rescue death pattern.
- **STRONGEST — Witnesses report tingling/shock/paralysis**: Multiple victims reported feeling electrical current in the water. Wife had electrical burns on feet and legs.
- **MODERATE — Near boat connected to shore power**: The boat was plugged into marina shore power, the exact ESD configuration.
- **MODERATE — Body found under dock**: Timothy's body was found under the dock, requiring the dock to be secured before retrieval — indicating live electrical current prevented safe recovery.

## Notes
This case is notable as an early-identified ESD event (initial response acknowledged possible electrical involvement within hours) but the official ruling went through a transition: initially called "drowning" while electrical factor was investigated, then electrically attributed once MCSO testing confirmed it. The wife's "burn marks on feet and legs" is forensically significant — she apparently received a higher-amperage shock because she was partially in contact with the boat/platform (entry/exit point), leaving visible burns, while the brothers who were fully immersed in the freshwater field showed no burns (classic ESD pattern in victims fully immersed). The family attorney's counter-narrative about a neighboring boat's cord in the water remains unresolved publicly. The ESDPA incident list includes this case.
