# SUSP-2007-052: Woman Paralyzed at Boat Ladder — Lake of the Ozarks, Missouri — July 28, 2007 — "Several People Had Reported Being Shocked by the Ladder"

## Classification
- **ESD Probability**: HIGH

- **File Number**: SUSP-2007-052
- **LOA Task**: LOA2-C

## Incident Summary

On July 28, 2007, a woman was paralyzed at a boat ladder at Lake of the Ozarks, Missouri. Prior to this incident, "several people had reported being shocked by the ladder." The combination of prior multi-person shock reports at the same ladder and a subsequent paralysis event is a HIGH-probability ESD pattern: the ladder was an ongoing electrical hazard that had shocked multiple people before finally causing paralysis.

## HIGH-Probability ESD Indicators

### Indicator A: Prior Multi-Person Shock Reports at Same Ladder
"Several people had reported being shocked by the ladder" prior to the paralysis event. Multiple people reporting shocks at the same location establishes that the ladder was a persistent electrical hazard — not an isolated incident. This is the pre-warning pattern: multiple people felt the hazard before it became severe enough to paralyze someone.

### Indicator B: Woman Paralyzed at Ladder
Paralysis at a boat ladder — the specific physiological response to AC current causing skeletal muscle tetany — is the clinical signature of ESD. Paralysis at a ladder means the victim cannot climb out of the water; ESD victims who are paralyzed while in the water drown.

### Indicator C: Repeated Prior Shocks Ignored — Failure to Inspect
The fact that "several people" had previously reported shocks at this ladder, yet the hazard was not corrected before the paralysis event, indicates a failure of dock/marina safety inspection and maintenance. This is a recurring pattern in ESD incidents.

## Geographic Context

Lake of the Ozarks is one of the most heavily boated lakes in Missouri, with thousands of private docks, marinas, and boat facilities. The lake was developed beginning in the 1930s; much of its dock electrical infrastructure is old and has never been systematically inspected. Missouri was identified as a research focus area in this LOA2-C investigation (see also SUSP-2006-043, Summerset Lake MO, and SUSP-2015-054).
