# SUSP-2004-001: Lake Travis Marina Drowning with Joule Marks at Autopsy

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Date** | August 8, 2004 |

| **Initial Ruling** | Drowning (presumed) |
| **Final Ruling** | Suspected ESD — "Accidental under investigation"; Joule marks found at autopsy |
| **ESD Probability** | HIGH (>70%) |
| **ESDPA Match** | In ESDPA incident list (Aug 8, 2004 entry, multiple versions) |

## What Happened
A young man in good health was swimming in the evening between two sections of a marina dock at Lake Travis, Austin, Texas. He disappeared under the water and was not recovered until two days later when his body came to the surface. The post mortem found no toxic substances. However, the autopsy revealed Joule marks (electrical contact points) on the right wrist and left leg and shin. These are classic markers of electrical current entry/exit points on skin. The case was listed as suspected electric shock drowning under investigation. Location: Austin, TX freshwater marina, shore power present.

## ESD Indicators Present
- **STRONGEST — Electrical burns found at autopsy**: Joule marks (electrical contact points) found on right wrist, left leg, and shin. This is a form of localized electrical burn identifying current path entry/exit. Extremely rare in standard drowning; strongly indicates electrical involvement.
- **MODERATE — Exceptional swimmer / healthy young man**: Described as "young man, in good health," no explanation for why he would drown while swimming in a marina.
- **MODERATE — Toxicology negative**: "No toxic substance found on post mortem."
- **MODERATE — Near boats connected to shore power**: Disappeared between two sections of marina dock where shore power was present.
- **MODERATE — Unobserved sudden disappearance**: Went under without witnesses being able to intervene — consistent with sudden ESD paralysis.

## Notes
The Joule marks on the right wrist and left leg/shin are the most significant finding. Joule marks are areas of focal heating caused by electrical current passing through tissue, leaving characteristic contact points. Their presence at two distinct body locations (wrist and leg/shin) suggests the body was the conductor between two points of electrical potential — exactly the mechanism of ESD when a swimmer is between an energized object and a ground path. The ESDPA listed this as "suspected ESD, accident under investigation." No subsequent public report of the investigation's conclusion was found. The case is not listed as "No ESDPA match" since it IS in the ESDPA database, but is not already documented in the known reclassifications list. The presence of Joule marks at autopsy is a forensic ESD indicator that goes beyond the usual "no post-mortem evidence" finding typical of ESD.
