# SUSP-1999-050: Double Child Drowning — Rio Vista, California — ~1999 — Older Boys Felt Tingle and Exited; Two Younger Boys Drowned at Same Spot; 40-Year-Old Wiring Fault

## Classification
- **ESD Probability**: HIGH
- **Incident Type**: Double fatality; older children warned of tingle but younger children drowned at same location
- **File Number**: SUSP-1999-050
- **LOA Task**: LOA2-C

---

## Incident Summary

Around 1999 in Rio Vista, California (Sacramento-San Joaquin Delta region), two young boys drowned at a dock location where older boys had previously felt a tingle and exited the water. Investigation revealed a wiring fault approximately 40 years old — meaning the dock had been energizing the surrounding water for decades before the fatal incident. The prior tingle reports from older boys who successfully exited constitutes direct warning evidence that the water was electrified before the fatal drowning occurred.

## HIGH-Probability ESD Indicators

### Indicator A: Prior Tingle Reports at the Same Location — Confirmed Pre-Warning
Older boys who had been in the water at the same dock location felt a tingle and were able to exit. This established that the water at that location was electrically hazardous prior to the drowning event. The existence of tingle reports at the same spot as the subsequent fatal drowning is strong evidence that electricity in the water caused or contributed to the death of the younger boys who could not exit.

### Indicator B: 40-Year-Old Wiring Fault Confirmed
Investigation confirmed a wiring fault approximately 40 years old. This means the dock had been energizing the water for decades — the electrical hazard was persistent, not transient. A 40-year-old wiring fault in a dock is consistent with corroded conductors, failed insulation, and unmonitored stray current continuously entering the water.

### Indicator C: Two Victims — Both Unable to Exit Where Older Boys Succeeded
The two younger boys drowned at the exact location where older boys had felt tingling and successfully exited. The differential outcome between younger (smaller, less physically capable, more vulnerable to low-level current) and older children at the same electrically hazardous location is consistent with ESD: younger, smaller children require lower current levels to experience incapacitating paralysis.

- **Implication**: The water at this dock had been electrified for decades before the fatal drowning, creating a persistent, low-level voltage gradient that had previously caused tingle symptoms in swimmers and ultimately killed two young boys.
