## What Happened
On the morning of July 10, 2025, at approximately 11:00 a.m., Gabriel Gonzalez (21) and his brother Francis Gonzalez (23), both of Hebron, Indiana, were at Marina Shores Marina on Burns Waterway when they spotted an object belonging to a boat owner that had fallen from "D dock" and was floating away. Gabriel voluntarily entered the water to retrieve the object. After retrieving the object and swimming back toward D dock, Gabriel appears to have entered an electrified field of water and went under the surface.

Francis witnessed his brother go under and immediately entered the water to rescue him. Francis was affected by the electrical field but remained on the surface. A nearby bystander threw a flotation device to Francis and pulled him to safety. Portage Fire Department divers recovered Gabriel's body after the water was confirmed safe. Gabriel was transported to a hospital where he was pronounced deceased.

An autopsy by the Porter County Coroner's Office returned a preliminary cause of death of "drowning secondary to electrocution, accidental." A licensed electrician helped investigators identify the likely source of electricity as equipment belonging to a nearby slip holder. All other docks at the marina were checked for electrical current and none were found to be additionally affected.

## ESD Indicators Present
- **STRONGEST — Rescue attempt injury**: Francis Gonzalez entered the water to rescue his brother and was immediately "affected by the electrified area" — he survived only because a bystander threw him a flotation device. Classic ESD rescue victim profile.
- **STRONGEST — Autopsy confirmed ESD mechanism**: Cause of death officially stated as "drowning secondary to electrocution" — the coroner explicitly recognized the ESD dual mechanism.
- **MODERATE — Near dock/marina with electrical equipment**: D dock at Marina Shores Marina; a piece of marina equipment (belonging to a slip holder) was identified as the likely electrical source.
- **MODERATE — Young healthy male**: A 21-year-old in apparent good health who had just successfully retrieved an object from the water and was swimming back — consistent with sudden ESD paralysis rather than medical episode.
- **MODERATE — Dock had to be confirmed safe before body retrieval**: Divers could not enter the water immediately; had to wait for the dock to be confirmed safe, confirming live electricity was present in the water.

## Notes
This is the most recently documented case found in this search (July 2025). It is significant because the Porter County Coroner correctly identified the cause of death as "drowning secondary to electrocution" rather than simply "drowning" — demonstrating improved ESD awareness among coroners compared to earlier cases where ESD was invariably classified as simple drowning. This case does not represent a reclassification (it was correctly identified initially) but documents current ESD occurrence at freshwater marina docks. The case has not yet appeared in the ESDPA incident database (which appears updated periodically). The ongoing investigation into the specific equipment source was not publicly resolved at time of research.
