## Key Findings

### SUSP-2016-020: Donna Berger & Randy Freeney — HIGH Priority

This is the strongest new case found in LOA2-B research. The simultaneous incapacitation of an "exceptional swimmer" (Donna Berger, 53) followed instantly by the paralysis and death of a "strong swimmer" rescuer (Randy Freeney, 74) at an anchored boat on Chickamauga Lake matches the ESD double-fatality pattern exactly.

The ESDPA did list this incident but with critical errors: they dated it July 3, 2016 (the date WSOCTV aired the segment) rather than the actual date of June 17, 2016, and noted "exact time and location are unknown" — meaning the ESDPA listing is effectively a placeholder without verified details. No electrical investigation of the water or surrounding boats was documented.

**ESD hallmarks present**: Two skilled swimmers incapacitated simultaneously; rescuer stopped and rolled over immediately on entering water; calm freshwater lake; boating season (other boats anchored nearby); no current, weather, or alcohol factors identified.

### SUSP-2017-020: Michigan Marina Owner — MODERATE Priority

Found via CDC FACE Report 17MI208 (published March 9, 2020). The case never received public coverage and is not in the ESDPA database. A marina owner in his 80s, described as a "good swimmer" by his spouse, drowned in 6 feet of water at his own marina boat slip. He was found floating upright, with the crown of his head visible above water — an unusual post-mortem position for a simple fall-and-drowning.

**Critical gap explicitly documented by CDC**: "Neither the sheriff or the medical examiner investigator checked the water for stray electrical current." The CDC report's own language flags the absence of electrical investigation. The marina had active shore power infrastructure. The victim had previously fallen in the same water without drowning (when his son was present). ESD is intermittent; the presence of a fault on a specific date when he was alone could explain why the prior falls were survivable.

### SUSP-2017-021: Monmouth Beach, NJ — MODERATE Priority (Salinity Caveat)

A 59-year-old male fell into marina water at Monmouth Beach, NJ while boarding a boat; a 51-year-old female jumped in to rescue him and also drowned. Both victims died. The rescuer-drowning pattern is a high-confidence ESD indicator.

**Important caveat**: Monmouth Beach is on the tidal Shrewsbury River, a brackish-to-saltwater waterway. ESD is physically impossible in saltwater. If the marina dock is in a freshwater-influenced zone or the incident occurred during a period of freshwater dominance, ESD remains plausible; otherwise, cold water shock (March), hypothermia, or inadequate swimming ability become more likely explanations. This case requires geographic and salinity verification before ESD probability can be revised upward.

## Cases Not Found (Expected but Absent from Search Results)

The LOA2-B searches did not surface additional previously undocumented cases with high ESD probability beyond those listed above. Searches for general patterns ("strong swimmer drowned dock") returned many results dominated by:
- Confirmed ESD cases already in ESDPA
- Ocean/rip current drownings (not ESD)
- Pool drownings (separate infrastructure)
- Non-dock lake drownings with clear alternative causes

This likely reflects both the rarity of cases where swimming ability is specifically noted in news coverage AND the effectiveness of the ESDPA in capturing the highest-profile cases. The LOA2-B approach is most valuable for cases that never received press coverage (e.g., SUSP-2017-020 from a CDC occupational report) or where the ESDPA listing has incomplete data (SUSP-2016-020).

## Source Reliability Notes

- **CDC FACE/MIFACE Reports**: Primary source; peer-reviewed occupational fatality documentation. High reliability for factual details. Privacy protections withhold specific location data.
- **Robson Forensic appendix**: Litigation-support document; useful as an index but limited narrative detail. Reliability for date/location/demographics is high; causal analysis is absent.
- **WSOCTV / ShockAlert.com**: Secondary journalism. Quotes and family accounts are attributed; ESD interpretation is asserted by family members, not confirmed by investigation.
- **TWRA / State agency press releases**: Primary for official classification. These agencies did not classify deaths as ESD.
- **ESDPA incident list**: Useful cross-reference but the August 2016 entry for the Berger/Freeney case confirms the ESDPA's own data has gaps (wrong date, no location).
