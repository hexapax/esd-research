## What Happened

In the early morning hours of July 4, 2024, 18-year-old Jesse Hamric — a recent Steamboat Springs High School graduate and son of the school's principal — jumped into Smith Mountain Lake at a private residence dock during a family vacation. He immediately became unresponsive. Two friends saw him struggling and jumped in to help. Both friends immediately felt electrical shocks upon entering the water. Despite being shocked themselves, they were able to drag Hamric out of the water and began CPR. Hamric was transported to hospital and pronounced dead.

The Smith Mountain Lake Marine Volunteer Fire Rescue Dive Team confirmed electrocution at the dock. First responders tested the water with a Shock Alert device and recorded readings above 200 millivolts per foot — the device's maximum measurable threshold. Assistant Fire Chief Ray Talley stated the source was the electric boat lift, which was submerged at the time. When the lift was raised out of the water, the voltage reading disappeared entirely. The homeowner was required to address the problem with an electrician.

A retired volunteer responder, Neil Harrington, stated he had previously tested over 209 docks at Smith Mountain Lake and found stray voltage at approximately 201-203 of them, coming from metal parts of boat lifts. Harrington had previously lobbied the Virginia legislature to update electrical codes with no success.

## ESD Indicators

- Two rescuers "immediately felt shocked" upon entering the water — simultaneous multi-person electrical exposure is a definitive ESD diagnostic
- First responder voltage measurements confirmed electricity in water at the scene
- Voltage disappeared when boat lift was raised out of water — direct causal identification
- >200 mV/ft reading at scene (above Shock Alert device maximum)
- Fatality at dock with confirmed stray voltage from electric boat lift

## Electrical Investigation Outcome

The electric boat lift at the private dock was confirmed as the stray voltage source. The voltage disappeared immediately when the lift was raised, per Fire Chief Talley. The homeowner was formally notified and required to hire an electrician. Bedford County Sheriff's Office conducted an ongoing investigation; medical examiner conclusions were still pending as of August 2024. No criminal charges were reported publicly. Harrington's testing data (>200 docks, ~97% with stray voltage) indicates this is a systemic problem at Smith Mountain Lake.
