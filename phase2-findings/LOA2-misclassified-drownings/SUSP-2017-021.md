## What Happened

On the morning of Friday, March 24, 2017, a 59-year-old male fell into the water at a marina dock in Monmouth Beach, New Jersey while attempting to board or disembark from a boat. A 51-year-old female — identified in the Robson Forensic marina drowning appendix as his friend — jumped into the water to rescue him. Both drowned.

The incident is documented in the Robson Forensic Expert Witness appendix on marina drownings (a litigation-support document cataloging marina-area drowning incidents by date, location, victim demographics, and incident type). The appendix categorizes the female victim as "Rescuer, drowned" and describes the male victim under the category "Embarking or disembarking from boat." The entry notes: "Victim fell into water trying to get on board a boat. Friend jumped in to save victim, both drowned."

Both victims died at the same location within the same incident. No additional public news coverage was identified in the research conducted for this file.

**Note on water type**: Monmouth Beach is on the Jersey Shore on the Shrewsbury River, which is a tidal inlet with brackish to saltwater composition, particularly in March. ESD is not possible in saltwater because saltwater conducts electricity around (not through) the body. This case is flagged MODERATE rather than HIGH because of the water salinity uncertainty at the specific marina location in March.

## ESD Indicators Present

- **MODERATE — Rescuer drowned immediately upon entering water**: A 51-year-old woman jumped in to rescue her friend and also drowned. The rapid incapacitation of a presumed-capable adult rescuer is a classic ESD pattern.
- **MODERATE — Double drowning at marina dock during boat operation**: Both victims died in the same location in a marina setting with likely shore power infrastructure nearby.
- **LOW — Water salinity uncertainty**: Monmouth Beach is on a tidal river/inlet; in March, water may be brackish or saline. If saltwater, ESD is physically impossible. The specific slip/dock location within the marina and salinity at that point are unknown.
- **LOW — No swimming ability descriptor**: Neither victim is described as a "strong swimmer" or "exceptional swimmer" in available sources; the male victim fell in while boarding (not swimming), and the female victim's swimming ability is uncharacterized.

## Follow-up Electrical Investigation

No electrical investigation of the marina is documented in available sources. The Robson Forensic appendix is a legal/litigation support document and does not indicate whether any ESD investigation was pursued. No ESDPA listing found for this incident.

## Notes

This case is flagged MODERATE primarily due to the rescuer-drowning pattern and marina dock setting. The critical limiting factor is water salinity: Monmouth Beach borders the Shrewsbury River, a brackish coastal waterway, which would largely preclude ESD as a mechanism. If the marina dock accessed a freshwater component (e.g., a marina on a freshwater tributary), ESD would become more plausible. The March date (cold water) also raises hypothermia and cold shock as alternative explanations for rapid incapacitation of both victims.

**Recommended follow-up**: Identify the specific marina name and dock location in Monmouth Beach; determine water salinity at that location in March; request Monmouth County ME records for both victims; check whether any local news coverage exists from March 24–28, 2017.
