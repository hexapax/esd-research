# SUSP-2010-045: Drowning Incident — Lake Waccamaw, North Carolina — June 2010 — Children Report "Tingling" in Water, Dock Owner Shocked

## Classification
- **ESD Probability**: HIGH
- **Incident Type**: Fatal or near-fatal drowning with simultaneous multi-person electrical sensation in water
- **File Number**: SUSP-2010-045
- **LOA Task**: LOA2-C

---

## Incident Summary

In June 2010 at Lake Waccamaw, North Carolina, a drowning incident occurred at a private dock where children in the water reported feeling tingling sensations, and the dock owner was shocked upon contact with dock infrastructure. The pattern mirrors the June 2004 fatality at Lake Waccamaw (SUSP-2004-044) and is consistent with Electric Shock Drowning caused by energized dock equipment creating a voltage gradient in the surrounding water.

## HIGH-Probability ESD Indicators

### Indicator A: Multiple Children Reported Tingling in Water
Children swimming at the dock reported feeling tingling sensations in the water — the classic subjective description of low-level AC current passing through the body via voltage gradient. Multiple simultaneous tingle reports from swimmers in the same body of water is not consistent with ordinary drowning.

### Indicator B: Dock Owner Shocked on Contact with Dock Structure
The dock owner received an electrical shock upon contact with dock infrastructure, confirming that dock equipment was actively energized at the time of the incident.

## Witness Accounts / Key Language

- Children in the water reported "feeling tingles in the water" — the standard ESD warning symptom.
- Dock owner reported being shocked upon contacting the dock.

## Relationship to Prior Lake Waccamaw Incident

This 2010 incident at Lake Waccamaw follows the confirmed fatal ESD incident at the same lake in June 2004 (SUSP-2004-044), in which a ten-year-old boy drowned while multiple children reported simultaneous shocks in the water near an energized boat lift. The recurrence of ESD-pattern incidents at the same lake six years apart suggests ongoing inadequacy of dock electrical safety inspections in the Lake Waccamaw area.
