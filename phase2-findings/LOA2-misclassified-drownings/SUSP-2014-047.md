# SUSP-2014-047: Thirteen-Year-Old Girl Death — Lake Bruin, Louisiana — August 24, 2014 — Two Girls Simultaneously Shocked at Pontoon Ladder

## Classification

- **File Number**: SUSP-2014-047

## Incident Summary

On August 24, 2014, two girls — a thirteen-year-old and a seventeen-year-old — were simultaneously shocked while attempting to board a pontoon boat via its ladder at Lake Bruin, Louisiana. The thirteen-year-old died; the seventeen-year-old was hospitalized. The simultaneous incapacitation of two individuals at the same boat ladder is a defining HIGH-probability indicator of Electric Shock Drowning.

## HIGH-Probability ESD Indicators

### Indicator A: Two Persons Simultaneously Shocked at Same Ladder
Both girls were shocked while in contact with or attempting to use the same pontoon boat ladder at the same time. Simultaneous incapacitation of two people at the same dock/boat contact point is a cardinal indicator of Electric Shock Drowning — the voltage gradient at the ladder was sufficient to shock both individuals simultaneously.

### Indicator B: Fatal Outcome in One, Hospitalization of Other
The thirteen-year-old died; the seventeen-year-old survived but required hospitalization. This outcome differential (one dies, one survives with injury from the same electrical exposure) is consistent with ESD: current levels, contact time, individual physiological response, and body position in the water determine whether a given exposure is fatal or survivable.
