## What Happened

On Friday, April 14, 2017, Shelly Darling (34, staff attorney at UA's Elder Law Clinic) and Elizabeth Whipple (41, interim director of UA's Domestic Violence Clinic) went to Darling's family lake house on Lake Tuscaloosa to sunbathe. Darling's husband reported her missing when she failed to return home for dinner. Family members found the women's belongings on the dock but the women were missing. Tuscaloosa Police dive teams were called in and located both bodies in the lake in the early morning hours of Saturday, April 15.

During the initial search Saturday night/morning, a Tuscaloosa police officer was shocked by a current on the dock where the women had been sunbathing. The officer was not injured but the shock confirmed to investigators that electricity was present in that location. Homicide Captain Kip Hart publicly stated: "We know there was electricity going through at least part of the pier."

Preliminary autopsies determined cause of death as electrocution for both women. Their deaths were ruled accidental. The ESDPA incident list specifically records this event and notes "at least one investigator felt an electrical shock on the dock during initial emergency response. The accident has been ruled an Electric Shock Drowning."

Note: Carmen Johnson (15) had died in April 2016 at Smith Lake, Alabama in a nearly identical dock ESD incident; the Tuscaloosa cases prompted renewed public attention. A third victim, Dr. Eric Hughes (37), had also died at Lake Tuscaloosa in August 2015 after being shocked near his pontoon boat.

## ESD Indicators

- Police investigator shocked on the dock during the initial search — confirming live electrical hazard at the exact location of the deaths
- Two women found drowned simultaneously in the same location
- Cause of death confirmed as electrocution by preliminary autopsy for both victims
- Dock was the last confirmed location of both victims
- Case officially classified as Electric Shock Drowning by the ESDPA

## Electrical Investigation Outcome

The ESDPA listed this as a confirmed ESD. Authorities confirmed electricity was running through "at least part of the pier." Investigators returned to the dock Monday to examine electrical involvement. Additional details on specific wiring fault not published in available sources. The Tuscaloosa County Homicide Unit handled the investigation and confirmed accidental electrocution as cause of death. No criminal charges were reported.
