## Task Criteria Recap

This task targeted incidents meeting **HIGH-probability ESD criteria**:

- **(a)** Multiple people simultaneously incapacitated in water at the same dock, AND/OR
- **(b)** Witnesses reported feeling electrical sensations (tingling, shock, numbness, paralysis) in the water

These criteria are diagnostically significant because:
- Simultaneous multi-person electrical sensation in the same body of water CANNOT occur in a normal drowning
- It is pathognomonic (virtually definitive) for AC voltage gradient in the water — the mechanism of Electric Shock Drowning
- As little as 10 mA of AC current can paralyze skeletal muscle; 2 V/ft voltage gradient in freshwater is lethal

## Key Diagnostic Patterns Identified

### 1. Chain-Rescue Shock Pattern (6 of 15 incidents)
In six incidents, individuals who entered the water to rescue a primary victim were themselves shocked or incapacitated. This pattern proves the water contained an active voltage gradient — it is independent confirmation of ESD that does not depend on interpreting the primary victim's cause of death.

Incidents: SUSP-2024-040, SUSP-2011-041, SUSP-1993-049, SUSP-1988-051, SUSP-2013-048, SUSP-2012-053

### 2. Prior Tingle/Shock Warning Ignored (4 of 15 incidents)
Four incidents occurred at locations where people had previously reported feeling tingling or shocks in the water, and no corrective action was taken. This is a critical preventability finding: the hazard announced itself before causing death, and deaths occurred because warnings were not acted upon.

Incidents: SUSP-2011-041, SUSP-1999-050, SUSP-2007-052, SUSP-2010-045

### 3. Simultaneous Mass Exposure (multiple incidents)
The Cherokee Lake incident (SUSP-2012-053) is the most extreme case: minimum 5 persons experienced electrical effects in the same water at the same time. Lake Waccamaw 2004 (SUSP-2004-044), Lake Freeman (SUSP-2010-046), and St. Louis/Summerset Lake (SUSP-2006-043) also involve four simultaneous victims.

### 4. Confirmed Electrical Faults (6 of 15 incidents)
Six incidents have documented electrical fault findings: disconnected bonding conductor (SUSP-2004-044), corroded junction box (SUSP-2011-041), energized boat lift (SUSP-2024-040, SUSP-2017-042), defective utility neutral (SUSP-2006-043), 40-year-old dock wiring fault (SUSP-1999-050).

### 5. Power-Cut Natural Experiment (1 incident)
The Lake of the Ozarks 2015 incident (SUSP-2015-054) provides a definitive natural experiment: cutting power to the dock eliminated the hazard and allowed the second swimmer to be rescued. This constitutes near-direct proof of ESD causation that is typically unavailable.
