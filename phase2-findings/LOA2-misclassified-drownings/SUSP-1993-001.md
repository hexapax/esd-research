# SUSP-1993-001: Alexandria Bay NY — Double Snorkeling Drowning Near Dock

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Date** | August 1993 |
| **Location** | Alexandria Bay, New York |
| **Victim** | Two teenage girls (names unknown); both drowned while snorkeling near a dock |
| **Initial Ruling** | Drowning (presumed) |
| **Final Ruling** | Suspected ESD — dock wiring fault suspected but not confirmed at time of ESDPA documentation |
| **ESD Probability** | HIGH (>70%) |
| **ESDPA Match** | In ESDPA incident list (August 1993, Alexandria Bay, NY entry) |

## What Happened
In August 1993, two teenage girls were snorkeling near a dock in Alexandria Bay, New York, on the St. Lawrence River. Both girls were paralyzed by what was described as electric shock and drowned. Two bystanders attempted to enter the water to render assistance but were unable to do so — the ESDPA record notes they "attempted to enter water to lend assistance, but were unable to do so," strongly suggesting the bystanders also felt electrical current in the water and were turned back.

The fault was thought to be in dock wiring but was not confirmed at the time of ESDPA documentation. The fact that two teenage girls — old enough to be competent snorkelers who voluntarily entered the water — were simultaneously paralyzed and drowned, with bystanders unable to approach the water, is the strongest possible circumstantial ESD pattern.

## ESD Indicators Present
- **STRONGEST — Multiple simultaneous victims at same dock**: Two girls drowned simultaneously in the same location — one of the defining ESD signatures.
- **STRONGEST — Bystanders unable to enter water to rescue**: This explicitly indicates the electrical field was also affecting bystanders who tried to enter the water — they experienced the shock and could not enter. This is the most extreme version of the rescue-attempt indicator.
- **STRONGEST — Witnesses describe "paralysis"**: The ESDPA record describes the girls as being "paralyzed by electric shock" — direct language consistent with ESD muscle paralysis.
- **MODERATE — Near dock infrastructure**: Fault suspected in dock wiring — the characteristic ESD source.
- **MODERATE — Competent swimmers drowned inexplicably**: Teenage snorkelers who had voluntarily entered the water for recreation were suddenly unable to help themselves.

## Notes
The phrase "were unable to do so" in reference to the bystanders is the most telling ESD indicator in this case. In standard drowning scenarios, bystanders are only unable to help due to their own swimming limitations or water conditions. The specific inability of bystanders to enter the water here — phrased distinctly from any statement about not knowing how to swim — implies they were repelled by something in the water (most likely electrical current). This case was almost certainly ruled simple drowning locally in 1993, before ESD was a recognized category. The dock wiring fault was suspected but could not be confirmed, possibly because the investigation did not proceed with sufficient electrical expertise. Victim names not found in available public sources.
