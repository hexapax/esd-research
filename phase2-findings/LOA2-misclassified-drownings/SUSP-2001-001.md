## What Happened
On September 15, 2001, at Farr Shores on Lake Hamilton in Hot Springs, Arkansas, a girl was in great distress in the water near a boat. A man entered the water to attempt a rescue and drowned. The girl was shocked but survived (her survival reportedly due to the boat being unplugged in time). 

Investigation determined the cause: reverse polarity and a neutral-to-ground fault in a light fixture on the boat, combined with poor bonding to the shore power service. This energized the water around the boat. The girl entered the electrical field and was shocked; the male rescuer entered the water and was also electrocuted and drowned. The electrical source was identified as a damaged power cable on the boat where the black (hot) lead energized the hull and the ground wire burned in two — the circuit breaker did not trip due to an incorrect connection.

## ESD Indicators Present
- **STRONGEST — Rescue attempt death**: A man entered the water to rescue the girl in distress and drowned. The canonical ESD rescue death pattern — confirms the water was electrified and the rescuer was killed by the same field.
- **STRONGEST — Multiple simultaneous victims**: Both the girl and the rescuer were incapacitated in the same body of water at the same time.
- **MODERATE — Near boat with confirmed electrical fault**: The cause was definitively established as reverse polarity and neutral-to-ground fault in the boat's electrical system, connected to shore power.
- **MODERATE — Girl survived because power was cut**: The report notes the boat was "unplugged in time" to save the girl — confirming that the electrical field in the water was the cause of distress, and that cutting power ended the hazard.
- **MODERATE — Circuit breaker failed to trip**: The breaker did not trip despite the fault — this is the standard ESD scenario where the fault current is too low to trip the breaker but sufficient to kill a swimmer.

## Notes
The description "Girl in great distress, man attempting rescue drowns, fault on boat, fresh water" combined with "Boat unplugged in time to save girl" is the textbook ESD rescue-death event. The rescuer's cause of death was listed as drowning in the ESDPA database (he was the victim), though the cause of his inability to swim/survive was the electrical field. No public news coverage of this incident was found by name, suggesting it may have been initially ruled a simple drowning locally without media coverage linking it to ESD. Exact victim names not located in available sources. The ESDPA incident list is the only documented source.
