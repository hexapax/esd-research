# SUSP-2010-046: Thirteen-Year-Old Boy Death — Lake Freeman, Indiana — July 26, 2010 — Four Children Simultaneously Feel Tingle Before Drowning

## Classification
- **ESD Probability**: HIGH
- **Incident Type**: Fatal drowning with simultaneous multi-person electrical sensation in water
- **File Number**: SUSP-2010-046
- **LOA Task**: LOA2-C

---

## Incident Summary

On July 26, 2010, a thirteen-year-old boy drowned at Lake Freeman, Indiana. He and three other children simultaneously began to "feel a tingle" in the water near a dock. The other three children were able to exit the water; the thirteen-year-old did not survive. The simultaneous onset of tingling sensations across four children in the same body of water at the same time is a hallmark indicator of Electric Shock Drowning caused by AC voltage gradient in the water.

## HIGH-Probability ESD Indicators

### Indicator A: Four Children Simultaneously Felt Tingle in the Same Water
The thirteen-year-old victim and three companion children all simultaneously "started to feel a tingle" in the water near the dock. This concurrent electrical sensation across four individuals in the same body of water at the same time cannot be explained by ordinary drowning mechanisms. It is the defining characteristic of an AC voltage gradient in the water column — the electrical mechanism of Electric Shock Drowning.

### Indicator B: Three Survivors Confirm the Electrical Sensation
The fact that three children who were in the same water experienced the same tingle and survived by exiting provides direct corroborating witness testimony to the presence of electricity in the water. Their testimony distinguishes this event from a simple drowning.

### Indicator C: Victim Unable to Exit Water
The thirteen-year-old, presumably subjected to the same electrical current as his companions, was unable to exit the water. This is consistent with ESD-induced skeletal muscle tetany or paralysis at current levels sufficient to prevent swimming — the mechanism by which ESD victims drown while conscious.
