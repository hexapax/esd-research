# SUSP-1999-001: Rio Vista CA Marina Double Drowning After Prior Tingle Reports

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Date** | Approximately 1999 |
| **Location** | Rio Vista, California (freshwater marina) |
| **Victim** | Two boys, approximately 8-10 years old; both drowned |
| **Initial Ruling** | Drowning (no public reclassification found) |
| **Final Ruling** | Electric shock drowning — 40-year-old power wiring under moored boats found with substantial fault to ground |
| **ESD Probability** | HIGH (>70%) |
| **ESDPA Match** | In ESDPA incident list (~1999, Rio Vista, CA entry) |

## What Happened
At a freshwater marina in Rio Vista, California, approximately 1999, several boys reported feeling a "tingle" while swimming in the marina and got out of the water. A short time later, two other boys aged 8-10 years old drowned at the same spot. Investigation found that 40-year-old power wiring running under moored boats had a substantial fault to ground due to insulation failure, creating an electrified field in the water.

The significance of this case lies in the prior "tingle" reports that were not acted upon. Children who recognized the tingling sensation and left the water survived; the two younger boys who subsequently entered the same water were fatally incapacitated.

## ESD Indicators Present
- **STRONGEST — Multiple simultaneous victims at same location**: Two children drowned at exactly the same spot within a short time of each other.
- **STRONGEST — Prior witnesses reported "tingling" in water**: Several boys felt a tingle and got out just before the fatal event — the canonical ESD warning sign that was not escalated. This is a direct witness report of the electrical field.
- **MODERATE — Children drowned near dock despite the ability to exit water**: The prior group of boys could exit on their own, but the two who drowned could not — suggesting the second group was more directly in the current field or more severely affected.
- **MODERATE — Electrical fault confirmed**: 40-year-old power wiring under moored boats was found with substantial insulation failure fault to ground — the confirmed ESD source.
- **MODERATE — Near boats with shore power**: The fault was in wiring running under moored boats in a marina, connected to the shore power system.

## Notes
This case exemplifies the most dangerous ESD scenario: prior warning signs (tingle reports) that were not escalated to authorities or marina management, followed by fatalities. The older wiring (40 years old at time of incident) is consistent with pre-NEC 555 era installations with no GFCI protection. The exact date is listed as "Approx. 1999" in ESDPA records. Victim names and specific marina name not found in available public sources. The case was likely ruled simple drowning locally — the fault was found through investigation after the deaths, not through forensic analysis of the victims. No public reclassification of the death certificate from drowning to ESD/electrocution has been documented.
