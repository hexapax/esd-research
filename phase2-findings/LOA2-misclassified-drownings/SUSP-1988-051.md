# SUSP-1988-051: Eighteen-Year-Old Death — Bay Haven Marina, Lake Macatawa, Michigan — July 1988 — Two Rescue Attempts "Thwarted by Severe Electric Shock as Rescuers Entered Water"

## Classification
- **ESD Probability**: HIGH
- **Incident Type**: Fatal drowning at marina; multiple rescuers incapacitated by electric shock entering water
- **File Number**: SUSP-1988-051
- **LOA Task**: LOA2-C

---

## Incident Summary

In July 1988, an eighteen-year-old died in the water at Bay Haven Marina on Lake Macatawa, Michigan. Two separate rescue attempts were "thwarted because of severe electric shock as rescuers entered water." This language — severe electric shock incapacitating rescuers upon entry into the water — is one of the most diagnostically clear ESD indicators possible: it proves that a lethal voltage gradient existed in the marina water, independent of the victim's cause of death.

## HIGH-Probability ESD Indicators

### Indicator A: Two Rescue Attempts Thwarted by "Severe Electric Shock as Rescuers Entered Water"
This is the chain-rescue ESD signature at its most unambiguous. Rescuers entering the water to assist a drowning victim were themselves incapacitated by "severe electric shock." This cannot happen in an ordinary drowning. The water itself was electrically hazardous. The severity of the shock — described as severe enough to thwart rescue — suggests current levels well above the 10 mA paralysis threshold.

### Indicator B: Two Separate Rescue Attempts Both Thwarted
At least two independent people attempted rescue and were both electrically incapacitated upon entering the water. Multiple rescuers experiencing severe shock at the same marina location confirms a persistent voltage gradient, not a transient or isolated contact event.

### Indicator C: Marina Venue — Common ESD Locus
Bay Haven Marina is a commercial/public marina facility. Marinas concentrate multiple boats with shore power connections, aging wiring, corroded connections, and often inadequate or absent ELCI protection — all conditions that create ESD hazards.

## Historical Context

In 1988, GFCI protection requirements for marina shore power were not yet widely mandated. ELCI requirements came much later. The combination of 1988-era marina electrical infrastructure with no ground fault protection creates ideal conditions for persistent, undetected ESD hazards.

## ESD Probability Assessment

**HIGH** — Two independent rescue attempts thwarted by severe electric shock as rescuers entered the water; one person died. The "rescuers shocked entering water" pattern is pathognomonic for voltage gradient in the marina water (Electric Shock Drowning). Meets both HIGH criteria: (a) multiple persons simultaneously incapacitated in water at the same dock, and (b) witnesses reported feeling severe electrical sensation in the water.
