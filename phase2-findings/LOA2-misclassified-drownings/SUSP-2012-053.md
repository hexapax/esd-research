# SUSP-2012-053: Two Boys Dead — Cherokee Lake, Tennessee — July 4, 2012 — Eight-Year-Old Girl and Multiple Adults Shocked Attempting Rescue; Five Persons Total Shocked

## Classification
- **ESD Probability**: HIGH

- **File Number**: SUSP-2012-053

---

## Incident Summary

On July 4, 2012, two boys — Noah Winstead (10) and Nate Lynam (11) — drowned at a dock on Cherokee Lake, Tennessee. An eight-year-old girl was shocked but pulled from the water and survived. "Several adults and another twelve-year-old boy were shocked trying to rescue the two boys." At least five separate individuals experienced electrical shock in the same body of water on the same day. This is one of the most severe documented ESD mass-casualty events: two fatalities, multiple shocked survivors, and the chain-rescue shock pattern all present simultaneously.

## HIGH-Probability ESD Indicators

### Indicator A: Two Children Simultaneously Drowned at Same Dock
Noah Winstead (10) and Nate Lynam (11) drowned simultaneously at the same dock. Two children dying at the same location at the same time is a defining indicator of an external incapacitating agent — in this case, electricity in the water.

### Indicator B: Eight-Year-Old Girl Shocked at Same Location — Survived
An eight-year-old girl was shocked in the same water at the same time and was pulled out before drowning. Her survival confirms that: (a) electricity was present in the water, and (b) the ability to exit or be pulled from the water determines survival. The two boys who drowned were unable to exit.

### Indicator C: Multiple Adults and a Twelve-Year-Old Boy Shocked Attempting Rescue
"Several adults and another twelve-year-old boy were shocked trying to rescue the two boys." This is the chain-rescue ESD signature in its most severe form: the water contained enough electrical current to shock every person who entered it. Multiple rescuers from multiple waves experiencing shock at the same dock proves persistent voltage gradient in the water.

### Indicator D: Five or More Persons Shocked in Same Water
Counting the two fatal victims, the eight-year-old survivor, "several adults," and the twelve-year-old boy, at minimum five and likely more individuals experienced electrical effects in the same water at Cherokee Lake on July 4, 2012. A mass electrical exposure event of this scale in a freshwater lake near a dock is unambiguous evidence of ESD.
