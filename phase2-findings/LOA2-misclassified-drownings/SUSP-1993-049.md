# SUSP-1993-049: Double Drowning — Alexandria Bay, New York — August 1993 — Two Snorkeling Girls Paralyzed; Two Bystanders Also Shocked Entering Water

## Classification
- **ESD Probability**: HIGH
- **Incident Type**: Double fatality with simultaneous multi-person electrical shock/paralysis in water
- **File Number**: SUSP-1993-049
- **LOA Task**: LOA2-C

---

## Incident Summary

In August 1993, two girls who were snorkeling in the St. Lawrence River at Alexandria Bay, New York, were simultaneously paralyzed by electrical current in the water and drowned. Two bystanders who entered the water to attempt rescue were also shocked. This incident involves four separate individuals across two waves all experiencing electrical incapacitation in the same body of water — one of the clearest multi-person ESD signatures documented in the pre-internet era.

## HIGH-Probability ESD Indicators

### Indicator A: Two Victims Simultaneously Paralyzed in Same Water
The two girls were snorkeling together and were simultaneously paralyzed by electrical current. Simultaneous incapacitation of two individuals by an external agent — electricity — in the same water is a defining ESD indicator.

### Indicator B: Two Additional Persons Shocked During Rescue
Two bystanders who entered the water to help the girls were also shocked. This chain of electrical injury — primary victims incapacitated, rescuers also shocked — proves that the water itself contained a dangerous voltage gradient. Four people experiencing electrical incapacitation or shock in the same body of water is unambiguous evidence of ESD.

### Indicator C: Paralysis Specifically Reported
The victims were "paralyzed" — the specific physiological response to AC current at levels sufficient to cause skeletal muscle tetany. Paralysis in the water (inability to swim) followed by drowning is the exact mechanism of Electric Shock Drowning.

## Historical Context

This 1993 incident predates widespread public awareness of Electric Shock Drowning by more than a decade. The simultaneous paralysis of two snorkelers and subsequent shocks to two rescuers — if investigated under current ESD protocols — would be classified as a confirmed ESD fatality. The incident likely was recorded as a drowning.

The Alexandria Bay area of the St. Lawrence River (Thousand Islands region) has numerous marinas, docks, and boat facilities that may have had aging or inadequate electrical systems in 1993.
