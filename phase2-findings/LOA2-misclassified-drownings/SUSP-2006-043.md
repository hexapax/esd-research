## What Happened

In the early evening of March 18, 2006, a teenage boy was swimming with three companions near a private dock on Summerset Lake (near DeSoto in the St. Louis area, Missouri). As the boy swam toward the metal ladder to exit the water, he became paralyzed by electric shock and drowned. Three other teens — two boys and a girl — were in the same area and all received shocks. Two of the three were rendered unconscious and required hospitalization; the third was uninjured.

A utility representative's subsequent investigation found 10 amps of current running in the dock with no known loads operational. A chain leading into the water from the dock showed signs of arcing. A dead muskrat and ten dead minnows were observed near the chain — suggesting a persistent electrical hazard in that area of the water predating the incident.

Extensive subsequent investigations determined the dock's own wiring was properly installed and the metal dock frame and ladder were bonded. However, clamping the dock supply cable with an AC ammeter disclosed 10 amps flowing (likely in the ground wire) and 4-6 VAC was measured approximately 2 feet from the ladder on several separate occasions. Legal action resulted in a jury finding the local utility company at fault: a nearby underground power distribution cable had a defective or missing neutral, causing neutral current to return to the substation through the earth and into the lake. This earth leakage current concentrated at the bonded swim ladder, creating a lethal gradient of more than 2 V/ft near the ladder.

## ESD Indicators

- All four swimmers in the same area received shocks simultaneously — definitive multi-person simultaneous ESD signature
- Two of four rendered unconscious — high-current ESD exposure
- Dead wildlife (muskrat, ten minnows) near arcing chain — evidence of persistent energized zone
- Post-incident testing measured 10 amps in dock supply cable, 4-6 VAC near ladder
- Utility found liable for defective underground neutral cable causing earth return currents

## Electrical Investigation Outcome

Multiple investigations over several months confirmed the dock's own wiring was properly installed. The true cause was a defective or missing neutral on a nearby underground utility power distribution cable, causing neutral return current to flow through the earth and lake water. The current concentrated at the bonded swim ladder (>2 V/ft). A jury found the local utility at fault. No appeal was filed by the utility defendant.
