## Description

A 36-year-old male was electrocuted at a lake in southern Oklahoma on June 1, 1989. He was working on a houseboat docked at the lake when his elbow contacted broken-lamp wiring connected to a 110-volt dock power line during a fuel pump installation. He died from low-voltage electrocution.

## Cause

Broken light bulb (110-volt) with exposed energized wiring connected to dock electrical line. Contact during houseboat fuel pump installation work.

## Government Record

Published in the CDC **Morbidity and Mortality Weekly Report (MMWR)**: *"Electricity-Related Deaths on Lakes — Oklahoma, 1989–1993,"* Vol. 45, No. 26, July 5, 1996 (reporting on 5 deaths investigated 1989–1993).

- **URL:** https://www.cdc.gov/mmwr/preview/mmwrhtml/00042081.htm
- This is the primary federal government document on ESD-type incidents. It documents five deaths at Oklahoma lakes; this is Case 2 (Lake B, southern Oklahoma).

## Cross-Reference to Existing Dataset

**NOT in existing ESDPA dataset.** The existing dataset has FI-1989-001 (December 1989, Oklahoma, dock wiring fault) which corresponds to CDC MMWR Case 1 (December 8, 1989, Lake A, pump installation). This June 1989 incident is separate and not represented in the ESDPA list.

## Notes

The CDC anonymized all lake names in the MMWR report using letters (Lake A through D) to protect location-sensitive information. Lake A = northeastern Oklahoma lake where 3 of the 5 deaths occurred. Lake B = southern Oklahoma lake. Exact lake names are not recoverable from the MMWR alone.
