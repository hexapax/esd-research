## Incident Narrative

On June 21, 2025, a 13-year-old boy from Fulton County, Georgia, was exiting the water at a private dock on Blue Ridge Lake by climbing a metal ladder. He was electrocuted upon contact with the ladder and died. Two other boys who attempted to rescue him were also shocked. A woman who attempted to assist was hospitalized from electrical shock.

A Fannin County official investigated the incident and found numerous electrical deficiencies at the dock. The specific deficiencies had not been publicly disclosed at the time of available reporting.

The investigation confirmed ESD as the cause of death. The energized metal ladder is consistent with a dock wiring fault energizing metal components at the dock/water interface — a classic ESD-mechanism failure.

---

## Classification Notes

**Confirmed classic ESD mechanism:** The victim was electrocuted while exiting water via an energized metal dock ladder — a textbook ESD scenario identical to numerous prior incidents (cf. FI-2002-002, Lake Cumberland KY 2013). The metal ladder became energized through a fault in the dock's electrical system.

**Blue Ridge Lake context:** Blue Ridge Lake is a TVA reservoir in north Georgia. This incident establishes Blue Ridge Lake as a location with a documented ESD fatality, which should be noted for future dock inspection priorities in Fannin County.

**Two other victims shocked:** The rescuers being shocked before the power was cut is the characteristic double-victim pattern of ESD incidents where the energized zone extends outward into the water.
