## What Government Records Show That News Records Don't

1. **Regulatory follow-through:** TN.gov documents confirm the Noah Dean and Nate Act led to 300+ marina inspections completed ahead of schedule — news coverage only reported the law's passage.

2. **USCG formalization:** CG-BSX Policy Letter 23-01 CH-1 (Sept 2023) made ESD a formal reportable category for the first time. Pre-2023 BARD data almost certainly undercounts ESD because deaths were coded as "drowning."

3. **KY Fire Marshal enforcement:** The Prospect Yacht Club (FI-2021-001/Elkins) was shut down by the KY Fire Marshal after the death — no agency took jurisdiction over the actual death investigation (OSHA declined, USCG referred back, KY Labor referred back). Government records show a regulatory gap.

4. **CPSC aggregate data:** Pool/spa electrocutions have killed approximately 60 people 1990–2003 and 33 more 2002–2018. The ESDPA list captures only a fraction of pool-related electrocutions.

5. **FOIFT records:** Texas Parks and Wildlife Department withheld their investigation reports on the 2017 Boy Scout deaths, per a Freedom of Information request. The content of those reports remains non-public.

## Search Limitations

- **Site:tn.gov and site:michigan.gov searches:** The .gov site restriction missed state-agency information published through news releases on non-.gov domains. TN.gov results were productive; Michigan yielded nothing incident-specific.
- **USCG BARD database:** Not directly queryable through web search. The full BARD release (2009–2023) is in Microsoft Access format, released July 2024. Individual ESD incidents are not extractable without database access.
- **State DNR boating accident reports:** Most state DNR agencies publish annual summary statistics only, not case-level data. No state DNR website yielded named individual ESD incidents through web search.
- **Overhead power line incidents (GOV-2017-001, GOV-2021-001):** These are documented because they produced fatalities from electrical current in lake water, but they fall outside the standard ESD definition (shore power / dock wiring to water). Classification as "ESD" should be noted as contested.
