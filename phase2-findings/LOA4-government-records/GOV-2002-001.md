## Description

A 14-year-old girl was electrocuted in an apartment complex swimming pool in Arlington, Texas in May 2002. Wiring problems in the pool's underwater lights had charged the water with electricity. A 16-year-old male who jumped into the pool to rescue her was also severely shocked. A third person used a fiberglass shepherd's hook to extract both victims from the water. The girl died; the male rescuer survived with serious injuries.

## Cause

Faulty wiring in underwater pool lighting fixtures — energized pool water. No GFCI protection.

## Government Record

Cited in CPSC (U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission) press release: *"Don't Swim With Shocks: CPSC, American Red Cross Warn of Electrocutions in Swimming Pools, Hot Tubs and Spas"* (2003).

- The CPSC used this case as the lead example in their nationwide public safety campaign on pool electrocutions.

## Classification Notes

This incident occurred in a swimming pool (above-ground pool infrastructure), not at a marina/dock. However, the mechanism is identical to ESD: AC current leaking from pool electrical infrastructure (underwater lights) energized the pool water, paralyzing the victim. The CPSC classifies this as an "electrocution in a swimming pool." The ESDPA list includes numerous pool-based incidents (see FI-2013-005, FI-2016-003, FI-2016-004, etc.).
