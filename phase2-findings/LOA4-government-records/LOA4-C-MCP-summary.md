## Key Findings Beyond Incident Data

### 1. Nate Davenport Incident: Prior Warning Ignored

The July 10, 2023 incident at Harbourside Place (15 people shocked, visible wire photographed, Harbourside employee notified) predates the fatal October 22, 2023 incident. This represents a documented failure to act on known electrical hazards in public water infrastructure — the same pattern seen in multiple dock ESD incidents where prior shocks were reported but not investigated.

### 2. Blue Ridge Lake: First GA ESD Fatality Outside Lake Lanier

Lake Lanier (Cumming/Gainesville/Forsyth County) had been the only documented Georgia ESD fatality lake. The June 2025 Blue Ridge Lake incident establishes a second Georgia lake with confirmed ESD infrastructure-related death. Both are in north Georgia and involve private dock electrical failures.

### 3. Pennsylvania Bill Still Not Enacted (4 Years Post-Death)

James DeAngelo died July 4, 2021. The Pennsylvania ESD prevention bill has been introduced and reintroduced multiple times through June 2025, still without passage. Pennsylvania has more than 80,000 registered boats and extensive freshwater dock infrastructure. The failure to enact this legislation despite bipartisan support and a named victim is a significant regulatory gap.

### 4. Missouri Bill Confirmed Dead

The Alexandra and Brayden Anderson ESD Prevention Act (SB 622, MO) is confirmed as not enacted — the 2018 committee hearing was cancelled and the bill was not revived. Lake of the Ozarks remains without specific ESD-related dock safety legislation despite being a documented hotspot (FI-2012-008, FI-2012-009, FI-2014-001).

### 5. Florida and Public Fountain ESD: A Distinct Risk Category

The Harbourside Place incident (GOV-2023-002) and the 2015 Palm Springs FL pool incident illustrate that ESD-mechanism deaths occur in public water infrastructure (fountains, municipal pools) that is outside both ESDPA's scope and typical marina-focused ESD prevention frameworks. CPSC and local building departments are the relevant regulatory agencies for these incidents, not state boating authorities.

## Search Coverage Gaps (Remaining)

- **USCG BARD database (2009–2023):** Full database is in Microsoft Access format, released July 2024. Individual ESD incidents coded as "drowning" before 2023 would not be retrievable through web search. Database access required.
- **State boating accident reports:** State DNR agencies (MN, WI, NY, NC, MD, FL, GA) do not publish case-level incident data publicly. FOIL/FOIA requests required for individual case records.
- **Pre-2010 Tennessee incidents:** Searches focused on post-2014 (post-Act) period. Pre-Act incidents in TN may exist in TWRA archives at digitaltennessee.tnsos.gov.
- **CPSC NEISS database:** Individual incident records from the National Electronic Injury Surveillance System would provide pool/spa electrocution case data. Database access required.
