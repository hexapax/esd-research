## Description

Three Boy Scout Troop 620 members (from Hallsville, TX) were killed during a sailing lesson on Lake O' the Pines when their Hobie catamaran's 30-foot mast struck an overhead 7,200-volt transmission power line owned by Upshur Rural Electric Cooperative. The line ran across the lake at insufficient clearance per NESC Standard 232-1 (which required at least 38.5 feet; the mast reached the line). The catamaran caught fire. Brannon and Faucheux died at the scene; Thomas Larry was pulled from the water unconscious and airlifted to LSU Medical Center in Shreveport, Louisiana, where he died two days later.

This occurred at the same lake and from the same type of hazard as a 1982 fatality (Gerald Brockett "Jerry" Irwin), despite the power lines having been raised after the 1982 lawsuit settlement. Clearance apparently became insufficient again.

## Cause

Overhead high-voltage transmission power line crossing the lake at insufficient clearance per NESC safety standards. Line struck by sailboat mast. Electrical current discharged through mast, boat, and surrounding water.

## Classification Notes

**This is a water-adjacent electrocution from an overhead power line, not standard ESD from dock/marina wiring.** The current entered the watercraft and surrounding water via a struck overhead transmission line, not from shore power or dock electrical infrastructure. The ESDPA list does include some non-marina incidents (flooded basements, extension cord puddles, etc.) but does not appear to include overhead power line strikes. This is documented here as a significant water electrocution incident found through state agency searches; whether it qualifies as ESD under the ESDPA definition is a classification judgment.

**Note on 1982 predecessor:** The 1982 Lake O' the Pines death of attorney Gerald Brockett Irwin followed the same mechanism (sailboat mast striking Upshur REC overhead line). A subsequent lawsuit resulted in the lines being raised. The 2017 incident suggests clearance at a different location or deck had again become insufficient.
