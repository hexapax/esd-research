## Description

A 13-year-old boy jumped from a dock into the lake at a northeastern Oklahoma lake on July 27, 1993, while dock lights were illuminated. He immediately surfaced screaming, then submerged and drowned. An electrical short in the dock wiring was transmitting current into the water. This is a textbook ESD mechanism: the boy was paralyzed by AC current upon water entry and was unable to swim or call for help.

## Cause

Electrical short in dock wiring — current transmitted into lake water via dock frame/structure. Dock lights were on at the time of entry. Insufficient or absent GFCI protection.

## Government Record

## Notes

The CDC anonymized all Oklahoma lake names in the MMWR. Lake A is described as a northeastern Oklahoma lake where three of the five fatalities occurred (Cases 1, 3, and 4). The screaming-then-submersion pattern described here is consistent with ESD: AC current at marina voltage levels can cause immediate skeletal muscle tetanic contraction, preventing swimming, calling for help, or escape.
