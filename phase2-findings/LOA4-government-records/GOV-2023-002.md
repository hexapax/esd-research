## Incident Narrative

On October 22, 2023, at approximately 3:33 p.m., Nathaniel Davenport was at the Harbourside Place outdoor shopping center with his family. Three of his children entered the interactive fountain/splash pad. Two of the boys soon appeared face-down and distressed in the shallow water. Davenport jumped into the fountain to rescue them, immediately collapsed from electrocution, and was pulled out by bystanders after a bystander located and activated an emergency power shutoff. Davenport was transported to Jupiter Medical Center and pronounced dead approximately one hour after the incident.

Three of Davenport's children and at least one other adult bystander were hospitalized with non-fatal electrical injuries. Multiple rescuers who entered the water also felt shocks before power was shut off. Eight people total required medical attention.

**Prior incident (July 10, 2023):** Approximately 3.5 months before Davenport's death, 38-year-old Dina Fleck and approximately 15 others were shocked by the same fountain. Witnesses photographed a visible wire protruding from the fountain water. A Harbourside employee was notified but dismissed the concern. The fountain was not taken out of service after this prior incident.

## Investigation Findings

| Agency | Finding |
|--------|---------|
| Jupiter Police Department | Released 149-page incident report October 31, 2023; death ruled accidental; no criminal charges filed |
| Town of Jupiter Building Department | November 6, 2023: 74-page letter from Interim Building Official Jim Brown declaring fountain electrical system unsafe; light fixtures confirmed to be leaking electrical voltage due to ground fault or improper system; required professional evaluation, repairs, or demolition |
| Medical Examiner | Cause of death: electrocution, accidental |
| Fountain builder | Van Kirk & Sons Pools & Spas — had not performed work in 5–7 years |
| Fountain maintainer | Tranquil Waterscapes — performed weekly cleaning but no electrical inspections |

The Town of Jupiter Building Department inspection confirmed light fixtures within the fountain were the source of the voltage leak. The electrical system was declared unsafe; the fountain was drained, tarped, and powered down.

---

## Legal Outcome

Davenport's widow filed a wrongful death lawsuit on December 27, 2023, alleging negligence in maintenance and prior knowledge of hazards (the July 2023 incident). An out-of-court settlement was reached during mediation on March 6, 2024, approved by a judge. Settlement terms are undisclosed. As of March 2024, Harbourside had not confirmed plans to repair or restart the fountain.

The James DeAngelo Foundation (PA, separate incident) and Carnegie Hero Fund both recognized Davenport's act of attempting to rescue his children from an electrified water hazard.

## Classification Notes

**ESD-adjacent but distinct mechanism:** This is not dock/marina ESD in the traditional sense. The electrical source is a public fountain with faulty light fixtures, not dock wiring or shore power. However, the mechanism — electrical current from infrastructure leaking into water, incapacitating bathers — is identical to ESD. This is the same phenomenon that occurs in marina water; the infrastructure type differs (outdoor fountain vs. dock).

**Why not in ESDPA:** The ESDPA/Shafer-Rifkin list focuses on marina and dock-adjacent incidents. Public fountain electrocutions are typically outside their scope. CPSC tracks pool/spa electrocutions which would include fountain incidents.

**Significance:** The July 10, 2023 prior incident — with a visible wire and 15 people shocked — that went uninvestigated represents a clear regulatory/operational failure that preceded the fatal October 22 incident. Jupiter town building department records confirm the fountain's electrical system was defective.
