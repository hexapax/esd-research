## Incident Narrative

In the early morning hours of July 4, 2024, Jesse Hamric entered the water at a private dock on Lee Drive at Smith Mountain Lake. He immediately became unresponsive. Two friends who observed him in distress jumped into the water to help and immediately felt electrical shock. The friends pulled Hamric out of the water and began CPR before rescue units arrived. Hamric was transported to a hospital where he was pronounced dead.

Smith Mountain Lake Marine Volunteer Fire and Rescue Assistant Chief Ray Talley responded at the scene. First responders used a Shock Alert device and measured voltage readings exceeding 200 millivolts per foot (maximum device reading). Talley reported that when the electric boat lift at the dock was raised out of the water, the voltage reading disappeared entirely, identifying the boat lift as the source of the stray voltage.

Per Cardinal News (August 7, 2024): stray voltage readings "reading off the charts" at scene; "The voltage reading disappeared once the boat lift was raised out of the water."

This was confirmed as the first ESD fatality responded to by the Smith Mountain Lake Marine Volunteer Fire Department, despite the department having responded to stray voltage calls for "maybe three years" prior.
