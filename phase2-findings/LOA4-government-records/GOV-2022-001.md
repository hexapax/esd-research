## Narrative

At 3:45 p.m. on August 3, 2022, an employee was operating a mobile boat lift to move boats into marina slips when the lift made contact with overhead high-power lines. When the lift contacted the electrical lines, the employee was electrocuted and fell out of the operator's cab to the ground. A customer of the marina called for emergency services. The employee was pronounced dead at the scene.

OSHA keywords: Aerial Lift, Boat, Contact, Crane Cab, Electrocuted, Fall, Fall From Elevation, Lift Operator, Overhead Power Line.

Industry classification: **NAICS 713930 (Marinas)** — this is a direct marina employee fatality in OSHA records.

## Analysis

**This is an occupational fatality at a marina involving electricity, but NOT ESD by mechanism.** The victim was a marina employee operating a mobile boat lift (a mobile mechanical crane used to move boats). The lift's boom contacted overhead high-voltage power lines, electrocuting the operator through the lift structure. The victim was in the operator's cab and fell to the ground — he was not in or near water when the electrocution occurred.

This incident is electrically unrelated to shore power / dock wiring / stray current in water (the ESD mechanism). The hazard is an overhead power line struck by a mobile piece of equipment — the same hazard type as GOV-2017-001 (Boy Scouts, overhead power line and mast) and GOV-2021-001 (sailboat mast and power line, Lake Clyde TX).

**Why not in ESDPA:** This is a marina worker fatality from an overhead power line, not recreational ESD from dock/shore power wiring. The setting is a marina but the mechanism is completely different from ESD.

**Significance for research:** This record confirms OSHA actively investigates marina-setting fatalities under NAICS 713930. Searches of OSHA's accident database using NAICS 713930 (Marinas) would identify all OSHA-investigated fatalities at marina workplaces, which could include ESD-mechanism incidents involving marina workers.
