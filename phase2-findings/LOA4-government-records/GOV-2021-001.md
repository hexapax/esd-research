## Description

Trevor Cate entered Clyde Lake on the night of June 2, 2021, to swim-tow a disabled sailboat back to shore. While he was in the water towing the sailboat, the boat's mast struck an overhead power line crossing the lake. The power line fell into the water and discharged electrical current. Cate was struck by the current, became paralyzed, and submerged. Lake staff cut power to the line; officers retrieved Cate from the water and began resuscitation. He was transported to Hendrick Medical Center in Abilene and died the following morning, June 3, 2021.

## Cause

Overhead power line crossing the lake struck by sailboat mast; line fell into water and discharged current into lake where swimmer was present. Cate was in the water at the moment of contact.

## Classification Notes

**This is a water electrocution from an overhead power line, not standard ESD from dock/marina wiring.** The mechanism involves an overhead power line crossing the lake being struck by a sailboat mast, causing the line to fall into water where the victim was swimming. This is the same overhead-line category as GOV-2017-001 (Lake O' the Pines Boy Scouts). The ESDPA list does not typically include overhead power line strikes. Documented here for completeness.

Note: Do not confuse with FI-2021-003 (June 3, 2021, Mission TX — a 14-year-old boy electrocuted in a puddle by an extension cord). Both are June 3, 2021 Texas incidents but completely separate.
