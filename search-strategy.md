# Electric Shock Drowning (ESD) Search Strategy

**Purpose:** Guide systematic research to find ESD incidents NOT listed in the ESDPA database, identify misclassified drownings that may be ESD, and quantify ESD's share of total drowning deaths.

**Based on:** Analysis of 175+ incident research files in incident-research/ (FI-* and NM-* series), esd-search-terms.md, research-summary.md, and ISSUES.md.

**Date:** March 5, 2026

---

## SECTION 1: Incident Pattern Profile

### What Do Verified ESD Incidents Look Like?

Based on research into 175+ incidents from 1981-2025, here are the characteristic patterns of confirmed Electric Shock Drowning events:

#### 1.1 Most Common Locations (by frequency)

**HIGH FREQUENCY:**
- **Marina docks** (30+ incidents) — Both commercial and private marinas
- **Houseboats** (15+ incidents) — At dock or anchored
- **Private residential docks** (12+ incidents) — Lakefront homes
- **Boat lifts** (5+ incidents) — Electrical faults in lift mechanisms

**MODERATE FREQUENCY:**
- Public swimming areas with docks (5+ incidents)
- Rivers near docks (3 incidents)
- Canals with infrastructure lighting (2 incidents)

**LOW FREQUENCY:**
- Swimming pools (2 incidents)
- Campus/community ponds (2 incidents)
- **Salt water locations** (2 incidents) — UNUSUAL: ESD rarely occurs in salt water due to high conductivity causing immediate circuit breaker trips; these cases may be misclassified

#### 1.2 Most Common Electrical Causes (by frequency)

**PRIMARY CAUSES:**
1. **Shore power cord faults** (20+ incidents)
   - Miswired connections (hot/ground reversed)
   - Damaged insulation from wear/corrosion
   - "125V plug rewired for 220V with ground wire cut" (multiple cases)
   - Improper modifications by boat owners

2. **Dock wiring faults** (18+ incidents)
   - Corroded junction boxes
   - Failed weatherproofing
   - Metal junction boxes used instead of fiberglass (code violation)
   - Underwater cable damage

3. **Reverse polarity** (8 incidents)
   - Hot and ground wires reversed in connections
   - Creates electrified water even when equipment is "off"

4. **Ungrounded/cut ground systems** (7+ incidents)
   - Ground wires intentionally cut during amateur modifications
   - Missing ground connections

5. **Boat lift electrical faults** (5 incidents)
   - Rebar penetrating electrical cables
   - Damaged lift motor wiring

6. **Submersible pump failures** (4+ incidents, Oklahoma cluster 1989-1993)
   - Failed pump seals allowing water ingress
   - Insulation breakdown

7. **Failed circuit protection** (multiple incidents)
   - Circuit breakers failing to trip despite dangerous current
   - GFCIs "rarely trip in freshwater conditions" (expert testimony)

#### 1.3 Most Common Victim Behaviors/Circumstances

**AT TIME OF INCIDENT:**
- **Swimming near dock or boat** (35+ incidents) — Most common scenario
- **Touching or climbing swim ladder** (12+ incidents) — Ladder often becomes energized path
- **Rescue attempts** (15+ incidents) — Parent, friend, neighbor, spouse, or stranger jumps in to help; often fatal for rescuer
- **Getting on/off boat** (8+ incidents) — Transition from water to vessel
- **Diving/scuba diving** (6 incidents)
- **Playing/jumping off docks** (5+ incidents) — Children and teenagers
- **Retrieving objects from water** (3 incidents) — Sunglasses, football, dropped items
- **Tubing, water sliding from boat** (3 incidents)
- **Working on boat electrical system** (3 incidents) — Tradespeople or owners

**CRITICAL PATTERN:** Victims wearing **life jackets still drown** due to paralysis from electric current. Multiple cases document face-down drowning despite flotation devices.

#### 1.4 Common Witness Language (Before ESD Confirmed)

These phrases appear in news reports, 911 calls, and witness statements BEFORE electrical cause was discovered:

**IMMEDIATE DISTRESS DESCRIPTIONS:**
- "In great distress"
- "Started screaming then went under"
- "Immediately surfaced screaming, then submerged"
- "Called for help once then disappeared"

**PARALYSIS/INABILITY TO MOVE:**
- "Paralyzed" / "couldn't move"
- "Couldn't swim" (despite being known swimmer)
- "Froze up"
- "Went rigid"
- "Shaking her head no" (while in water, unable to speak)

**ELECTRICAL SENSATIONS:**
- "Felt a shock"
- "Felt tingling" (often 10-20 feet from source)
- "Numbness"
- "Felt like being tased"
- "Saw a red flash"
- "Started to shake and then fell into the water"

**UNUSUAL PHYSICAL SYMPTOMS:**
- "Bleeding from eyes, ears, and nose"
- "Burns on skin"
- "Face never touched water" (but drowned — Lucas Ritz case)

**CIRCUIT/EQUIPMENT DESCRIPTIONS:**
- "The boy on the ladder said he was being shocked"
- "Felt shock sensation 20 feet from dock"
- "Knocked unconscious as soon as he hit the water"
- "Seized by electrical current"

**PHRASES INDICATING SKILLED SWIMMER:**
- "Exceptional swimmer" — Often used to describe ESD victims, emphasizing the drowning was unexpected
- "Strong swimmer"
- "Swim team member"

#### 1.5 Seasonal Patterns

**PEAK SEASON (May-August): ~80% of incidents**
- **May:** 8+ incidents
- **June:** 12+ incidents
- **July:** 15+ incidents (highest)
- **August:** 12+ incidents
- **September:** 5 incidents

**OFF-SEASON (Sept-April): ~20% of incidents**
- **October-November:** 2-3 incidents
- **December:** 2 incidents (Oklahoma cluster — unusual)
- **January-February:** 1-2 incidents
- **March-April:** 3-4 incidents

**PATTERN:** Strong correlation with recreational boating season. Peak aligns with holiday weekends (Memorial Day, July 4th, Labor Day).

#### 1.6 Geographic Hotspots

**STATES WITH MULTIPLE DOCUMENTED INCIDENTS:**

**HIGH FREQUENCY (5+ incidents):**
- **Oklahoma** (5+ incidents) — "Lake A" cluster in northeastern OK (1989-1993): 4 deaths at same submersible pump system
- **Tennessee** (8+ incidents) — Tims Ford Lake (2+), Center Hill Lake, Chickamauga Lake, Cherokee Lake, Norris Lake
- **Arkansas** (5 incidents) — **Lake Hamilton (3 deaths: 1991, 2001, 2008)**, Lake Ouachita, Bull Shoals Lake
- **Michigan** (5+ incidents) — Bay Haven Marina, River Street Marina, Clinch Marina, Lake Pepin

**MODERATE FREQUENCY (2-4 incidents):**
- **Florida** (3+ incidents) — Cape Coral/North Fort Myers area, Palmetto Bay
- **California** (3 incidents) — Lake Sonoma, Rio Vista
- **Kentucky** (2+ incidents) — Lake Cumberland, Harrods Creek, Rough River Lake
- **Virginia** (2+ incidents) — Lake Montclair (double fatality 2001), Smith Mountain Lake
- **West Virginia** (2 incidents) — Stonewall Jackson Lake
- **Ohio** (2 incidents) — Put-in-Bay

**SPECIFIC WATER BODIES WITH MULTIPLE INCIDENTS:**
- **Lake Hamilton, AR:** 3 confirmed deaths (1991, 2001, 2008) — likely more unreported
- **"Lake A" (northeastern Oklahoma):** CDC documented 5 deaths 1989-1993 at same location
- **Tims Ford Lake, TN:** 2+ incidents documented, "approximately 2013" incident referenced
- **Smith Mountain Lake, VA:** Multiple incidents spanning decades

#### 1.7 Victim Age Ranges and Demographics

**AGE DISTRIBUTION (incidents reviewed):**
- **Children (under 13):** 7yo, 8yo (2), 10yo, 11yo, 13yo — approximately 8-10 incidents
- **Teenagers (13-19):** 13yo, 14yo, 15yo (4), 16yo, 18yo (2), 19yo, 20yo (3) — approximately 12-15 incidents
- **Adults (20-50):** 21-22yo, 32yo, 36yo (2), 41yo, 46yo, 50yo (2), 52yo — approximately 20-25 incidents
- **Older adults (50+):** 55yo, 74yo — approximately 5-8 incidents

**GENDER PATTERN:**
- **Predominantly male victims** (approximately 70-75% of reviewed incidents)
- Notable female victims: Mothers (Donna Berger, age 55), daughters (Deirdre McCormick, age 10), women swimmers

**OCCUPATION/ROLE PATTERNS:**
- **First responders/heroes:** Detective Jack Deuser (off-duty police officer, 41, died rescuing wife)
- **Tradespeople:** Plumber electrocuted snaking flooded basement drain (James Stanley, 2023)
- **Rescue attempts by family:** Parents rescuing children (15+ incidents)

#### 1.8 Types Most Likely to Be Misclassified as Plain Drowning

**HIGH MISCLASSIFICATION RISK:**

1. **"Exceptional swimmer" drownings near docks/boats**
   - Lucas Ritz (8yo, Oregon, 1999): Initially ruled drowning; father's investigation revealed electrocution; death certificate later amended
   - Glen Howard (52yo, Arkansas, 2003): Initially suspected diving accident; electrical fault discovered later
   - Donna Berger (55yo, Tennessee, 2016): Described as "exceptional swimmer"; official report says drowning (family believes ESD but TWRA found no evidence)

2. **Drownings where autopsy shows "no water in lungs"**
   - Michael Cunningham (West Virginia, 2010): "Autopsy showed no water in lungs — death was instantaneous electrocution, not drowning"
   - Indicates victim died BEFORE submersion (electrical cause likely)

3. **Multiple simultaneous victims in same water**
   - Lake Montclair double fatality (Joseph Salute, 15; Maxwell Casillas, 11; Virginia 2001)
   - Lopez brothers (Alex 12, Isaiah 11; Texas 2017 — downed power line)
   - Lake Hamilton incidents with witnesses reporting "others felt shock"

4. **Witness reports of "paralysis" or "couldn't move"**
   - Bryan Higgins (SUNY Albany, 1988): Initially investigated as hazing/drowning death; autopsy confirmed electrocution
   - Multiple incidents where skilled swimmers "froze" in water

5. **Drownings during/after electrical storms**
   - Downed power lines in water
   - Lightning damage to dock electrical systems

6. **Rescue attempt deaths**
   - Skilled swimmer enters water to save someone, immediately struggles and drowns
   - Detective Jack Deuser case (1986): Off-duty police officer drowned rescuing wife

7. **Cases with GFCI/breaker failures**
   - "GFCIs rarely trip in freshwater conditions" (expert testimony, Clinch Marina case)
   - Circuit breakers failing to trip despite dangerous current levels

## SECTION 2: Gaps in the ESDPA List

Based on independent source verification of 175+ incidents, the following systematic gaps, errors, and underrepresented categories have been identified:

### 2.1 Incidents Where ESDPA Date/Location/Classification May Be Wrong Enough to Be Different Incidents

**SEVERE DATE ERRORS (>6 months off):**

1. **FI-1999-001 (Tims Ford Lake, TN)**
   - ESDPA date: August 1, 1999
   - Actual date: September 30, 2000
   - **ERROR: 13+ months off, wrong year entirely**

2. **FI-2002-001 (North Fort Myers, FL)**
   - ESDPA date: May 31, 2002
   - Actual date: May 8, 2003
   - **ERROR: ~1 year off, wrong year**

3. **FI-2008-001 (Stonewall Jackson Lake, WV)**
   - ESDPA date: Aug 23, 2008
   - Actual date: May 29, 2010
   - **ERROR: 2 years off**

4. **FI-2010-001 (Tennessee)**
   - ESDPA date: July 26, 2010
   - Actual date: May 28, 2011
   - **ERROR: 1 year off**

5. **FI-2017-001 (Oklahoma City Bricktown Canal)**
   - ESDPA date: Sep 1, 2017
   - Actual date: September 30, 2018
   - **ERROR: 394 days off, wrong year**

6. **FI-2016-001 (Lopez Brothers, Fort Worth TX)**
   - ESDPA date: Sep 10, 2016
   - Actual date: March 29, 2017
   - **ERROR: 18 months off, wrong year**

**LOCATION ERRORS:**

1. **FI-1986-003 (Detective Jack Deuser)**
   - ESDPA location: Lexington, KY
   - Actual location: Louisville, KY (Harrods Creek)
   - **ERROR: ~80 miles apart, different metro areas**

2. **FI-2002-001**
   - ESDPA location: Cape Coral, FL
   - Actual location: North Fort Myers, FL

3. **FI-1999-004**
   - ESDPA location: "Cedar Hill Lake"
   - Actual location: **Center Hill Lake** (lake name wrong)

**OUTCOME MISCLASSIFICATIONS:**

1. **NM-2016-001 (Chickamauga Lake, TN) — CRITICAL ERROR**
   - ESDPA classification: **Near Miss** ("All survived")
   - Actual outcome: **DOUBLE FATALITY** (Donna Berger, 55; Randy Freeney, 74)
   - Only 13yo boy survived
   - **Should be reclassified as FI-2016-**
   - ADDITIONAL ISSUE: ESD classification itself unverified — TWRA official report says standard drowning; only family believes it was ESD

**PHANTOM/DUPLICATE ENTRIES:**

1. **FI-2022-001 — LIKELY DOES NOT EXIST**
   - Listed as: Aug 16, 2022, Detroit MI, plumber electrocuted in flooded basement
   - **PROBLEM:** Description is word-for-word identical to **FI-2023-001** (James Stanley, Feb 7, 2023, same location)
   - No news coverage found for August 2022 incident
   - Extensive coverage found for February 2023 incident
   - **ASSESSMENT:** FI-2022-001 is almost certainly a phantom duplicate that inflates incident count by 1

2. **FI-1997-001 — POSSIBLE DUPLICATE**
   - Listed as: July 1997, boy electrocuted on ice at dock, AF base, Washington DC
   - **PROBLEM:** Ice in Washington DC in July is physically impossible (avg high ~90°F)
   - Very similar to **FI-1994-001** (also boy at AF base dock in DC)
   - No independent sources found
   - May be duplicate with severely wrong date

**SCIENTIFICALLY IMPLAUSIBLE INCIDENTS:**

1. **FI-1993-001 (Texas, salt water)**
   - Listed as ESD in salt water
   - **PROBLEM:** ESD extremely rare in salt water due to high conductivity causing immediate circuit breaker trips
   - Zero independent verification found
   - May be misclassified contact electrocution OR may not have occurred as described

### 2.2 Years or Regions That Seem Underrepresented

**YEARS WITH ZERO OR VERY FEW INCIDENTS (Gaps suggesting missing data):**

- **1990** — No incidents found
- **1992** — No incidents found
- **1996** — No incidents found
- **2004** — Very few incidents (1-2) despite surrounding years having 4-6
- **2005** — Very few incidents
- **2007** — Minimal incidents
- **2009** — No incidents found

**PATTERN:** Some years show zero incidents despite being within high-activity periods (late 1980s-early 1990s had many; 2006 had 6+ incidents; 2008-2010 had many). The gaps likely reflect reporting/documentation failures rather than actual absence of incidents.

**GEOGRAPHIC REGIONS UNDERREPRESENTED:**

**SEVERELY UNDERREPRESENTED (expected more based on boating activity):**
- **Pacific Northwest** — Oregon (1 incident), Washington (0-1), Idaho (0)
  - Extensive lake/river recreational boating culture suggests underreporting
- **Southwest** — Arizona (0 beyond Lakes Mead/Mohave reference), Nevada (minimal), New Mexico (0)
  - Lake Mead, Lake Mohave, Lake Havasu are major boating destinations
- **Great Plains** — Kansas (0), Nebraska (0), South Dakota (0), North Dakota (0)
  - Large reservoirs on Missouri River system (Lake Sakakawea, Lake Oahe, etc.) with significant boating
- **Texas** — Only 1 documented salt water incident (1993, scientifically questionable) + 1 downed power line (2017)
  - Texas has extensive lake recreation (Lake Travis, Lake Texoma, Toledo Bend, Sam Rayburn, etc.)
  - Only 2 incidents in state with 30+ million people and year-round boating season
- **Northeast** — New York (1-2 incidents), Pennsylvania (1), New England nearly absent
  - Finger Lakes (NY), Lake Champlain, Maine lakes significantly underrepresented
- **Mid-Atlantic** — Beyond Virginia (2), very minimal representation
  - Maryland (0), Delaware (0) despite extensive Chesapeake Bay boating
- **Carolinas** — North Carolina (1-2), South Carolina (1 approximate date)
  - Many large lakes (Norman, Wylie, Murray, Hartwell, Keowee) with minimal incidents reported

**MODERATELY UNDERREPRESENTED:**
- **Wisconsin** — 1-2 incidents despite extensive Great Lakes and inland lake boating
- **Minnesota** — 1 incident (Lake Pepin) despite "Land of 10,000 Lakes"
- **Illinois** — Very minimal despite Chain O'Lakes, Lake Shelbyville, Carlyle Lake
- **Georgia** — 1-2 incidents (Blue Ridge Lake 2025) despite Lake Lanier, Oconee, Sinclair

### 2.3 Cases Where ESD Was Suspected But Not Officially Confirmed

**SUSPECTED BUT UNCONFIRMED ESD:**

1. **NM-2016-001 (Chickamauga Lake, TN — Berger/Freeney)**
   - Family (Annette Goodman) convinced it was ESD
   - Featured in WSOC-TV investigative report on ESD dangers
   - Official TWRA investigation: standard drowning, no mention of electrical cause
   - All local Chattanooga news: reported as drowning without electrical component
   - **STATUS:** Suspected by family, no official confirmation

2. **FI-2025-002 (Blue Ridge Lake, GA)**
   - "Details pending" per raw data
   - Scuba diver boy drowning
   - Primary source paywalled (The News Observer, Blue Ridge GA)
   - Electrical cause suspected but not confirmed in available sources
   - **STATUS:** Awaiting official confirmation from coroner

3. **Multiple "details pending" cases:**
   - **FI-2024-001 (Smith Mountain Lake, VA):** Initially listed as "details pending" but later confirmed as ESD
   - **FI-2021-001 (Louisville, KY):** "Details pending — coroner noted electricity, no specific source"

### 2.4 Special Case: NM-2016-001 Misclassification

**VERIFIED DETAILS:**
- **ESDPA Classification:** Near Miss (NM)

- **ESD Status:** UNVERIFIED
  - Sister Annette Goodman believes it was ESD
  - Official TWRA investigation: standard drowning
  - No electrical fault found
  - May not be ESD at all

1. Reclassified from NM to FI
2. Date corrected to June 17, 2016 (not May 29 or July 3)

## SECTION 4: Search Terms for Misclassified Drownings

### 4.1 Core Concept

ESD is frequently initially classified as plain drowning because:
1. Victims appear to have drowned (water in/near lungs, submersion, etc.)
2. Electrical cause not immediately obvious (wiring underwater, no visible sparks)
3. First responders/coroners may not check for electrical current in water
4. Electrical faults may be intermittent or resolved by time of investigation

**PROVEN PATTERN:** Lucas Ritz (1999), Michael Cunningham (2010), Bryan Higgins (1988), Glen Howard (2003) were ALL initially classified as drowning, later changed to electrocution.

1. Pacific Northwest (WA, OR, ID)
2. Arizona/Nevada (Lake Mead, Mohave, Havasu, Powell)
3. Carolinas (Lake Norman, Murray, Hartwell, etc.)
4. Minnesota/Wisconsin (severely underrepresented)
5. Tennessee lakes (known hotspot, likely more unreported)

**TIER 3 (Moderate Value):**
1. Northeast (Finger Lakes, Lake Champlain)
2. Holiday weekend searches (Memorial Day, July 4, Labor Day)
3. Houseboat-specific incidents
4. Military base incidents
5. University/college campus incidents

### A.2 Database/Archive Access

**FREE RESOURCES:**
- Google News Archive
- Local library newspaper archives (many have free access with library card)
- CourtListener (free federal court records)
- Legacy.com (obituaries)
- OSHA.gov, CPSC.gov, CDC.gov (government data)

**PAID/SUBSCRIPTION RESOURCES:**
- Newspapers.com ($)
- NewspaperArchive.com ($)
- GenealogyBank ($)
- PACER (federal court records, minimal fee per page)
- LexisNexis (expensive but comprehensive)

### A.3 Verification Standards

When an incident is found, verify with:
1. **Minimum 2 independent sources** (not ESDPA-derived)
2. **Primary sources preferred:** News articles, court records, coroner reports
3. **Secondary sources acceptable:** Obituaries, industry publications, government reports
4. **Tertiary sources questionable:** Forum posts, social media (unless verified against primary)

### A.4 Red Flags for False Positives

**EXCLUDE:**
- Contact electrocution (direct contact with live wire, not through water)
- Lightning strikes (not infrastructure electrical fault)
- Electrocution that occurred on land, body fell into water afterward
- International incidents (unless explicitly seeking global data)
- Bathtub electrocutions (different hazard profile than ESD)

### A.5 Data Recording

For each incident found, record:
- **Date** (exact if possible, approximate if not)
- **Location** (city, state, water body, marina name if applicable)
- **Victim(s)** (name, age, gender if available)
- **Circumstances** (swimming, rescue, boat ladder, etc.)
- **Electrical cause** (if determined)
- **Source(s)** (URL, publication, date)
- **ESDPA status** (listed/not listed; if listed, ID number)
- **Verification level** (confirmed/probable/suspected)
