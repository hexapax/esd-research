## Patterns and Observations

1. **Date accuracy is very poor**: 13 of 16 serious flaws involve incorrect dates. Even among verified incidents, the ESDPA date was wrong in 8 out of 15 cases. Errors range from one month to over a year.

2. **Older incidents are harder to verify**: Pre-2000 incidents have a much lower verification rate, likely due to limited digital archives for local news from the 1980s-1990s.

3. **Recent incidents are well-documented**: FI-2023-001, FI-2024-001, FI-2025-001, and FI-2025-002 are all strongly verified with rich independent detail.

4. **Data quality concerns**: The high rate of date errors, the phantom duplicate (FI-2022-001), the possible duplicate (FI-1997-001 / FI-1994-001), and the scientifically questionable salt water incident (FI-1993-001) suggest the ESDPA source list has significant data quality issues that should be considered when citing incident counts or specific case details.

5. **Oklahoma cluster well-documented**: The 1989-1993 Oklahoma lake deaths are well-documented by a CDC MMWR report, though the ESDPA dates for individual incidents within this cluster are consistently wrong.
