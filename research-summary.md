### Internally Impossible Dates

Two incidents have dates that are physically or meteorologically impossible:

- **FI-1997-001:** Listed as July 1997 in Washington, DC, but describes a boy walking on **ice**. July temperatures in DC average ~90F. The actual month is likely January or February.
- **FI-2020-002:** Listed as August 30, 2020, during "Tropical Storm Eta." Tropical Storm Eta did not exist until **November 2020**. The correct date is November 11, 2020.

### The FI-1995-001 Date Anomaly

FI-1995-001 (Lake Mead houseboat drowning) is listed as February 1995 in the current ESDPA list. However, all earlier publicly accessible versions of the same list record this incident as **July 1997**. No independent sources exist to resolve which date (if either) is correct.

## Most Significant Corrections Found

### 1. NM-2016-001: Double Fatality Classified as "All Survived"

**The single most significant error in the dataset.** This incident at Chickamauga Lake, TN (June 17, 2016) is classified in the ESDPA list as a Near Miss with "all survived." Independent sources unanimously confirm this was a **double fatality**: Donna Berger (55) and Randy Freeney (74) both died. Only the 13-year-old boy (Zachary Berger) survived. Multiple local news outlets, obituaries, and emergency dispatch records confirm the deaths.

### 2. FI-2022-001: Phantom Duplicate Entry

FI-2022-001 (Detroit flooded basement, August 16, 2022) is word-for-word identical to FI-2023-001 (James Stanley, Russell Woods Apartments, February 7, 2023). No evidence exists for any August 2022 incident matching this description. This is a phantom entry that inflates the ESDPA incident count.

### 3. FI-2017-010: Two Separate Incidents Merged Into One

The ESDPA entry for FI-2017-010 (Laredo, TX) describes "two brothers" dying in a flooded field. Independent sources confirm only **one victim** died in Laredo (Aldo Jordani Rojas, 14, May 21, 2017). The "two brothers" detail was conflated from a completely separate incident in Fort Worth, TX (Lopez brothers, March 29, 2017).

### 4. FI-2008-001: Date Off by Nearly Two Years

The ESDPA date of August 23, 2008 for this Stonewall Jackson Lake, WV incident is off by nearly two years. The correct date is May 29, 2010. Furthermore, the cause of death was **electrocution** (autopsy showed no water in lungs), not drowning. Victim Michael Andrew Cunningham's death led to the Michael Cunningham Act in West Virginia.

### 5. FI-2002-001: Wrong Date, Wrong Year, Wrong Location

Listed as May 31, 2002 in Cape Coral, FL. The correct date is **May 8, 2003** in **North Fort Myers, FL**. Both the year and the city are wrong.

### 6. FI-1986-003: Wrong Date and Wrong City

Listed as June 8, 1986 in Lexington, KY. The correct date is **July 29, 1986** in **Louisville, KY** -- approximately 80 miles from the listed location. The victim was Detective Jack S. Deuser of the Jefferson County Police Department.

## Pattern Summary: What Verified ESD Incidents Look Like

Based on the verified incidents, a typical ESD event has these characteristics:

**Location:** Marina dock (most common), houseboat, residential dock, boat lift, or swimming pool. Less commonly: irrigation canals, fountains, flooded areas during storms.

**Cause:** Shore power faults, dock wiring defects, missing/failed GFCI protection, improper grounding, reverse polarity, unpermitted electrical work, deteriorated wiring insulation, wrong-amperage connections (e.g., 30-amp plug in 50-amp outlet).

**Timing:** Overwhelmingly May through August (~80% of incidents). July is the peak month. Holiday weekends (July 4th, Memorial Day, Labor Day) are disproportionately represented.

**Victims:** Predominantly male (70-75%). Children and teenagers are overrepresented relative to their share of water recreation. Rescue attempts are a major secondary cause of death -- multiple incidents involve 2-3 additional victims who entered the water to help.

**Warning signs frequently present but unrecognized:**
- Prior reports of "tingling" in the water
- Circuit breakers repeatedly tripping
- Dogs refusing to enter water or dying in water near docks
- "Reverse polarity" indicator lights flickering

**Common misclassification:** Many ESD deaths were initially classified as ordinary drownings. Autopsies sometimes rule "electrocution" rather than "drowning" when no water is found in the lungs.

## Limitations

1. **Pre-internet incidents (pre-1995):** Newspaper archives from the 1980s and early 1990s are largely not digitized. Many incidents from this era cannot be verified or refuted.

2. **Near misses are inherently hard to verify.** Non-fatal incidents where everyone survived rarely generate lasting documentation.

3. **Paywalled sources:** Several potentially relevant articles (e.g., The News Observer in Blue Ridge, GA; Arkansas Democrat-Gazette archives) are behind paywalls that could not be accessed.

4. **ESDPA-derivative sources excluded:** Many online references to these incidents trace back to the ESDPA list itself (often via mikeholt.com). These were not counted as independent verification to avoid circular sourcing.

5. **Date corrections are based on best available evidence.** In some cases, independent sources disagree with each other. Where this occurred, the date supported by the most sources and internal consistency checks (day-of-week matching, weather records, etc.) was preferred.

6. **Verification ≠ Confirmation of ESD.** Some verified incidents may not actually be electric shock drowning -- they may be ordinary drownings, direct electrocution on land, lightning strikes, or other causes. Verification confirms that an incident occurred roughly as described, not necessarily that ESD was the mechanism.

7. **Unknown unknowns.** We cannot assess the completeness of the ESDPA list. The search strategy document identifies significant geographic and temporal gaps suggesting the true count of ESD incidents is likely higher than 175.
