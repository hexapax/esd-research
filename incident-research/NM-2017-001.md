## Corrections Needed

1. **DATE DISCREPANCY.** The original file states "July 24, 2017." Multiple independent sources (CBC, Castanet.net, Vernon Morning Star) consistently report the incident occurred on **July 23, 2017**. The date should be corrected to July 23, 2017.

2. **LOCATION IS IMPRECISE.** The original states "Kelowna, British Columbia Canada." The incident actually occurred in **Spallumcheen**, a township in the Okanagan region between Vernon and Enderby. Kelowna was where the victim was airlifted for medical treatment, not the location of the incident. The location should be corrected to "Spallumcheen, British Columbia, Canada" or "Spallumcheen (near Vernon/Enderby), BC, Canada."

3. **PUMP TYPE.** The original describes it as a "pond pump." The CBC News article and fire chief specifically identified it as an **irrigation pump** (submersible pump in the creek). While functionally similar, "irrigation pump" is more accurate.

4. **VICTIM DESCRIPTION.** The original says "a man was badly shocked." Independent sources reveal the victim was **Cameron (Cam) Brooks, age 28**, who waded into the water up to his thighs, fell backwards, pulled himself out, suffered leg burns, was airlifted to hospital, and was released the same night. The description could be more complete.

5. **SEVERITY CLARIFICATION.** While the man survived, the incident was more severe than "badly shocked" might suggest. He required emergency airlift to a hospital and suffered burns. Fire officials stated he would likely have died if he had gone deeper into the water. The description understates the severity.
