### Notes

- The description refers to the victim as "a boy," which suggests a minor. A fatality involving a child would typically receive local news coverage, yet no newspaper articles were found. This may indicate: (a) the incident occurred but was not covered by media accessible through modern web searches; (b) the details in the file are sparse enough that search engines cannot match them; or (c) the incident details may be imprecise.
- The incident is categorized under "Electric Shock Drownings" in the source material, but the description does not mention drowning -- it says "electrocuted," which could mean either direct electrocution or electric shock drowning. The classification notes say "Commercial/fishing context," suggesting this occurred at a commercial shrimp operation (dock, boat, or processing facility) rather than a recreational marina setting.
- The OSHA database contains no record of this fatality. If the victim was a minor working informally in a family fishing operation (common in Gulf Coast shrimping communities), OSHA may not have been notified or may not have had jurisdiction.
- The incident file lists the date as April 10, 2001. One search result from the 2CoolFishing forum referenced an April 10, 2001 incident at "Norris Lake, Lafayette, TN" rather than New Orleans -- this may indicate date or location confusion across different entries in the source list, though without access to the original forum content this cannot be confirmed.
