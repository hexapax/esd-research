Multiple independent sources (Deseret News, The Salt Lake Tribune, Fox 13 Now, Lake Powell Life, and the obituary) confirm the incident occurred on **June 10, 2014**. The date should be corrected from "April 23, 2014" to "June 10, 2014" (a nearly 2-month discrepancy).

2. **Victim's identity.** The original only identifies the victim as "22yo." He should be properly identified as Alec McQueen, 22, of Salt Lake City, Utah.

3. **Location name correction.** The original states "Lake Powell UT, Bullfrog Lake Marina." The correct name is "Bullfrog Marina" (not "Bullfrog Lake Marina"). Bullfrog Marina is located on Lake Powell within Glen Canyon National Recreation Area, on the Utah side of the lake.

4. **Description of "leaking current from adjacent slip" is accurate.** The original correctly identifies that the electrical current came from an adjacent slip. The investigation confirmed that a boat in the neighboring slip had a 30-amp power cord improperly inserted into a worn 50-amp outlet, causing electricity to discharge into the water.

5. **Detail about surfacing briefly.** The original states the victim "surfaced briefly" after jumping. According to witness accounts in the lawsuit, McQueen did call out to his friend after jumping in, suggesting he was briefly at the surface or visible before disappearing. This detail is accurate.

6. **Electrical cause confirmed.** The original correctly identifies electrocution as the cause. This is confirmed by the shocks received by McQueen's friend and two rescuers, and by the investigation that found the electrical hazard in the adjacent slip.
