# Research: FI-1991-001

## Original Incident Summary
On May 11, 1991, a single drowning occurred in Oklahoma. A fault in dock lights had energized the dock frame in a fresh water environment. One person died. The incident is listed as item #107 (inferred) in the ESDPA source document.

## Sources Found

### Source 1
**URL:** https://www.cdc.gov/mmwr/preview/mmwrhtml/00042081.htm
**Title:** Electricity-Related Deaths on Lakes -- Oklahoma, 1989-1993 (CDC MMWR, March 10, 1995)
**Relevant Information:**
- Between June 1989 and September 1993, five males aged 13-50 died from electrical contact on Oklahoma lakes.
- Four deaths occurred at "Lake A" in northeastern Oklahoma; one at "Lake B" in southern Oklahoma.
- **Case 3 (July 24, 1991):** A 50-year-old male was found unconscious on a boat dock at Lake A. He was wearing wet socks and shoes. An electrical short was detected in the dock wiring. His son was shocked while attempting rescue. Cause of death: electrocution.
- **Case 2 (July 27, 1993):** A 13-year-old boy jumped from a dock into Lake A while dock lights were operating. An electrical short in the wiring transmitted current into the water through the dock's metal frame. Cause of death: drowning, possibly secondary to electrical shock.
- A 1989 inspection of 116 commercial docks at Lake A revealed 96% violated the National Electrical Code. Ungrounded electrical systems were the most common violation.
- OSDH recommended requiring electrical inspections of all commercial docks before permit issuance.

## Additional Details Not in Original File
- The CDC MMWR report documents a July 24, 1991 case at "Lake A" in northeastern Oklahoma where a 50-year-old male was found unconscious on a boat dock. He was wearing wet socks and shoes, and an electrical short was detected in the dock wiring. His son received a shock while attempting to rescue him. The cause of death was determined to be electrocution. This appears to be the same incident as FI-1991-001, but with a July 24 date rather than May 11.
- The CDC report notes that Lake A had 116 commercial docks, with 96% violating the National Electrical Code at the time of inspection.
- A separate CDC case (Case 2, July 27, 1993) specifically involved dock lights and dock metal frame energization -- which more closely matches the description in FI-1991-001 ("fault in dock lights had energized the dock frame") than the July 1991 case does.

## Conflicting Information
- **Date discrepancy:** The original file gives the date as **May 11, 1991**. The CDC MMWR report documents the 1991 Oklahoma lake death as occurring on **July 24, 1991**.
- **Cause description:** The original file says "fault in dock lights had energized the dock frame." The CDC report (Case 3) says "electrical short detected in dock wiring" for the July 1991 case, without specifically mentioning dock lights or dock frame. The description involving dock lights energizing a metal dock frame more closely matches CDC Case 2 (July 27, 1993, the 13-year-old boy), not the 1991 incident.

## Suggested Corrections
- **Date:** Change from "May 11, 1991" to "July 24, 1991" (per CDC MMWR) or at minimum "July 1991."
- **Cause description:** Consider revising from "fault in dock lights had energized the dock frame" to "fault in dock wiring" or "electrical short in dock wiring." The dock lights/frame description may have been conflated with the July 1993 incident (CDC Case 2).
- **Additional victim details (from CDC):** The victim was a 50-year-old male found unconscious on the dock wearing wet clothing. His son was shocked during a rescue attempt. The cause of death was electrocution (not drowning). The incident occurred at a lake in northeastern Oklahoma with widespread National Electrical Code violations at commercial docks.
