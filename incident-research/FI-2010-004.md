# Research: FI-2010-004 — Lake Waccamaw NC Boat Lift Electrocution (2010)

## Original Incident Summary

Per the ESDPA-derived file, on May 29, 2010, at Lake Waccamaw, North Carolina, a boy was killed swimming near a boat lift. The electrical investigation revealed an open ground in the lines and a junction box filled with water.

**Electrical root causes identified:**

1. **Severed Ground Wire:** The underground feeder cable's equipment ground conductor had been severed at a junction box in the house crawl space, disconnecting the safety ground path that would have tripped the circuit breaker.

2. **Water-Filled Junction Box:** A plastic junction box beneath the dock, positioned above the waterline, contained water when inspected post-incident. The live black wire and severed ground wire were "clearly below" the water accumulation line inside the box, creating a direct path for current to flow into the water.

3. **Missing GFCI Protection:** The circuit lacked GFCI protection at the main circuit breaker, which was required by 1995-1998 NEC standards for marina and dock installations.

4. **Unpermitted Installation:** The electrical work was performed by an unlicensed contractor and was unpermitted.

5. **Inadequate Cable Protection:** The cable lacked proper protection along exposed deck areas.

## Research Notes

This case represents a classic example of multiple electrical safety failures combining to create a fatal hazard:

1. **The immediate cause** was water intrusion into the dock junction box, allowing current from the live wire to energize the water.

2. **The enabling cause** was the severed equipment ground conductor in the house crawl space, which prevented the circuit breaker from tripping when the fault occurred.

3. **The missing safeguard** was the absence of GFCI protection, which would have detected the current leakage and tripped even without a functioning equipment ground.

The article notes a mystery regarding how water entered the "gasketed, sealed" junction box. The explanation offered is that the two plastic conduits feeding the junction box were not sealed from the outside humid environment, possibly allowing condensation to accumulate over time. This points to improper installation practices.

The fact that one child reported feeling "tingles" before the fatal incident is significant — this warning sign of energized water was present but not recognized in time. This underscores the importance of ESD education: any tingling sensation in water near docks or boats should be treated as an emergency requiring immediate evacuation from the water and power shutoff.

The unpermitted nature of the work and use of an unlicensed contractor represents a regulatory failure that allowed substandard electrical installation to endanger lives.

**Day of week verification:** May 29, 2010 was a Saturday, which is consistent with a recreational family outing to the lake.
