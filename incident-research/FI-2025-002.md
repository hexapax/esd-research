**Description:** Blue Ridge Lake, Fannin County GA. A 13-year-old boy was electrocuted when trying to exit the water of a private dock via a metal ladder. Two other boys tried to rescue him but were shocked themselves, along with another woman who was hospitalized from the electric shock. A Fannin County official investigated and found numerous electrical deficiencies, but specifics are not yet known.

### Note on "Drowning" vs. "Electrocution"
The News Observer frames the death as "investigated as a drowning." The ESDPA classifies it as electrocution at a dock via metal ladder. This is a common pattern in ESD cases: initial reports say "drowning" and only later investigation reveals the electrocution. The ESDPA document was revised August 15, 2025 — nearly two months later — allowing time for the electrical cause to be identified.

### Note on Limited Coverage
Despite involving the death of a child and multiple injuries, this incident received remarkably little news coverage beyond the local Blue Ridge newspaper. No Atlanta TV stations (WSB-TV, 11Alive, Fox 5 Atlanta) appear to have covered it, which is unusual for a child's death involving a Fulton County (Atlanta metro) resident. This may be because the initial classification as a "drowning" did not attract the same attention that an "electrocution" label would have.

### Supporting Circumstantial Evidence
The fact that the victim was a **certified scuba diver** (per The News Observer) lends support to the ESDPA's electrocution classification. A certified scuba diver is unlikely to have drowned under normal circumstances while climbing out of the water on a dock ladder.
