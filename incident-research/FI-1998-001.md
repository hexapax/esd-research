# Research: FI-1998-001

## Original Incident Summary
A single drowning of a young girl in distress at Lake Sonoma, California, approximately 1998. A fault on the dock was found. The incident occurred in a fresh water environment. Classified as a fatal incident with 1 dead. Minimal details were provided in the original source, with the date noted as approximate.

## Sources Found

### Source 1
**URL:** https://www.tahoedailytribune.com/news/death-of-10-year-old-convinces-d-a-legislator-to-push-for-new-law/
**Title:** Death of 10-year-old convinces D.A., legislator to push for new law (Tahoe Daily Tribune, December 2001)
**Relevant Information:**
- The victim was Deirdre McCormick, a 10-year-old girl from Windsor, California
- She was swimming alongside the dock at Lake Sonoma Marina when she touched a faulty conduit and was electrocuted
- She was swimming with friends at the time
- The marina was operated by a concessionaire running it for the U.S. Army (Corps of Engineers)
- The marina owners allegedly knew there was danger from the faulty wiring
- Sonoma County District Attorney Michael Mullins stated there was "no doubt that the concessionaire was negligent" and the dock wiring was faulty
- The DA could not bring criminal charges against the company operating the marina
- Her grandmother, Linda McCormick of South Lake Tahoe, said "there is always room for an accident but (the marina owners) were not careful"
- A memorial park and playground called the "Deirdre McCormick Memorial Play Area" was established to honor her memory
- Her family was described as a "large Irish-American family" who honored her with a picnic at the park

### Source 2
**URL:** http://leginfo.ca.gov/pub/99-00/bill/sen/sb_2001-2050/sb_2040_cfa_20000526_182330_sen_floor.html
**Title:** SB 2040 Senate Bill - Bill Analysis (California Legislature, 2000)
**Relevant Information:**
- SB 2040 was authored by Senator Wesley Chesbro (D-Arcata) in direct response to the Lake Sonoma electrocution death
- Investigators found "several defects that could have been to blame" on the floating dock's electrical system but did not positively determine the single specific cause of the electrical current escaping
- The bill required the California Building Standards Commission, in consultation with the State Fire Marshal, to conduct a study on hazards to public safety presented by marinas in California
- The study report was due to the Legislature by January 1, 2002 with recommendations on inspection frequency and inspector staffing
- California had approximately 800 marinas at the time, typically inspected only during construction or renovation work requiring permits
- Local inspectors lacked jurisdiction over state or federal property marinas (relevant since Lake Sonoma is federal property)
- The bill passed the Senate Housing & Community Development Committee 6-1 (April 3, 2000) and the Senate Appropriations Committee 11-0 (May 22, 2000)
- The Sonoma County District Attorney supported the bill
- The bill had a sunset provision expiring January 1, 2002
- Estimated annual cost of marina inspections: $500,000

## Additional Details Not in Original File
- The victim has been identified as Deirdre McCormick, age 10, from Windsor, California
- She was swimming alongside the dock at Lake Sonoma Marina when she touched a faulty electrical conduit
- The marina was operated by a concessionaire under contract with the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers (Lake Sonoma is a federal facility)
- Investigators found multiple defects in the floating dock's electrical system but could not pinpoint a single definitive cause
- The Sonoma County District Attorney determined the concessionaire was negligent but could not bring criminal charges
- The incident prompted California Senate Bill SB 2040 (authored by Senator Wesley Chesbro, D-Arcata) requiring a statewide study of marina safety hazards
- At the time, California had approximately 800 marinas that were generally only inspected during construction or renovation
- Local building inspectors had no jurisdiction over marinas on state or federal property, creating a regulatory gap
- A memorial play area was established in Windsor, California in Deirdre McCormick's honor
- The date is more specifically noted as September 1998 in the Mike Holt source (rather than just "approximately 1998")

## Conflicting Information
- The original file states the date as "Approximately 1998." The Tahoe Daily Tribune article (published December 2001) states the death occurred "almost two years ago," which would place it closer to late 1999 or early 2000. However, the Mike Holt ESD list gives the date as September 1998, and SB 2040 was introduced in the 1999-2000 legislative session (amended May 2000), which is more consistent with an incident in late 1998 or 1999. The exact date remains somewhat uncertain.
- The original file describes a "fault on the dock" as a single cause. The SB 2040 bill analysis states investigators found "several defects" but could not positively determine the specific cause -- suggesting the situation was more complex than a single identifiable fault.

## Suggested Corrections
- The victim can be identified by name: Deirdre McCormick, age 10, from Windsor, California
- The description could be more specific: she was swimming alongside the dock at Lake Sonoma Marina and touched a faulty electrical conduit
- The date "Approximately 1998" could potentially be refined to "September 1998" based on the Mike Holt source, though the Tahoe Daily Tribune timeline suggests it may have been closer to late 1999. Further research in local newspaper archives could resolve this discrepancy.
- The cause description could note that multiple electrical defects were found on the floating dock, not just a single fault
