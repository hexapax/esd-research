# Research: FI-1998-001

## Original Incident Summary
A single fatal drowning of a young girl in distress at Lake Sonoma, California, approximately 1998. A fault on the dock was found. The incident occurred in a fresh water environment. Classified as a fatal incident with 1 dead. Minimal details were provided in the original source, with the date noted as approximate.

### Source 2: CA SB 2040 Bill Analysis
**URL:** http://leginfo.ca.gov/pub/99-00/bill/sen/sb_2001-2050/sb_2040_cfa_20000526_182330_sen_floor.html
**Title:** SB 2040 Senate Bill - Bill Analysis (California Legislature, 1999-2000 session)
**Relevant Information:**
- SB 2040 was authored by Senator Wesley Chesbro (D-Arcata) in direct response to the Lake Sonoma electrocution death
- Investigators found "several defects that could have been to blame" on the floating dock's electrical system but did not positively determine the single specific cause of the electrical current escaping
- The bill required the California Building Standards Commission, in consultation with the State Fire Marshal, to conduct a study on hazards to public safety presented by marinas in California
- The study report was due to the Legislature by January 1, 2002 with recommendations on inspection frequency and inspector staffing
- California had approximately 800 marinas at the time, typically inspected only during construction or renovation work requiring permits
- Local inspectors lacked jurisdiction over state or federal property marinas (relevant since Lake Sonoma is federal property)
- The bill passed the Senate Housing & Community Development Committee 6-1 (April 3, 2000) and the Senate Appropriations Committee 11-0 (May 22, 2000)
- The Sonoma County District Attorney supported the bill
- The bill had a sunset provision expiring January 1, 2002
- Estimated annual cost of marina inspections: $500,000
- Fire inspectors generally focused on ingress/egress, fire hydrants, and sprinkler systems; electrical inspections were limited to checking use of extension cords

## Additional Details Not in Original File
- The victim has been identified as Deirdre Mary McCormick, age 10, from Windsor, California
- She was swimming alongside the dock at Lake Sonoma Marina when she touched a faulty electrical conduit on a Saturday in September 1998
- She was swimming with her sister and her cousin at the time of the incident
- A rescuer who pulled her from the water reported feeling an electrical current when approaching the dock
- The marina was operated by a concessionaire under contract with the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers (Lake Sonoma is a federal facility north of Healdsburg)
- U.S. Army Corps of Engineers inspectors found a frayed wire that could have allowed current to flow through the metal conduit
- The SB 2040 bill analysis noted "several defects" on the dock's electrical system, suggesting multiple issues beyond the single frayed wire
- The Sonoma County District Attorney determined the concessionaire was negligent but could not bring criminal charges
- The incident prompted two rounds of California legislation:
  1. SB 2040 (1999-2000 session, Sen. Chesbro) -- a study bill requiring the Building Standards Commission to assess marina hazards statewide
  2. A subsequent bill (2001 session) signed by Gov. Gray Davis on September 29, 2001, requiring the Department of Boating and Waterways to conduct actual inspections of marina electrical systems every two years for state-financed marinas
- The Deirdre McCormick Memorial Play Area ("Deirdre's Park") was established at the base of Warm Springs Dam near Lake Sonoma
- Her grandmother, Linda McCormick of South Lake Tahoe, was an advocate for the legislation
- A key limitation noted: federal ownership of Lake Sonoma meant state legislation could not directly address the specific marina where the death occurred

## Conflicting Information
- **Age discrepancy:** The initial SFGate report (Source 4) states Deirdre was 11 years old. All other sources -- including the SFGate follow-up (Source 5), the Tahoe Daily Tribune, the Stockton Record/AP wire, and the Press Democrat -- consistently state she was 10. The initial report was likely written before her age was confirmed and is almost certainly incorrect.
- **Cause complexity:** The original file describes a "fault on the dock" as a single cause. The SB 2040 bill analysis states investigators found "several defects" but could not positively determine the specific cause. The SFGate follow-up identified a specific frayed wire as a probable cause. These are not necessarily contradictory -- there may have been multiple defects with one frayed wire being the most likely culprit.
- **Date:** The original file states "Approximately 1998." The incident occurred on a Saturday, and the Press Democrat article (circa 2008) says "10 years ago this week." The Stockton Record/AP confirms "1998." The Tahoe Daily Tribune (December 2001) says "almost two years ago," which would suggest late 1999 or 2000 -- but this conflicts with the AP wire and SB 2040 timeline. The Tribune's "almost two years" phrasing is likely imprecise; September 1998 is the most reliable date based on multiple corroborating sources.
- **SFGate headline error:** Source 5's headline reads "Faulty Wire Discovered After Pool Electrocution" -- but the incident occurred in a lake/marina, not a pool. This appears to be a headline error by the newspaper.
