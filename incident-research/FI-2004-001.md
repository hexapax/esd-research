# FI-2004-001 — Research Notes

## Incident Summary (from source PDF)
A 19-year-old girl drowned at Scott's Creek Marina on Cave Run Lake in Moorhead, Kentucky in September 2004. The incident was caused by a submerged battery charger that energized the boat hull, with the boat lacking a proper bonding system.

## Sources Found

### Source 1: Lexington Herald-Leader (Kentucky.com)
- **URL:** https://www.kentucky.com/news/politics-government/article44497146.html
- **Publication:** Lexington Herald-Leader
- **Date:** 2014 (article about proposed legislation)
- **Details:** Reports that Samantha Chipley, 19, drowned in Cave Run Lake near Scotts Creek Marina. She was swimming near a houseboat when she started screaming in pain, her muscles seized, and she couldn't move. Friends swam toward her but were repelled by an electrifying sensation in the water. The shock was relatively weak with no physical marks of electrocution on her body when recovered. The hull of the nearby houseboat was charged with electricity, which was transferred into the water.
- **New Info:** Mother's name is Roberta Chipley. Rep. Kevin Sinnette (D-Ashland) authored House Bill 107, the "Samantha Chipley Act," to prohibit swimming within 50 yards of a boat dock or marina where houseboats receive electrical power. Similar bills passed in Tennessee and West Virginia. Other Kentucky ESD deaths mentioned: Kevin Short (Lake Cumberland, June 2013) and Kyle McGonigle (Rough River Lake, May 2013).
- **Conflicts with ESDPA:** Date is 2005, not September 2004.

### Source 2: BoatTEST (boattest.com)
- **URL:** https://boattest.com/article/add-isolation-transformer-your-boat
- **Publication:** BoatTEST
- **Details:** Provides a detailed narrative of the incident. On June 27, 2005, Samantha Chipley and friends Margaret, Susie, and Courtney arrived at Scott Creek Marina on Cave Run Lake in Eastern Kentucky, planning to spend the night on a houseboat owned by Susie's father. It was a hot June afternoon and the four girls jumped into the water. "No Swimming" signs warned of electrocution danger, but swimming was common practice at the marina. Samantha and Margaret were in the water while Susie and Courtney retrieved inflatable toys from another boat. Samantha tried to climb on a raft with Margaret and suddenly started jerking in the water. Margaret jumped off the raft to help and felt a shock go through her entire body. The other girls pulled Margaret to safety, but they watched helplessly as Samantha was shocked for several minutes. Another boater dove in to save her, was shocked, and barely escaped with his life.
- **New Info:** Names of friends present (Margaret, Susie, Courtney). Houseboat belonged to Susie's father. "No Swimming" signs were posted. Incident date given as June 27, 2005. Detailed rescue attempt by another boater who was also shocked.
- **Conflicts with ESDPA:** Date is June 27, 2005, not September 2004. Detailed narrative differs from brief ESDPA summary.

### Source 4: Boating Industry
- **URL:** https://boatingindustry.com/news/2013/12/11/new-membership-alliance-between-ami-kytn-mta/
- **Publication:** Boating Industry
- **Date:** December 11, 2013
- **Details:** Reports on a membership alliance between the Association of Marina Industries (AMI) and Kentucky/Tennessee Marina Associations (KY/TN MA). Over three years the two groups worked to revise "the Chipley Act — which was the electric shock drowning of Samantha Chipley — and offered up educational opportunities as an alternative to regulation." AMI coordinated with ABYC to hold webinars on electric shock drowning causes and prevention and produced a "Best Management Practices for Marina Electrical Safety."
- **New Info:** Marina industry actively engaged with the Chipley Act legislation. AMI and ABYC produced educational materials and best practices documents in response. Industry preferred educational approach over regulation.
- **Conflicts with ESDPA:** None directly; confirms incident identity.

## Corrections Needed
- **Date:** ESDPA lists this incident as September 2004. Multiple independent sources (obituary, news articles, BoatTEST narrative) confirm the incident occurred on June 27, 2005, with death on June 28, 2005. The correct date is **June 28, 2005**.
- **Location spelling:** Sources use both "Moorhead" and "Morehead" for the Kentucky location; also "Scott's Creek" vs "Scotts Creek" vs "Scott Creek." The marina name appears most commonly as "Scotts Creek Marina" or "Scott Creek Marina."

## Conflicting Information
- The incident date in BoatTEST (June 27, 2005) differs by one day from the obituary death date (June 28, 2005). This likely means the incident occurred on the evening of June 27 and death was recorded on June 28, or the incident and death occurred on June 28 with the BoatTEST article having a minor error.
- ESDPA describes causation as "submerged battery charger" while some sources describe it more generally as AC current leakage from the houseboat energizing the hull. The Perplexity/California CRLEA source mentions "faulty battery charger on the houseboat" which is consistent with ESDPA's description.
