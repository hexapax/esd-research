# Research: FI-1993-003

## Original Incident Summary
In July 1993, two teenage girls were snorkeling near a dock in Alexandria Bay, New York when they were paralyzed by electric shock and drowned. The cause was identified as dock wiring that had been gnawed by rodents. The incident is classified as a fatal incident (double fatality) and is catalogued in the "Electric Shock Drowning Incidents -- Marinas" list compiled by James D. Shafer and Capt. David E. Rifkin.

## Additional Details Not in Original File
- Two bystanders attempted to enter the water to rescue the girls but were shocked by the voltage present in the water. They were treated at a hospital and released. This is a significant detail not mentioned in the original incident file.
- Some versions of the source list note that the fault in dock wiring was "thought to be" from rodent damage but was "not confirmed," suggesting the rodent cause may be suspected rather than definitively established.
- The incident is catalogued as entry #62 in earlier versions of the Shafer/Rifkin incident list, and as #106 in the most recent (Rev. 8/15/2025) version, indicating the list has been renumbered or expanded over time.

## Conflicting Information
- **Date discrepancy:** The original file states "July 1993." The Mike Holt sources (Source 1 and Source 2) state "August 1993." The OnTime Service source (Source 3) states "September 1993." All three months appear in different reproductions of the same underlying Shafer/Rifkin list. The original file's classification notes acknowledge this is an "approximate date (month only)."
- **Cause certainty:** The original file states definitively that "dock wiring had been gnawed by rodents." However, Source 2 (Mike Holt, alternative listing) states the "Fault thought to be in dock wiring, not confirmed," suggesting the rodent-damaged wiring explanation may not have been conclusively verified. Other reproductions of the list do state the rodent damage as fact.

## Suggested Corrections
- **Date:** Consider changing "July 1993" to "August 1993" or noting the date uncertainty more explicitly. The majority of sources cite August 1993, though there is no definitive independent confirmation. At minimum, the month should be flagged as uncertain.
- **Cause:** Consider qualifying the rodent-damage cause. At least one version of the source list states the fault was "thought to be in dock wiring, not confirmed." The original file presents the rodent damage as established fact, but the evidence may be less certain.
- **Additional victims:** The file should note that two bystanders who attempted to rescue the girls were also shocked and hospitalized, as this is consistently reported across all sources and is a significant detail for understanding the full scope of the incident.
- **No independent news or government sources found:** Despite extensive searching, no newspaper articles, police reports, or government records were found to independently verify this incident outside of the Shafer/Rifkin ESD incident compilation and its various reproductions. All sources trace back to the same underlying list.
