# Research: FI-1999-004

## Original Incident Summary
On an unspecified date in July 1999, two boys wearing flotation devices were found face down in the water behind a houseboat at "Cedar Hill Lake" in Smithville, Tennessee. The 7-year-old could not be revived (fatal). The other boy survived. The incident is classified as an electric shock drowning (ESD). The original file notes that the date is approximate (month only) and that flotation devices were present, indicating shock-induced paralysis despite flotation.

## Conflicting Information
- The original file and its underlying source document (Shafer/Rifkin) refer to the location as "Cedar Hill Lake." However, there is no lake called "Cedar Hill Lake" near Smithville, Tennessee. The actual Cedar Hill Lake in Tennessee is a small lake in Davidson County (Nashville), roughly 60 miles from Smithville. The large reservoir near Smithville with marinas and houseboats is **Center Hill Lake**. Sligo Marina, which appears in some references to this incident, is confirmed to be on Center Hill Lake at 350 Sligo Road, Smithville, TN 37166. This naming discrepancy appears to originate in the primary Shafer/Rifkin source document and has been propagated through multiple secondary sources.

## Suggested Corrections
- **Lake name:** "Cedar Hill Lake" should likely be corrected to **"Center Hill Lake."** Center Hill Lake is the well-known reservoir near Smithville, TN, managed by the US Army Corps of Engineers. There is no "Cedar Hill Lake" near Smithville. The real Cedar Hill Lake is a small body of water in Davidson County (Nashville area), approximately 60 miles away, and does not have marinas or houseboats. This error appears to originate in the primary ESD incident list itself.
- **Date:** The date can be made more specific: **July 18, 1999** (per the Mike Holt/Shafer-Rifkin incident list entry #18).
- **Outcome description:** The original file states "Dead: 1" but could be expanded to note the surviving boy was 8 years old, per the source material.
