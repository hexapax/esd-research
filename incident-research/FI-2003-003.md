### Assessment

This is a wildlife-only incident (ducks, no human casualties) that occurred at a private houseboat dock. Incidents of this nature are unlikely to generate news coverage, official investigations, or regulatory filings because:

1. No human injuries or deaths occurred
2. OSHA jurisdiction applies to workplaces, not recreational boating
3. Wildlife agencies typically do not investigate individual waterfowl deaths from electrical causes
4. The Army Corps of Engineers manages Allatoona Lake but would not typically investigate dock electrical incidents
5. The incident likely came to the attention of ESD researchers through informal reporting by marina operators or boaters, not through any official investigative process

The only notable detail found across search result summaries (but not independently verifiable) was a technical description stating that the 125V plug at the boat end of the shore cord had been rewired by the owner for 220V, with L2 connected to the "GR" (ground) pin and the ground lead in the 4-wire cord cut and taped off -- causing the hull to rise to line potential and energize the surrounding water. This detail does not appear in the FI-2003-003.md source file but is present in some versions of the ESDPA list.

### Possible Date Discrepancy

The incident file lists the date as **May 2003**. Some web search result summaries referenced the incident as occurring in **June 2003**. Since the original ESDPA PDF could not be parsed for direct verification, it is unclear which month is correct. This may reflect an approximate date in the original source (month-level precision only) or an error in the extracted file.
