## Additional Context

- **Salt water ESD rarity:** This incident is notable as one of the very few documented ESD-related fatalities in a salt water environment. Most ESD literature emphasizes that electric shock drowning is primarily a freshwater hazard because freshwater is far less conductive than the human body, causing current to pass through a swimmer. Salt water, being 50 to 1,000 times more conductive than fresh water, typically diverts current around a swimmer rather than through them. The classification of this incident as an electric shock drowning in salt water is therefore highly unusual and raises questions about whether this was truly an ESD event or a drowning coincidentally associated with a boatlift electrical fault.

- **Minimal details available:** The ESDPA list notes the cause of death was "not available," and no victim name or specific location details exist in any online source.
