# Research: NM-2019-002 — Universal Studios Volcano Bay Water Park Shock (2019)

## Original Incident Summary

Per the ESDPA-derived file, on June 2, 2019, numerous staff and guests were shocked at Universal Studios Volcano Bay water park in Orlando, Florida. No injuries were found after examination. A water coaster short circuit was the cause.

## Corrections Needed

1. **Cause description is incorrect/incomplete.** The original states "A water coaster short circuit was the cause." This is **wrong**. According to OSHA's investigation, the cause was a construction defect: a grounding rod inadvertently driven through an electrical conduit during construction, which damaged the outer sheath of electrical wiring. This caused electrical current to be sent through the ground, energizing wet surfaces and water throughout the park. The issue was not limited to a water coaster and was not a "short circuit" in the traditional sense. The cause should be corrected to: "Construction defect: grounding rod driven through electrical conduit, damaging wiring and energizing wet surfaces and water."

2. **"No injuries were found after examination" is misleading.** Multiple workers (4-5 lifeguards) were hospitalized as a precautionary measure, though all were released the same day. Additionally, at least one guest (April Carlino) filed a lawsuit claiming injury from electrocution. While there were no life-threatening injuries or fatalities, saying "no injuries" is inaccurate. This should be corrected to: "No serious injuries; multiple workers hospitalized for precautionary evaluation and released same day; at least one guest subsequently claimed injury in lawsuit."

3. **Missing important details:** The original file is very brief. Important context includes:
   - The park closed early on June 2, 2019
   - OSHA investigation concluded Universal was not aware of the construction defect
   - At least one lawsuit was filed (April Carlino)
   - Guests and staff reported various sensations (numbness, tingling, "bee sting" feelings)
   - The problem affected multiple areas, not just one attraction

4. **"Water coaster" specificity is misleading.** While Volcano Bay has water coasters, the electrical problem was not specific to one ride. The damaged wiring energized wet surfaces and water throughout the park, affecting walkways, pools, and various areas.
