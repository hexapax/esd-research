# Research: FI-1986-001

## Original Incident Summary
On July 29, 1986, a single fatal drowning of a diver occurred at Grosse Pointe Yacht Club, Michigan, in a fresh water environment. The incident is classified as a Fatal Incident with 1 death. It is listed as sub-incident (A) of ESDPA item #113, which also includes a same-date diver drowning at Petoskey, MI (sub-incident B). The information was relayed third-hand, making its reliability uncertain. The source document is "Electric Shock Drowning Incidents -- Marinas" by James D. Shafer and Capt. David E. Rifkin, Quality Marine Services, LLC (Rev. 8/15/2025).

Founded in 1918 (originally as the Grosse Pointe Ice Boat Club in 1910), the club features a Mediterranean Revival clubhouse and a harbor with boat slips. Lake St. Clair is a fresh water body connecting Lake Huron and Lake Erie.

## Conflicting Information
- **Date discrepancy:** The ESDPA Rev. 8/15/2025 list specifies July 29, 1986, while the earlier Harbor Marine Consultants / Mike Holt newsletter version lists the date as "1987 or 1988" with the qualifier "no year confirmation." The more recent ESDPA revision appears to have resolved this to a specific date, but given the third-hand nature of the report, the exact date remains uncertain.
- **Level of detail discrepancy:** Web search engine snippets referencing the Harbor Marine Consultants document describe considerably more detail about the incident cause (home-made shore cord, sheet metal siding, chafed insulation, friend feeling a shock) than appears in the ESDPA Rev. 8/15/2025 entry (item #113), which is very brief. It is unclear whether these details actually apply to this specific Grosse Pointe sub-incident or were conflated from a different entry during search engine indexing.

## Suggested Corrections
- **Spelling:** The ESDPA source document spells the location as "Gross Pointe Yacht Club" (missing the 'e'). The correct spelling is "Grosse Pointe Yacht Club." The raw incident file already uses the correct spelling.
- **Date uncertainty:** Given the earlier version of the list uses "1987 or 1988" and the third-hand nature of the report, the July 29, 1986 date in the current ESDPA revision should be treated with caution. The raw incident file could benefit from a note about this date uncertainty.
- **No independent verification found:** No sources independent of the Shafer/Rifkin incident list were found to corroborate this incident. All references trace back to the same compiled list. This does not mean the incident did not occur, but it limits the ability to verify specific details.
