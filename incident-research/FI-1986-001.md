# Research: FI-1986-001

## Original Incident Summary
On July 29, 1986, a single fatal drowning of a diver occurred at Grosse Pointe Yacht Club, Michigan, in a fresh water environment. The incident is classified as a Fatal Incident with 1 death. It is listed as sub-incident (A) of ESDPA item #113, which also includes a same-date diver drowning at Petoskey, MI (sub-incident B). The information was relayed third-hand, making its reliability uncertain. The source document is "Electric Shock Drowning Incidents -- Marinas" by James D. Shafer and Capt. David E. Rifkin, Quality Marine Services, LLC (Rev. 8/15/2025).

No independent sources found. Only ESDPA-derived lists were located, which are excluded as they add no independent verification.

## Additional Details Not in Original File
- The Grosse Pointe Yacht Club is a private yacht club located at 788 Lake Shore Road in Grosse Pointe Shores, Michigan, on the shore of Lake St. Clair. Founded in 1918 (originally as the Grosse Pointe Ice Boat Club in 1910), the club features a Mediterranean Revival clubhouse and a harbor with boat slips. Lake St. Clair is a fresh water body connecting Lake Huron and Lake Erie.
- The companion sub-incident (B) at Petoskey, MI (FI-1986-002) shares the same date and general characteristics (diver drowning in Michigan fresh water), both relayed third-hand. It is possible these represent a single confused report rather than two separate incidents, though the ESDPA list treats them as distinct events at different locations.
