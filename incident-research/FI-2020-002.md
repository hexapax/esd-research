# Research: FI-2020-002 — Mark Mixon Tropical Storm Eta Electrocution (2020)

## Original Incident Summary

Per the ESDPA-derived file, on Aug 30, 2020, in Bradenton Beach, Florida, during Tropical Storm ETA, a man was sandbagging the outside of his home. He went into the ground floor storage space where there was 3 inches of standing water. He was electrocuted in the water. Authorities reported the clothes dryer was the source of the current. A friend walked in the water in an attempt to rescue but received electrical shocks and had to abandon the effort.

## Corrections Needed

1. **DATE IS COMPLETELY WRONG.** The original file states "Aug 30, 2020." Multiple independent sources (Tampa Bay Times, FOX 13, MySuncoast, CBS News, AMI Sun, Anna Maria Islander, WFLA) all confirm the incident occurred on **Wednesday evening, November 11, 2020**, not August 30, 2020. The month (August vs. November) and day (30 vs. 11) are both incorrect, though the year (2020) is correct. The date should be corrected to "November 11, 2020."

2. **The August 30 date is meteorologically impossible.** The original file correctly identifies the storm as "Tropical Storm ETA," but Tropical Storm Eta made landfall in Florida on November 9-12, 2020, not in August. The August 30, 2020 date is impossible for a Tropical Storm Eta incident. This is a major error.

3. **The original file notes the date discrepancy between ESD section (Aug 30, 2020) and NM section (Nov 4, 2020).** The NM section date of November 4 is closer to correct but still wrong. The actual date was November 11, 2020. Neither date in the ESDPA document is correct.

4. **Victim identification.** The original file does not name the victim. He was Mark Mixon, age 65, of Bradenton Beach, Florida.

5. **Location precision.** The original says "Bradenton Beach, Florida (flooded home storage space)." More precisely, the location was 211 Bay Drive N., Bradenton Beach, and the specific room was the laundry room (not just "storage space"). "Laundry room" is more accurate.

6. **Water depth discrepancy.** The original states "3 inches of standing water." The Tampa Bay Times reports "about four inches of water." This is a minor discrepancy (3 vs. 4 inches) but sources indicate 4 inches is more accurate.

7. **"A friend walked in the water in an attempt to rescue" - Clarification needed.** The sources describe "a witness" who was helping sandbag but stepped away to get more sandbags from a truck outside. When the victim began yelling for help, the witness attempted to help but was shocked. The person is described as a "witness" helping with sandbagging, not specifically identified as a "friend," though they may have been. This is a minor point.

8. **First responder injury.** The original file does not mention that a firefighter suffered a minor injury when attempting to access the scene due to the ongoing electrical hazard. This is an important detail showing the persistent danger.

9. **Tropical Storm Eta timeline context.** Tropical Storm Eta made landfall in the Florida Keys on November 9, 2020, and moved up the Gulf Coast, affecting the Bradenton area on November 11, 2020. This was Eta's third landfall in Florida (after two previous landfalls in Central America and Florida). Adding this context helps explain the flooding severity.
