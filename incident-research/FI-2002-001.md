**Summary of information:**
The Orlando Sentinel reported that two teenage boys died in a canal electrocution incident in North Fort Myers, Florida. The victims were:

The boys were jumping on a trampoline in a neighbor's yard when Brandenburg spotted a football in the canal. He jumped into the water to retrieve it and was electrocuted upon grabbing an electrified boat lift attached to a dock. Kopinetz called for nearby adults and jumped into the water to rescue his friend, but as he pulled Brandenburg toward shore, he too touched the electrified boat lift and was electrocuted.

Resuscitation attempts by both eyewitnesses and paramedics failed. Both boys were pronounced dead at Cape Coral Hospital.

The investigation was conducted by Lee County sheriff's officials.

## Analysis of Discrepancies

### Date Discrepancy

The ESDPA file lists the incident date as **May 31, 2002**. Independent sources consistently indicate the incident occurred on **May 8, 2003**:

1. The Orlando Sentinel published its article on May 10, 2003, reporting on the incident as recent news.
2. The obituary for D.J. Brandenburg Jr. states he "died Thursday, May 8, 2003" -- and May 8, 2003 was indeed a Thursday (verified by calendar).
3. Multiple search engine summaries of the source material consistently reference "May 2003" rather than 2002.

The ESDPA file's date of "May 31, 2002" appears to be wrong in both year (should be 2003) and day (should be May 8). The "May 31" portion may be a data entry error or confusion with another incident.

### Location Discrepancy

The ESDPA file lists the location as "Cape Coral FL." The Orlando Sentinel identifies the location as **North Fort Myers**, which is a separate (unincorporated) community in Lee County, Florida. Cape Coral and North Fort Myers are adjacent, separated by the Caloosahatchee River. The boys were pronounced dead at Cape Coral Hospital, and one victim (Brandenburg) attended Cape Coral High School, which may explain why "Cape Coral" was used. However, the incident itself appears to have occurred in North Fort Myers.

### Description Details

The ESDPA file's description -- "Re-bar had penetrated through a power cable to a boat lift, energizing the lift frame. Salt water environment." -- is broadly consistent with news reporting that the boat lift was electrified. The specific detail about rebar penetrating a power cable is not mentioned in the Orlando Sentinel article (which simply states the lift was "electrified"), but this technical detail likely came from the subsequent investigation by Lee County authorities or from the Shafer/Rifkin research. The "salt water" detail is consistent with the canal environment in the North Fort Myers / Cape Coral area, where canals connect to the Caloosahatchee River estuary and ultimately the Gulf of Mexico.

## Corrections Needed

Based on independent source research, the following corrections to the original FI-2002-001 incident file are indicated:

1. **Date: WRONG.** The file states "May 31, 2002." Based on the Orlando Sentinel article (published May 10, 2003) and the Brandenburg obituary (died Thursday, May 8, 2003), the correct date is **May 8, 2003**. Both the year and day are incorrect in the ESDPA file.

2. **Location: PARTIALLY WRONG.** The file states "Cape Coral FL." The Orlando Sentinel identifies the location as **North Fort Myers, FL** (an adjacent community in the same county). Cape Coral Hospital is where the victims were pronounced dead, and one victim attended Cape Coral High School, but the incident itself occurred in North Fort Myers.

3. **Incident ID may need updating.** If the correct year is 2003, the file ID "FI-2002-001" is based on an incorrect year. It should logically be reclassified as a 2003 incident.

4. **Additional context available.** The victims were teenagers (ages 15 and 16), not adults. One was retrieving a football from a canal; the second died attempting a rescue. This context significantly enriches the bare-bones description in the original file.
