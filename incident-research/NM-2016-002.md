# Research: NM-2016-002 — Shiloh Construction Worker Electrocution (2016)

## Original Incident Summary

Per the ESDPA-derived file, on October 7, 2016, a worker in an electrified puddle was cutting concrete in Shiloh, Missouri. A second worker was shocked. CPR was performed and he was revived. Two shocked/survived, no deaths.

- **Publication:** e-Hazard (electrical safety training and consulting company)
- **Date:** August 21, 2020

**Summary:** The article describes how two Shiloh, IL police officers saved a construction worker's life in October 2016. A construction worker was operating an industrial concrete saw which came into contact with an energized electrical line, rendering him unconscious. The worker ended up lying in approximately 6 inches of standing water. At least one other person attempted to move the victim but was shocked in the process. One of the responding police officers used a canvas tie-down strap to wrap around the worker's wrist and safely pull him away from the water and the electrical source. The officers then performed emergency procedures (CPR/first aid) until paramedics arrived. The victim survived. The officers were presented with a Life Saving Award at a November Village Board of Trustees meeting. Police Chief Jim Stover was quoted: "In my opinion, if my two officers had not used the professional training they received, I don't believe the victim would have survived until our ambulance got there." The article notes the electrical source was likely not protected with a GFCI, which would have been required at a construction site.

## Corrections Needed

1. **STATE IS WRONG.** The original file states "Shiloh MO" (Missouri). The independent source clearly identifies the location as **Shiloh, Illinois** (Village of Shiloh, St. Clair County, IL 62269). This is confirmed by the reference to a Village Board of Trustees (Shiloh, IL has one; Shiloh, MO does not), a police department with Chief Jim Stover, and the Life Saving Award ceremony. The "MO" vs. "IL" error is likely a transcription mistake or confusion between the similarly-named communities.

2. **Cause is more specific than described.** The raw file says "cutting concrete" in "an electrified puddle." The independent source clarifies the worker was using an industrial concrete saw that contacted an energized electrical line, and the worker was lying in approximately 6 inches of standing water. The puddle was electrified because of the saw contacting a power line, not from a pre-existing electrical fault in the water.

3. **The "second worker" was a rescuer.** The raw file says "A second worker was shocked." The e-Hazard article describes "at least one other person tried to move him, but he or she was shocked also" — this was someone attempting a rescue, not necessarily a second construction worker.

4. **CPR was performed by police officers.** The raw file says "CPR was performed and he was revived" without specifying who performed it. The independent source credits two Shiloh police officers who used a canvas tie-down strap to extract the victim and then performed emergency procedures until paramedics arrived. They received a Life Saving Award for their actions.
