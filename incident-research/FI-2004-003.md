# FI-2004-003 — Research Notes

## Incident Summary (from source PDF)
A 16-year-old boy screamed and disappeared while swimming near a houseboat at Ross Barnett Reservoir in Ridgeland, Mississippi on August 8, 2004. The fatal electrocution was caused by a homemade shore power cord that had shorted to the houseboat's metal siding.

## Conflicting Information
- **Date of incident:** ESDPA source lists August 8, 2004. HuffPost article (with direct mother's quote) states September 12, 2004.
- **Location state:** HuffPost article says "Jackson, Michigan" but Ross Barnett Reservoir is located near Jackson, Mississippi. This is likely a transcription error in the HuffPost article; the reservoir name is correct.
- **Mechanism of electrocution:** ESDPA source describes a homemade shore power cord shorting to houseboat metal siding. Mother's account in HuffPost describes Ken grabbing a boat ladder that was leaking voltage. These may describe different aspects of the same electrical fault (faulty shore power causing voltage on the boat's metal components including the ladder).
