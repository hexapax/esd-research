# FI-2001-002 Research: Farr Shores, Lake Hamilton Electric Shock Drowning (2001)

## Incident Summary (from source file)

- **Date:** June 6, 2001
- **Location:** Farr Shores, Lake Hamilton, Hot Springs, AR
- **Outcome:** 1 man dead (rescuer), 1 girl shocked/survived
- **Cause:** Reverse polarity and a neutral-ground fault on the boat
- **PDF Item Number:** #89

## Notable Finding: Date Discrepancy Between List Revisions

The earlier version of the ESD incident list (September 2007 revision, published on mikeholt.com) lists this incident as occurring on **September 15, 2001**. This version, which appeared as item #19 in a shorter list, describes the incident as:

> "Girl in great distress, man attempting rescue drowns, ground fault on boat, fresh water."
> Date: September 15, 2001
> Location: Farr Shores, Lake Hamilton, Hot Springs, AR

The current 2025 revision of the same list (PDF item #89) gives the date as **June 6, 2001**.

Additionally, in the 2025 revision, the incident immediately preceding this one (PDF item #88, FI-2001-001, Bay Marina Boat Works, Biloxi, MS) is dated **September 15, 2001**. It is possible that the September 15 date was at some point reassigned from the Farr Shores incident to the Biloxi incident, or vice versa, during a list revision. Without an independent source to confirm the actual date, it is impossible to determine which date is correct.

## Additional Details from ESDPA-Derived Sources (not independently verified)

The 2007 version of the ESDPA list (via mikeholt.com) contains slightly more detail than the 2025 version. It describes:

- The girl received an electric shock after **water-sliding into the water at the stern** of a boat
- The electrical faults were: **reverse polarity, neutral to ground fault in a light fixture on the boat, and poor bonding to the service**
- The boat was **unplugged in time to save the girl**
- A man who attempted to rescue the girl **drowned**
- The environment was **fresh water**

The 2025 revision in the incident file has a shorter description: "A man attempting a rescue drowned at Farr Shores on Lake Hamilton, Hot Springs, Arkansas. A girl was shocked but saved. Reverse polarity and a neutral-ground fault on the boat were found."

## Context: Other Lake Hamilton ESD Incidents

Three ESD deaths have occurred at Lake Hamilton total, according to KATV news reporting (https://katv.com/news/local/attorney-calls-for-stricter-boat-dock-and-marina-regulations-in-arkansas). The other two are:

1. **May 11, 1991** -- A canoe with four boys capsized on Lake Hamilton. As they swam toward a dock, they felt a tingle. Three boys diverted; the fourth continued into the electric field and drowned. Cause: broken insulation on a dock wire. This incident resulted in the court case *Shackelford v. Patterson* (Arkansas Supreme Court), providing independent verification. (FindLaw: https://caselaw.findlaw.com/ar-supreme-court/1021065.html)

2. **August 23, 2008** -- Sara Crotts, 14, of Sherwood, AR, died from electrocution with drowning at Lake Hamilton Resort. Tamara Keele, 40, and her son Devin Keele, 7, were also injured. Cause: unbonded metal pole with halogen light and chafed wires energizing dock. This incident was independently reported by:
   - Arkansas Democrat-Gazette (Aug. 31, 2008): https://www.arkansasonline.com/news/2008/aug/31/teen-electrocuted-lake-official-says-20080831/
   - KAIT8 (Aug. 2008): https://www.kait8.com/story/8891852/teen-dies-2-injured-at-lake-hamilton/

Neither of these other incidents references or corroborates the 2001 Farr Shores incident.

## Corrections Needed

1. **Date is uncertain and possibly wrong.** The incident file states **June 6, 2001**, but the earlier (2007) version of the same ESDPA source list gives the date as **September 15, 2001**. The September 15 date may have been moved to a different incident (FI-2001-001, Biloxi MS) in a later revision. Without independent verification, the correct date cannot be confirmed. This discrepancy should be noted in the incident file.

2. **Description may be incomplete.** The 2007 version of the ESDPA list includes additional details not in the incident file: (a) the girl was shocked after **water-sliding into the water at the stern** of the boat; (b) the fault was specifically in **a light fixture on the boat**; (c) there was also **poor bonding to the service**; (d) the girl survived because **the boat was unplugged in time**. While these details come from the same ESDPA source (not independent), they were present in an earlier revision and may have been lost in summarization.

3. **No independent verification exists.** The entire incident, including the date, location, cause, and outcome, rests solely on the ESDPA list. No news articles, official investigations, court records, or other independent sources have been found to confirm any detail of this incident.
