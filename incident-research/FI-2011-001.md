## Research Notes

This incident represents a particularly dangerous scenario where active construction or repair work created an electrical hazard that was not recognized by people swimming nearby. The fact that the dock "was being worked on" at the time is critical — this suggests that:

1. Live electrical circuits may have been exposed during the work
2. Safety protocols (lockout/tagout, posting warning signs, restricting access) may not have been followed
3. Workers may not have anticipated that swimmers would approach the area during the work

The involvement of houseboats adds complexity, as houseboats typically connect to shore power via extension cords or shore power cables, creating multiple potential points of failure. The description "box or bare wire between 2 docked houseboats" suggests the electrical hazard may have been associated with shore power connections.

The fact that three people were affected (25-year-old woman died, 8-year-old girl shocked but survived, second woman shocked during rescue) indicates the electrical current was present in the water over a period of time, not just a momentary fault.

The lack of news coverage is somewhat surprising given:
1. The fatal outcome
2. Multiple victims (including a child)
3. Ongoing dock work suggesting potential contractor liability
4. Lake Sinclair is a major recreational lake in Georgia

Possible explanations for limited coverage:
- Privacy request by the family
- Incident occurred at a private residence/private dock away from public areas
- Local news archives from 2011 not digitized
- Settlement agreements with non-disclosure provisions

**Day of week verification:** August 15, 2011 was a Monday, which is less typical for a recreational swimming incident. This may suggest the dock work was professional (scheduled on a weekday) rather than weekend DIY work.
