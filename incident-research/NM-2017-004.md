1. **DATE IS WRONG.** The original file states "June 27, 2017." The Martha's Vineyard Times clearly reports the incident occurred on **June 21, 2017**. June 27 was the publication date of the news article, not the incident date. The date should be corrected to June 21, 2017.

The follow-up article from July 10, 2017, indicates the cause was suspected/identified as **faulty grounding work from a contractor performed 5-8 years prior, specifically associated with the transient dock's electrical system**. 3. **LOCATION CAN BE MORE SPECIFIC.** The original states "Menemsha Beach, Martha's Vineyard MA." The more specific location is **Crab Corner at Menemsha, Chilmark, Martha's Vineyard**. Menemsha is in the town of Chilmark.

4. **VICTIMS MORE SPECIFICALLY IDENTIFIED.** The original says "third graders" and "management," which is accurate. More specifically, the victims were **several West Tisbury third graders on a school beach outing** and **Chilmark harbormaster Dennis Jason**, who investigated and confirmed the electrical tingle.

5. **RECURRENT PROBLEM.** The follow-up article reveals this was a "recurrent" problem—the dock had leaked voltage before. The original file does not indicate this was an ongoing or recurring issue.
