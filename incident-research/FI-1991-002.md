# Research: FI-1991-002

## Original Incident Summary
In July 1991, one of four boys in a canoe continued into an electric field and drowned at Lake Hamilton, Hot Springs, Arkansas. The cause was broken dock wire insulation. The incident is classified as a fatal incident with 1 death. It is listed as PDF item #108 in the "Electric Shock Drowning Incidents -- Marinas" document by James D. Shafer and Capt. David E. Rifkin (Rev. 8/15/2025). The classification notes indicate an approximate date (month only) and that the same lake was the site of ESD #70 (2007) and ESD #89 (2001).

## Additional Details Not in Original File
- **Specific date:** Multiple sources consistently give the date as **May 11, 1991**, not "July 1991" as stated in the original file
- **Distance from dock:** The canoe capsized approximately **75 yards** from the dock (per the 2007 version of the list), or "several dozen yards" (per later versions)
- **Warning signs:** The boys felt a "light tingle" or "slight electric sensation" as they swam toward the dock, which served as a warning -- three boys recognized the danger and diverted away
- **Mechanism of death:** The fourth boy continued into the electrical field, where the current presumably caused muscle paralysis (electric shock drowning), preventing him from swimming and resulting in drowning
- **Specific cause detail:** The broken insulation was on "a dock wire hanging in the water," indicating the energized wire was submerged
- **Lake Hamilton ESD history:** Lake Hamilton has seen three total ESD fatalities, making it one of the most recurrent ESD locations in Arkansas. The other two Lake Hamilton incidents referenced in the original file's classification notes (ESD #70 in 2007 and ESD #89 in 2001) are separate events at the same lake

## Conflicting Information
- **Date discrepancy:** The original file states "July 1991" while multiple independent versions of the Shafer/Rifkin ESD incident list consistently report the date as **May 11, 1991**. The original file's classification notes acknowledge the date is approximate ("month only"), but the more detailed sources all agree on May 11, 1991.
- **Item numbering:** The original file references PDF item #108, which corresponds to the Rev. 8/15/2025 version of the Shafer/Rifkin list. Earlier versions of the same list numbered this incident as #38 (2007 version) and #64 (a mid-period version). This is not a true conflict but reflects the list growing over time as new incidents were added and renumbered.

## Suggested Corrections
- **Date should be corrected from "July 1991" to "May 11, 1991."** All three independently fetched versions of the Shafer/Rifkin ESD incident list (2007, mid-period, and current) consistently report the specific date as May 11, 1991. The "July 1991" date in the original file appears to be an error in transcription or extraction.
- **Description could be expanded** to note that the canoe capsized approximately 75 yards from the dock, that the boys felt an electrical tingle as they swam toward the dock, and that three boys recognized the danger and diverted away while the fourth continued and drowned. This additional context better conveys the nature of electric shock drowning.
- **Cause description could be made more specific:** Rather than "broken dock wire insulation," the more precise description from the sources is "broken insulation on a dock wire hanging in the water," clarifying that the energized wire was submerged.
