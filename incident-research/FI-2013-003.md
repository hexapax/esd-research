5. **Rescue delay not mentioned.** First responders on scene could not reach her immediately due to the arcing electricity and had to wait for Georgia Power to cut power to the line. The current was so strong it scorched the ground and melted cable into the ground.

6. **Classification questionable.** The original file lists this in the "Electric Shock Drownings" section, but this was not a drowning incident — it was a direct electrocution from a downed power line. The victim was on land, not in water. This should be reclassified as "Electrocution" or "Electrical Accident" rather than "Electric Shock Drowning."

7. **Standing water detail important.** The electrical current traveled approximately 25 feet through standing water / wet ground to reach the victim, demonstrating the danger zone around downed lines in wet conditions.
