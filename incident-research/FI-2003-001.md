## Critical Date Discrepancy

The original incident file lists the date as **August 3, 2003**.

However, **every version of the ESDPA source list that could be verified online dates this incident as June 5, 2004**:

- MikeHolt.com Rev. 6 (8/4/05): Lists as **June 5, 2004**, incident #39
- MikeHolt.com later revision (letterID=1442): Lists as **June 5, 2004**, incident #39
- OnTime59.com (Rev. 5/8/14): Lists as **June 5, 2004**, incident #39

The date "August 3, 2003" in the incident file does not match the ESDPA source list. The August 3 date may have been confused with:
- **Incident #37 (August 3, 2004):** A young man at Lake Travis, Austin, TX (the Joule marks case -- different incident entirely)
- **Incident #40 (August 3, 2003):** A diver at Bull Shoals Lake, Bull Shoals, AR -- a different location and incident

The year "2003" appears to be wrong based on the ESDPA's own data. The ESDPA list in all verified versions dates the Lake Wylie houseboat incident to **June 5, 2004**.

The fuller description from the ESDPA list (as reproduced in multiple secondary sources) provides the following details not present in the original incident file:

- **Two boys** were swimming at the bow of the houseboat (not just one)
- The boys "called for help"
- The **father** of one victim and a **friend** rushed forward

- The other boy **in the water** was not moving
- The friend "rushed aft to pull the shore cord" (disconnected shore power)
- The father went into the water but his son could not be resuscitated
- The ESDPA notes "the sequence may not be exact"
- The wiring errors were "apparently done by non qualified individuals"
- The ESD number in the older list versions is **#39**, not #83 (the #83 designation may reflect renumbering in the Rev. 8/15/2025 edition)
