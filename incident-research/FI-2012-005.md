## Corrections Needed

1. **DATE IS WRONG.** The original file states "July 25, 2012." Multiple independent sources (LAPD official press release, ABC News, CBS News, NBC Los Angeles, Daily News, KTLA) all confirm the incident occurred on **Wednesday, August 22, 2012**, at approximately 8:25 p.m. The date is off by approximately 28 days. This should be corrected to "August 22, 2012."

2. **Day of the week verification:** August 22, 2012 was a Wednesday, which matches all news reports. July 25, 2012 was a Wednesday as well, so the day of week happens to align, but the actual date is still wrong.

3. **Victim identification:** The original file does not name the victims. They were:
   - Irma Yolanda Zamora, 40, of Burbank, California
   - Stacey Lee Schreiber, 39, of Valley Village, California

4. **Location more specific:** The original file says "Los Angeles CA" which is accurate but incomplete. The specific location was Magnolia Boulevard at Ben Avenue, Valley Village (a neighborhood within the City of Los Angeles). The northwest corner of this intersection is the precise location.

5. **"Puddle" is accurate:** The description "electrocuted in puddle" is accurate. Water from the damaged fire hydrant created a puddle that became electrified when it contacted the downed electrical wires.

6. **"Car hit fire hydrant and electrical pole" is accurate:** This correctly describes the crash sequence. The driver (Arman Samsonian) lost control of his SUV and struck both a fire hydrant and a light pole.

7. **Additional victims:** The original file doesn't mention that approximately **eight other people** were also injured (shocked) attempting to help, including an LAPD officer. Only the two women died, but multiple others sustained electrocution injuries.

8. **Voltage:** The electrical current was estimated at **4,800 volts** by LAFD.

9. **Circumstances:** The original file doesn't clarify that both women were "Good Samaritans" who witnessed the crash and rushed to help the driver. They were not passengers in the vehicle; they were bystanders attempting a rescue.

10. **Driver outcome:** The driver, Arman Samsonian, pleaded no contest to vehicular manslaughter and received three years formal probation and 70 days of community labor.

11. **Civil litigation:** The families of both victims filed wrongful death lawsuits against the City of Los Angeles and the Department of Water and Power.
