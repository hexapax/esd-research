# Research: FI-1994-001

## Original Incident Summary
Approximately 1994, a young boy grabbed the support structure of an electrical junction box at Bolling Air Force Base, Washington, DC and received a lethal electric shock. Investigation revealed bare wires and a ground wire that had been cut. The incident is classified as a Fatal Incident with 1 death, categorized under "Electric Shock Drownings" in the ESDPA incident list (PDF item #103). The source document is "Electric Shock Drowning Incidents -- Marinas" by James D. Shafer and Capt. David E. Rifkin, Quality Marine Services, LLC (Rev. 8/15/2025).

## Conflicting Information
- **Date discrepancy:** The ESDPA Rev. 8/15/2025 source document (PDF item #103) gives the date as "approximately 1994," while the Mike Holt newsletter version (item #59) and the OnTime Service reproduction both date the incident to "February 1995." This discrepancy may reflect different source information, a correction in a later revision of the list, or ongoing uncertainty about the exact date. The Mike Holt/OnTime versions provide a more specific date (month and year), which could suggest they draw from a more detailed or different source, or it could reflect an earlier version of the list before the date was marked as approximate in later revisions.
- **Item numbering discrepancy:** The incident is listed as item #103 in the ESDPA PDF (Rev. 8/15/2025) but as item #59 in the Mike Holt newsletter version. This reflects the fact that the two sources use different numbering systems or that the list has been reorganized and expanded over time between versions.

## Suggested Corrections
- **Date:** The raw incident file lists the date as "Approximately 1994." Based on two independent reproductions of the incident list (Mike Holt newsletter and OnTime Service), the date may more accurately be "February 1995." Consider updating the date to "Approximately February 1995" or adding a note about the discrepancy between the "approximately 1994" date in the ESDPA Rev. 8/15/2025 list and the "February 1995" date in earlier versions of the list.
- **Description enhancement:** The raw incident file could be enhanced with the detail that the boy "reached from the water" to grab the junction box support structure, and that the bare energized wires were specifically "touching metal case inside junction box." These details, present in the Mike Holt and OnTime Service sources, provide a clearer picture of the incident mechanism.
- **No independent verification found:** All sources for this incident trace back to the same Shafer/Rifkin compiled incident list. No independent corroboration (news reports, military records, legal proceedings, OSHA data) was found. This does not mean the incident did not occur, but it limits the ability to independently verify the specific details.
