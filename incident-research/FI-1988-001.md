# Research: FI-1988-001

## Original Incident Summary
On April 23, 1988, a single drowning fatality occurred in Oklahoma in a fresh water environment. A fault in a submersible pump was identified as the cause. The incident is cataloged as item #110 in the ESDPA Electric Shock Drowning Incident List (Rev. 8/15/2025) compiled by James D. Shafer and Capt. David E. Rifkin of Quality Marine Services, LLC. The classification notes reference similarity to ESD #105 (August 1993, Oklahoma), another submersible pump fault fatality.

## Sources Found

### Source 1
**URL:** https://www.cdc.gov/mmwr/preview/mmwrhtml/00042081.htm
**Title:** Electricity-Related Deaths on Lakes -- Oklahoma, 1989-1993 (CDC MMWR)
**Relevant Information:**
- Government epidemiological report documenting five electricity-related drowning deaths on Oklahoma lakes from June 1989 through September 1993
- Two of the five deaths involved submersible pump faults: Case 1 (Sept 5, 1993 -- a 46-year-old scuba diver contacted a submersible water pump at "Lake A" in northeastern Oklahoma) and Case 4 (Dec 8, 1989 -- a 32-year-old man rewiring a submerged water pump at "Lake A")
- Four of the five deaths occurred at two adjoining lakes in northeastern Oklahoma; one occurred at a lake in the southern part of the state
- The report explicitly covers only 1989-1993 and does NOT include the April 1988 incident
- A 1989 inspection by the Oklahoma State Electrical Inspector of 116 commercial docks at the two adjoining lakes found 96% violated the National Electrical Code, with ungrounded electrical systems being the most common violation
- In January 1994, inspections of both commercial and private docks revealed 96% were not in NEC compliance, with open ground being the most frequent fault
- This establishes that northeastern Oklahoma lakes had a well-documented, chronic pattern of submersible pump electrical faults causing drowning deaths

### Source 2
**URL:** https://www.upi.com/Archives/1988/04/22/Student-electrocuted-in-pond-hazing-stunt/7322577684800
**Title:** Student electrocuted in pond -- hazing stunt? (UPI Archives)
**Relevant Information:**
- While this reports on a different incident (FI-1988-002, SUNY Albany, item #111), it is useful for cross-referencing
- The UPI article dates the SUNY Albany electrocution to April 21-22, 1988, just one day before the April 23, 1988 date of FI-1988-001
- Victim was Bryan Higgins, 20, a sophomore who waded into a campus pond and was electrocuted by a broken underground electrical cable
- This confirms that item #111 in the revised ESDPA list corresponds to a verified late-April 1988 incident, lending credibility to the adjacent item #110 also being from this time period

## Additional Details Not in Original File
- The CDC MMWR report describes the northeastern Oklahoma lake system ("Lake A" and adjoining lake) as having chronic, widespread electrical code violations at both commercial and private docks. The 96% NEC non-compliance rate found in inspections strongly suggests the 1988 incident occurred at the same lake system that produced fatalities in 1989, 1991, and 1993.
- The CDC report identifies the Dec 1989 submersible pump victim as a 32-year-old male who was rewiring a submerged water pump, and the Sept 1993 submersible pump victim as a 46-year-old scuba diver who contacted a pump. Both occurred at "Lake A" in northeastern Oklahoma.
- The two adjoining lakes in northeastern Oklahoma are not named in the CDC report, but the geographic description and clustering of incidents (four of five at the same lake system) points to a specific area with severe, long-standing electrical infrastructure problems.
- No victim name, specific lake name, marina name, or other identifying details were found for the April 1988 incident in any online source.
- The incident predates widespread online news archiving (1988), and the lack of a victim name or specific location makes targeted searching extremely difficult.

## Conflicting Information
- The CDC MMWR report covering Oklahoma lake electrocution deaths begins its coverage at June 1989 and does not mention any 1988 incident. This could reflect the report's stated time scope (1989-1993) rather than a factual conflict, but it also means there is no independent government documentation of this specific 1988 event.
- The classification notes in the raw file state the incident is "similar to ESD #105 (Aug 1993, Oklahoma)," but FI-1993-002 in the repo lists that incident's date as "August 1993" while the CDC MMWR report dates it to September 5, 1993. This is a minor discrepancy in the related incident, not in FI-1988-001 itself.

## Suggested Corrections
- No definitive corrections can be suggested, as the April 23, 1988 incident could not be independently verified or contradicted by any source found online.
- The cross-reference to "ESD #105 (Aug 1993, Oklahoma)" may warrant a date check: the CDC MMWR report places the corresponding incident on September 5, 1993, not August 1993. If the classification notes are referencing the CDC-documented case, "Sept 1993" may be more accurate than "Aug 1993."
- Consider adding a note that this incident could not be independently corroborated.
