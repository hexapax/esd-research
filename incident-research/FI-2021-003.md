## Corrections Needed

1. **DATE IS WRONG BY ONE DAY.** The original file states "June 3, 2021." All five independent sources (KRGV x2, Valley Central x2, Star-Telegram) confirm the incident occurred on **Friday, June 4, 2021** at approximately 11:20-11:30 a.m. The date must be corrected from "June 3, 2021" to "June 4, 2021." June 3 was a Thursday and corresponds to the severe weather that caused the flooding, not to the electrocution itself.

2. **Victim identification.** The original file only states "14-year-old boy." The victim was **Gustavo Delgado, age 14**, of La Homa/Mission, TX. Adding the victim's name would improve the record.

3. **Location could be more specific.** The original says "Mission, Texas." The specific location was the **3700 block of North Palm Leaf Circle in La Homa** (an unincorporated community in Hidalgo County, near the city of Mission). "La Homa, near Mission, Texas" or "Mission area, Hidalgo County, Texas" would be more precise.

4. **Circumstances are accurate but could be more detailed.** The original states "stepped in a puddle containing a live extension cord." This is accurate, but can be clarified: The boy was walking through a flooded street when he came into contact with (and was reportedly attempting to unplug) an extension cord that was fully submerged in floodwater. The extension cord ran from a house to a trailer home. The flooding was caused by severe thunderstorms the previous night and that morning. Note: Valley Central initially reported a "wire from the child's mobile home" before correcting to "extension cord," indicating early confusion about the electrical source.

5. **Extension cord characterization is accurate.** The original correctly notes "Extension cords are only for temporary use and this one was likely always exposed to elements and had breaks in insulation." While the news reports don't specifically mention insulation breaks, the fact that the cord was being used as a permanent connection between buildings (house to trailer) confirms improper use. The severe weather and submersion in water would have created or exacerbated any insulation failures.

6. **Classification is appropriate.** While this is technically not a "drowning" case (the victim did not drown but was directly electrocuted while standing in water), the inclusion in the ESD list is appropriate as it involves electrocution in a water environment due to improper electrical wiring.

7. **Day of week verification.** June 4, 2021 was indeed a Friday, which matches news reports. June 3, 2021 was a Thursday, which matches the timing of the severe weather that caused the flooding. This suggests the original file may have confused the date of the storm with the date of the electrocution.
