## Corrections Needed

1. **DATE IS COMPLETELY WRONG.** The original file states "June 27, 2005." Multiple independent sources (Lake Expo article, Find A Grave memorial, jury records, WeRemember.com) confirm the incident occurred on **March 18, 2006**. This is approximately 8-9 months later than stated, and in the wrong year. The date should be corrected to "March 18, 2006."

2. **Day of week verification:** March 18, 2006 was a Saturday, which is consistent with the circumstances described (teens hanging out at a friend's house, jumping in the lake from a hot tub). June 27, 2005 was a Monday.

3. **Number of survivors is understated.** The original file says "Dead: 1, Shocked/survived: 2" but the actual count was: Dead: 1 (Nicholas Harbison), Shocked/survived: 3 (Morgan Milfeld, Timothy Fitzpatrick, and Joshua McClure). The outcome field should read "Dead: 1, Shocked/survived: 3."

4. **Location name variation.** The lake is referred to as both "Spring Lake" and the subdivision as "Summer Set" or "Summerset." The original file uses "Summerset Lake" which appears to be a conflation of the subdivision name (Summer Set) and the actual body of water (Spring Lake). More precise: "Spring Lake, Summer Set subdivision, De Soto, Missouri."

5. **Description could be more precise.** The original correctly identifies "utility at fault (defective neutral)" which is accurate. However, the more specific cause was: Ameren's underground power distribution cable had a defective or missing neutral, causing neutral current to flow through the earth and into the lake, concentrating at the bonded metal swim ladder with a lethal voltage gradient of more than 2 V/ft.

6. **The incident was confirmed by legal proceedings.** The 2009 jury verdict provides strong independent corroboration beyond news reports. The jury awarded $2.3 million after finding Ameren liable 9-3, rejecting the utility's defense that dock wiring was responsible.
