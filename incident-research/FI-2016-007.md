# Research: FI-2016-007 — Smith Lake, Alabama Dock Electrocution (2016)

## Original Incident Summary

Per the ESDPA-derived file, on March 27, 2016 (or April 16, 2016 per near-miss listing — 20-day discrepancy), at Smith Lake, Princeville (or Priceville), Alabama, two teenage girls entered the water from a private dock. Both girls were getting shocked in the water. One girl drowned, the other was treated and released from hospital. The victim's father and his son jumped into the water to assist. The father blacked out after both were feeling the shocks. The power was turned off at the house, after which the father came to and survived along with son. A missing ground and faulty lighting fixture are suspected to be the cause.

## Corrections Needed

1. **DATE RESOLVED.** The original file notes a 20-day discrepancy between the ESD section (March 27, 2016) and Near Miss section (April 16, 2016). **The correct date is April 16, 2016** (with death occurring April 17, 2016). Multiple independent sources confirm this. The ESD section date of March 27, 2016 is incorrect and should be changed to April 16, 2016.

2. **LOCATION SPELLING.** The original file shows "Princeville" in the ESD section and "Priceville" in the NM section. **The correct spelling is "Priceville"** (Carmen was a Priceville High School student). However, it should be noted that the lake house was actually in Winston County near Double Springs, not in the town of Priceville itself. More precise location: "Smith Lake, Winston County, Alabama (near Double Springs; victim from Priceville)."

3. **Victim should be identified.** The victim was Carmen Elizabeth Johnson, age 15, a 10th-grade student and cheerleader at Priceville High School. This information should be added to the file.

4. **Friend who survived should be identified.** The other teenage girl was Reagan Gargis, Carmen's friend who jumped with her. She survived after treatment.

5. **Father and son should be identified.** The father who jumped in was Jimmy Johnson (Carmen's father) and the son was Zach Johnson (Carmen's brother). Both survived after the power was cut.

6. **Electrical cause more specific than stated.** The original says "a missing ground and faulty lighting fixture are suspected." The more accurate description based on family statements is: **water filled an electrical box on the boat dock, and the ground fault circuit breaker (GFCI) was not working**. The electrical current traveled through the metal dock components into the water. This is more specific than a "faulty lighting fixture."

7. **Date of death clarification.** Carmen drowned on April 16, 2016 (the incident date) but died on April 17, 2016. Both dates are relevant — the incident was on the 16th, death pronounced on the 17th.

8. **Time of incident.** The incident occurred at approximately 5:30 p.m. on April 16, 2016.
