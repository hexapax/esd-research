## Technical Analysis

### Significance of tingling sensation during dock maintenance:

The diver experiencing tingling near a pontoon houseboat suggests:

1. **Active electrical leakage** from the pontoon houseboat into the water
2. **Voltage gradient in the water** near the boat or dock
3. **Diver was in or touching the water** to experience the sensation
4. **Fresh water environment** (Saguaro Lake is fresh water, which can support voltage gradients)

### Likely causes:
- **Shore power fault** on the pontoon houseboat
- **Damaged shore power cable** with insulation breakdown
- **Faulty onboard electrical system** leaking current to hull or through-hull fittings
- **Lack of GFCI protection** allowing fault current to persist
- **Reverse polarity** or neutral-ground fault in shore power connection
- **Galvanic corrosion** creating current pathways through water

### Why diver recognized the hazard:
- Professional divers may be trained to recognize electrical hazards
- Tingling sensation is a warning sign of voltage in the water

- Immediate reporting and work stoppage prevented potential serious injury

### Effectiveness of disconnecting shore cable:
- If the hazard was eliminated after disconnecting the shore cable, this confirms the electrical fault was in:
  - The shore power system
  - The boat's electrical connection to shore power
  - The pontoon houseboat's onboard electrical system receiving shore power
- The fact that "work resumed without incident" after disconnection indicates the hazard was successfully eliminated
- This suggests the electrical fault was **not** in the dock wiring itself, but in the boat's electrical system or shore power connection
