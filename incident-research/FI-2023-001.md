# FI-2023-001 — Research File

## Original Incident Summary

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Date** | Feb 7, 2023 |
| **Location** | Detroit, Michigan (flooded home basement) |
| **Classification** | Fatal Incident |
| **Outcome** | Dead: 1 (man), EMS: 45 min to secure power |

**Description:** Detroit, MI. Man electrocuted while working in a flooded home basement. He was snaking a drain when flood water poured in and filled the basement. The electrically charged water caused the death. The exact source is unknown. Took 45 min for EMS to get the power off to retrieve the man.

## Corrections Needed

- **Location type:** ESDPA record says "flooded home basement" — should be corrected to "flooded apartment complex basement" (Russell Woods Apartments).
- **45-minute EMS delay:** The specific 45-minute figure appears in no news source. All sources confirm a delay waiting for DTE Energy to cut power, but the duration is unverified. Consider flagging as unconfirmed.
- **No OSHA record found:** Despite Stanley apparently working as a plumber at the time of death, no OSHA fatality investigation record was located. This may warrant further investigation via OSHA IMIS database or FOIA request.
