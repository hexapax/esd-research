# FI-2003-002: Research Notes

## Incident Summary (from original file)

- **Date:** June 2003
- **Location:** Bull Shoals Lake, Bull Shoals, AR
- **Description:** Diver found drowned 8ft from dock; 117 VAC on dock components from bad junction box; rescue diver felt shock 20ft away.
- **Outcome:** 1 dead

---

## Independent Source #1: Mountaineer Echo newspaper (via ARGenWeb Marion County Obituaries)

- **URL:** http://www.argenweb.net/marion/obit2003/query008.~htm
- **Publication:** Mountaineer Echo (local Marion County, Arkansas newspaper), week of August 14, 2003
- **Source type:** Local newspaper report republished on ARGenWeb genealogy archive

### Information contained:

- **Victim identified:** Glen Howard, age 52, of Sherwood, Arkansas
- **Date of death:** Found on Tuesday evening (August 12, 2003, based on the publication date of the week of August 14, 2003). Note: The ESDPA list gives a date of August 3 for the incident and August 5 for finding the body, which conflicts with this newspaper's timeline. The ARGenWeb summary states he was found on "Tuesday evening" and the article was published the week of August 14, 2003 -- Tuesday of that week would be August 12.
- **Location:** Twin Coves area of Bull Shoals Lake, near his dock
- **Circumstances:** Howard was found floating in the water wearing a diving suit and scuba gear. Initially suspected as a diving accident or medical problem. A diver investigating the scene experienced an electrical shock while in the water near the dock. An electrician then discovered that an electrical outlet on the dock was wired improperly, sending a current of up to 118 volts through the dock, dock ramp, cables, and into the water.
- **Autopsy:** His body was sent to the Arkansas State Police crime lab for autopsy. The autopsy concluded he died from drowning caused by electrocution.
- **Ruling:** Death ruled an accident; no foul play suspected.
- **Personal details:** Howard's wife called friends to check the property when he did not report to work. The family owned property at Bull Shoals and frequented the area to dive.

### Details NOT present in the original incident file:

- Victim's name: Glen Howard
- Victim's age: 52
- Victim's residence: Sherwood, Arkansas
- Specific location within Bull Shoals Lake: Twin Coves area
- That Howard was wearing a diving suit and scuba gear when found
- That an electrician was brought in to diagnose the electrical fault
- That the body was sent to the Arkansas State Police crime lab
- That the autopsy formally concluded drowning caused by electrocution
- That the death was officially ruled an accident
- That Howard's wife initiated the search when he failed to report to work
- That the family owned property at Bull Shoals

### Details that CONFLICT with the original incident file:

1. **Voltage:** The original file says "117 VAC"; the Mountaineer Echo article says "up to 118 volts." This is a minor discrepancy (likely rounding differences in reporting).
2. **Date:** The original file says "June 2003." The newspaper article (week of August 14, 2003) and the ESDPA list itself (August 3/5, 2003) both indicate the incident occurred in **August 2003**, not June 2003. The "June 2003" date in the incident file appears to be an error.
3. **Description of electrical fault:** The original file says "bad junction box"; the newspaper says "an electrical outlet on the dock was wired improperly." These are consistent but the newspaper is more specific about it being an outlet rather than a junction box per se.

## Corrections Needed

Based on the independent research above, the following corrections should be made to the original incident file (`FI-2003-002.md`):

1. **Date: "June 2003" should be corrected to "August 2003"** -- Both the Mountaineer Echo newspaper article (week of August 14, 2003) and the ESDPA's own incident list (which gives dates of August 3 and August 5, 2003) confirm the incident occurred in August, not June. The "June" date in the incident file appears to be a transcription error.

2. **Location could be made more specific:** The Twin Coves area of Bull Shoals Lake, per the newspaper report. The current entry says "Bull Shoals Lake, Bull Shoals AR" which is correct but less specific.

3. **Voltage (minor):** The newspaper reports "up to 118 volts" rather than "117 VAC." Both values are within the normal range of US residential voltage (nominally 120V) and this is a trivial difference, but "118 volts" is what the investigating electrician reportedly found.

4. **"Junction box" vs. "electrical outlet":** The newspaper describes the fault as being in "an electrical outlet on the dock" that was "wired improperly," not specifically a "junction box." The ESDPA list uses the term "junction box." The newspaper account, being sourced from a local electrician's investigation, may be more precisely descriptive.

5. **Victim identity (addition, not correction):** The victim was Glen Howard, age 52, of Sherwood, Arkansas.
