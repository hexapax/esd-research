# Research: FI-2001-003 — Lake Montclair Electric Shock Drowning (2001)

## Original Incident Summary

Per the ESDPA-derived file, in May 2001, two young boys died in the water near a pontoon boat at Lake Montclair, Dumfries, Virginia. Electric shock drowning was the cause. Battery chargers on a modified extension cord were found.

## Corrections Needed

1. **DATE IS WRONG.** The original file states "May 2001." Multiple independent sources (two Washington Post articles and the Morning Call obituary) confirm the incident occurred on **June 22, 2001**. The date should be corrected from "May 2001" to "June 22, 2001" (or at minimum "June 2001").

2. **Description could be more precise.** The original says "Two young boys died in the water near a pontoon boat." While accurate, the independent sources reveal the victims were not both "young boys" in the same sense -- one was 15 years old (Joseph John Salute) and the other was 11 (Maxwell Scott Casillas). The term "young boys" somewhat understates that one victim was a teenager.

3. **"Battery chargers on a modified extension cord" is partially accurate but incomplete.** The Washington Post investigation revealed the more precise cause: the charging system used an unapproved outlet on the dock, a spliced orange utility cord, and critically, the cord was plugged into the wrong outlet on the boat (a 117V appliance outlet rather than the charging outlet), which sent current directly into the aluminum hull. The "modified extension cord" is confirmed, but the wrong-outlet connection was the proximate electrical cause.

4. **Location is slightly imprecise.** The original says "Lake Montclair, Dumfries VA." The specific location was a residence on Timber Ridge Drive, Dumfries, VA, on Lake Montclair. "Lake Montclair, Dumfries VA" is acceptable shorthand, though the lake is more precisely within the Montclair community (an unincorporated area in Prince William County near Dumfries).
