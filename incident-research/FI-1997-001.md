# Research: FI-1997-001

## Original Incident Summary
A boy walking on ice slipped and grabbed exposed wires on a dock at an Air Force Base in Washington, DC, and was electrocuted. The incident is classified as a fatal incident with 1 death, dated July 1997. The source file lists it as PDF Item #101 from the "Electric Shock Drowning Incidents -- Marinas" document by James D. Shafer and Capt. David E. Rifkin, Quality Marine Services, LLC (Rev. 8/15/2025). Classification notes indicate an approximate date (month only), winter/ice conditions, exposed dock wires, and a military base setting.

## Conflicting Information
- **Date/season inconsistency:** The raw incident file lists the date as "July 1997" but the incident involved a boy walking on ice. Ice conditions in Washington, DC would occur in winter months (December-February), not July (average high temperature ~90F/32C). The "July 1997" date appears problematic and is almost certainly incorrect given the winter/ice conditions described.

## Suggested Corrections
- **Date should likely be corrected from "July 1997."** The "July 1997" date is almost certainly incorrect because the incident involved a boy walking on ice, which is impossible in Washington, DC in July. A winter date (e.g., January-February 1998, or December 1997-February 1998) would be consistent with the ice conditions described.
