# Research: FI-1997-001

## Original Incident Summary
A boy walking on ice slipped and grabbed exposed wires on a dock at an Air Force Base in Washington, DC, and was electrocuted. The incident is classified as a fatal incident with 1 death, dated July 1997. The source file lists it as PDF Item #101 from the "Electric Shock Drowning Incidents -- Marinas" document by James D. Shafer and Capt. David E. Rifkin, Quality Marine Services, LLC (Rev. 8/15/2025). Classification notes indicate an approximate date (month only), winter/ice conditions, exposed dock wires, and a military base setting.

## Additional Details Not in Original File
- The wires on the dock **"were supposed to have been de-energized"** -- this critical detail from the earlier version of the incident list (OnTime59/Rev. 5/8/14) is absent from the raw incident file. It indicates a lockout/tagout or de-energization procedure failure, not simply the presence of exposed wires.
- A separate but potentially related fatal incident occurred at **Bolling AFB, Washington, DC in February 1995** (Item #59 in the Rev. 5/8/14 list): a young boy grabbed the support structure of an electrical junction box while in the water and received a lethal shock. Bare energized wires were found touching the metal case inside the junction box, and the grounding wire had been cut and never reattached. The occurrence of two dock electrocution fatalities involving boys at Air Force bases in Washington, DC within approximately 3 years raises the question of whether these occurred at the same facility.
- The Air Force Safety Center published an article titled "Electricity and water can be lethal" (https://www.safety.af.mil/News/Article-Display/Article/1199545/) on dock electrical safety, though it was not accessible for review due to a 403 error.

## Conflicting Information
- **Date discrepancy:** The raw incident file (FI-1997-001) lists the date as **"July 1997"**, but the earlier version of the ESD incident list (OnTime59, Rev. 5/8/14) lists it as **"Approx. 1998"**. Given that the source file also notes "Approximate date (month only)" and the incident involved winter/ice conditions (inconsistent with July in Washington, DC), the "July 1997" date appears problematic. Ice conditions in Washington, DC would occur in winter months (December-February), not July. The "Approx. 1998" date from the earlier list version is more consistent with winter ice conditions (e.g., winter of 1997-1998).
- **Item number discrepancy:** The raw file references PDF Item #101, while the OnTime59 source (Rev. 5/8/14) lists this as Item #57. This is explained by the list being substantially reorganized and expanded between the 2014 and 2025 revisions.

## Suggested Corrections
- **Date should likely be corrected from "July 1997" to "Approx. 1998" or "Winter 1997-1998."** The "July 1997" date is almost certainly incorrect because: (1) the incident involved a boy walking on ice, which is impossible in Washington, DC in July (average high temperature ~90F/32C); (2) the earlier version of the source list gives the date as "Approx. 1998"; and (3) the raw file's own classification notes acknowledge "winter/ice conditions." The date was likely either a transcription error or the month "July" was incorrectly associated with this record during extraction from the PDF. A winter date (e.g., January-February 1998, or December 1997-February 1998) would be consistent with all known details.
- **Description should include the detail that wires "were supposed to have been de-energized."** This is present in the earlier version of the incident list and provides important context about the cause of the fatality (failure of de-energization procedures rather than merely the presence of exposed wires).
- **The specific Air Force base is likely Bolling AFB (now Joint Base Anacostia-Bolling)**, which is the primary AF installation in Washington, DC and has a marina/dock facility (Capital Cove Marina). This could be noted in the location field for clarity.
