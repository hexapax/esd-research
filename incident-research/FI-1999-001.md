# Research: FI-1999-001

## Original Incident Summary
Two boys (ages 21 and 22) died from electric shock drowning at Tims Ford Lake, Winchester, Tennessee on August 1, 1999. The cause was a live wire in the water near a dock. The incident is classified as a fatal incident (double fatality) and is listed as PDF item #94 in the ESDPA source document. A classification note indicates this is the same lake as ESD #51 (Sep 2012) but at a different location/year.

## Additional Details Not in Original File
- A rescue diver felt an electrical shock while attempting to recover the victims, confirming that the electrical hazard was active and dangerous at the time of the rescue effort (Mike Holt sources)
- The electrical work on the dock was described as "improperly installed" on a newly constructed dock, providing more detail on the root cause beyond just "live wire" (Tennessee Injury Law Center)
- Tennessee subsequently developed new residential dock installation standards in response to multiple ESD fatalities including this one (Tennessee Injury Law Center)
- There was a separate ESD death at Tims Ford Lake around 2013 involving an adult male and an improperly grounded dock, making this lake the site of at least three ESD fatalities across multiple incidents (Tennessee Injury Law Center)
- The incident has been assigned different item numbers across different revisions of ESD incident compilations: #15 (early Mike Holt list of ~34 incidents), #50 (later expanded list), and #94 (ESDPA Rev. 8/15/2025 list)

## Conflicting Information
- **Date discrepancy:** The original file lists the date as **August 1, 1999**. However, multiple independent sources (Mike Holt's ESD incident lists, OnTime Service's compilation) consistently record the date as **September 30, 2000**. No source found during this research corroborates the August 1, 1999 date for this incident. Notably, the August 1, 1999 date corresponds to the well-documented Lucas Ritz ESD death in Portland, Oregon (an 8-year-old boy at a marina on a tributary of the Willamette River), suggesting a possible date mix-up in the source document.
- **Terminology:** The original file describes the victims as "boys" aged 21 and 22. While "boys" is used across multiple sources, at ages 21 and 22 the victims were legally adults/young men. This appears to be a colloquial usage consistent across all sources rather than an error.

## Suggested Corrections
- **Date should likely be corrected from August 1, 1999 to September 30, 2000.** All independent sources that document this specific incident (Tims Ford Lake, Winchester, TN, two victims ages 21 and 22, live wire near dock) consistently list the date as September 30, 2000. The August 1, 1999 date may have been inadvertently assigned from the Lucas Ritz ESD incident in Oregon.
- **Consider updating the description** to note that a rescue diver also felt electric shock, and that the cause was specifically improperly installed electrical work on a newly constructed dock, not merely a generic "live wire."
- **Item number reference (#94)** appears to reflect a later revision of the ESDPA incident list. Earlier versions of the same list numbered this incident as #15 and #50, which may be relevant for cross-referencing.
