## Additional Context

- **Occupational incident:** The victim was performing electrical work (rewiring a submerged water pump and stringing wire to a relay station) rather than swimming or recreating. This distinguishes it from typical recreational ESD cases.

- **Physical evidence:** Co-workers found blood on rocks along the shoreline, and the victim was found under 25 feet of water with a laceration above his right eye. This suggests he may have been shocked, fell and struck rocks, then entered the water or was pulled underwater.

- **Regulatory response:** Following the five deaths documented in the CDC report (1989-1993), the Oklahoma State Department of Health recommended that the local dam authority require electrical inspection of all commercial docks before issuing permits, terminate electrical service to docks failing inspection, and require regular inspections (every 3 years for commercial, every 2 years for private docks).
