# Research: FI-1989-001

## Original Incident Summary
A single drowning fatality occurred in Oklahoma in December 1989. A fault in dock wiring was found. The incident took place in a fresh water environment. The original source provides only minimal details and an approximate date (month only).

## Sources Found

### Source 1
**URL:** https://www.cdc.gov/mmwr/preview/mmwrhtml/00042081.htm
**Title:** Electricity-Related Deaths on Lakes -- Oklahoma, 1989-1993 (CDC MMWR)
**Relevant Information:**
- Between June 1989 and September 1993, five males aged 13-50 died from electricity-related incidents at Oklahoma lakes.
- Four of the five deaths occurred at two adjoining lakes in northeastern Oklahoma (referred to as "Lake A" in the report); one occurred at a lake in the southern part of the state ("Lake B").
- The December 1989 incident is identified as **Case 4** in the CDC report: a **32-year-old male** who was **rewiring a submerged water pump and stringing electrical wire to a relay station** on December 8, 1989, at Lake A (northeastern Oklahoma).
- Co-workers found blood on rocks along the shore. A lake patrol diver discovered the victim under 25 feet of water, alive but with a laceration above his right eye. Resuscitation efforts failed.
- The cause of death was listed as **drowning**.
- A 1989 inspection of 116 commercial docks in the area found that **96% did not comply with the National Electrical Code (NEC)**, with the most common violation being ungrounded electrical systems.

### Source 2

**Title:** Electric Shock Drowning Incidents (Mike Holt Enterprises)
**Relevant Information:**
- Lists a December 1989 Oklahoma incident (Item #66 in their chronological listing) as a single drowning.
- The cause is described as a "fault in submersible pump, fresh water."
- No victim name or specific location is provided in this source.
- The listing notes that many early cases have limited information, as the database was compiled from various sources including investigations, press reports, third-party accounts, and eyewitness reports.

### Source 3
**URL:** https://www.cdc.gov/Mmwr/preview/mmwrhtml/00042081.htm
**Title:** CDC MMWR -- Additional Context from Investigation
**Relevant Information:**
- The CDC report documents that four of the five Oklahoma lake deaths (June 1989 through September 1993) occurred at two adjoining lakes in northeastern Oklahoma.
- The investigation found widespread electrical code violations at commercial docks in the area.
- The report identified submersible water pumps and dock wiring as recurrent sources of electrical hazards.
- Another Oklahoma death in June 1989 (Case 5) involved a 36-year-old man installing a fuel pump on a houseboat who died from contact with broken light bulb wires connected to a 110-volt line, also at Lake A.

## Additional Details Not in Original File
- The CDC report gives the **exact date as December 8, 1989**, whereas the original file states only "December 1989."
- The **victim was a 32-year-old male**.
- The victim was **actively rewiring a submerged water pump and stringing electrical wire to a relay station** at the time of the incident, not simply swimming or recreating near faulty dock wiring.
- Co-workers found **blood on rocks** along the shore, and the victim was found **under 25 feet of water** with a **laceration above his right eye**, suggesting he may have been incapacitated by electrical shock, struck his head on rocks, and then fell or was pulled into the water.
- The incident occurred at a lake in **northeastern Oklahoma** (one of two adjoining lakes designated "Lake A" in the CDC report).
- The CDC report identified this as one of five electricity-related lake deaths in Oklahoma between 1989 and 1993, prompting an investigation that found **96% of 116 commercial docks** in violation of the National Electrical Code.

## Conflicting Information
- The original file describes the cause as "a fault in dock wiring," but the CDC report (Case 4) and the Mike Holt listing both identify the fault as being in a **submersible pump**, not dock wiring per se. The victim was rewiring a submerged water pump, and the electrical fault appears to have originated from that pump or its associated wiring rather than from general dock wiring infrastructure.
- The original file categorizes this under "Electric Shock Drownings" in the source section, which is consistent with the CDC findings, though the CDC report lists the cause of death simply as "drowning" with electrical contact as the contributing factor.

## Suggested Corrections
- **Cause/Fault description:** Change "A fault in dock wiring was found" to "A fault in a submersible pump was involved" or "The victim was electrocuted while rewiring a submerged water pump." The CDC report clearly identifies the hazard as a submersible water pump, not general dock wiring. Other Oklahoma incidents in the same CDC series did involve dock wiring (e.g., July 1991 and July 1993 cases), but the December 1989 case specifically involved a submersible pump.
- **Date precision:** The exact date of December 8, 1989 is available from the CDC report and could replace the approximate "December 1989."
- **Additional context:** The victim was a 32-year-old male who was performing electrical work (rewiring a submerged water pump) at the time, not a swimmer or recreational user of the water. This is a significant contextual detail for classification purposes.
