# Research: FI-1988-002 — SUNY Albany Indian Lake Electrocution (1988)

## Original Incident Summary

A sophomore at the State University of New York at Albany waded into a campus pond and was electrocuted. A broken electrical cable underground was found. The incident is dated July 1988, with one fatality. Classified under Electric Shock Drownings.

## Corrections Needed

1. **DATE IS WRONG.** The original file states "July 1988." Multiple independent sources — the contemporary UPI article (April 22, 1988), the NYT article (April 23, 1988), and The Athletic's 2018 retrospective — all confirm the incident occurred on **April 21, 1988** (Thursday evening). The date should be corrected from "July 1988" to "April 21, 1988."

2. **University name minor correction.** The original file says "New York State University at Albany." The correct name is "State University of New York at Albany" (SUNY Albany), now known as the University at Albany.

3. **Missing fraternity context.** The original file does not mention that this occurred during a Tau Kappa Epsilon (TKE) fraternity initiation ritual, which is significant context reported by all sources. While officials ruled the death was accidental and not technically hazing (since all parties entered voluntarily without knowledge of the electrical hazard), the fraternity context is important for understanding the circumstances.

4. **Victim identification available.** The victim was Bryan Higgins, age 20, a sophomore from Mahopac, New York. The pond was known as Indian Lake (approximately 150 yards long, concrete-bottom drainage pond).

5. **Additional injuries.** Two other students (Scott Payne and Vincent Mancino) were also shocked when they entered the water to rescue Higgins but managed to exit and survived.

6. **Cause of death.** Autopsy confirmed cardiac arrest due to electrocution. The source of the current was a faulty underground cable near an electrically powered pump house approximately 20 feet from shore.
