# Research: FI-1988-002

## Original Incident Summary
A sophomore at the State University of New York at Albany waded into a campus pond and was electrocuted. A broken electrical cable underground was found. The incident is dated July 1988, with one fatality. Classified under Electric Shock Drownings.

**Title:** Student electrocuted in pond -- hazing stunt? (UPI Archives, April 22, 1988)
**Relevant Information:**
- The victim was Bryan Higgins, age 20, a sophomore from Mahopac, New York
- He was electrocuted on Thursday evening, April 21, 1988 (not July 1988 as the original file states)
- Higgins stripped to his underwear and waded into the pond at about 6:15 p.m. Thursday
- He was up to his waist in water when he started to shake and then fell into the water
- Two other students, Scott Payne and Vincent Mancino, went in after Higgins but were shocked and managed to get out
- An autopsy showed Higgins died of cardiac arrest due to electrocution
- The source of the current was not immediately known, but there was an electrically powered pump house about 20 feet from shore
- The pond was used to water the grounds of the campus
- The current in the pond disappeared Thursday night when switches to the circuits were shut off
- Albany County District Attorney Sol Greenberg said the death appeared to be accidental
- The university was investigating whether illegal hazing was involved

## Conflicting Information
- **Date discrepancy:** The original file states "July 1988" but multiple sources (including the UPI article dated April 22, 1988, reporting on the previous day's events) confirm the incident occurred on April 21, 1988. The date is unambiguously April, not July.
- **Cause description:** The original file describes "a broken electrical cable underground." Sources describe both a "faulty underground cable" and an "electrically powered pump house about 20 feet from shore" as potential sources of the current. The UPI article from the day after the incident notes the source was "not immediately known" but mentions the pump house; later sources (The Athletic) attribute it to a faulty underground cable. Both descriptions may be correct (a faulty underground cable connecting to the pump house).
- **University name:** The original file says "New York State University at Albany." The correct name is "State University of New York at Albany" (SUNY Albany), now known as the University at Albany.
- **Hazing context omitted:** The original file does not mention the fraternity initiation context, which is a significant detail reported by all sources.

## Suggested Corrections
- **Date:** Change from "July 1988" to "April 21, 1988" -- the UPI article was published April 22, 1988 reporting on the prior evening's events, making this date certain.
- **University name:** Change "New York State University at Albany" to "State University of New York at Albany" (SUNY Albany).
- **Description:** Update to reflect that the victim was Bryan Higgins, age 20, a Tau Kappa Epsilon fraternity pledge who entered Indian Lake during an initiation ritual. Note that two other students were also shocked but survived.
- **Outcome:** Consider noting the two additional non-fatal injuries (Scott Payne and Vincent Mancino were shocked).
- **Cause:** The description of "a broken electrical cable underground" is broadly consistent with sources but could be refined to note the nearby electrically powered pump house as the likely connection point for the faulty cable.
