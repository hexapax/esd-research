# Research: FI-2016-001 — Lopez Brothers Electrocuted by Downed Power Line (2017)

## Original Incident Summary

Per the ESDPA-derived file, on September 10, 2016, two brothers (ages 11 and 12) were playing in a field after a large storm downed power lines at Oakland Lake Park, Fort Worth, Texas. One boy stepped in a puddle electrified by live wires and was electrocuted. The brother jumped in to help and was also killed. The area was in a very remote low traffic area which delayed investigation of the downed power lines.

## Corrections Needed

1. **DATE IS COMPLETELY WRONG.** The original file states "Sep 10, 2016." Multiple independent sources (CBS News, Washington Post, NBC 5, and the funeral home obituary) confirm the incident occurred on **March 29, 2017**. This is an error of approximately 18 months. The date should be corrected to "March 29, 2017."

2. **The incident did NOT involve a puddle.** The original description says "One boy stepped in a puddle that was electrified by live wires down in the area." The actual incident involved direct contact with a downed power line hidden in overgrown brush in a wooded area. Alex stepped ON the downed power line itself, and Isaiah was electrocuted when he touched Alex to try to help. There was no mention of a puddle in any independent source.

3. **The sequence is slightly different.** The original says "The brother jumped in to help him." The actual sequence was: Alex stepped on the line and was electrocuted; a friend went to get help and told Isaiah; Isaiah then went to check on Alex and was electrocuted when he touched him. The description of "jumped in" makes it sound more immediate than it was.

4. **Additional context missing.** The incident occurred more than 12 hours after the storm, raising questions about utility response time — this became a central issue in the wrongful death lawsuit. The original file doesn't capture this significant detail about the delayed utility response.
