# Research: FI-2006-006 — Weiss Lake Electric Shock Drowning (2006)

## Original Incident Summary

Per the ESDPA-derived file, on March 18, 2006, a 24-year-old man was killed while rescuing a shocked friend who was trying to exit the water via a metal ladder at Weiss Lake, Cherokee County, Alabama. An electric windmill on a dock was involved in the incident. One person was shocked and survived.

## Corrections Needed

1. **Date correction (CONFIRMED):** The incident occurred on **May 21, 2006**, not March 18, 2006 as stated in the original file. This is confirmed by two independent newspaper articles and the funeral home obituary, all of which state Sunday, May 21, 2006.

2. **Victim identity (NEW):** The victim was **Jeremy David Whiteside**, age 24, of Rome, GA. Born November 7, 1981 in Floyd County, GA.

3. **Activity (NEW):** Whiteside and friends had been wake boarding / skiing / knee-boarding on the lake and were returning to shore when the incident occurred.

4. **Rescue sequence (CLARIFIED):** Friend Mitch Bowles was the one swimming to the pier who first felt the shock while reaching for the ladder. Thomas Ayer and Whiteside both entered the water to rescue Bowles. Whiteside was shocked within 5 feet of the pier. Bowles survived and helped pull Whiteside from the water; CPR was administered but Whiteside was pronounced dead at Cherokee Medical Center at 8:40 p.m.

5. **Electric windmill (UNVERIFIED):** The news articles do not mention an "electric windmill on a dock." They describe a pier with an unidentified electrical source. The windmill detail from the original ESDPA file cannot be confirmed from independent sources.

6. **Coroner (NEW):** Cherokee County Coroner Don Rogers ruled the cause of death as electrocution.
