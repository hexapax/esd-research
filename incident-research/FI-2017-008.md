# Research: FI-2017-008 — Evan Currie, 19, electrocuted at Miller Marina, Put-In-Bay OH

## Original Incident Summary

**Date:** May 22, 2017 (ESD section) / Jun 17, 2017 (NM section) — 26-day discrepancy noted in raw file
**Location:** Put-In-Bay, Ohio — Lake Erie, Miller Marina
**Victims:** 1 dead (19-year-old son), father shocked/survived, brother shocked/survived, dog recovered
**Outcome:** Fatal (1); near-miss (2)

**Summary from ESDPA list:** A 19 year old boy was killed by electricity in the water around the family boat docked at the Miller Marina. The boy's father jumped in to the water to rescue the family dog who had fallen in. The man struggled and went under. His two boys jumped into rescue. Both were shocked. The mother pulled the plug for the boat and the electricity stopped. The 19 year old was unresponsive and died. *NM section adds:* The father and other brother survived after receiving electrical shocks.

Multiple independent sources (13ABC, Cleveland Scene, Fox 8, Cleveland 19, WTOL, EC&M) consistently and specifically confirm the incident occurred on **Friday, June 16, 2017 at approximately 6:30 p.m.** Both dates in the original listing are incorrect. The actual date falls between the two listed dates.

Note: The NM section date of June 17, 2017 is likely the publication date of news articles (articles were published the day after the incident), not the incident date. The ESD section date of May 22, 2017 is off by nearly a month and source is unclear.

### Investigation findings
Sources indicate that **both the boat and shore power were inspected and found to be properly grounded and functioning correctly**. This suggests the electrical fault may have been elsewhere in the marina system or from another source, though no definitive cause was reported in available sources. This is notable as it represents a case where the obvious suspects (boat electrical system and shore power connection) were ruled out.

### Historical significance
This incident is documented as **Ohio's first reported electric shock drowning** in state history, according to ODNR officials.
