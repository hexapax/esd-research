# Research: FI-1999-002

## Original Incident Summary
An 8-year-old boy was tubing in a marina on the Multnomah Channel, Portland, Oregon in July 1999 and was diagnosed as electrocuted. An AC to DC short on a boat was found with 84 VAC measured behind the stern. The incident resulted in one fatality and is classified as an Electric Shock Drowning.

## Additional Details Not in Original File
- The victim has been identified as Lucas Ritz, age 8
- The specific date was August 1, 1999 (the original file says "July 1999")
- The family was a live-aboard family at the marina; the Ritz family lived aboard their boat with three children
- Lucas was swimming (not tubing) with his brother and friends, wearing a Type II life jacket
- His mother, Sheryl Ritz (a graduate nurse), was supervising from the dock and jumped into the water to rescue him, experiencing paralysis/numbness herself from the electrical current
- His father, Kevin Ritz, performed CPR but Lucas never regained consciousness
- The coroner initially ruled the death as a drowning; the death certificate was later amended to "electrocuted in water while swimming" after Kevin Ritz's own investigation
- The electrical cause was more specifically described as: a 12V wire lying on top of an AC wire had gotten hot enough to melt its own insulation and that of the hot (black) AC wire, putting 120V AC into the entire ground system of the boat (including engines and propellers), coupled with lack of an AC safety ground, forcing voltage and current into the surrounding water
- Kevin Ritz subsequently became an ABYC Certified Marine Master Technician and co-founded the Electric Shock Drowning Prevention Association (ESDPA) as a direct result of this tragedy
- The Ritz family lives in Scappoose, Oregon, near the incident location

## Conflicting Information
- **Date:** The original file states "July 1999" but multiple independent sources consistently report the date as August 1, 1999. The "July" date in the original file appears to be incorrect.
- **Activity:** The original file says the boy was "tubing," but multiple sources describe the children as "swimming" or "floating down the channel" while wearing life jackets. No source mentions tubing.
- **Age vs. source discrepancy:** One source (Steve D'Antonio Marine Consulting) describes the victim as a "nine year old boy," while all other sources consistently say 8 years old. The majority consensus is age 8.
- **Voltage measurement:** The original file states "84 VAC measured behind the stern." No external source corroborates the specific 84 VAC measurement. Sources describe Kevin Ritz detecting "AC voltage" with a digital voltmeter in the water, and the boat fault involved 120V AC shore power leaking into the water. The 84 VAC figure may represent the actual voltage measured at the stern (reduced from 120V due to resistance/distance), but this specific number is not confirmed in publicly available sources.
- **Fault description:** The original file describes an "AC to DC short on a boat." External sources describe the fault more precisely as a melted wire that allowed AC shore power (120V) to energize the boat's DC ground system due to lack of proper AC safety grounding/bonding. The characterization as an "AC to DC short" is broadly consistent but simplified.

## Suggested Corrections
- **Date:** Change from "July 1999" to "August 1, 1999" based on multiple corroborating sources
- **Activity:** Consider changing "tubing" to "swimming" -- multiple sources consistently describe the children as swimming while wearing life jackets, with no mention of tubing
- **Victim identification:** Add the victim's name: Lucas Ritz
- **Electrical details:** The "AC to DC short" description could be expanded to note that a melted wire allowed 120V AC shore power to energize the boat's DC ground system (engines, propellers) due to lack of proper AC safety ground bonding
