# Research: FI-1986-003

## Original Incident Summary
On June 8, 1986, a husband and wife drowned at Harrods Creek on the Ohio River, described as near Lexington, Kentucky. Two dogs survived the incident. The cause was attributed to a faulty light switch and missing ground on a nearby houseboat. Classified as a double fatality (married couple).

## Additional Details Not in Original File
- The husband has been identified as Detective Jack S. Deuser of the Jefferson County Police Department, age 41, a 20-year veteran of the department. His death is officially classified as a line-of-duty death (off-duty rescue attempt).
- The couple's boat was specifically a 20-foot runabout.
- The incident occurred at approximately 8:30 PM (2030 hours).
- Rescuers who attempted to help the couple felt strong electric shock themselves and were unable to approach the victims due to the electrified water.
- The dogs were rescued later, after the electrical hazard was presumably addressed.
- Detective Deuser is survived by three children.
- Harrods Creek Boat Harbor is the specific location within the Harrods Creek area where the incident occurred.

## Conflicting Information
- **Date discrepancy:** The original file states the date as "June 8, 1986." However, multiple independent sources consistently report the date as **July 29, 1986** (Tuesday). The ODMP, Louisville Metro PD, PSDiver archives, and the Mike Holt ESD incident list all agree on July 29, 1986. No sources were found supporting June 8, 1986.
- **Location discrepancy:** The original file states the location as "Harrods Creek, Lexington KY." However, Harrods Creek is a neighborhood of **Louisville, Kentucky** (in Jefferson County), not Lexington. Lexington is approximately 80 miles east of Louisville. All corroborating sources place this incident in the Louisville metro area. The Jefferson County Police Department (Detective Deuser's employer) served Jefferson County, where Louisville is the county seat.

## Suggested Corrections
- **Date:** Change from "June 8, 1986" to "July 29, 1986" -- corroborated by ODMP, Louisville Metro PD, PSDiver, and the Mike Holt ESD incident list.
- **Location:** Change from "Harrods Creek, Lexington KY -- Ohio River" to "Harrods Creek, Louisville KY -- Ohio River" (or more precisely, "Harrods Creek Boat Harbor, Louisville/Jefferson County, KY -- Ohio River"). Harrods Creek is a neighborhood of Louisville in Jefferson County, not Lexington.
- **Victim identification:** The husband can be identified as Detective Jack S. Deuser, age 41, a 20-year veteran of the Jefferson County Police Department. His wife's name was not found in available online sources.
- **Description enhancement:** The original description could note the time (~8:30 PM), the specific vessel (20-foot runabout), and the significant detail that rescuers were also shocked and unable to approach the victims.
