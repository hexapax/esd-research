# Research: FI-1986-003 — Harrods Creek Double Drowning (1986)

## Original Incident Summary

On June 8, 1986, a husband and wife drowned at Harrods Creek on the Ohio River, described as near Lexington, Kentucky. Two dogs survived the incident. The cause was attributed to a faulty light switch and missing ground on a nearby houseboat. Classified as a double fatality (married couple).

## Corrections Needed

1. **DATE IS WRONG.** The original file states "June 8, 1986." Multiple independent official sources (ODMP, Louisville Metro PD, PSDiver) consistently confirm the incident occurred on **July 29, 1986** (Tuesday). The date should be corrected from "June 8, 1986" to "July 29, 1986."

2. **LOCATION IS WRONG.** The original file states "Harrods Creek, Lexington KY." Harrods Creek is actually a neighborhood and waterway near **Louisville, Kentucky** (Jefferson County), not Lexington. Lexington is approximately 75-80 miles southeast of this location and is not on the Ohio River. The location should be corrected to "Harrods Creek, Louisville KY" or more precisely "Harrods Creek Boat Harbor, Louisville/Jefferson County, KY."

3. **Victim identification available:** The husband can be identified as Detective Jack S. Deuser, age 41, a 20-year veteran of the Jefferson County Police Department. His wife's name was not found in available online sources.
