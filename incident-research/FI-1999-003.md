# Research: FI-1999-003

## Original Incident Summary
On July 18, 1999, at Lake Mohave, Arizona, a young man was disabled and drowned while swimming to the stern of a houseboat. The cause was a homemade shore cord Y-connector that had disconnected. The incident is classified as a fatal electric shock drowning (ESD), with one death. The original source lists this as item #96 in the "Electric Shock Drowning Incidents -- Marinas" document by James D. Shafer and Capt. David E. Rifkin, Quality Marine Services, LLC.

## Additional Details Not in Original File
- The houseboat had a **neutral-ground bond** installed, which is a relevant electrical configuration detail not mentioned in the original file
- **17 VAC was measured behind the stern-drive** after the incident, providing a specific voltage measurement that quantifies the hazard
- The hull of the houseboat became **energized** due to the partially disconnected Y-connector, meaning the boat's metal hull was carrying dangerous voltage into the surrounding freshwater
- The incident occurred in **freshwater**, which is a critical factor in ESD events because freshwater's higher resistivity (compared to saltwater) creates voltage gradients that can paralyze swimmers rather than simply shocking them
- The incident is listed as **item #52** in the earlier (pre-2019) version of the Shafer/Rifkin incident list, and as **item #96** in the current Rev. 8/15/2025 version, indicating the list was renumbered or expanded over time

## Conflicting Information
- The original file states the date as **July 18, 1999**, while all external sources found reference only **July 1999** without specifying the exact day. The specific date of July 18 could not be independently confirmed or denied from available web sources.
- The item number differs between versions of the Shafer/Rifkin document: #52 in the version cited on Mike Holt's newsletter vs. #96 in the Rev. 8/15/2025 version referenced by the original file. This is not a true contradiction but reflects the document being updated and renumbered over time.
