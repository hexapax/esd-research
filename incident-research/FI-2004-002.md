# FI-2004-002 — Research Notes

## Incident Summary (from source PDF)
A 22-year-old male stepped on an electrical cable while exiting the water at Lake of the Ozarks, Missouri on September 13, 2004. He fell unconscious and could not be revived, resulting in a fatal electrocution.

### Source 3: Kansas City Star (dock safety follow-up)
- **URL:** https://www.kansascity.com/news/local/article26201008.html
- **Title:** "Death again stirs dock safety concerns at Missouri's Lake of the Ozarks"
- **Details:** 2015 article about ongoing dock safety issues at Lake of the Ozarks following the electrocution death of Marcus Colburn. References that at least five electrocution fatalities occurred at the lake since 2007, and that approximately 75% of the roughly 25,000 docks did not comply with safety guidelines when inspected after the 2012 incidents.

## Conflicting Information
- **Mechanism of electrocution:** The source PDF states the victim "stepped on an electrical cable while exiting the water." Sources 1 and 2 (Columbia Missourian and Kansas City Star) instead report he "was shocked by a dock cable and fell into the water while helping to remodel a lake house." These are materially different accounts — one describes contact with a cable underfoot while leaving the water, the other describes being shocked and falling in. The newspaper accounts should be considered more reliable as primary reporting.
- **Activity at time of incident:** The source PDF does not mention remodeling work. Sources 1 and 2 specify he was helping to remodel a lake house, which provides important context about why he was at the dock.

## Additional Details Found
- Victim confirmed as Tyler Deeds, 22, of Olathe, Kansas.
- He was helping to remodel a lake house when the incident occurred.
- His death directly led to 2006 code changes governing dock electrical installations at Lake of the Ozarks.
- Those code changes were largely ineffective; inspections after later fatalities (2012) found ~75% of docks non-compliant.
- Lake of the Ozarks has roughly 25,000 docks, most installed before 2006.
- Multiple jurisdictions at the lake complicated enforcement of electrical safety standards.

## Corrections Needed
- The incident summary should be revised to reflect that Deeds was shocked by a dock cable and fell into the water (per newspaper sources), not that he stepped on a cable while exiting.
- The summary should note he was helping remodel a lake house at the time.
- The regulatory impact (2006 code changes) should be added to the incident record.
