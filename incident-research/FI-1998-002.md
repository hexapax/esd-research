# Research: FI-1998-002

## Original Incident Summary
Two boys (ages 8-10) drowned at Rio Vista, California in September 1998. Prior to the fatal drownings, other boys had reported feeling a tingle while in the water and got out. Forty-year-old faulty wiring running under moored boats was identified as the cause, with insulation failure leading to ground faults. The incident is classified as a Fatal Incident with 2 deaths, sourced from the "Electric Shock Drownings" section of the ESDPA incident list.

## Additional Details Not in Original File
- The incident occurred in a **freshwater marina** environment, which is the typical setting for ESD incidents. Freshwater is less conductive than the human body, causing electrical current to pass through swimmers rather than around them, leading to skeletal muscular paralysis and drowning.
- The specific cause was **AC shore power system ground fault leakage** -- the deteriorated insulation on the 40-year-old wiring created substantial fault-to-ground conditions, energizing the water around moored boats.
- The warning signs (tingling) that the first group of boys reported are a well-documented precursor to electric shock drowning. ESD safety literature now emphasizes that any tingling sensation in water near docks or marinas should be treated as an emergency, and swimmers should immediately exit the water without touching metal objects.
- Rio Vista is located on the Sacramento River in the Sacramento-San Joaquin River Delta, a major boating and recreation area in Northern California. Multiple marinas operate in the area, with Delta Marina Yacht Harbor (built late 1950s) being the largest and oldest, though the specific marina involved is not named in any source.
- No victim names were found in any source. The ESDPA list and all derivative compilations describe the victims only as "two boys, 8-10 years old."
- No news articles, obituaries, OSHA reports, or court records were found for this specific incident. This is likely due to the approximate date, lack of victim names, and the fact that ESD was not widely recognized as a phenomenon in the late 1990s (many such deaths were classified as ordinary drownings at the time).

## Conflicting Information
- **Date discrepancy:** The raw incident file (FI-1998-002) lists the date as "September 1998," while the Mike Holt newsletter sources list it as "Circa 1999" or "Approx. 1999." The ESDPA source document (Rev. 8/15/2025) from which the raw file was derived uses PDF Item #99 -- the discrepancy between "September 1998" and "circa 1999" suggests either the date was refined between revisions of the list, or there is genuine uncertainty about when the incident occurred. No independent source (news article, police report) was found to resolve this discrepancy.
- No other factual contradictions were found between the original file and external sources. All sources agree on: location (Rio Vista, CA), victims (two boys, ages 8-10), cause (40-year-old faulty wiring/insulation failure under moored boats), warning signs (tingle reported by other swimmers), and outcome (two drowning deaths).

## Suggested Corrections
- **Date:** Consider changing "September 1998" to "Circa 1998-1999" or "Approx. 1999" to reflect the uncertainty found across sources. The Mike Holt newsletters (which are based on the same Shafer/Rifkin data) consistently use "circa 1999" rather than "September 1998." It is possible the "September" specificity in the raw file came from a different revision of the ESDPA list or an editorial inference. Without an independent corroborating source, the more conservative "circa 1998-1999" may be more accurate.
- **Description enhancement:** The original description could be expanded to note that the marina was a freshwater environment and that the wiring was specifically AC shore power wiring with insulation failure causing ground faults, rather than simply "faulty wiring."
