## Corrections Needed

1. **DATE IS WRONG.** The original file states "May 28, 2016." The independent sources (ABC15 Phoenix, Fox4KC, AP/Albuquerque Journal) all confirm the incident occurred on **Monday, June 27, 2016**. This is approximately one month later than stated. The date should be corrected to "June 27, 2016."

2. **He was not killed immediately.** The original says "He was shocked and killed while working on the light." More accurately, he was shocked while working on the light, was pulled from the pool by firefighters, was transported to the hospital in critical condition, and then died from his injuries. The outcome (death) is correct, but the timeline was not immediate.

3. **Additional critical context missing: wife and daughter were also shocked.** The original file doesn't mention that his wife and three children (including an 8-year-old daughter) witnessed the incident and were themselves shocked when attempting rescue. The wife specifically reported "feeling voltage in the water" (AP wire via ABQ Journal). This is a crucial detail illustrating the danger of secondary victims and energized water — a significant ESD safety lesson.

4. **"Trying to repair" vs. "change."** The original says "trying to repair a broken light fixture." The news reports say he was trying to "change" a pool light (ABC15, Fox4KC) or "change a lightbulb" (AP wire). While these could be the same activity, "change" is more specific — he was replacing the light, not necessarily repairing it in place. This is a minor distinction but affects understanding of what he was doing.

5. **Official cause of death classification.** The AP wire report and ABC15 both note that the death was officially classified as a **drowning**, with electrocution listed as a possible contributing factor. The original file implies a straightforward electrocution death. The distinction matters for ESD research: this is consistent with the electric shock drowning (ESD) mechanism where electrical current causes incapacitation leading to drowning.
