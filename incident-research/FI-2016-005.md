# Research: FI-2016-005 — Phoenix Pool Light Repair Electrocution (2016)

## Original Incident Summary

Per the ESDPA-derived file, on May 28, 2016, in Phoenix, Arizona, a 27-year-old man in a private pool was trying to repair a broken light fixture. He was shocked and killed while working on the light. The exact cause of the accident is unknown.

### Source 2: Safety recommendations from Phoenix Fire Department

**Source:** Same ABC15 article referenced above

**Phoenix Fire safety recommendations following the incident:**
- Turn off circuit breakers before pool maintenance work
- Install ground fault interrupters (GFIs), especially in homes over 25 years old
- Use non-contact voltage testers to detect electrical current
- Avoid entering the pool; work from the deck when possible
- Leave pool electrical work to licensed professionals

**Key details not in the original file:**
- Phoenix Fire Captain Reda Bigler emphasized this type of work should be left to licensed professionals
- For those who choose to do their own pool maintenance, shutting off the circuit breaker first is critical

## Corrections Needed

1. **DATE IS WRONG.** The original file states "May 28, 2016." The independent source (ABC15 Phoenix) confirms the incident occurred on **Monday, June 27, 2016**. This is approximately one month later than stated. The date should be corrected to "June 27, 2016."

2. **He was not killed immediately.** The original says "He was shocked and killed while working on the light." More accurately, he was shocked while working on the light, was pulled from the pool by firefighters, was transported to the hospital in critical condition, and then died from his injuries. The outcome (death) is correct, but the timeline was not immediate.

3. **Additional critical context missing.** The original file doesn't mention that his wife and three children (including an 8-year-old daughter) witnessed the incident and were themselves shocked when attempting rescue — a crucial detail illustrating the danger of secondary victims and energized water. This is a significant ESD safety lesson.

4. **"Trying to repair" vs. "change."** The original says "trying to repair a broken light fixture." The news reports say he was trying to "change" a pool light. While these could be the same activity, "change" is more specific — he was replacing the light, not necessarily repairing it in place. This is a minor distinction but affects understanding of what he was doing.
