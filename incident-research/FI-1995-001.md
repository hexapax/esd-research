## Conflicting Information
- **Date discrepancy (critical):** The source file (ESDPA Rev. 8/15/2025, item #102) lists this incident as occurring in "February 1995." However, all publicly accessible earlier versions of the Shafer/Rifkin ESD incident list consistently document the only Lake Mead houseboat ESD drowning as occurring in "July 1997" -- not February 1995. The description in all versions is identical: "Single drowning, fault on houseboat, freshwater." This raises the question of whether (a) the date was changed/corrected in the Rev. 8/15/2025 list based on new information, (b) there is a data entry error in either the older or newer version, or (c) these are two separate incidents at the same location with identical descriptions.
- **Item numbering inconsistency:** The Lake Mead houseboat incident appears as item #23, #32, or #58 in various older versions of the list, reflecting the list's expansion over time. The source file references item #102, which does not exist in any publicly accessible online version of the list (which top out at ~72 items). This is consistent with the Rev. 8/15/2025 PDF being a significantly expanded version.

## Additional Details Not in Original File
- Lake Mead is a freshwater reservoir on the Colorado River straddling the Nevada-Arizona border, managed by the National Park Service as Lake Mead National Recreation Area. It is consistently ranked among the deadliest national parks/recreation areas in the U.S.
- The 1990s was the decade with the highest number of drownings at Lake Mead according to historical records.
- Houseboat electrical faults are a well-documented cause of electric shock drowning (ESD) in freshwater environments. In such incidents, faulty wiring on the houseboat can energize the surrounding water, causing skeletal muscle paralysis in nearby swimmers and leading to drowning.
- No victim name could be identified through web research for this incident.
- The only Lake Mead houseboat ESD incident found in any publicly accessible online version of the Shafer/Rifkin list is dated July 1997, not February 1995.
