## Additional Details Not in Original File
- Lake Mead is a freshwater reservoir on the Colorado River straddling the Nevada-Arizona border, managed by the National Park Service as Lake Mead National Recreation Area. It is consistently ranked among the deadliest national parks/recreation areas in the U.S.
- The 1990s was the decade with the highest number of drownings at Lake Mead according to historical records.
- Houseboat electrical faults are a well-documented cause of electric shock drowning (ESD) in freshwater environments. In such incidents, faulty wiring on the houseboat can energize the surrounding water, causing skeletal muscle paralysis in nearby swimmers and leading to drowning.
