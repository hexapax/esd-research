# ESD-2006-03-18-1: Nicholas "Nic" Harbison — Spring Lake, DeSoto MO

## Summary

On March 18, 2006, Nicholas "Nic" Harbison, 16, a sophomore basketball player at DeSoto High School, was electrocuted while swimming near a private dock in Spring Lake (Summerset Lake subdivision), Jefferson County, Missouri. Three companions were also in the water: Morgan Milfeld (15) and Timothy Fitzpatrick (15) were rendered unconscious and airlifted to St. John's Mercy Medical Center; a fourth teenager was present but not seriously injured.

Post-incident investigation revealed 10 amps of current flowing in the dock's supply cable and 4-6 VAC measured just 2 feet from the swim ladder. A chain leading into the water showed evidence of arcing, and a dead muskrat and ten dead minnows were found at the water's edge. The root cause was a defective or missing neutral conductor on a nearby AmerenUE 7,200V underground power distribution cable, which caused neutral return current to flow through the earth and concentrate at the bonded dock ladder.

## Legal Outcome

In 2009, a Jefferson County civil jury returned a **$2.3 million verdict** against AmerenUE in a 9-3 vote. This was the first stray voltage utility liability verdict in Missouri history. The case established that a utility company could be held liable for neutral current flowing through earth and energizing private dock infrastructure.
