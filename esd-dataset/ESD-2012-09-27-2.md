# ESD-2012-09-27-2: Adriana Rhine — South Georgia Technical College Fountain, Americus GA

## Summary

On September 27, 2012, Adriana Rhine, 18, a student at South Georgia Technical College in Americus, Georgia, was electrocuted after entering a decorative fountain on campus to retrieve her young son's ball. When bystanders attempted to rescue her, they also received electrical shocks, preventing immediate intervention. Rhine was pronounced dead at Phoebe Sumter Medical Center. Post-incident inspection revealed 17 electrical code violations in the fountain. The state of Georgia later agreed to a $1.4 million settlement, the maximum allowed under state law, which was placed in trust for Rhine's 4-year-old son.
