# ESD-2012-07-04-1: Alexandra & Brayden Anderson — Lake of the Ozarks, MO

## Summary

On July 4, 2012, Alexandra Anderson (13) and her brother Brayden Anderson (8), both of Ashland, Missouri, were killed by electric shock drowning while swimming near the family's vacation home dock at the 6.5 mile marker of the Gravois Arm, Lake of the Ozarks. A chafed hot wire had shorted to the unbonded dock framing, energizing the water around the dock. Several adults removed the children from the water and performed CPR before emergency responders arrived. Both children were pronounced dead at Lake Regional Hospital in Osage Beach.

## Legal/Regulatory Outcome

Angela Anderson (mother) filed a wrongful death lawsuit against Ameren Missouri, arguing the utility regulated dock installations and should be held liable. The Missouri Supreme Court ruled **5-2 in favor of Ameren** in June 2015, citing Missouri's Recreational Use Act — which limits liability for companies that allow free public use of their property.

Angela Anderson subsequently became one of the most prominent national advocates for dock electrical safety. She testified before Missouri lawmakers in support of State Rep. Jake Hummel's dock safety bill, which would require new docks and those changing ownership to meet electrical safety standards and undergo inspections.
