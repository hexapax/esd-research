# ESD-1998-09-00-1: Deirdre McCormick — Lake Sonoma, CA

## Summary

In September 1998, Deirdre Mary McCormick, a 10-year-old girl from Windsor, California, was electrocuted while swimming alongside a floating dock at Lake Sonoma Marina. She touched a faulty electrical conduit with a frayed wire that had energized the metal. The marina, operated by a concessionaire for the US Army Corps of Engineers, was found to have multiple electrical defects. The incident prompted California legislation requiring marina electrical inspections, signed into law by Governor Gray Davis in 2001.
