# ESD-2002-05-00-1: Mother and Daughter — Lake Cumberland, Monticello KY

## Summary

During Memorial Day weekend 2002, a mother and her adult daughter were enjoying the sundeck of their moored houseboat on Lake Cumberland near Monticello, Kentucky. They entered the water to cool off and were both killed by electric shock drowning. A third daughter survived. Investigation revealed the houseboat owner had modified the shore power connection from 125V to 125/250V, miswiring the ground and hot wire, which energized the aluminum hull, railing, and ladder. Current levels in the water were estimated at 0.01-0.02 amps — enough to cause loss of muscle control in fresh water. The mother could not be resuscitated despite CPR by a witness, and the daughter was found more than 50 feet below the surface.
