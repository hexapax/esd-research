# ESD-2006-07-14-1: Michael McNash — River Street Marina, Port Huron MI

## Summary

On July 14, 2006, Michael John McNash, 20, of Port Huron Township, Michigan, was electrocuted at River Street Marina on the Black River near the 10th Street Bridge. McNash was attempting to board a docked boat when a faulty shore power cord energized the vessel and surrounding water. Despite the main power switch being off, electricity continued flowing through the defective cord, and the circuit breaker failed to trip. Two friends were also reportedly shocked. A state electrical expert from the Michigan Department of Labor and Economic Growth inspected the scene. Police determined no criminal wrongdoing. McNash was a 2004 Port Huron Northern High School graduate who had been a lifeguard at Lakeside Beach for three summers.
