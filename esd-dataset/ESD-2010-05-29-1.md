# ESD-2010-05-29-1: Michael Cunningham — Stonewall Jackson Lake, Weston WV

## Summary

On Saturday, May 29, 2010, Michael Andrew Cunningham, 15, of Bridgeport, West Virginia, was electrocuted at Stonewall Jackson Lake Marina near Weston. Cunningham, a Bridgeport High School sophomore, was swimming with three other youths behind a docked boat. He had exited the water and was standing on the back of the boat when he touched the swim ladder and received a fatal electrical shock, falling into the water. His body was recovered at a depth of 34 feet, nearly five hours later. The autopsy showed no water in his lungs, confirming death by electrocution rather than drowning. Another youth was also shocked but survived. His mother Amy Cunningham became a leading ESD safety advocate, and the Michael Cunningham Act was signed into West Virginia law on the third anniversary of his death, requiring GFCI outlets, emergency shut-offs, and recurring safety inspections at marinas statewide.
