# ESD-2005-06-28-1: Samantha Chipley — Cave Run Lake, Morehead KY

## Summary

On June 27, 2005, Samantha "Sam" Chipley, 19, of Wilmore, Kentucky, and three friends arrived at Scotts Creek Marina on Cave Run Lake to spend the night on a houseboat. Despite "No Swimming" signs, the four young women jumped into the water. Chipley suddenly began jerking in the water due to electric shock. A friend who jumped in to help also felt the shock. Another boater dove in to rescue Chipley but was shocked and barely escaped. Chipley could not be saved and was pronounced dead on June 28, 2005. The cause was a faulty battery charger on the houseboat that was energizing the hull and leaking current into the water. Her death inspired the Samantha Chipley Act in the Kentucky legislature, which sought to prohibit swimming near electrified boat docks and require NFPA compliance and GFCI protection.
