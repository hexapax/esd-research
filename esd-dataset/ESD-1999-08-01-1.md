# ESD-1999-08-01-1: Lucas Ritz — Multnomah Channel, Scappoose OR

## Summary

On August 1, 1999, Lucas Ritz, an 8-year-old boy, was electrocuted while swimming in the Multnomah Channel of the Willamette River near Scappoose, Oregon. He was wearing a life jacket and his face never touched the water. A melted wire on a docked boat had allowed 120V AC shore power to leak into the surrounding water. His mother was paralyzed by the current during a rescue attempt. The coroner initially ruled it a drowning, but Lucas's father Kevin Ritz discovered the electrical cause through his own investigation, leading to the founding of the Electric Shock Drowning Prevention Association (ESDPA).
