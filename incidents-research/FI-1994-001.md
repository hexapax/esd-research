# FI-1994-001 Research Notes

## Incident Summary (from source document)
A young boy grabbed the support structure of an electrical junction box at Bolling Air Force Base, Washington, DC and received a lethal electric shock. Bare wires were found and the ground wire had been cut.

## Additional Details
- The Mike Holt newsletter provides additional detail not present in the ESDPA source document: it specifies the boy "reached from the water" to grab the junction box support structure, clarifying that the victim was in or near a body of water when the incident occurred. This is consistent with the incident's classification as an electric shock drowning.
- The Mike Holt version also specifies that bare energized wires were found "touching metal case inside junction box," providing more detail on the electrical fault mechanism than the ESDPA source document, which simply notes "bare wires were found."
- Both sources agree that the ground wire had been cut, indicating either intentional tampering or negligent maintenance. The ESDPA classification notes describe this as "Ground wire intentionally or negligently cut."
- The incident occurred on a military installation (Bolling Air Force Base), which may explain the limited public documentation. Military base incidents may be investigated internally rather than through civilian OSHA or local law enforcement channels, and records may not be publicly accessible.
- Bolling Air Force Base is located on the east bank of the Potomac River in Southeast Washington, DC (now part of Joint Base Anacostia-Bolling since 2010). The base has waterfront areas along the Potomac and is adjacent to the Anacostia River, consistent with the victim being in or near water.
