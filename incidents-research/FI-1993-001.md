## Additional Details
- This incident is notable as one of the very few documented ESD-related fatalities in a salt water environment. Most ESD literature emphasizes that electric shock drowning is primarily a freshwater hazard because freshwater is far less conductive than the human body, causing current to pass through a swimmer. Salt water, being 50 to 1,000 times more conductive than fresh water, typically diverts current around a swimmer rather than through them. The classification of this incident as an electric shock drowning in salt water is therefore unusual.
