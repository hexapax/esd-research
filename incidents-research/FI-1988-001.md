# FI-1988-001 Research Notes

## Incident Summary (from source document)
A single fatal drowning occurred in Oklahoma on April 23, 1988 in a fresh water environment. A fault in a submersible pump was identified as the cause. The incident is noted as similar to ESD #105 (August 1993, Oklahoma), another submersible pump fault fatality.

## Sources Found

### CDC MMWR - Electricity-Related Deaths on Lakes, Oklahoma, 1989-1993
- **URL:** https://www.cdc.gov/mmwr/preview/mmwrhtml/00042081.htm
- **Type:** Government epidemiological report
- **Key Information:**
  - Documents five electricity-related drowning deaths on Oklahoma lakes from June 1989 through September 1993
  - Two of the five deaths involved submersible pump faults: Case 1 (Sept 5, 1993, a 46-year-old scuba diver contacted a submersible water pump at "Lake A" in northeastern Oklahoma) and Case 4 (Dec 8, 1989, a 32-year-old man rewiring a submerged water pump at "Lake A")
  - The report explicitly covers only 1989-1993 and does NOT include the April 1988 incident
  - However, this establishes that northeastern Oklahoma lakes had a documented pattern of submersible pump electrical faults causing drowning deaths, and the 1988 incident likely occurred at the same lake system ("Lake A" -- two adjoining lakes in northeastern Oklahoma)
  - A 1989 inspection of 116 commercial docks at the two adjoining lakes found 96% violated the National Electrical Code, with ungrounded electrical systems being the most common violation

## Conflicting Information
- The ESDPA source document (Rev. 8/15/2025) lists this incident as April 23, 1988 (item #110). However, earlier versions of the same incident list -- as reproduced on OnTime Service, Mike Holt newsletters, and the AuGres Maritime Museum website -- do not include a 1988 Oklahoma incident. The earliest Oklahoma submersible pump fatality in those older lists is December 1989. This suggests the April 1988 incident was identified and added to the ESDPA list in a later revision (possibly the 8/15/2025 update), which may explain why it has a higher item number (#110) than the chronologically later August 1993 Oklahoma incident (#105).
- The CDC MMWR report covering Oklahoma lake electrocution deaths begins its coverage at June 1989 and does not mention any 1988 incident, though this could simply reflect the report's stated time scope rather than a factual conflict.
