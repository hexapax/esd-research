# FI-1986-001 Research Notes

## Incident Summary (from source document)
A single fatal drowning of a diver occurred at Grosse Pointe Yacht Club, Michigan on July 29, 1986 in a fresh water environment. The information was relayed third-hand, making its reliability uncertain. This is sub-incident (A) of ESDPA item #113, which also includes a same-date diver drowning at Petoskey, MI (sub-incident B, tracked as FI-1986-002).

## Conflicting Information
- **Date discrepancy:** The ESDPA Rev. 8/15/2025 list gives the date as July 29, 1986, while the earlier Harbor Marine Consultants / Mike Holt newsletter version lists the date as "1987 or 1988" with the qualifier "no year confirmation." The more recent ESDPA revision appears to have resolved this to a specific date (July 29, 1986), but given the third-hand nature of the report, the exact date remains uncertain.
- **Level of detail discrepancy:** Web search result snippets from searches referencing the Harbor Marine Consultants document and related sources indicate considerably more detail about this incident than appears in the ESDPA Rev. 8/15/2025 PDF entry (item #113). The snippets describe a home-made shore cord, sheet metal siding with no chafe protection, the insulation being cut by boat motion, a friend feeling a shocking sensation, and the diver being recovered but not revived. It is unclear whether these details apply specifically to the Grosse Pointe sub-incident (A) or to the companion Petoskey sub-incident (B), or whether they may have been conflated from a different incident in the search engine's indexing. The brief ESDPA entry for #113 does not include any of these details.

## Additional Details
- The Grosse Pointe Yacht Club is a private yacht club located at 788 Lake Shore Road in Grosse Pointe Shores, Michigan, on the shore of Lake St. Clair. Founded in 1918 (originally as the Grosse Pointe Ice Boat Club in 1910), the club features a Mediterranean Revival clubhouse opened July 4, 1929, and a harbor with 252 slips. Lake St. Clair is a fresh water body connecting Lake Huron and Lake Erie.
