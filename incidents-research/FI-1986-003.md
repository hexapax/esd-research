# FI-1986-003 Research Notes

## Incident Summary (from source document)
Double drowning of a husband and wife at Harrods Creek on the Ohio River. Two dogs survived. A faulty light switch and missing ground on a nearby houseboat were found to be the cause.

| Outcome | Dead: 2 |

## Conflicting Information

1. **Date discrepancy:** The source document lists the date as "June 8, 1986," but all external sources (ODMP, Louisville Metro PD, PSDiver) consistently report the date as **July 29, 1986**. The July 29 date appears far more reliable given the convergence of multiple independent official sources.

2. **Location discrepancy:** The source document states "Harrods Creek, Lexington KY -- Ohio River." However, Harrods Creek is actually a neighborhood and waterway near **Louisville, Kentucky** (Jefferson County), not Lexington. Harrods Creek flows into the Ohio River in the Prospect/eastern Jefferson County area of Louisville. Lexington is approximately 75 miles southeast of this location and is not on the Ohio River. The Jefferson County Police Department (Det. Deuser's employer) further confirms the Louisville-area location.

3. **Description of victim entry into water:** The source document states the wife entered the water, then the husband followed, and "two dogs survived." The ESD incident list descriptions say two dogs jumped in first from a 20-foot runabout and showed distress, prompting the wife to enter the water, then the husband. This is a minor sequencing clarification rather than a true conflict.

4. **Dog survival detail:** The source document says "Two dogs survived," while one search result summary initially stated "all four drowned" (husband, wife, and two dogs). However, the more detailed ESD incident list descriptions confirm the dogs were rescued later, consistent with the source document. The "all four drowned" appears to have been an inaccurate summary.

## Additional Details

- **Victim identified as law enforcement officer:** The husband was Detective Jack S. Deuser, a 20-year veteran of the Jefferson County Police Department, age 41. He was off-duty at the time. This is classified as a line-of-duty death because he died attempting a rescue.
- **Time of incident:** Approximately 2030 hours (8:30 PM).
- **Vessel involved:** The couple's own 20-foot runabout was nearby; the electrical fault was on a separate houseboat in the area.
- **Rescue attempts impeded:** Other rescuers who attempted to help felt strong electric shock in the water and were unable to approach the victims, demonstrating the widespread area of electrified water.
- **Surviving family:** Detective Deuser was survived by three children. One son appears to be Jack S. Deuser Jr. (goes by Jon Deuser), who later became President/CEO of Smith-Free Group LLC.
- **Burial:** Detective Deuser is buried at Cave Hill Cemetery in Louisville, KY.
- **Agency:** Jefferson County Police Department (merged into Louisville Metro Police Department in 2003).
- **Wife's name:** Not definitively confirmed in available online sources. A Marilyn Deuser obituary from Louisville exists but appears to be a different person.
