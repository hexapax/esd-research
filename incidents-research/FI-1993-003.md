# FI-1993-003 — Research Notes

## Incident Summary (from source PDF)
Two teenage girls snorkeling near a dock at Alexandria Bay, New York were paralyzed by electric shock and drowned. The dock wiring had been gnawed by rodents, creating the electrical fault. The source document lists the date as "July 1993" (approximate, month only). Alexandria Bay is a village in Jefferson County, New York, on the St. Lawrence River in the Thousand Islands region.

## Conflicting Information

1. **Date discrepancy:** The source PDF lists the date as "July 1993." An ESD summary site consulted during research (ontime59.com, excluded per guidelines but noted for completeness) lists the date as "August 1993." This is a minor discrepancy — both are summer months, consistent with recreational snorkeling. The source PDF's notation of "approximate date (month only)" acknowledges the uncertainty.

2. **Bystander injuries:** ESD summary sources indicate that two bystanders who entered the water to assist the victims were also shocked. Per those accounts, the bystanders were treated at a hospital and released. This detail is not in the source PDF, which only records the two fatal victims.
