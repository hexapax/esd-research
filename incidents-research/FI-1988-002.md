# FI-1988-002 — Research Notes

## Incident Summary (from source PDF)
A sophomore waded into a campus pond at the State University of New York at Albany and was electrocuted. A broken electrical cable underground was found to be the source of the electric current in the water. The source document lists the date as "July 1988" (approximate, month only). One fatality is recorded.

## Sources Found

### Source 1
- **URL:** https://www.upi.com/Archives/1988/04/22/Student-electrocuted-in-pond-hazing-stunt/7322577684800
- **Title:** Student electrocuted in pond -- hazing stunt? — UPI Archives
- **Date:** April 22, 1988 (publication date of news wire story)
- **Summary:** UPI wire report published the day after the incident. Identifies the victim as Bryan Higgins, 20, a sophomore from Mahopac, New York. Reports that Higgins waded into a campus pond at SUNY Albany on Thursday evening (April 21, 1988), stripped to his underwear as part of an apparent fraternity initiation. He was up to his waist in water when he began to shake and then fell into the water. Rescue personnel removed him from the pond approximately 20 minutes later; he was pronounced dead at Albany Medical Center Hospital. An autopsy found cause of death was cardiac arrest due to electrocution. Source of the current was not immediately determined, but an electrically powered pump house approximately 20 feet from shore was noted. Albany County District Attorney Sol Greenberg stated the death appeared accidental and said "It appears it would not have been from roughhouse hazing." The university was investigating whether illegal hazing was involved. Two other students — Scott Payne and Vincent Mancino — entered the water after Higgins but were shocked and were able to exit; they were reportedly injured.

## Conflicting Information

1. **Date discrepancy — significant:** The source PDF lists the date as "July 1988." However, the UPI Archives article (published April 22, 1988) and the 2018 Athletic retrospective both place the incident on **April 21, 1988** (a Thursday). This is a clear and significant discrepancy. The April 21, 1988 date from the contemporary UPI wire report is far more reliable than the approximate month listed in the source PDF. The "July" date in the source document appears to be an error.

2. **Date discrepancy — minor:** The ontime59.com ESD summary page (excluded per guidelines but noted here for completeness) lists the date as "April 23, 1988," which conflicts slightly with the UPI article's implicit "Thursday evening" date (April 21, 1988). The UPI article itself was published April 22, 1988, stating the incident occurred "Thursday evening," which was April 21. April 23 may represent an error in a secondary source.

3. **Hazing context:** The source PDF describes this simply as a sophomore who "waded into a campus pond." Independent sources add the important context that this occurred during a Tau Kappa Epsilon fraternity initiation ritual, which changes the nature of the incident. The DA determined the death was accidental and not the result of hazing per se, but the fraternity context is significant.

## Additional Details Found

- **Victim identified:** Bryan Higgins, 20 years old, sophomore, from Mahopac, New York (Putnam County).
- **Actual date:** April 21, 1988 (Thursday evening), not "July 1988."
- **Pond name:** Known as "Indian Lake" on the SUNY Albany campus (also referred to as "Indian Pond" in some sources). The pond serves as a stormwater collection and irrigation facility.
- **Fraternity:** Tau Kappa Epsilon (TKE). The initiation involved walking into the pond in underwear. Higgins dove in and was the first person in the water.
- **Cause of death:** Cardiac arrest due to electrocution. Source was a broken/faulty underground electrical cable; a pump house approximately 20 feet from shore was a suspect source.
- **Rescuers injured:** Two students — Scott Payne and Vincent Mancino — entered the water to help Higgins, were shocked, and exited. They were treated and survived.
- **Hospital:** Pronounced dead at Albany Medical Center Hospital.
- **Investigation:** Albany County DA Sol Greenberg said death appeared accidental and not related to hazing. University investigated potential hazing violations.
- **Legacy:** Bryan Higgins was a notable football player at Mahopac High School. He is the only Mahopac player to have his jersey number permanently retired. The 2018 Athletic article written on the 30th anniversary of his death describes him as a "legend."
