# FI-1991-002 Research Notes

## Incident Summary (from source document)
One of four boys in a canoe continued into an electric field near a dock and drowned at Lake Hamilton, Hot Springs, Arkansas. The cause was broken dock wire insulation.

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Date | July 1991 (source document); May 11, 1991 (per Mike Holt / earlier ESD list) |
| Location | Lake Hamilton, Hot Springs, AR |
| Classification | FI (Fatal Incident) |
| Outcome | Dead: 1 |

## Conflicting Information
- **Date discrepancy:** The ESDPA source document (FI-1991-002.md) lists the date as "July 1991," while the Mike Holt newsletters (both the 2007 and 2016 versions, which are earlier compilations of the same ESD incident data) list the date as "May 11, 1991." The ESDPA classification notes also describe the date as "approximate (month only)," suggesting the exact date may be uncertain. The May 11 date in the Mike Holt list appears more specific but may itself be approximate or erroneous.
- **Item numbering:** The incident appears under different item numbers across versions of the ESD list (#38 in the 2007 version, #64 in the 2016 version, #108 in the 2025 version), reflecting the list's expansion over time as new incidents were added and the numbering was revised.

## Additional Details
- No victim name was found in any online source. The incident is consistently described without naming the deceased boy.
- Lake Hamilton has been the site of at least three ESD fatalities: this 1991 incident, a 2001 incident, and the August 2008 death of Sarah Crotts (age 14, of Sherwood, AR), who was electrocuted near a dock with a faulty halogen light that energized the dock frame and swim ladder to 103 VAC. The 2008 incident also injured Tamara Keele (age 40) and her son Devin Keele (age 7).
- The pattern of repeated ESD deaths on Lake Hamilton contributed to the passage of Jesse's Law in Arkansas in 2011, which established minimum electrical standards for boat docks and marinas, though the law lacks enforcement penalties.
- No OSHA reports, court records, lawsuits, or obituaries were found for this specific 1991 incident. Given the age of the incident (1991) and the lack of a victim name, digitized records may simply not exist online.
