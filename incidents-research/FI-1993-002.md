## Sources Found

### CDC MMWR Report: "Electricity-Related Deaths on Lakes -- Oklahoma, 1989-1993"
- **URL:** https://www.cdc.gov/mmwr/preview/mmwrhtml/00042081.htm
- **Type:** Government epidemiological report (CDC Morbidity and Mortality Weekly Report)
- **Key Information:**
  - This incident is described as **Case 1** in the MMWR report.
  - Date: September 5, 1993 (not August 1993 as listed in the ESDPA source document).
  - Victim: 46-year-old male.
  - Activity: Scuba diving at "Lake A" (a lake in northeastern Oklahoma; the report anonymizes the lake name).
  - Cause: The victim contacted a submersible (submergible) water pump while diving. A witness reported seeing "a blue softball-size ball of flame emitting from a pipe" containing power cables.
  - Medical examiner determination: Electrocution.
  - This was one of five electricity-related deaths on Oklahoma lakes between June 1989 and September 1993.
  - Four of the five deaths occurred at "Lake A" (two adjoining lakes in northeastern Oklahoma); one occurred at "Lake B" (a lake in southern Oklahoma).
  - A 1989 inspection of 116 commercial docks at "Lake A" found 96% were not in NEC (National Electrical Code) compliance; the most common fault was an open ground.
  - A follow-up 1994 inspection by the Oklahoma State Department of Health of 11 commercial docks and 5 private docks found 96% still not in NEC compliance.
  - Recommendations included requiring electrical inspection of all commercial docks before permits were issued, terminating electrical service to non-compliant docks, and mandating inspections every 3 years for commercial and 2 years for private docks.

## Conflicting Information
- **Date discrepancy:** The ESDPA source document (FI-1993-002) lists the date as "August 1993," while both the CDC MMWR report and the Mike Holt incident lists give the date as September 1993 (specifically September 5, 1993 in the MMWR). The CDC MMWR report, based on medical examiner records, is likely the more precise and authoritative source for the exact date. The "August 1993" date in the ESDPA list may be an error or approximation.
- **Lake identity:** The CDC report anonymizes the lake as "Lake A" (described as two adjoining lakes in northeastern Oklahoma). Lake Tenkiller Ferry Lake in northeastern Oklahoma is a well-known scuba diving destination and a strong candidate, but the specific lake name is not confirmed in available sources.

## Additional Details
- The victim was a 46-year-old male scuba diver, not mentioned in the original incident file.
- The specific mechanism was contact with a submersible water pump while diving, with a dramatic visual indicator: a witness saw "a blue softball-size ball of flame emitting from a pipe" containing power cables.
- The medical examiner ruled the cause of death as electrocution (not drowning).
- This incident was part of a cluster of five electricity-related deaths at Oklahoma lakes over a four-year period (1989-1993), four of which occurred at the same lake system ("Lake A"). The concentration of deaths at one location triggered the CDC investigation and MMWR publication.
- The related incidents at the same lake include: a 13-year-old boy who jumped from a dock to swim and was electrocuted by a short in dock wiring energizing the metal frame (July 27, 1993, Case 2); a 50-year-old man electrocuted by a short in dock wiring (July 24, 1991, Case 3); and a 32-year-old man who drowned while rewiring a submerged water pump (December 8, 1989, Case 4).
- The widespread NEC non-compliance (96% of docks) found during inspections underscores the systemic nature of the electrical hazard at this lake.
